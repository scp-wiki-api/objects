**9023**

##grey|**CONTAINMENT CLASS:**##

**KETER**

 _

**Special Containment Procedures:** The vessel formerly containing SCP-9023 is to be kept in a standard object storage locker, and is only to be retrieved for the purposes of interpreting the hieroglyphics on its surface.

The individual who ingested SCP-9023 must be identified and apprehended at all costs.

**Description:** SCP-9023 refers to the contents of a rectangular stone jar with rounded corners, recovered from the dig site at the Temple of Taworakhty in the West Valley of the Valley of Kings. Upon recovery, the stone jar was still fully sealed. The jar itself is non-anomalous and is framed on three sides by a cartouche of hieroglyphics.

Due to the subject matter of the cartouche, Foundation agents embedded at the dig site discretely moved the object to a private Foundation tent. Approval was given to break the seal and inspect the contents. Within the jar was a translucent substance with a light green tint. Although researchers did not have the opportunity to perform official tests, all individuals present for the unsealing unanimously agree that the contents of the jar strongly resembled lime-flavored gelatin.

The assigned overseer at the dig site excused himself to confer with the O5 Council, and shortly thereafter, the object was left unattended in the tent. The exact cause for this lapse in security has not yet been identified, and a full-scale investigation is planned once containment for SCP-9023 has been established.

While the object was unattended, an unknown individual entered the tent and consumed all of SCP-9023. Due to the assumed nature of the anomaly, Foundation presence at the dig site would not have been capable of singling out the individual following consumption. It is unknown if the perpetrator, hereby referred to as PoI-9023, fled the scene, or remained in hiding amongst Foundation forces. The identification and recovery of PoI-9023 is presently the highest order of business, and takes precedent over all other assignments.

**Supplemental:** *Analysis of the Cartouche*

The current, most accepted translation of the cartouche is as follows:

> = Osiris-N,"Osiris-N" is a placeholder name commonly found on Egyptian funerary objects, used to refer to the deceased in a semi-divine form as an aspect of the death god, Osiris. "N" is replaced with the name of the deceased during ceremonies. No name is specified on the cartouche itself. who is smiled at by Set, is welcome to this [unknown] meal, which shall make Osiris-N a king in all places.

> = Osiris-N, who passed the judgment of Anubis, is blessed by this [unknown] meal, which shall make Osiris-N strong in the Field of Rushes,One of many names for the Egyptian afterlife. and deemed worthy of any task.

> = Osiris-N, whose name is recorded by Thoth, is transformed by this [unknown] meal, and shall never again be questioned, for all will know that Osiris-N needs no aid.

> = Osiris-N, who is warmly regarded by Ra, is eternal through this [unknown] meal, and may take of any name or power, for Osiris-N shall rise to all. 

This spell or prayer includes the determinativeA determinative is a non-phonetic series of characters used to describe the idea or action of the associated word. 𓍰𓍸𓎃, which has been found on several artifacts recovered from the Taworakhty digsite. From context, 𓍰𓍸𓎃 appears to indicate a subject that is in some way derived from the gods, and therefore requires special patterns or structures to safely interact with. It is believed to be an Ancient Egyptian term for the anomalous, and was the original reason for the Foundation's presence at the digsite.

**Dr. A. Kraken:** Director Leiner?

**Dir. Y. Leiner:** Yes?

**Dr. A. Kraken:** Thank you for helping me.

**Dir. Y. Leiner:** ...Anytime, Kraken. Just try to ask a little sooner, next time? Now, I need to confer with the representative. I'll be right back.

*[Dir. Y. Leiner disables their camera feed.]*

*[15 seconds of stillness.]*

*[On her camera, Dr. Kraken gets up from the terminal. She walks to the window behind her, where a triangle slice of the sky is visible on screen.]*

*[The sun is setting. Kraken turns her head to face it.]*

*[They regard each other warmly.]*
