Special Containment Procedures

SCP-9031 is contained at Site-███, sublevel 14, within a purpose-built vacuum-sealed chamber (designated Chamber 9031-Alpha). The threshold aperture is to be enclosed in a reinforced steel housing bolted to a seismically isolated platform. The chamber is to be maintained at negative pressure relative to the surrounding corridor to prevent atmospheric exchange with SCP-9031-A.

No personnel may enter SCP-9031-A without written authorization from two Level-2 researchers. Exploration teams are to remain tethered at all times via magnetically anchored filament cable rated to 12kN. Untethered entry is prohibited under all circumstances. Excursion time within SCP-9031-A to be no greater than 8 hours. Excursion teams must carry supplemental oxygen, closed-loop thermal regulation suits rated for environments below 1K, and electromagnetic shielding rated for hard vacuum.

All personnel involved in research on SCP-9031 must undergo psychological evaluation before assignment and twice a week thereafter. Personnel exhibiting symptoms consistent with SCP-9031-Σ (see *Psychological Effects*) must be reassigned immediately and entered into a Foundation-administered psychiatric monitoring program.

Per O5 directive, following the establishment of SCP-9031-Σ's cognitohazard advisory, access to compiled cosmological analyses of SCP-9031-A (documents 9031-COSMO-1 through 9031-COSMO-23) is restricted to personnel with 9031/Sigma clearance, granted only to researchers who have passed the Nkomo-Vasquez Cognitive Resilience Battery with a score above the 90th percentile after having been informed of the nature of the anomaly. Failure to score in the 90th percentile requires immediate administration of Class-B amnestics and reassignment.

Description

SCP-9031 is a spatially stable aperture approximately 3.1 meters in diameter, located 1.4 meters above the floor of its containment chamber. SCP-9031 has no detectable thickness. When viewed from either side under standard lighting conditions, it appears as a region of absolute blackness with no detectable albedo. No electromagnetic radiation directed at SCP-9031 has been observed to reflect or scatter. Spectrophotometric analysis returns a measured reflectance of 0.0000, consistent with a total absence of interaction between incident photons and any surface or medium.

SCP-9031 functions as a traversable threshold. Physical objects, electromagnetic radiation, and atmospheric gases may pass through in both directions, though no light or thermal radiation has ever been observed emerging from within SCP-9031-A (see below). The boundary exhibits no detectable gravitational lensing, frame-dragging, or tidal forces, distinguishing it from known singularity geometries.

Upon crossing the threshold, subjects enter SCP-9031-A: a spatially unbounded region characterized by total darkness, hard vacuum, and the absence of gravitational acceleration. Ambient temperature within SCP-9031-A has been measured at 0.003 K, approximately three orders of magnitude below the cosmic microwave background temperature of our universe (2.725 K). No baryonic matter has been detected within sensor range of the aperture beyond trace concentrations of iron-56 nuclei in diffuse distribution on the order of 10^^-4^^ particles per cubic meter.

Project History

SCP-9031 was recovered from a sealed sub-basement beneath the Knightridge Observatory, located on the grounds of Indiana University, on 04/██/2017, following intelligence from embedded Foundation assets within the local urban exploration community. The observatory had been decommissioned since 1964. SCP-9031 was found suspended at the approximate center of a concrete-walled utility room with no visible anchoring or support mechanism. No documentation pertaining to SCP-9031 or its origins was recovered from the site.

During initial containment efforts, exterior Hume variance was observed within one meter of SCP-9031. The variance was found to range from precisely 1.0 to precisely 0.5 Humes over a distance of exactly one meter, with 0.5 measured only while detectors are within SCP-9031's circumferential plane and pressed against the interior of the ring. The Hume variance from 1.0 to 0.5 has been found to be linear across a one-meter distance, to within tolerance of all known measurement equipment. (see Report 32.f) 

When no Scranton Reality Anchor (SRA) is active within a 4.6-meter radius, the SCP-9031 maintains a fixed position relative to Earth's center of mass. Laser interferometry has detected micron-scale adjustments in the aperture's fixed position consistent with shifts in Earth's measured center of mass. These movements have a causal relationship to fluctuations attributable to liquid core dynamics and tidal forces exerted by the Sun and Moon according to current models.

### Day 4 · Automated Transcript · Initial Probe Telemetry Review

*Recorded by automated transcript system*

*Present: Strauss, Levy, Chandrasekaran, Tanaka, Obaje*

**STRAUSS:** Probe's back. Sensors intact, telemetry's clean. Sam, Yuki, take us through it.

**OBAJE:** Right. So. Full-spectrum sweep out to about four thousand klicks from the aperture. Nothing on radio, nothing on infra, nothing on visible. Particle collector picked up trace ferric material, very sparse. Temperature data is... complicated.

**STRAUSS:** Complicated how?

**OBAJE:** Well, the probe was at room temperature when it crossed over. In a hard vacuum environment, it's going to cool by radiation only, and that takes time. A... uh, lot of time. Thousands of years, ballpark, for an object this size. The onboard thermistors are still reading the probe's own thermals, not the environment. We're drowning whatever's actually there. What I can say is we pointed the bolo away from the probe body at the widest angle the housing allows and got a photon background reading that was low. Extremely low.

*[silence]*

**TANAKA:** Alright. How low?

**OBAJE:** The detector bottomed out. It's not rated below about 2 Kay. Whatever's out there is colder than that, but I can't give you a number from this hardware. We'd need a dedicated cryogenic sensor package with active shielding, and even then we'd have to let it soak for weeks before the readings mean anything.

**TANAKA:** To put that in context, our universe sits at 2.72ish. Whatever this space is, it's far colder than that. We're going to need dedicated equipment. Cryogenic radiometers, long integration times, proper shielding. This isn't a weekend project.

**LEVY:** *[Interjecting]* Hang on. Back up. The iron?

**OBAJE:** Right, yeah. Particle collector picked up a small number of ferric atoms. Very small. We're talking single digits over the full mission duration. Essentially nothing. Iron-56, almost exclusively. Maybe one nickel-62. Probably noise.

**LEVY:** That's not nothing, Sam. Iron doesn't just show up. Either something put it there or something made it.

**TANAKA:** Something did make it. Stars. Iron-56 is the endpoint of stellar nucleosynthesis. Every star that burns long enough ends up producing iron.

**LEVY:** Stars we haven't detected. In a void that contains nothing else, as far as we can tell.

**TANAKA:** That's what I'm saying. The stars are gone. They burned out. The iron is what you get when--

**LEVY:** That's a lot of assumptions built on a particle count, Yuki.

**TANAKA:** It's one assumption. The last hundred years of astrophysics.

*[several seconds of crosstalk]*

**CHANDRASEKARAN:** Or it's a YK-Class we haven't encountered yet.

*[room quiets]*

**CHANDRASEKARAN:** I've seen anomalies that strip matter down and leave exactly what they can't metabolize. If something consumed everything in that space and left only what it couldn't use, the residue would look identical. We'd have no way to tell the difference from this data.

**OBAJE:** *[exhales]* She's right about that. Either explanation produces the same observables.

**STRAUSS:** We can't make a determination. This is the first probe. We send more. Anton, what do you need?

**LEVY:** Threat-detection suites on the next three probes. Full spectrum. Hume variance, Scranton drift, a TRD if we can get one allocated. Everything we've got. When the Foundation encounters a space with nothing in it, the answer has never once been "it was always empty."

**STRAUSS:** Yuki?

**TANAKA:** I'm going to need real equipment. Not survey probes. Cryogenic radiometers rated to sub-millikelvin, with passive cooling stages and enough thermal soak time to get meaningful ambient readings. Weeks, minimum. Long-baseline infrared interferometry for source detection at extreme range. A dedicated radio antenna array, something we can deploy on the other side and leave there, tuned for meter-wavelength photons. If there's a photon background in this environment, that's where it'll peak. And I want large-aperture particle collectors on long-duration missions, multiple deployments, known sweep volumes, so we can start putting real density numbers on the iron.

**STRAUSS:** *[shuffling papers]* That's a big ask.

**TANAKA:** If we're looking at a pocket void, none of this matters and we close the book. If we're looking at a full cosmological space, the instruments we have now are equivalent to measuring an ocean with a bathroom cup. I'd rather build the right toolkit once than send twenty more probes that can't see what they're sitting in.

**STRAUSS:** *[long pause]* You'll both get what you need. I want answers before I put my name on a classification. We're done. *[sound of chairs]*

### Months 1-6 · Summary · Internal Division

The division evident in these early sessions hardened over the following months. By the end of the first six months of active research, two distinct positions had consolidated within the team despite repeated directives from Dr. Strauss to maintain a unified research framework.

Dr. Levy and Dr. Chandrasekaran led what became informally known as the **Causalists**. Their position held that the apparent absence of matter and energy within SCP-9031-A was evidence of an unidentified destructive anomaly, likely still present or active within the space. They argued that the void was the signature of a hostile entity or process operating at a scale the Foundation had not previously encountered, and advocated for escalated containment protocols and reclassification to Euclid or Keter. Their reasoning drew on precedent: the Foundation has catalogued multiple anomalies whose primary observable characteristic is absence (cf. SCP-████, SCP-████).

Dr. Tanaka and Dr. Obaje led what became informally known as the **Nullists**. Their position held that SCP-9031-A represented a non-anomalous cosmological space accessed through an anomalous threshold. They maintained that the aperture itself was the anomaly, and that the environment beyond it, while extreme, was consistent with known physics operating at timescales beyond standard observational experience. They argued that null findings on threat-detection instruments should be interpreted as null findings, and noted that the Foundation had encountered stable portals to empty or near-empty extradimensional spaces on at least two prior occasions, none of which had produced a hostile entity. They advocated for maintaining Safe classification.

Dr. Strauss declined to endorse either position, authorizing continued probe deployments under Causalist threat-detection protocols while simultaneously approving Nullist requests for long-range cosmological survey equipment. This compromise held for approximately fourteen months.

During this period, three junior researchers assigned to cosmological analysis under the Nullists requested transfer to other projects. Exit interviews cited difficulty maintaining professional detachment from the dataset and intrusive thoughts regarding the thermodynamic models. No Causalist personnel requested transfer during the same period. This asymmetry was not flagged for review at the time.

### Year 2 · Summary · Probe Results and Cosmological Survey

Over the course of the second year of research, unmanned probes continued to return null findings. Each successive Causalist deployment was designed to detect increasingly exotic threats including, topological reality defects, perception-filtering signatures, eigenstate collapse patterns, acausal interaction traces, counter-conceptual detection arrays calibrated for entities defined by observational absence, and Becquerel-Maillard consciousness-residue assays on collected particulate. Every probe returned a null result.

The Nullist cosmological survey began producing results.

Ultimately this demonstrated the void space as being approximately three orders of magnitude below the cosmic microwave background temperature of our own universe.

Dr. Tanaka presented these findings at an internal review along with a theoretical framework. The data, she argued, was consistent with a universe in an extreme state of thermodynamic equilibrium. Specifically, a universe so old that every physical process capable of sustaining structure had run to completion. Dr. Levy rejected the framework outright in his formal response, characterizing it as an unfounded extrapolation.

Dr. Tanaka's research aide was notably absent from the internal review, having sought psychiatric care for intrusive thoughts following a prolonged session compiling cosmological data. Her transfer request was the fourth from Nullist-assigned personnel in eighteen months.

Project standard psychological evaluations during this period flagged a growing disparity between the two groups. Causalist personnel reported elevated stress and interpersonal friction but no clinically significant changes in affect or cognition. Nullist personnel, particularly those working directly with the cosmological survey data, showed a pattern of declining scores on measures of future orientation and self-reported purpose. 

Dr. Obaje requested a two-week leave of absence, which was granted. He returned to duty but reduced his involvement in the long-range data analysis, delegating primary authorship of the survey reports to Dr. Tanaka and a junior researcher, Dr. Elaine Caro.

Dr. Caro had transferred to Project LONG WINTER voluntarily from the Foundation's Astrophysical Anomalies Division following recommendation by Project WYRM LIGHT's lead. Dr. Caro's personnel file notes a commendation for psychological stability, scoring in the 99th percentile on a standard Nkomo-Vasquez Cognitive Resilience Battery.

The six-month extension did not resolve the dispute. Two additional Nullist researchers were reassigned after failing routine psychological evaluations. Dr. Caro, who had assumed an increasingly central role in the cosmological analysis, was flagged for persistent low affect but was permitted to continue at her own request.

### Year 3 · Summary · Classification Dispute and O5 Intervention

On ██/██/20██, Dr. Levy submitted a reclassification request for SCP-9031 from Safe to Euclid, bypassing Dr. Strauss and submitting directly to the Site Director. The reclassification was approved provisionally on the basis of Dr. Levy's threat assessments, which the Site Director reviewed without consulting the project lead due to perceived urgency. SCP-9031 was designated Euclid for four days before Dr. Strauss filed a formal objection supported by the Nullist compiled dataset, citing both insufficient evidence for Euclid classification and lack of consultation with the lead of Project LONG WINTER. The Site Director reverted the classification to Safe following a meeting with an O5 liaison.

Dr. Chandrasekaran submitted a request for classification change to Keter, arguing that SCP-9031 represented a potential pathway for an undetected entity to enter our universe, citing a transient energetic event on Day 31 of the Project followed by 142 days of dormancy. A statistical model appended to the request (Diag. 2e) projected an increasing probability of recurrence over time, arguing that the extended dormancy interval was consistent with an exponentially distributed activation cycle rather than a singular event. The request was denied within forty-eight hours.

**Internal Memorandum**

**Dr. Kessler to Site Director, Final Report**

*Classification: 2/9031 Eyes Only*

SCP-9031-A is a universe that has reached thermodynamic equilibrium. The technical term for this state is heat death.

Every star that ever existed in SCP-9031-A burned through its fuel and died. Every black hole that formed from those stars is now evaporating, converting their mass to thermal energy photon by photon over timeframes beyond comprehension. SCP-9031-Σ: Psychological Effects

In the weeks following publication of the final report, Site-███ psychiatric services reported an increase in intake requests among Project LONG WINTER personnel that exceeded four standard deviations from the site baseline. The onset was gradual. Initial presentations were unremarkable in isolation: difficulty sleeping, reduced social engagement, a reported loss of interest in long-term personal plans. Several personnel described the experience of reading the 9031-COSMO documents as a threshold they could identify in retrospect but had not recognized at the time. Two researchers used the same phrase independently, telling Dr. Farid they felt they had learned "the answer to a question they should not have been able to ask".

Within three months of the final report, 14 of the 20 research personnel assigned to Project LONG WINTER had exhibited clinically significant psychological deterioration. Documented symptoms include persistent anhedonia, depersonalization, insomnia, fixation on entropic and eschatological themes, and social withdrawal. Five personnel have required inpatient psychiatric intervention. Three have attempted suicide.

A notable asymmetry exists between the two original research factions. Of the 7 personnel affiliated with the Causalists, only 2 have exhibited symptoms meeting the diagnostic threshold for SCP-9031-Σ, and none have required inpatient treatment. All Causalist symptom onset occurred around or after the publication of the final report. Of the 13 personnel affiliated with the Nullists or subsequently assigned to cosmological analysis under Dr. Kessler's restructured program, 12 have exhibited symptoms, 5 have required inpatient treatment, and all 3 suicide attempts originated from this cohort. Dr. Farid has noted that the Causalist threat-oriented framework, regardless of its empirical validity, may have provided a psychologically protective function. The Nullist framework seems to offer no such structure.

The onset of SCP-9031-Σ does not correlate with direct exposure to SCP-9031 or physical entry into SCP-9031-A. It correlates exclusively with depth of engagement with the compiled analytical data, specifically the documents modeling the full thermodynamic history and projected future of SCP-9031-A and, by extension, the implied trajectory of our own universe.

*Date: ██/██/20██*

*Filed by: Dr. Hassan Farid, Site Psychologist*

Dr. Caro was found unresponsive in her quarters on the morning of ██/██/20██. Cause of death: self-administered overdose of laboratory-grade sodium pentobarbital. The substance was traced to Laboratory storage 19-theta. Access protocols are under review.

Dr. Caro had been the primary author of 9031-COSMO documents 14 through 21, comprising the bulk of the thermodynamic projection models for SCP-9031-A. She had passed her most recent psychological evaluation eleven days prior. The evaluator in later review noted that Dr. Caro's responses were textbook-accurate, consistent with a subject who understood the evaluation.

Her final submission to the project archive, 9031-COSMO-21, was uploaded to the shared drive at 02:14 on the night of her death. It is a complete and methodologically sound analysis of projected photon wavelength decay in SCP-9031-A over the next 10^^200^^ years. The document contains no irregularities. Peer review identified no errors.

A note was recovered from her terminal. It read, in full:

Dr. Farid has noted the content of the terminal message for the record. His clinical assessment remains that SCP-9031-Σ cannot be ruled out as a contributing factor.

Addendum 9031-A: Σ Classification Dispute

The designation of SCP-9031-Σ as a cognitohazard has been challenged by both the Cosmological Analysis Team and the Ethics Committee's independent review panel. The dispute centers on whether the psychological effects observed in affected personnel represent an anomalous information-borne pathogen, or a non-anomalous human psychological response to a genuinely distressing body of knowledge.

### Position A · Cognitohazard Classification

*Dr. Farid, Site Psychology*

> The rate and severity of psychological deterioration among exposed researchers exceeds baseline statistical expectations for professionals working in high-stress containment environments. The uniformity of symptom presentation suggests an information-borne anomalous vector. Standard therapeutic interventions have shown markedly reduced efficacy compared to their application in non-anomalous depressive episodes. Amnestic treatment targeting knowledge of SCP-9031-A's cosmological data has produced symptom remission in 6 of 6 trial cases, further suggesting that the information itself is the vector.

### Position B · Non-Anomalous Response

*Dr. Liang, Ethics Committee Review*

> There is no evidence of an anomalous mechanism. The data compiled from SCP-9031-A constitutes the most complete empirical confirmation ever assembled that all structure, complexity, and life in any universe is temporary and will end in featureless thermodynamic equilibrium on a timescale that makes the entire history of biological evolution a rounding error. The question is not why fourteen of twenty researchers experienced psychological collapse. The question is what cognitive structure the other six possess that permitted them to continue. Classifying the natural human inability to bear certain truths as an "anomaly" risks pathologizing a normal reaction to an abnormal body of knowledge. The success of amnestic treatment does not confirm a cognitohazard; it confirms only that people feel better when they do not know the thing that was making them feel terrible.

### Excerpt · Ethics Committee Review Session

*Present: Dr. Farid, Dr. Liang, Ethics Committee panel (3 members), Dr. Kessler (observer)*

**LIANG:** Yes, Hassan. For the second time, I recognize that the intake rate is extraordinary. I don't dispute that. But an extraordinary response to extraordinary information is not, by itself, evidence of an anomalous mechanism.

**FARID:** Every subject who received targeted amnestic treatment showed full symptom remission. Six of six. All of them. One. Hundred. Percent. In twenty years of Foundation psychiatric practice, I have never seen a non-anomalous depressive episode resolve with that kind of consistency through any intervention. The information is the pathogen. Remove the information, the symptoms disappear.

**LIANG:** If I tell you your house has burned down, you get upset, and I administer an amnestic and you forget your house has burned down, you feel better. That doesn't make the news a cognitohazard. It makes it bad news. The house is still gone.

**FARID:** *[responding immediately]* That is hardly fair. A house fire is a specific, personal loss. What we're observing is a uniform psychological collapse across trained professionals exposed to the same information. But set that aside. People lose houses. People grieve. People recover. We have fifty years of clinical literature on trauma response and grief resolution. These people are not recovering. Standard therapeutic interventions that produce measurable improvement in non-anomalous depression are showing no efficacy. Zero. Not reduced efficacy. *[sound of hand  slammed on table]* None! The information isn't just causing distress. It's preventing recovery through every non-amnestic channel we have. That is not a normal response to abstract data!

**LIANG:** *[responding immediately - nearly interrupting]* The data is not abstract. This is the most concrete evidence ever gathered that everything ends and nothing follows. The consistency of the response is evidence that the conclusion is obvious. Twenty people understood the same data and arrived at the same place because the data only goes to one place.

**LIANG:** *[brief pause - continuing]* And to the therapeutic resistance point. You do not treat someone's grief over a lost house by convincing them the house is still standing. If the therapeutic model requires the patient to reach a point of acceptance, and the thing they're being asked to accept is that acceptance itself is meaningless on a sufficient timescale, the model fails. That isn't an anomaly. It's a limitation of the model-

**FARID:** *[overlapping]* Then explain the amnestic results.

**LIANG:** *[taking a breath]* Fine. You made six people forget the worst thing they ever learned, and they felt better. What did you expect? *[shifting in chair]* What failure rate would satisfy you that it's not anomalous? There's no control group for this. There's no version of this experiment where you make someone forget that the universe ends in iron and they still feel terrible about it. The amnestic data is consistent with both hypotheses. It tells us nothing.

**FARID:** *[long pause]* It tells us that forgetting works.

**LIANG:** Yes. And that should concern everyone in this room. We are now deciding whether the Foundation is in the business of classifying truth as a containment problem.

*[end of excerpt]*

The O5 Council has tabled the dispute. SCP-9031-Σ remains provisionally classified as a potential cognitohazard pending further review. Access restrictions to 9031-COSMO documents remain in force regardless of final classification, on the grounds that the information is demonstrably harmful to personnel who engage with it in depth.

*Note appended by O5-██:* "Whether the damage is caused by something wrong with the information or something right about it, personnel are still being harmed. Contain accordingly."

Addendum 9031-B: Personal Log, Dr. Miriam Strauss

*Recorded during off-duty hours. Recovered from Dr. Strauss's personal terminal following Incident Report 396.b, after her voluntary transfer to an unrelated project. Included in the SCP-9031 file at the discretion of Dr. Kessler, who noted: "This belongs here after what happened."*

I stood in front of it last night. For an hour, maybe longer.

When I took this project I thought the argument would be the hard part. I keep wondering what it means if Σ is a cognitohazard. If it is, then the grief is an illness, and illness can be treated. If it isn't, then what we're feeling is just the weight of knowing what's true.

I think about Dr. Caro sometimes.
