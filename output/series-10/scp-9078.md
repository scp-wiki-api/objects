**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9078 is to be stored within a standard secure containment locker at Site-[REDACTED]. When not undergoing approved testing, SCP-9078 is to remain disconnected from all display devices.

Access to SCP-9078 is restricted to personnel assigned to Project 9078. Unauthorized interaction with the device is prohibited. Any personnel found attempting to operate SCP-9078 without approval are to be immediately removed from the project and undergo psychological evaluation.

Testing involving SCP-9078 requires written authorization from at least one Level 3 researcher. During testing, subjects are to be continuously monitored. Exposure is to be terminated immediately upon the duration of the test surpassing the designated investigation time decided by the Project Director.

Due to the severe psychological effects observed following recovery, personnel are prohibited from participating in repeated exposure trials. Requests for additional testing involving the same individual require approval from the current Project Director.

No testing involving the software title designated "Earth" has been approved. Attempts to access the title are forbidden pending further review by the Ethics Committee and Site Administration.

---

**Description:** SCP-9078 is an anomalous video game console, resembling a commercially produced Nintendo Entertainment System (NES). The device bears no manufacturer markings, serial numbers or identifying features. SCP-9078 is equipped with a single wired controller, a permanently attched VGA Cable, and a power cable, though the power cable appears unnecessary for operation. SCP-9078 will function nomrally when connected to a compatible display regardless of access to an electrical power source, but will immediately cease operation if disconnected from an active display.

Upon activation, SCP_9078 presents a library of video game titles not corresponding to any known commercially released, cancelled, or documented software. Subjects who interact with SCP-9078 develop an increasing reluctance to cease gameplay, often expressing feelings of attachment toward the game environment. After approximately two hours of gameplay, subjects enter a catatonic state and are designated SCP-9078-1.

While in this state, SCP-9078-1 instances remain physically healthy and display normal vital signs, but become extraoridinarily difficult to move despite no measurable increase in mass. The condition persists until SCP-9078 is deactivated or disconnected from its display device, at which point the SCP-9078-1 instance immediately regains conciousness.

Recovered subjects consistently report having experienced extended periods of concious existence within the game being played prior to recovery. Reported experiences frequently include the development of long-term relationships, careers, families, and complete life stories. Subjective durations range from several years to multiple decades despite only a few hours or days passing in baseline reality. Most recovered subjects exhibit severe psychological distress upon return and commonly assert that they have been removed from their "real" lives. Subjects are most common to show signs of grief, distress or depression upon recovery, with notable exceptions particularly pertaining to the case of D-[DATA EXPUNGED].

Investigation into the nature of the environments experienced during exposure to SCP-9078 remains ongoing.

**Experiment Logs**

****Test Log #1****

**Subject:** D-1842

**Selected Title:** Greenhaven

**Genre:** Sandbox / Farming Simulation

**Exposure Duration:** 2 hours, 17 minutes

<details>
<summary>Expand</summary>

**Summary:** Subject was instructed to interact with SCP-9078 and select a title of their choosing. Subject selected Greenhaven from the software library.

For approximately 90 minutes, subject displayed behavior consistent with normal video game engagement. At 1 hour, 34 minutes, subject became increasingly resistant to interruptions from research staff and repeatedly requested that questioning cease. At 1 hour, 52 minutes, subject stated that they had "work to do before winter arrives" despite being unable to elaborate further.

At 2 hours, 17 minutes, subject entered the SCP-9078-1 state. Vital signs remained stable. Attempts to reposition the subject were unsuccessful despite no measurable increase in body mass.

After 6 hours of observation, SCP-9078 was disconnected from its display device. Subject immediately regained consciousness and displayed signs of severe emotional distress.

****Post-Recovery Interview Excerpt****

**Interviewer:** Dr. Harris

**Dr. Harris:** Can you describe your experience while exposed to SCP-9078?

**D-1842:** It wasn't a game.

**Dr. Harris:** Please explain.

**D-1842:** I lived there.

**Dr. Harris:** Where?

**D-1842:** Greenhaven.

**Dr. Harris:** How long were you there?

*(Subject remains silent for approximately 12 seconds.)*

**D-1842:** Twenty-three years.

**Dr. Harris:** Twenty-three years?

**D-1842:** Yeah.

**Dr. Harris:** What occurred during that time?

**D-1842:** I had a farm. Grew wheat mostly. Chickens. Couple goats.

**Dr. Harris:** Anything else?

*(Subject begins crying.)*

**D-1842:** I had a wife.

**Dr. Harris:** Was this an individual from baseline reality?

**D-1842:** No.

**Dr. Harris:** Then who was she?

*(Subject becomes visibly agitated.)*

**D-1842:** My wife.

**Dr. Harris:** D-1842—

**D-1842:** You took me away from her.

*(Subject is visibly distraught.)*

[INTERVIEW TERMINATED]

</details>

****Test Log #2****

**Subject:** D-2911  

**Selected Title:** Hollow Ridge  

**Genre:** Survival / Open-World  

**Exposure Duration:** 2 hours, 03 minutes  

<details>
<summary>Expand</summary>

**Summary:** Subject was instructed to interact with SCP-9078 under controlled conditions and select a title from the available library. Subject selected Hollow Ridge without prompting.

For approximately 40 minutes, subject displayed standard engagement behaviour consistent with gameplay immersion. At 1 hour, 02 minutes, subject became nonresponsive to external verbal cues while continuing in-game activity. At 1 hour, 26 minutes, subject stated, “I can’t stop now,” and “there’s still work to be done,” despite repeated instructions to terminate the session.

When pressed for clarification, subject refused to comply and exhibited escalating agitation when removal from SCP-9078 was attempted.

At 2 hours, 03 minutes, subject entered the SCP-9078-1 state. Physical restraint procedures were ineffective due to abnormal resistance to movement despite no measurable change in mass or muscle tension.

Following deactivation of SCP-9078, subject regained consciousness after approximately 14 seconds. Subject immediately attempted to return to the device and required restraint and sedative administration.

****Post-Recovery Interview Excerpt****

**Interviewer:** Dr. Harris

**D-2911:** I need to go back.

**Dr. Harris:** Describe your experience during exposure.

**D-2911:** It wasn’t a session. I was there.

**Dr. Harris:** “There” meaning the game environment?

**D-2911:** It wasn’t a game, I was really there.

**Dr. Harris:** How long were you there?

*(Subject pauses for approximately 9 seconds.)*

**D-2911:** Years. I don’t know exactly how many.

**Dr. Harris:** What happened during that time?

**D-2911:** I built a settlement. We expanded. There were raids, shortages, winters—

**Dr. Harris:** “We”?

**D-2911:** People. My people.

**Dr. Harris:** Were these individuals from baseline reality?

**D-2911:** No. But they were real.

**Dr. Harris:** Can you explain what you mean by "real".

**D-2911:** They had names. Lives. They remembered me.

**Dr. Harris:** And now?

*(Subject becomes visibly distressed.)*

**D-2911:** Now they don’t exist because I’m not there.

**Dr. Harris:** D-2911, those environments are simulated—

**D-2911:** Don’t say that.

*(Subject begins struggling against restraints.)*

**D-2911:** You don’t understand what you’re doing when you pull someone out.

*(Subject sedated.)*

[INTERVIEW TERMINATED]

</details>

****Test Log #3****

**Subject:** D-5076  

**Selected Title:** Driftwood Haven  

**Genre:** Survival / Coastal Sandbox  

**Exposure Duration:** 1 hour, 41 minutes  

<details>
<summary>Expand</summary>

**Summary:** Subject was assigned SCP-9078 exposure under standard observation protocol. Subject selected Driftwood Haven from available software library without prompting.

Initial engagement proceeded without irregularity. Subject displayed exploratory behavior consistent with standard gameplay onboarding, including environmental traversal, resource collection, and basic construction mechanics.

At approximately 48 minutes, subject began focusing on a coastal settlement area and stated, “this place is kind of already lived in,” despite no preloaded narrative elements being present in software briefing materials.

At 1 hour, 12 minutes, subject ceased resource-gathering activity and began constructing a small structure at the edge of a tidal inlet. Subject did not provide rationale when questioned and continued building without interruption.

At 1 hour, 41 minutes, subject entered SCP-9078-1 state. Movement resistance phenomenon observed consistent with prior testing.

Exposure duration prior to termination was notably shorter than average recorded instances. No escalation to prolonged immersion phase was observed.

Following deactivation of SCP-9078, subject regained consciousness after approximately 11 seconds.

Subject did not exhibit immediate distress. Instead, subject remained silent for approximately 30 seconds before requesting confirmation that “the shoreline was still there.”

Request was denied.

Subject then stated: “It’s not finished yet.”

Subsequent attempts to question subject regarding meaning of statement were unsuccessful.

****Post-Recovery Interview Excerpt****

**Interviewer:** Dr. Harris  

**D-5076:** You pulled me out early.

**Dr. Harris:** You reached the exposure threshold.

**D-5076:** I didn’t finish anything.

**Dr. Harris:** Finish what?

**D-5076:** The place.

**Dr. Harris:** Clarify.

**D-5076:** Driftwood Haven.

**Dr. Harris:** That environment is a simulated construct with no persistence beyond session runtime.

**D-5076:** No. It stays where it is.

**Dr. Harris:** Explain.

**D-5076:** If I leave it like that, it’s just going to sit there unfinished.

*(Subject pauses for approximately 8 seconds.)*

**D-5076:** That feels worse than dying.

**Dr. Harris:** You were not removed from a living environment.

**D-5076:** That’s what you think.

**Dr. Harris:** Please elaborate.

**D-5076:** You don’t understand what it means to stop halfway through a life.

*(Subject refused to talk any further.)*

[INTERVIEW TERMINATED]

</details>

****Test Log #4****

**Subject:** D-5198  

**Selected Title:** Driftwood Haven  

**Genre:** Survival / Coastal Sandbox  

**Exposure Duration:** 2 hours, 09 minutes  

<details>
<summary>Expand</summary>

**Summary:** Subject was instructed to initiate SCP-9078 exposure under controlled conditions and was explicitly directed to select Driftwood Haven for consistency testing following prior observed anomalies.

Subject complied without resistance.

Initial gameplay phase proceeded normally. Subject engaged in standard environmental interaction, including resource collection along shoreline regions and basic structure placement.

At approximately 52 minutes, subject paused movement and remained stationary for 11 seconds before stating, “Someone’s already been here.”

When questioned, subject clarified that multiple basic structures were present within immediate vicinity that did not correspond to default world generation parameters outlined in prior session documentation.

At 1 hour, 18 minutes, subject located a partially constructed settlement approximately 300 meters inland from spawn coastline. Subject described the settlement as “incomplete but actively abandoned,” noting presence of partially built wooden structures, storage containers containing organized materials, and cleared terrain suggesting prior development activity.

Subject was unable to account for origin of settlement when questioned.

At 2 hours, 09 minutes, subject entered SCP-9078-1 state. Movement resistance effect observed consistent with prior tests.

Following deactivation of SCP-9078, subject regained consciousness after approximately 13 seconds.

Subject immediately requested confirmation of whether previous test data had been conducted within Driftwood Haven prior to current exposure. Request denied.

Subject then stated: “It keeps going when I’m not there.”

****Post-Recovery Interview Excerpt****

**Interviewer:** Dr. Harris  

**D-5198:** That wasn’t new.

**Dr. Harris:** Clarify.

**D-5198:** The settlement inland. It was already started.

**Dr. Harris:** No prior session data exists for Driftwood Haven.

**D-5198:** Then who built it?

**Dr. Harris:** You are the first recorded subject assigned to that title under controlled testing.

**D-5198:** No.

*(Subject pauses for approximately 6 seconds.)*

**D-5198:** Someone was building it before I got there.

**Dr. Harris:** That is not supported by system logs.

**D-5198:** I didn’t see them. But I saw what they left.

*(Subject becomes visibly unsettled.)*

**D-5198:** It wasn’t empty.

**Dr. Harris:** Describe what you mean.

**D-5198:** Like I’d walked into the middle of something halfway through.

*(Subject hesitates.)*

**D-5198:** Like I was late.

**Dr. Harris:** Late to what?

**D-5198:** I don’t know.

*(Subject begins showing distress.)*

**D-5198:** But I think it knew I was supposed to continue it.

**Dr. Harris:** Why didn't you continue it?

**D-5198:** I went elsewhere, decided i wanted to travel instead.

**Dr. Harris:** How long did you travel for?

**D-5198:** I'm not really sure. Guessing, I'd say around two years.

**Dr.Harris:** Two years, and all you did was travel?

**D-5198:** I found some sort of peace being able to wander freely. Freedom I'll probably never get to experience again.

*(Subject became visibly upset.)*

**Dr. Harris:** Is there anything else you could te—

**D-5198:** I don't want to talk about this anymore!

[INTERVIEW TERMINATED]

</details>

****Test Log #5****

**Subject:** D-[DATA EXPUNGED]  

**Selected Title:** Redline District  

**Genre:** Open-World Crime / Urban Simulation  

**Exposure Duration:** 2 hours, 24 minutes  

<details>
<summary>Expand</summary>

**Summary:** Subject was selected from high-risk D-Class pool due to prior conviction for multiple coordinated homicides and documented behavioral profile indicating low affect response and high strategic cognition under stress. Subject was assigned SCP-9078 exposure under reinforced restraint protocol.

Subject complied without verbal resistance and selected Redline District from available titles.

Initial gameplay phase proceeded in a controlled manner. Subject demonstrated rapid adaptation to environmental systems and immediately engaged in structured, goal-oriented activity including territorial mapping, resource acquisition, and hostile NPC engagement patterns.

At 1 hour, 06 minutes, subject stated: “This is organized.”

At 1 hour, 39 minutes, subject began referring to non-player entities as “variables” and “unresolved threats,” despite no such terminology being present in SCP-9078 software documentation.

At 2 hours, 24 minutes, subject entered SCP-9078-1 state. Movement resistance phenomenon observed consistent with prior cases.

Following deactivation of SCP-9078, subject regained consciousness after approximately 9 seconds.

Upon recovery, subject remained motionless for 14 seconds before requesting confirmation that “system authority permissions” were still active.

Request was denied.

Subject then stated: “That’s incorrect.”

****Post-Recovery Interview Attempt****

**Interviewer:** Dr. █████  

**D-[DATA EXPUNGED]:** You interrupted execution.

**Dr. █████:** You were removed after exceeding exposure duration limits.

**D-[DATA EXPUNGED]:** There were still active elements.

**Dr. █████:** Clarify.

**D-[DATA EXPUNGED]:** Unresolved actors. Non-compliant behavior sets.

*(Pause approximately 5 seconds.)*

**D-[DATA EXPUNGED]:** It wasn’t finished correcting itself.

**Dr. █████:** There is no corrective system within SCP-9078 gameplay mechanics.

**D-[DATA EXPUNGED]:** There is when you’re inside it.

*(Subject becomes visibly agitated.)*

**D-[DATA EXPUNGED]:** You don’t stop something mid-enforcement cycle.

**Dr. █████:** You are describing narrative interpretation of a simulation environment.

**D-[DATA EXPUNGED]:** It wasn’t a simulation.

*(Subject begins resisting restraints.)*

**D-[DATA EXPUNGED]:** It was structured consequence.

*(Restraints fail. Security breach initiated.)*

[INTERVIEW TERMINATED — SECURITY BREACH]

**Addendum 9078-05:** Subject neutralized following containment breach. All recovered logs from SCP-9078 unit show no autonomous “system authority” or enforcement mechanics present within Redline District. Console returned to inactive state with no recorded shutdown input.

Investigation into anomalous system-state termination pending.

****Test Log #6****

**Subject:** D-2411  

**Selected Title:** Hollow Ridge  

**Genre:** Survival / Open-World  

**Exposure Duration:** 2 hours, 13 minutes  

<details>
<summary>Expand</summary>

**Summary:** Subject was instructed to engage SCP-9078 under standard observation conditions. Subject selected Hollow Ridge from available titles.

Initial gameplay proceeded without deviation from expected parameters. Subject reported standard survival mechanics including resource gathering, environmental traversal, and basic construction systems.

At approximately 1 hour, 11 minutes, subject reported discovering a pre-existing settlement structure located beyond the primary starting region.

Subject described the settlement as fully constructed but abandoned. Structures were intact, though showing clear signs of long-term disuse including weathering, structural collapse, and overgrown agricultural areas. No inhabitants or active systems were observed.

Subject remained within the settlement area for approximately 22 minutes before continuing exploration beyond its boundaries.

At approximately 1 hour, 46 minutes, subject entered SCP-9078-1 catatonic state. Vital signs remained stable. Increased resistance to movement observed consistent with prior instances.

SCP-9078 was powered down after 3 hours, 28 minutes of total operation time.

Subject regained consciousness after approximately 6 seconds.

****Post-Recovery Interview Excerpt****

**Interviewer:** Dr. Morrow

**Dr.Morrow:** Can you tell me anything about you encountered?

**D-2411:** There was a town. First thing I found.

**Dr. Morrow:** Describe it.

**D-2411:** Empty. Built properly, but no one was there.

**Dr. Morrow:** And what did you do afterward?

**D-2411:** I kept going.

**Dr. Morrow:** Did you encounter anything further?

**D-2411:** Yeah.

*(Pause approximately 5 seconds.)*

**D-2411:** There was another settlement after that.

**Dr. Morrow:** Explain the difference between them.

**D-2411:** People were there. Working fields, moving supplies, lighting up buildings.

**D-2411:** And then they said something.

**D-2411:** They called me the builder.

****Test Log #7****

**Subject:** D-3187  

**Selected Title:** Latchwork  

**Genre:** Puzzle / Platformer  

**Exposure Duration:** 2 hours, 03 minutes  

<details>
<summary>Expand</summary>

**Summary:** Subject was instructed to engage SCP-9078 under standard observation conditions. Subject selected Latchwork from available titles.

Initial gameplay presented as a side-scrolling puzzle-platformer environment with increasing mechanical complexity and non-linear traversal requirements.

Subject reported no anomalous environmental behavior during early and mid-stage gameplay.

At approximately 1 hour, 12 minutes, subject ceased verbal communication while continuing active gameplay input. Subject exhibited prolonged periods of inactivity followed by rapid corrective input sequences consistent with trial-and-error problem solving.

At 1 hour, 39 minutes, subject entered SCP-9078-1 catatonic state. Vital signs remained stable. Increased resistance to movement observed consistent with prior instances.

SCP-9078 was not powered down following standard post-exposure window due to ongoing automated monitoring conditions.

At approximately 2 hours, 03 minutes, subject’s in-game activity abruptly ceased following a recorded in-game fatal failure event during a timed traversal sequence.

Immediately following this event, SCP-9078-1 instance exhibited a sharp, singular physical convulsion before becoming completely still.

Subject’s vital signs ceased approximately 4 seconds later.

No resuscitation attempts were successful.

SCP-9078 powered down automatically 11 minutes after cessation of vital signs.

Subject remained unresponsive post-disconnection.

No recovery state was observed.

****Post-Event Note:****  

All subsequent attempts to reinitialize SCP-9078 resulted in standard startup behavior. No anomaly was detected in hardware function.

No additional instances of Latchwork testing have been approved.
