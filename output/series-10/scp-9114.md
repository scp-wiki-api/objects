**Object Class:** Euclid

**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9114 is housed in a standard Humanoid Containment Cell at Site-282. To maintain long-term containment stability, SCP-9114 is assigned a designated *“squire,"* selected from D-class personnel. The squire is responsible for basic maintenanceSuch as derusting and oiling. of SCP-9114 and must sustain its perceived hierarchical expectations. Religious services are available to SCP-9114 every Sunday.

Due to repeated incidents of targeted hostility, direct interaction with SCP-9114 is restricted to ethnically European personnel who identify as both Catholic and heterosexual. Communications with it must be conducted via the observation window and should acknowledge, but not reinforce, its expectations of deference.

SCP-9114 must be fully restrained prior to any transport. During transit, it is to be escorted by no fewer than 4 security personnel. Instances of aggression or rapid behavioral escalation are to be met with appropriate force.

**Description:** SCP-9114 is an animate 15th-century suit of plated armour, approximately 1.75 m in height. Its construction and ornamentation match armaments from midland regions of the Holy Roman Empire. Despite lacking a gambeson or internal framework, all components remain suspended and articulate as though worn by a human body. Individual plates can be removed but autonomously return to their correct positions once released.

SCP-9114 produces vocalizations from within its helm consistent with an adult male of roughly 40 years. It is capable of verbal communication limited to Low German and basic Latin.

SCP-9114 displays courteous behaviour toward personnel it deems virtuous. However, it becomes verbally hostile and may escalate to physical aggression when confronted with individuals or statements it labels as *"heretical."* The exact criteria it uses to determine this heresy remain unknown but strongly correlate with late-medieval worldviews and associated Catholic doctrine.

Personnel are advised that SCP-9114 possesses the physical strength of a professionally trained combatant and demonstrates significant proficiency with close-quarters weapons.

**Addendum:**

= **Interview 9114.01**All interviews involving SCP-9114 were originally conducted in German and regional archaic Low German. For archival and analytical purposes, SCP-9114’s speech has been rendered in a deliberately archaic English to reflect psychologically relevant patterns.

---

**Date:** 29/07/1949

**Interviewed:** SCP-9114 

**Interviewer:** Mr. Justin Josef Holzer; Father Jule.

**Foreword:** During renovation work at Burg Bruck, Austria, SCP-9114 was uncovered in previously unknown subterranean chambers. Panicked by modern machinery, it fled the site, seeking ecclesiastical asylum at a church near Lienz. All civilian witnesses were amnesticised except for Father Jule, who voluntarily entered Foundation custody to aid in its evaluation.

---

[BEGIN LOG]

[Father Jule enters the interview chamber. SCP-9114 is sitting at the table in the center of the room.]

**Father Jule:** Good evening, my son. You may remember me, Father Jule? May I sit with you a moment?

**SCP-9114:** I ken thee. Thou art the priest of Lienzina.Late-medieval name of Lienz, Austria. Aye, please.

[Father Jule sits down at the table, opposite SCP-9114.]

**Father Jule:** If you are willing, I will need to ask you a few questions about yourself. They are required by those overseeing our care.

[SCP-9114 straightens up and gestures with its gauntlet.]

**SCP-9114:** Cert! Ask, Father.

**Father Jule:** Let us begin simply. Your name, and the role you held.

**SCP-9114:**  I am Sire Sebastian of Almain, sworn knight unto His Lordship Friedrich III. Ac men call me Sebastian the Great. I rode where law was feeble and order troubled. I kept His orchard whole... and His folk sof... Think me a shepard!

**Father Jule:** You speak with great certainty, Sebastian. And yet… I sense a burden behind your words. As though something remains unresolved. You have been carried far from your own time, and into a world unfamiliar to you. Such a trial would weigh heavily on any man.

**SCP-9114:** Nay, Father. That is not the wound.

**Father Jule:** Then tell me what troubles you.

**SCP-9114:** *[Pauses]* I am not troubled by dread, not by distroubling. Swich things beeth for lesser men.

[SCP-9114 shifts in its chair.]

**SCP-9114:** Ac thou understand well. Unright is at werk.

**Father Jule:** Unright? What do you mean by that?

**SCP-9114:** Thou art Godes man. And so I shall speak plain to thee. Fram the day I held first a sword, til the hour I fell on field, I served His name. Each breath fram dawn til dusk given to His bliss. I bore His rood across the land. Whilst other men fallan, I stood fast. Whilst they bartered faith for comfort, I held sooth. *[Voice rising]* By right, I should stand at His golden throne! I earned mine stand in His Kingdom! And yet, here I langode, bitwixen life and death.

[SCP-9114 slams its gauntlet on the table]

**SCP-9114:** Tell me, Father, why the scythe tard to fetch me? Why leave His true to rusten, while lesser men fest in His light? WHY!

[A long silence.]

**SCP-9114:** …Forgive me, Father. I have spoken unbidden.

**Father Jule:** You have endured much, Sebastian. Any man placed under such strain might cry out as you have. *[Pauses]* I cannot claim to understand the Lord’s purposes. He does work in ways we seldom understand. But I can say this: you are not abandoned here. You need not face this alone. If truth is to be found, we will seek for it together.

**SCP-9114:** I thank thee, Father, and bid thee speak sooth. *[Pauses]* Truth... That is all that I have ever served.

[Father Jule proceeds with standard sapient anomaly intake questionnaire. Transcript redacted for brevity.]

[END LOG]

= **Anthropology Department Report**

---

**Compiled by:** Dr. Emmat Hartmann

**Date:** 21/08/1949

---

Confirming the identification of a knightly personality *"Sir Sebastian, the Great,"* active within southern territories of the Holy Roman Empire between 1430 and 1450.

Sources characterise the individual as a militant figure employed by various Habsburg officials to enforce political and religious authority. In recognition of his unusual resolve and efficiency, he was subsequently knighted and granted operational autonomy. While exact numbers cannot be reliably reconstructed, multiple independent accounts describe his involvement in:

* Military action against Hussite groups.

* Participation in witch trials (frequently cited as the primary executor).

* Suppression of Saracen merchants through violence.

Below is an excerpt from the English version of a monastic chronicle authored by Brother Marcus of St. Afra:

= **Interview 9114.02**

---

**Date:** 27/08/1949

**Interviewed:** SCP-9114

**Interviewer:** Mr. Justin Josef Holzer; Father Jule.

**Foreword:** Following the release of the Anthropology Department’s report involving SCP-9114, Father Jule was requested for an exceptional interview, on the basis of testing its knowledge of the report’s details.

---

[BEGIN LOG]

[Father Jule enters the room holding documents in his hands. He sits down at the table, opposite SCP-9114.]

**SCP-9114:** Ah! Father, thou comest out your wonted hour. A glad sight fore  sooth. Saga me, bringest thou good tiding?

**Father Jule:** I... I’m afraid I cannot say that I do, Sebastian. These papers before me… they claim you were responsible for many deaths. Thousands, perhaps more. I hope, truly hope, there might be some fuller context.

**SCP-9114:** Aye, most impressive, is it not? Ac was a reason, Father.

**Father Jule:** I am relieved to hear this, at least. But I must ask plainly... Why?

**SCP-9114:** They forespilled Godes orchard with their wearness.

**Father Jule:** Sebastian… they were human beings! You drowned an infant!

**SCP-9114:** Thou grantest too fine words to describen such foul. They were heathens. And heathe sprit as rot, all corrupting. Would thou had me sheathe mine sword? Stondeth idle and watch His orchard choke?

**Father Jule:** Yes! Listen to me carefully.

[Father Jule holds up the documents and then places them in front of SCP-9114.]

**Father Jule:** *This* is the reason you have not been granted freedom.

**SCP-9114:** Thou jest, Father? Come now, sureliche not speak thou earnest?

**Father Jule:** I am entirely in earnest. What is described here is cruelty.

[Silence.] 

[SCP-9114 leans back in its chair.]

**SCP-9114:** Naught He foresaken the millward for grinding corn? Other the merchant for selling his fabric? Ac I- *I* who have done naught ac fulfil mine sacred charge in full mesure, nor shronk fram no part thereof- I all one am foresaken?

**Father Jule:** Sebastian, hear me. They do not kill men in the course of their labor. What you did-

[SCP-9114 suddenly leans forwards.]

**SCP-9114:** And naught did I, Father. I swear on mine name, never have slew I a man. These beasts not be of even worth.

**Father Jule:** Bea-! *[Pauses]* Perhaps they did not believe as we did. Perhaps they prayed differently, or not at all. But they were still souls, Sebastian. Souls placed into the world by the same hand as yours. It is not for us to weigh their worth.

**SCP-9114:** The Church blessed our banners. Bishops’ hands upon our helms. Hymns were sung as we riden.

**Father Jule:** That does not make it right.

[SCP-9114 straightens abruptly, becoming rigid.]

**SCP-9114:** -It maked it holy.

[Silence.] 

**Father Jule:** Perhaps-

**SCP-9114:** Enough! Leave me. I would be all one a little.

[Father Jule watches it for a moment, then exits quietly.]

[END LOG]

= **Internal Letter**

---

**To:** Ernst Galmiz, Site-282 Director.

**From:** Dr. Johan Müller, Head of Psychology Department.

**Date:** 04/09/1949

---

Ernst,

@@@@

I am writing to you privately, as discussion concerning SCP-9114 has moved beyond procedural matters and now calls for professional judgment.

@@@@

Following its recent disagreement with Mr. Holzer, the subject has demonstrated increasing resistance to cooperation. Standard protocol would recommend psychological disengagement and a return to purely custodial containment. While administratively sound, I believe such a course would be premature.

@@@@

SCP-9114 constitutes a living historical consciousness: a mind formed outside the development of modern ethical frameworks, yet fully capable of articulation and dialogue. From both an anthropological and historical standpoint, it is without equivalent. However, its utility is presently limited by a marked rigidity, through which all experience is filtered.

@@@@

This rigidity is not, in itself, inaccessible to psychiatric engagement. On the contrary, the subject demonstrates affective responsiveness and the capacity for structured reflection: conditions under which modification of interpretive patterns may reasonably be pursued.

@@@@

To withdraw at this stage would represent not only a loss of a unique historical resource, but also a concession that such rigidity lies beyond the scope of our discipline. Given the ideological extremities witnessed in the past decade, I would argue the opposite must be demonstrated: that even the most fixed systems of belief is neither sacred nor immutable, but subject to analysis, and therefore, correction.

@@@@

Mr. Holzer has requested to remain involved, motivated by his belief the subject is not merely an anomaly, but a man whose moral world collapsed long before he could understand why. His reasoning is compassionate, if imprecise. Nevertheless, it arrives at the same conclusion: abandonment would represent a failure not only of duty, but of understanding.

@@@@

Accordingly, I request authorization to initiate an extended therapeutic program under my direct supervision.

@@@@

Yours,

Johan

= **Psychological Report 9114.A**

---

**Compiled by:** Junior Dr. Emilie Hanne

**Date:** 12/09/1949

---

Baseline assessment of SCP-9114 diagnoses the subject with identity destabilization and narcissistic personality disorder. Probability of a meaningful cognitive reframing is low, but not impossible under sustained intervention.

The subject will participate in biweekly psychiatric sessions emphasizing guided self-reflection and structured dialogue intended to encourage cognitive flexibility. The subject will retain the ability to terminate sessions at its discretion.

---

**Additional:** Session Excerpt - Transcribed 11/09/1949

[BEGIN EXCERPT]

**Dr. Müller:** Such is the treatment I have outlined. However, your cooperation will determine how quickly improvement follows.

**SCP-9114:** If I right understand, thou and thine attendance practice as leechcraftMedieval term for a physician. of the mind? Thou wilt drag ailement fram mine giest as one drags foul humors fram blood?

**Dr. Müller:** In principle, yes. The intention is to examine harmful patterns and correct their influence.

**SCP-9114:** Be it! I shall submit mine to thine werk, leechcraft. When this ailement belifted, I will be granted mine rightful stand.

[END EXCERPT]

@@@@

= **Psychology Report 9114.B**

---

**Compiled by:** Junior Dr. Emilie Hanne

**Date:** 11/10/1949

---

SCP-9114 has demonstrated consistent engagement during scheduled sessions. While conversations remain frequently redirected toward affirmations of personal significance and historical purpose, the subject now exhibits a greater tolerance for sustained examination of these themes.

Notably, the subject has begun to frame its continued existence less as divine negligence and more as a state of purgation. Within this framework, participation in treatment is interpreted by the subject as a necessary process through which it may obtain divine recognition.

Dr. Müller assesses this development as supportive of the working hypothesis that ideologically rigid belief structures remain accessible to guided cognitive restructuring. He further notes that the subject’s willingness to submit to the evaluative process represents a significant procedural advance.

Agitation events have decreased in frequency, and rapport with attending personnel has shown measurable improvement over the first month.

---

**Additional:** Session Excerpt - Transcribed 30/09/1949

[BEGIN EXCERPT]

**Dr. Müller:** You frequently end discussion when conversation approaches personal fault, yet you continue attending sessions. Does this mean you feel the work we are doing is improving you?

**SCP-9114:** The smith heateth the iron, not to forespill it… Ac to make it worthy.

**Dr. Müller:** That is a very encouraging perspective. Reflection is often uncomfortable, yet brings meaningful change.

[END EXCERPT]

@@@@

= **Psychology Report 9114.C**

---

**Compiled by:** Junior Dr. Emilie Hanne

**Date:** 11/11/1949

---

SCP-9114 recently entered a period of marked withdrawal, requesting extended isolation and limiting direct interaction with the Psychology Department.

Dr. Müller interprets this development as a phase of internal consolidation, consistent with delayed processing following sustained engagement. He has therefore recommended maintaining indirect continuity of care rather than suspending intervention.

To facilitate supervised contact under these conditions, the introduction of an assigned squire was approved. The subject responded positively, initiating regular requests for Scripture readings, which are conducted privately at its insistence.Subsequent confirmation indicated this was due to SCP-9114's limited literacy.

During this period, the subject resumed post-service discussions with Father Jule.

Current indicators are interpreted by Dr. Müller as consistent with behavioural stabilisation, which he believes has been achieved.

---

**Additional:** Monthly Psychiatric Review Excerpt - Transcribed 10/11/1949

[BEGIN EXCERPT]

**Father Jule:** Doctor, I must raise a concern. Sebastian’s manner has changed. The way he speaks of his condition, it is no longer as it was. He does not speak as one awaiting passage. The supplication verses I find us discussing are often removed from their proper context.

**Dr. Müller:** Change is to be expected. We may be observing the early stages of resolution. A shift from supplication to interpretation suggests increasing internalisation.

**Father Jule:** ... Or a closing of the door.

**Dr. Müller:** On the contrary, Mr. Holzer, it indicates movement.

**Father Jule:** With respect, Doctor, movement is not the same as seeking grace.

**Dr. Müller:** Nor is seeking grace the stated objective of this programme. *[Pauses.]* You are asked to continue your role. Guide where you are able. The rest will be addressed within session.

[END EXCERPT]

= **Interview 9114.03**

---

**Date:** 21/11/1949

**Interviewed:** SCP-9114

**Interviewers:** Dr. Johan Müller, Head of Psychology Department. Mr. Justin Josef Holzer; Father Jule.

**Foreword:** Interview organised by Dr. Müller at the request of SCP-9114.

---

[BEGIN LOG]

[SCP-9114 paces slowly within the interview chamber. Upon the entrance of Dr. Müller and Father Jule, it stops and turns toward them.]

**SCP-9114:** Eala! So hour is blessed. Thou hart come as summoned. Sit, good friends. Much is now revealed.

[SCP-9114 gestures toward the table. Dr. Müller and Father Jule take seats.]

**Father Jule:** It is good to see you again, Sebastian. You seem… lighter of spirit.

**Dr. Müller:** Yes, remarkably so. Your recent progress has been very encouraging. What matter did you wish to discuss?

[SCP-9114 seats itself opposite both personnel. Its performs small repetitive motion with its right index finger.]

**SCP-9114:** Aah, nis that not what the world has been asking himself for ealdum now, Johan.

[Father Jule and Dr. Müller briefly look at each other.]

**SCP-9114:** The priest of Lenzina wert right. Right fram the foremost word spoken.

**Father Jule:** I was?

**Dr. Müller:** *He* was?

**SCP-9114:** For sooth. Thee said He werk on ways unseen. Long time did I doubt thee. I wondered how Godes servant might fail me… How thou leechcraft of the mind might labour and bear no fruit… Why I was kept enclosed within this castel whilst the landes might yet hold answers. 

[SCP-9114 faces Father Jule.]

**SPC-9114:** Yet now, thine wordes have lifted up veil, aer seeds now grown.

**Father Jule:** Sebastian… If you have found peace, then I thank God for it. Truly.

[Dr. Müller straightens his tie.]

**Dr. Müller:** Yes. Well, insight often emerges only after prolonged reflection. The patient rarely perceives the labour guiding him there. Still, I must admit, your progress has exceeded even what I believed possible when we began.

**SCP-9114:** Misthinken not, Johan, I yield thou most gramercy. Thou didst well guide me. In questioning, thou windest mine gaze. In solitude, thou gavest room for revelation. Through thee, God sharpened mine understanding.

[Dr. Müller nods.]

**SCP-9114:** Aye friends, Not was I prisoned. Ac I was held.

[Silence.]

**SCP-9114:** These years I langode, believing me forsaken. Thought this half-life a curse. Nay, it was waiting. For now I see. I see The Lord had recognised me. Kept His blade sheathed for the tide of need.

[SCP-9114 rises from its seat and raises both gauntlets skyward.]

**SCP-9114:** I am chosen. I am *The* Holy Blade, unsheathed eft, on age of wounds and confusions.

[SCP-9114 gestures toward Dr. Müller and Father Jule.]

**SCP-9114:** And Thou! Thou hast showed me this godgiven purpose. The sooth of this world, is that unorder has spread again, unbelieve wander ever yet. Even within very stronghold walk heathens, unlawful.

[Father Jule's posture lowers. He remains silent.]

**Dr. Müller:** …I’m not certain I follow you, 9114. We've been working on self reflection- *You've* been working on self reflection. You moved past these zealous delusions.

**SCP-9114:** Delusion? Nay, Johan. Completion.

**Father Jule:** *[To himself]* God does not call men to slaughter. He calls them to mercy.

**SCP-9114:** For sooth! Mercy for the faithful. Doom for the forecorrupt.

[SCP-9114 places both gauntlets flat against the table surface.]

**SCP-9114:** Ac fear not thou. For *I*, Sire Sebastian of Almain, chosen knight unto the Eternal Father, shall cleansian the land new!

[Dr. Müller stiffens.]

**Dr. Müller:** This is not what we were building. You understand that. You must understand that!

[Father Jule places a hand upon Dr. Müller’s shoulder. Dr. Müller immediately shifts his posture, breaking contact.]

[SCP-9114 walks around the table, stopping beside them.]

**SCP-9114:** On dawn I depart. If thou wouldst walk beside righteousness, gather thine belonging, give them mine squire.

[Dr. Müller abruptly stands and grasps SCP-9114’s right rerebrace.]

**Dr. Müller:** No, I won’t let you undo this! You remain under Foundation authority!

[SCP-9114 slowly rotates its helm toward Dr. Müller.]

**SCP-9114:** Authority - is sworn unto *me*.

[SCP-9114 strikes Dr. Müller across the face with the back of its gauntlet. Dr. Müller falls to the floor.]

**SCP-9114:** I would not strike thee, leechcraft. Alas thou persist in error, even though God spoke to me through thee. I weep to foreloose swell aid, ac dutdom must be served.

[SCP-9114 forces the chamber door open. The sounds of physical confrontation with security personnel follow.]

**SCP-9114:** *[Audible from beyond the door]* Bar thou Godes path? Stondeth aside, judgment wandreth!

[Dr. Müller is prone on the floor, holding his cheek. He attempts to speak several times, but no coherent words are produced. Father Jule remains seated, gaze unfocused and gripping his crucifix.]

**Father Jule:** *[Barely Audible]* We failed you, Sebastian… God forgive us… 

[END LOG]

= **Incident Report *“Stairwell Event”***

---

**Date:** 21/11/1949

**Type:** Containment breach

---

**Level -2 Sector II (13:40)**

SCP-9114 forcibly exited the interview chamber. Two assigned security officers attempted physical restraint but were overpowered. SCP-9114 incapacitated one officer through blunt-force assault.

The second officer successfully activated the sector alarm before being rendered unconscious by a security baton recovered by SCP-9114 from the downed officer.

**Level -2 Sector II (13:43)** 

SCP-9114 proceeded toward the emergency exit stairwell. The subject halted at a wall-mounted emergency fire axe cabinet. The subject shattered the glass enclosure with a single strike and removed the axe.

SCP-9114 briefly held the implement in both gauntlets and raised it to its helm before proceeding through the emergency exit door.

**Level -2 Emergency Stairwell (13:43)** 

Personnel evacuating were ascending the stairwell when they encountered SCP-9114 exiting Sector II.

Panic ensued among staff. SCP-9114 struck the stairwell railing with the axe, obstructing movement, and forcibly pushed ascending personnel down the staircase.

**Level -3 Emergency Stairwell (13:44)** 

Multiple personnel fell from upper stair sections, causing injuries and obstructing evacuation routes. SCP-9114 descended the stairwell and attacked incapacitated individuals.

*(Transcriber’s Note: Review of footage indicates SCP-9114 selectively targeted personnel it had identified as non-Catholic during earlier interactions.)*

**Level -3 Emergency Stairwell (13:45)** 

SCP-9114 encountered Site Photographer Mrs. Tuğba Sibel whose leg was broken. Mrs. Sibel attempted to disorient the subject using her camera's flash device. The subject displayed no observable impairment and fatally struck her.

*(Transcriber’s Note: Although Mrs. Sibel was Catholic, she was of Turkish descent.)*

Response Team units entered the stairwell simultaneously from upper access points and Sector II corridors.

**Level -3 Emergency Stairwell (13:50)**

Response personnel subdued SCP-9114 through coordinated physical restraint. The axe was recovered without further incident.

**Level -3 Emergency Stairwell (13:51)** 

Medical teams arrived and began triage and evacuation of injured personnel.

**Containment Facility (14:20)** 

SCP-9114 was returned to containment.

---

**Deceased:** 4

**Wounded:** 10

---

**Additional:** SCP-9114 Special Containment Procedures revised. Therapeutic interaction suspended indefinitely. Testing and questioning interactions restricted.

Dr. Johan Müller removed as Head of Psychology Department. Disciplinary action issued under administrative review: [REDACTED].
