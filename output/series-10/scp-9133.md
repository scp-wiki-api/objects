**Item #:** SCP-9133

**Object Class:** ~~Safe~~

**Special Containment Procedures:** ~~SCP-9133 is held in a standard containment locker at Site-46. All experiment proposals must be submitted to Dr. Joxal for approval.~~*See -- Addendum.*

**Description:** SCP-9133 is a beach ball, possessing a low density. Despite this, the object is negatively buoyant.

SCP-9133-A is SCP-9133's reflection. SCP-9133-A frequently desynchronizes in water, ranging from being latent in its movements to performing incorrect actions entirely.

= **{{**BACKGROUND**}}**

SCP-9133 was first recovered from the pool of a resort hotel in ██████, California. SCP-9133 alongside other anomalies were planned to be shipped to Site-12 via maritime transport. Unfortunately, the *MV Jodi* became shipwrecked; SCP-9133 was not accounted for.The *MV Jodi* was shipwrecked due to suspected structural integrity failure in severe weather conditions.

On 01/01/20██, civilian oceanographers encountered SCP-9133 residing within the Marianas Trench -- specifically, the Challenger Deep. Following amnesticization protocols, SCP-9133 was reacquired through the use of an AUV.Autonomous Underwater Vehicle. Upon successful retrieval, Foundation assets witnessed SCP-9133-A's first recorded desynchronization in the water's surface. This prompted SCP-9133's reassessment.

= **{{**REASSESSMENT #: 01**}}**

**Attribute:** Buoyancy remained consistent regardless of salinity.

**Deviation:** SCP-9133 deviated when placed in water possessing a salinity of 35 g/L or higher. Following SCP-9133's removal, SCP-9133-A remained in the water for 2 hours before resynchronizing.

**Notes:** Dr. Joxal was perplexed, citing the lack of a catalyst for this behavior.

= **{{**REASSESSMENT #: 02**}}**

**Attribute:** SCP-9133 was not capable of advanced locomotion.

**Deviation:** Once submerged, SCP-9133 always gravitated towards the pool's deep end. SCP-9133-A remained on the water's surface, spinning in place.Testing took place within Site-46's natatorium.

**Notes:** Dr. Joxal reviewed all documentation regarding SCP-9133's background.

= **{{**REASSESSMENT #: 03**}}**

**Attribute:** Identical to Reassessment 02.

**Deviation:** SCP-9133 was submerged within various areas of the ocean and monitored. Empirical positioning data indicates the object universally translocates towards the Marianas Trench.

**Notes:** *(See Below.)*

= **[BEGIN LOG]**

**Dr. Joxal:** ...Full disclosure, this wasn't my idea.

*[No response.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** For some reason, my colleagues believe your actions point to some kind of intelligence. Like that [calculator](scp-168) or that... [piano thing](scp-4088). It ain't our first rodeo.

*[No response.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** [Sigh] Alright. The hard way then.

*[Dr. Joxal reaches into the tub, pulling out and grasping onto SCP-9133.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** Look. You're gonna give me something I can work with or I can just lock you in the box forever. I'm perfectly fine either way.

*[No response.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** I'm not joking. Last chance.

*[Dr. Joxal sets SCP-9133 into the water; it sinks. There is no further response.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** *[Whisper]* <indecipherable> damn trench.

*[Dr. Joxel prepares to leave when SCP-9133 abruptly floats in the tub. He freezes in place before sitting back in the chair again.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** The trench... the Marianas one, right?

*[SCP-9133 resumes floating.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** No. What if it's not? What if--

*[SCP-9133 sinks; Dr. Jones is silent.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** ...Yes Marianas Trench.

*[SCP-9133 floats.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** No Marianas Trench.

*[SCP-9133 sinks.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** Marianas Trench.

*[SCP-9133 floats.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** Literally any other trench.

*[SCP-9133 sinks.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** ...I remember the pool. Is it about... how deep it--

*[SCP-9133 floats.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** I see. I... I can *definitely* work with this.

*[SCP-9133 continues floating; Dr. Joxal quickly writes notes.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** Of course, you realize we're not gonna throw you back in there, right?

*[SCP-9133 sinks; Dr. Joxal chuckles.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** Hey, it's the rules.

*[No response.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** Secure. *Contain.* Protect -- it's literally in our motto.

*[No response.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** But as long as you *do as I say*... I'm sure we can work something out. We have a pool~.

*[No response. Dr. Joxal frowns; he leans forward.]*

**Dr. Joxal:** Alright, this little *silent treatment* is starting to wear pretty thin--

*[SCP-9133 propelled itself out of the water, striking Dr. Joxal's face. Startled, he inadvertently tips the chair over backwards, slightly injuring himself.]*

*[SCP-9133 slowly sinks back into the water. Dr. Joxal gets up; his eye is visibly twitching.]*

**Afterword:** On conclusion of Reassessment 04, Dr. Joxal sought to establish potential variables that could affect the object's buoyancy.

= **{{**TEST #: 01**}}**

**Variable:** Deflation.

**Results:** SCP-9133 sank; SCP-9133-A inflated itself enormously.

**Notes:** Following manual reinflation, Dr. Joxal found SCP-9133's inflation valve was unable to be accessed again; the object was now incapable of losing air, even through natural effusion.

= **{{**TEST #: 02**}}**

**Variable:** Ratchet straps.

**Results:** SCP-9133 sunk; SCP-9133-A breached its restraints by thrashing itself repeatedly.First recorded instance of SCP-9133-A affecting the reflection of another object.

**Notes:** Dr. Joxal discovered SCP-9133 was now incompressible following the experiment.

= **{{**TEST #: 03**}}**

**Variable:** Coffee.

**Results:** SCP-9133 sank, before quickly becoming positively buoyant. SCP-9133-A constantly vibrated; it resynchronized once the object was removed from the tub.

**Notes:** Dr. Joxal modified experiment parameters.

= **{{**SUBMERSION #: 01**}}**

**Item:** Paint.

**Results:** SCP-9133 rapidly became positively buoyant; SCP-9133-A spun with circular motion. SCP-9133 soon resynchronized with SCP-9133-A's movements.

**Notes:** This behaviour only occurred with water-based paints.

= **{{**SUBMERSION #: 02**}}**

**Item:** Washer fluid.

**Results:** SCP-9133 forcibly ejected itself from the tub. SCP-9133-A was observed thrashing widely.

**Notes:** The tub was later supplied with a support net.

= **{{**SUBMERSION #: 03**}}**

**Item:** Hot sauce.

**Results:** SCP-9133 immediately drifted to the tub's rim. SCP-9133-A was observed exiting the tub prematurely.

**Notes:** The object's extreme positive buoyancy rendered manual submersion infeasible.

= **{{**SUBMERSION #: 04**}}**

**Item:** Milk.Expired.

**Results:** SCP-9133 interacted with the liquid as if it were a solid. SCP-9133-A was observed distancing itself from the tub; it appeared to be floating in midair.

**Notes:** Dr. Joxal theorizes SCP-9133's hydrophobicity was possibly due to the item's "rancidity."

= **{{**SUBMERSION #: 05**}}**

**Item:** Liquid manure.

**Results:** SCP-9133 was strapped to the tub's basin. Once submerged, SCP-9133-A was observed thrashing violently, rapidly contorting itself in a wide variety of shapes. Halfway through testing, SCP-9133-A suddenly faced one of its end panels towards Dr. Joxal before entirely dissipating.

**Notes:** *(See -- Incident-9133-Alpha.)*

= **{{**INCIDENT-9133-ALPHA**}}**

Following Submersion 05, SCP-9133 lost its buoyancy-based properties; additionally, SCP-9133-A no longer appeared near the object.Only applicable for water-based liquid; other mediums were exempt. Shortly after, multiple Foundation personnel had witnessed SCP-9133-A manifesting in their presence before vanishing. Attached below is an abridged list of recorded sightings:

**Location:** Houston, Texas.

**Description:** While consuming coffee, Dr. Heron spotted SCP-9133-A within her drink. Startled, she spilled the contents on herself, inadvertently burning herself.

**Notes:** Notably, Dr. Heron originally suggested the test item for Test 03.

**Location:** Pacific Ocean.

**Description:** Foundation assets confirmed SCP-9133-A routinely manifested within the Pacific Ocean, specifically near the Marianas Trench. A research vessel was stationed on site for observation purposes.

**Notes:** N/A.

**Location:** I-90, United States of America.

**Description:** While carpooling Dr. Joxal, Dr. Eaton encountered SCP-9133-A during activation of the windshield wipers. This caused Dr. Eaton to become distracted and crash his vehicle.

**Notes:** Notably, Dr. Eaton originally suggested the test item for Submersion 02.

**Location:** Pacific Ocean.

**Description:** A Scranton Reality Anchor was deployed and activated on location. SCP-9133-A promptly demanifested and was reported not to have remanifested in the Pacific Ocean.

**Notes:** Dr. Joxal's request for a portable SRA was approved.

= **{{**INCIDENT-9133-BETA**}}**

The research vessel tasked with monitoring SCP-9133-A had failed to respond to a routine radio check. Investigation revealed the vessel sank was shipwrecked under inexplicable circumstances. Upon learning this, Research staff attempted to inform Dr. Joxal.

**Location:** Site-46.

**Description:** Researchers found Dr. Joxal with his head inside one of the restroom's toilets. Dr. Joxal was removed from the receptacle; SCP-9133 was observed in the toilet bowl water before demanifesting.

**Notes:** Dr. Joxal was pronounced dead at the scene. An autopsy revealed he had succumbed to drowning; water and trace amounts of human excreta were present in the respiratory tract.

**Location:** Various Foundation facilities.

**Description:** SCP-9133-A sightings have increased by a massive statistical margin.

**Notes:** Coincidentally, these facilities had a sharp increase in plumbing issues; cases involving personnel being afflicted with severe vertigo and/or cerebrovascular accidents also increased.

**Location:** [Yellowstone National Park](scp-2000).

**Description:** SCP-9133-A was observed manifesting in this location at progressive intervals.

**Notes:** *(See -- Memorandum.)*

= **{{**MEMORANDUM**}}**

SCP-9133 was intentionally deployed into the Pacific Ocean. Not only did SCP-9133-A immediately resynchronize with SCP-9133, the object regained negative buoyancy, subsequently sinking into the water; all complications associated with SCP-9133-A ceased.

SCP-9133 was reclassified Archon until a more permanent solution is found. Updated protocols mandate remote monitoring within Challenger Deep; unauthorized civilian observers require amnesticization. Procedures will be finalized within {{96}} hours.

= **{{**INCIDENT-9133-CHARLIE**}}**

A research vessel was monitoring SCP-9133. Personnel reported SCP-9133-A had manifested around the watercraft while debating the necessity of the object's containment procedures. SCP-9133-A instantly demanifested upon termination of the conversation topic. Further containment procedure amendments are pending.
