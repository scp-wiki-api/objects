**Document: Research Logs**

Further research will be treated with caution as SCP-9135 develops a hail core. Testing will determine changes to SCP-9135 from pending to a fitting class to happen immediately for proper protocol on containment. This testing is available to a third party connected to The National Weather Service called CoCoRaHSCommunity Collaborative Rain, Hail and Snow Network.

**Subject:** Hailstone from SCP-9135.

**Procedure:** Expose the hailstone to a bunsen burner to attempt to melt it since the hailstone did not melt in warm air when falling. The bunsen burner was set on the lowest setting which is around 300°C–600°C then set to the highest setting around 1,500°C to check signs of melting.

**Result:** Hailstone did not react to the bunsen burner at the lowest setting. It was later attempt to raise the heat with the bunsen burner. The hailstone melted at the highest temperature. 

**Hailstone Data Collection**

* **Temperature Set:** 1560 °C (2840 °F)

* **Melt Speed:** 10 mintues, hailstone didn't show signs of melting.

* **Temperature Set:** 1,600 °C (2912 °F)

* **Melt Speed:** 15 mintues, hailstone shown signs of melting.

* **Temperature Set:** 1,100–1,200 °C (2,000–2,200 °F)

* **Melt Speed:** 12 seconds, hailstone fully melted.

After the hailstone fully melted, the liquid from the hailstone turnt a green color. This is unusual behavior that was noted.

**Subject:** Liquid from hailstone

**Procedure:** Skin exposure 

**Result:** 

*Did the liquid present harm towards the skin or any other body part?*

* Harmful factors are not present. See below for details.

**Details:** The liquid did not burn through the gloves nor the skin. The liquid also did not show any signs of causing rashes or any other skin infections. The liquid was put into a proper biowaste bin after testing.

**Subject:** Hailstone from SCP-9135.

**Procedure:** Harmful factors that are similar or different from SCP-9135.

**Result:** 

*Hailstone Harm*

**Similar or different?**

* Neither

**Harmful factors or are harmful factors not present?**

* Harmful factors are not present. See below for details.

**Details:** The hailstone presented an anomalous behavior that is not harmful towards citizens. Research is to continue as rapid growth is being watched.

The National Weather Service scanned the area around SCP-9135, the scan was later sent in to the Foundation. SCP-9135 is expected to rapidly grow. Due to this behavior, SCP-9135 is to be immediately changed from pending to euclid due to SCP-9135 being a risk to freely roam with a harm to human life. An additional experiment is to be held to determine any harmful factors inside SCP-9135 that the radar scan may have missed.

**Description:** SCP-9135 is an extremely violent tornado that is attached to the base of a highly organized thunderstorm.

SCP-9135 is able to jump from cloud to cloud, causing SCP-9135 to be able to appear in another location. Due to this ability, SCP-9135 has been known to cause mass ruination.

**Discovery Log:** SCP-9135 was first discovered when the National Weather Service (NWS) first reported the thunderous supercell on the WSR-88D (Weather Surveillance Radar-1988 Doppler), also known as [NEXRAD.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NEXRAD)

**NEXRAD Scan:**

On Sunday, May 22, 2011, the National Weather Service first pinged the thunderous tornadic supercell on the WSR-88D.

The Foundation requested to have the radar scan from the instance to further analyze the data.

SCP-9135 has been classified as an particularly devastating tornado by the National Weather Service, as SCP-9135 has been found to hit over 200 mph (322 km/h) in over an hour due to rapid growth. Any personnel willing to enter the tornado zone must sign a liability waiver for their life possibly being taken. **Discovery Report:** New data collection from the National Weather Service has found that SCP-9135 has caused affected people to form a rare fungal infection called mucormycosis or *Apophysomyces trapeziformis*.

This fungal infection has been deemed flesh-eating after a citizen of Joplin reported blackening of wounds. The city of Joplin's hospital has since removed the affected citizens' skin to avoid the fungus further spreading. 

This fungal infection has been noted to include other effects such as:

* Headaches.

* Inflammation.

* Visible white/fluffy mold on wound.

* Severe pain.

* Death

It is believed that MTF personnel fell dead from the symptoms above. These symptoms are not caused directly from SCP-9135. Any personnel reading this file must scrap the direct cause of SCP-9135 from any documentation. ~~Otherwise, there is no found cause of the fungus at this time.~~

**Discovery Report:** It was later discovered by Department of Biology that the cause of the fungus was contaminated soil getting into the effected citizens wounds. It is heavily believed that SCP-9135 had kicked up the contaminated soil, which spread it into the wounds.

Mucormycosis or *Apophysomyces trapeziformis* is noted to be commonly found in soils, decaying vegetation, and water containing organic matter such as leaves and soil after this discovery. It is also airborne. 

**Additional Note:** Due to the airborne state of the rare fungal infection, all personnel must wear proper PPE to ensure the infection does not spread.

**Discovery Log:** After surveying the aftermath of SCP-9135, it has been confirmed this rare fungal infection has been caused as a side-effect of SCP-9135's debris path. It is uncertain how widespread this rare fungal infection is.
