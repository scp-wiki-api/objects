**Special Containment Procedures:**

All instances of SCP-9168 are to be housed in a 4m × 4m × 3m hermetically sealed, opaque biocontainment chamber at Site-95, modified to replicate the sub-Saharan African savanna environment native to *Sphaerocoris annulus*. Chamber interior is to be maintained at 24–32°C with relative humidity between 55–70%. Appropriate host flora, specifically *Gossypium hirsutum* (upland cotton) and *Citrus sinensis* (sweet orange), are to be cultivated within the chamber and replenished on a bi-weekly basis consistent with the dietary requirements of the Scutelleridae family.

All behavioral assessments are to be conducted remotely via Foundation-standard thermal imaging monitors mounted outside each unit. Observation windows are to remain sealed with matte black opacity film at all times. Under no circumstances may personnel view any SCP-9168 instance with the naked eye.

**Description:**

SCP-9168 is a previously undocumented insect species designated *Mutoscutum variabile* by Foundation taxonomists. Specimens measure between 12 and 18mm in length and are morphologically near-identical to *Sphaerocoris annulus* (Fabricius, 1775), the Picasso Bug, a species of shield-backed bug belonging to the family Scutelleridae, order Hemiptera. Like *S. annulus*, SCP-9168 exhibits a prominent, dome-shaped thoracic scutellum and a similarly stocky build, which causes it to be frequently mistaken for a beetle at first glance.

However, comprehensive molecular analysis has confirmed that SCP-9168 belongs to order Coleoptera. SCP-9168 possesses the full genetic profile of a beetle while simultaneously displaying the precise anatomical structure of a Hemipteran shield bug, a biological impossibility under standard taxonomy. The mechanism underpinning this contradiction is not currently understood and remains a subject of active investigation by the Site-95 Entomology and Genetics divisions.

Whereas *S. annulus* displays fixed geometric patterning as a form of aposematic (warning) coloration, SCP-9168 instances can actively and voluntarily alter the designs displayed on their scutellum. Pattern transitions take approximately 0.8–2.4 seconds to complete and are accompanied by a faint, low-frequency vibration detectable only by sensitive audio equipment. To date, Foundation researchers have catalogued an estimated 1,200 distinct pattern configurations, though it has not been confirmed whether this represents an absolute limit.

Behavioural observation suggests pattern shifts are at least partially volitional. Instances appear to display specific patterns in response to perceived threats, during feeding, and during interactions with other specimens. The function of pattern variety beyond standard aposematism has not been determined.

Direct observation of an active SCP-9168 instance by a human subject causes a progressive neurological and psychological deterioration. The effect does not manifest through photographic or video reproduction of SCP-9168’s patterns; the dynamic motion of a live pattern transition appears necessary for the effect to initiate. The deterioration progresses through five documented stages:

| 10 seconds - 30 seconds | Mild disorientation, visual afterimages of SCP-9168’s patterns, and a subjective compulsion to continue observing the specimen despite awareness of risk |
| --- | --- |
| 31 seconds - 1 minute | Intensifying geometric visual hallucinations. Auditory hallucinations begin, typically described as rhythmic or patterned sounds. Short-term memory impairment. |
| 1 minute - 3 minutes | Severe multimodal hallucinations. Subjects lose the ability to respond to verbal communication. Significant memory disruption, including retrograde amnesia in approximately 40% of cases. |
| 3 minutes - 9 minutes | Full catatonia. Subjects are unresponsive to all external stimuli. EEG readings are consistent with a waking-dream state. Intervention at this stage is possible but carries high risk of permanent neurological damage. |
| 9+ minutes | Cardiac failure or cerebral haemorrhage, resulting in death. No subject who has reached Stage 4 without immediate medical intervention has survived. |
The compulsion reported in Stage 1 is of particular research interest. Across all documented exposure events, affected subjects have described a conscious awareness of the danger combined with an inability or unwillingness to look away. Current hypotheses suggest SCP-9168 may generate low-frequency infrasound concurrent with its pattern shifts, potentially inducing a trance-like susceptibility in nearby observers. This has not yet been confirmed experimentally.

To date, fourteen (14) SCP-9168 specimens have been recovered and are housed at Site-95 under the care of the Entomology Department

**Addendum 9168-A: Discovery Log**

SCP-9168 was brought to the Foundation's attention following the deaths of three entomological field researchers affiliated with ██████ University, Nairobi, in ████. The researchers had been conducting a routine biodiversity survey in ██████ National Reserve when the incident occurred. Surviving fieldwork notes describe the discovery of what the team described as "*a remarkable undescribed variant of the Picasso Bug — patterns unlike anything in the literature.*"

Foundation operatives embedded within the Kenya Wildlife Service were notified within 36 hours. A containment team was dispatched and seven specimens were secured from the field station; seven additional specimens were recovered from a 3km radius of the site over the following 48 hours. A cover story citing a carbon monoxide leak caused by a faulty generator was disseminated to regional press outlets. Six university staff members and two field support personnel received Class-B amnestic treatment.

No further wild populations have been located. Whether SCP-9168 exists in numbers beyond the fourteen recovered specimens is unknown.

**Addendum 9168-B: Interview Log — Incident 9168-1 Pre-amnestic**

*Full incident report filed separately as IR-9168-1. Summary: On ██/██/20██, Research Assistant Dr. ███ ████████ (Level 1) removed her GNG-GF31 goggles inside the communal containment area, citing equipment malfunction. Goggles were found undamaged. Thermal footage from the 12 minutes preceding the incident shows all fourteen SCP-9168 specimens had aligned to face the same direction and were cycling through identical pattern sequences in synchronisation. This behaviour had not been previously documented. Dr. ████████ reached Stage 3 before extraction. Class-C amnestics were administered. The following interview was conducted upon her partial recovery, prior to amnestic treatment.]*

---

**Transcript — Interview 9168-B-1**.                                

  Interviewer: Dr. ██████. Interviewed: Dr. Andrews 

**<Begin Log>**

**Dr. ██████:** How are you feeling?

**Dr. Andrews:** I — what? Sorry. I'm (pause) sorry. My head is, it's like there's static. You know? Just… (gestures vaguely) static.

**Dr. ██████:** That's expected. Do you know why you're here?

**Dr. Andrews:** Someone said I took my goggles off. In containment. (long pause) Was that me?

**Dr. ██████:** Yes. Do you remember doing that?

**Dr. Andrews:** Sort of. It's like…it's behind glass. I can see the shape of it, I just can't, (rubs eyes) yeah. Yeah, I took them off. (quietly) Shit. I took them off

**Dr. ██████:** Can you tell me why?

**Dr. Andrews:** (pause) It seemed like the right thing to do. (laughs) That sounds insane. I know that sounds insane. I knew it was wrong when I was doing it, I just … it was like… have you ever been in the middle of a sentence and the word just goes? It's right there and then it's not? It was like that but backwards. Like I was about to remember something really, really … (trails off)

**Dr. ██████:** Take your time.

**Dr. Andrews:** Something important. And taking the goggles off felt like the thing that would get me there. (pause) Which is… I know how that sounds. I'm not … I'm not crazy, I have a PhD, I know what those things do to people, I wrote the intake notes for the last two exposure incidents…

**Dr. ██████:** I know. What did you see when you removed the goggles?

**Dr. Andrews:** (long pause) The patterns. (quietly) God, the patterns. They kept… they were changing so fast and I know I should have looked away immediately, I know that, but they were…(stops)

**Dr. ██████:** Go on

**Dr. Andrews:** They were beautiful. Which I know is not the point. I know that's not the.. but they were. And then I started to… I don't know how to say this without sounding completely fucking unhinged.

**Dr. ██████:** Please continue.

**Dr. Andrews:** I started to understand them. Not like … not translating, it wasn't words, it was more like … you know when you hear a piece of music and you just get what it's trying to do? Even if you can't explain it? It was like that. I could feel that they were saying something. Not to me, not .. just saying something. Out into — (trails off)

**Dr. ██████:** Out into what?

**Dr. Andrews:** (long pause) I don't know. And I was almost… I was right there. I was right at the part where I'd know what it was and then… (snaps fingers) nothing. I woke up in medical with a needle in my arm and someone shining a light in my eyes.

**Dr. ██████:** Do you feel any residual effects?

**Dr. Andrews:** (pause) That's the thing. I still feel… close. To whatever it was. Like if I just...(stops abruptly) like it's still there.

**Dr. ██████:** We're still assessing that. One more question. Before you removed the goggles, do you remember noticing anything unusual about the specimens?

**Dr. Andrews:** (long pause) They were all facing the same way. I remember thinking that was weird. (pause) And they were all doing the same pattern. All fourteen. All at once. (pause) Is that… did they do that on purpose?

**Dr. ██████:** We're looking into it.

**Dr. Andrews:** (quietly) Because it felt like… it felt like they were trying to… (trails off)

**Dr. ██████:** Dr. Andrews?

**Dr. Andrews:** Sorry. (pause) What were we talking about?

**<End Log>**

[Interview suspended at 15:58. Class-C amnestics administered at 16:04. Dr.Andrews cleared for return to non-anomalous duties pending full psychological evaluation.]
