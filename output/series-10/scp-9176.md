**Item #:** SCP-9176

**Object Class:** Nihil

**Special Containment Procedures:** No active containment procedures are currently required. Prior to Nihil classification, SCP-9176 was surveyed annually by SCP field agents. As official documentation pertaining to the item was subject to disappearance, all observations were recorded by hand and stored within a secured physical archive at Site-19.

**Description:** SCP-9176 was a 2,834-acre ranch outside the town of Hondo, TX, owned and inhabited by Eric Bauer (aged 86), who exhibited no paranormal properties. SCP-9176 was discovered by Agent R. Kowalska during a survey expedition for an unrelated investigation. His handwritten notes and drawings of the property were found to be unaffected by its paranormal effect. Bauer claimed the land had been inherited from his parents, who had likewise inherited it from previous generations.

Beginning in 1986, portions of SCP-9176 periodically ceased to exist. These disappearances followed no observable schedule or pattern. Following the disappearance of a given section:

* The affected land would no longer be considered part of SCP-9176.

* Structures and livestock located on the affected land would vanish.

* All official documentation referencing the affected land would vanish.

* Memories relating to the affected land would be erased.

In all observed cases, the erased land was replaced by a non-paranormal development. Replacement structures included residential subdivisions, retail centers, warehousing facilities, and utility infrastructure. Public records consistently indicated these developments had existed prior to the disappearance of the affected land. Residents of Hondo lived and worked within the new structures and, when questioned, maintained that they had done so for years.

Residents of Hondo, as well as Bauer himself, showed no awareness that any alteration had occurred. When questioned regarding erased portions of SCP-9176, all interviewed individuals maintained that the removed land had never existed.

Given that SCP-9176 was discovered by chance, the existence of analogous unidentified instances is considered possible.

On 08/12/2022, personnel assigned arrived at the paranormality's recorded coordinates at 08:14 CST. No portion of SCP-9176 was present. The house and Bauer were absent. Residents were shown an artist's depiction of Bauer; previously interviewed individuals who had claimed to know him now claimed not to recognize him.

The site where the house once stood is now occupied by a 93-acre data center campus.
