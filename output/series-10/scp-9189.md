**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9189 is contained within a purpose-built reinforced containment cell at Site-█. The cell is constructed of semi-flexible composite materials and should be subject to weekly structural assessment as part of monitoring SCP-9189's continuous lateral expansion. Standard large-animal husbandry protocols are in effect. SCP-9189 is to be provided with the standard nutrition of a member of its species, along with veterinary care on a scheduled basis. SCP-9189 should under no circumstances be exposed to the outdoors.  

**Description:** SCP-9189 is a cinnamon bear (*Ursus americanus cinnamomum*) whose body extends horizontally at a consistent rate. SCP-9189 is wider than its containment cell at all times. Despite this, SCP-9189 is fully contained within the cell. The mechanism by which this occurs is not currently understood. Investigation is ongoing.

SCP-9189's anomalous width and expansion do not affect its typical biological functions. All of its internal biological processes operate within standard parameters for a specimen of its type. SCP-9189 exhibits no anomalous behavior and does not display awareness of its anomalous properties. SCP-9189 does not present a threat to personnel. 

Since its acquisition in 2011, SCP-9189 has expanded to an estimated width of ██,███ kilometers. SCP-9189's lateral expansion occurs at approximately █ kilometers per hour. The rate of expansion has not increased during this period. No upper limit to the width of SCP-9189 has been established. 

SCP-9189 was created by anartists via previously unknown thaumaturgical means and recovered from a warehouse in Portland, Oregon, by members of Mobile Task Force Upsilon-23 ("Art Critics"). The existence of SCP-9189 was discovered by a Foundation webcrawler upon the flagging of several emails sent to various institutions asking for permission for an art display.

**Addendum 9189.2: Exploration Log**

---

**Exploration Video Log Transcript**

**Date:** 22/11/2011

**Exploration Team:** Mobile Task Force Upsilon-23 ("Art Critics")

**Subject:** Spatial anomaly report, Portland, Oregon

**Team Lead:** Captain R. Park

**Team Members:** Agent D. Osei, Agent L. Eliezer

---

[BEGIN LOG]

*Footage opens on the exterior of a warehouse near the docks. A hand-painted sign on the door above reads "STRETCHED CREATURE PROJECT - OPEN STUDIO".*

**Park:** Upsilon-23 entering the premises. No hostiles observed. 

*The team enters. The interior is a single large open space. Art supplies are visible along the walls on shelves. There is a large corkboard with paint swatches, fabric, and photographs of various animals. At the far end of the room, a bear is visible. Two individuals, one at either end of the bear, are pulling on it. The bear is wider than the room.*

**Osei:** I'm sorry. What.

**Park:** Document everything. 

**Eliezer:** Park. It's wider than the room.

**Park:** I can see that. 

**Eliezer:** It's wider than the building. 

**Park:** Eliezer.

**Eliezer:** I'm just noting it for the record.

*The team approaches the two individuals. Both are wearing matching printed shirts. The shirts read "STRETCHED CREATURE PROJECT" in large multicolor block letters. The lettering extends beyond the edge of the fabric on both sides.*

**Park:** Step away from the bear.

**Anartist 1 [later identified as Alyx Reitzen]:** Someone official, finally. We've been trying to get in touch with someone about permits for like two or three weeks. 

**Park:** Permits.

**Anartist 1:** For the installation. We just have the bear for now, but we need outdoor display permission, and the city keeps stonewalling our emails.

**Anartist 2 [later identified as Olivia Suh]:** We think it might be because of the width situation.

**Anartist 1:** She's really a meditation on the impression of infinity as experienced through the encounter with a concrete object that-

**Park:** Step away from the bear, please.

**Anartist 2:** Can we just finish pulling? We're kind of mid-session, and if we stop now, we lose all of our progress.

**Park:** Now please. 

*The anartists step back. SCP-9189 does not react.*

**Park:** Upsilon-23, spatial anomaly secured. Large animal, no hostile behavior. Requestion transport and a containment team. Secure the premises.

*Agents Eliezer and Osei begin to secure the warehouse. Art materials, equipment, along with relevant documentation are retrieved and catalogued. SCP-9189 does not react to this process. The anartists are escorted from the premises into separate Foundation vehicles. SCP-9189 is transported to Site-█ for containment. SCP-9189 does not resist transport.*

[END LOG]

**Addendum 9189.3: Post-Recovery Interview**

---

> **Interviewed:** Alyx Reitzen and Olivia Suh

>

> **Interviewer:** Dr. H. Moshenko

>

> ~~-~~

> 

> *Dr. Moshenko enters the interview room. The two subjects are already seated. Reitzen has a copy of the artist's statement along with photographs from the warehouse in a Manila folder on the table in front of them. Suh is looking at the door.*

> 

> **Interviewer:** Thank you for your patience. I'm Dr. Moshenko. I'll be asking you some questions about SCP-9189. 

> 

> **Reitzen:**  Misha.

> 

> **Interviewer:** I'm sorry?

> 

> **Reitzen:** Misha. The bear. Her name is Misha. 

> 

> **Interviewer:** Noted. Can you describe how the anomaly was created?

> 

> **Suh:** We stretched her.

> 

> **Interviewer:** Can you elaborate on that?

> 

> **Reitzen:** We really just got the ball rolling, kept it going faster and faster. It evened out around month two. She's been going on her own since then. She doesn't need us to pull her anymore. 

> 

> *Reitzen sighs.*

> 

> **Reitzen:** We just do it anyways. 

> 

> **Interviewer:** Was the bear acquired for this purpose in mind? 

> 

> **Suh:** Yeah, we got her from a sanctuary. She was being rehomed, I imagine you guys took the paperwork when you raided the studio.

> 

> **Interviewer:** And the intention behind the project was?

> 

> **Reitzen:** It's hard to explain without her here.

> 

> **Interviewer:** Try. 

> 

> **Reitzen:** It's about infinity, it's about being there. Right in front of you, breathing. She has a form you can identify, that you can look at. And she keeps going. You stand next to her and you feel it in a way you can't feel it anywhere else. 

> 

> **Interviewer:** Feel what specifically. 

> 

> **Reitzen:** That she doesn't end. 

> 

> *Dr. Moshenko makes a note. Suh is still looking at the door.*

> 

> **Interviewer:** Is the process replicable?

> 

> **Suh:** Probably. We documented everything. We had plans for others, after Misha. We were going to do a whole series.

> 

> **Reitzen:** We had a wide elk, a wide salmon, and a wide moth planned that we were very excited about. 

> 

> **Suh:** The moth was our centerpiece.

> 

> **Reitzen:** We had our whole series mapped out, Misha was just the first.

> 

> *Neither subject speaks for a moment.*

> 

> **Reitzen:** She was just the first.

> 

> **Interviewer:** Are you aware of any other individuals who had contact with the bear prior to Foundation recovery.

> 

> **Reitzen:** Just us and our friends who supplied the projects. We all cried when it was finished. She was so real. 

> 

> *Suh stops looking towards the doorway and sits up.*

> 

> **Suh:** Can we see her?

> 

> **Interviewer:** That won't be possible. 

> 

> **Suh:** Is she okay?

> 

> **Interviewer:** The anomaly is contained and stable.

> 

> **Reitzen:** Does it mean anything to you that we made something?

> 

> *Dr. Moshenko looks at his notes. The room is very quiet. He does not answer. After a moment he closes his folder.*

> 

> **Interviewer:** Thank you for your cooperation. You'll be processed for release shortly. As a concession for your assistance during recovery, it has been approved to give you a release of a preliminary containment file on SCP-9189. 

> 

> **Reitzen:** What does that mean?

> 

> **Interviewer:** You'll both be able to review a copy of the documentation we have produced on the anomaly.

> 

> **Suh:** Can we see her instead?

> 

> **Interviewer:** No.

> 

> *Moshenko leaves. A Foundation aide enters and places another Manila folder on the table between the two subjects and leaves without speaking. Reitzen opens the folder. A majority of the first page is black bars. A majority of the second page is black bars. Almost every aspect of SCP-9189 is redacted. Reitzen closes the folder.*

> 

> **Suh:** That's all that's left?

> 

> *Neither subject speaks for a moment.*

> 

> **Reitzen:** Yeah. 

> 

> **<End Log>**

>
