SCP-9202-A is the designation for a class of sub-networks contained within SCP-9202. SCP-9202-A instances are characterized by geographic significance, large size, and cultural or religious identity. The creation of new SCP-9202-A instances is rare, but it is typically denoted by the creation of new national, state, or religious identities. Typically, a person can only be a part of two SCP-9202-A networks, one denoting religious ideology (SCP-9202-Aa) and one denoting national ideology or political stance (SCP-9202-Ab).

SCP-9202-B is the designation for a class of sub-networks contained within SCP-9202. SCP-9202-B instances are characterized by sparse occupation, smaller size, tighter connections, and less "realistically meaningful" culture (examples include art movements, online "fandoms", large-scale community projects, etc.). SCP-9202-B instances do not seem to be affected by the-ongoing-ΨK-class-Psychic-Reversion-scenario, unlike SCP-9202-A instances.

SCP-9202-α to SCP-9202-δ are designations for different levels of xenoton emission observed in humans. Interestingly, apart from the α band, each band is neatly separated by several orders of magnitude, with SCP-9202-α instances within the 100 to 8000Xe/s range, SCP-9202-β in the 8 to 15kXe/s band, SCP-9202-γ in the 230 to 800MXe/s band, and SCP-9202-δ theoretically existing in the 94-530GXe/s band. SCP-9202-α and SCP-9202-β, while not as directly separated as -γ or -δ instances, have two separate peaks within their ranges, at 400 Xe/s and 12 kXe/s respectively.

Pearson is often unable to grasp abstract concepts and requires tutoring and one-on-one sessions often. She needs to set aside more time to study; if possible, a private tutor may be required. I personally do not have enough time to spend one-on-one sessions with her but if her Benefactor is so willing I could be employed over breaks.

**AP Sociology**: A+

Pearson is extraordinarily gifted for her age. She is showing advanced growth beyond her age and is often ahead of the rest of the class. It is my recommendation that she continue in personally-oriented classes. She may want to take a job as a counselor, therapist, or psychiatrist in the future; combined with her psychic abilities, she could help a lot of people.

**Sub-veil History**: B

Pearson shows interest in the more personal aspects of history but is often unable to memorize the required material. I would recommend that she set aside time at home to study.

**Thaumatology 3**: A-

Pearson shows remarkable aptitude for spellcasting and spellweaving. She has already advanced to experimental glyphs from newer studies; however, her understanding of theory is lacking. She shows aptitude in areas relating to affecting people but I have noticed she has trouble emotionally connecting with others. Rituals have proved to be an area of difficulty; although she is able to coordinate with others of her caliber, the other students have a hard time matching her strength. I've noticed she's been trying to connect the other students, which is good, but they've been rejecting her for the most part (whether intentionally or not I don't know), which is especially concerning since she's been here almost four years now. This may be a long running difficulty but I haven't personally been at the school long enough to tell.

**Intro to Psychisism**: A-

Pearson is one of the most powerful psychics I have ever met, however, she has trouble harnessing and controlling her power. It is of my opinion that she continue in psychisism classes so that she is not a danger to those around her. I have been spending time with her to help her control the power but she may soon exceed even my ability. Unfortunately, I have noticed that her classmates have been excluding her from activities, shielding against her, and even sending back their own probes, sometimes to the point of causing pain. I've tried to talk to her about it but she puts up her walls whenever I do.

**Addendum IV (Interviews with personnel affected by SCP-9202-Ψ)**:

Due to this document's reorganization, the parts of this interview that are not displayed above have been highlighted. For a full list of interviews, please submit a GR-43 Multiple Document Request Form for Documents A7KCG002-int-01 - A7KCG002-int-72 (total 143 pages) to your RAISA office or virtually through a work terminal. Be aware that some parts of these interviews may be redacted.

> **Interviewer**: [REDACTED] [REDACTED]

> **Interviewee**: Dr. Natasha Atacama

> **Date**: 27/12/2026

> ~~-~~-

> **[Interviewer]**: State your name and station for the record, please.

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: I am Natasha Atacama, doctor of the Foundation and current research head of SCP-9202.

> **[Interviewer]**: Can you describe the containment procedures for SCP-9202 for me?

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: Isn't it already in the file?

> **[Interviewer]**: We would like your perspective.

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: Of course.

> **[Interviewer]**: The first thing I'd like to go over would be the containment procedures for SCP-9202-γ instances. Why terminate them immediately?

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: We call them puppet masters, or just pups for short, because of the way they can control people. You just fall to their will. If one of them manages to figure out their powers and exert them, it can go south *very* quickly. We only barely took out Pearson and that was mid-stage. A late-stage pup would probably require a full MTF to take out, with xenoproof suits and infohazard scanning. 

> **[Interviewer]**: What is the "downregulation" talked about in the file?

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: Do you know what xenos are?

> **[Interviewer]**: That was something I was meaning to ask you about later, but if it's important now, go ahead.

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: Xenos are particles emitted by the 9202 network connections. It comes from the Dictionary of Obscure Sorrows, the word *xeno* meaning the smallest unit of human connection. The typical human emits twelve kiloxenos per second [12 kXe/s]. The more xenos you emit, the brighter you shine on the sky cameras. A typical pup emits around a half thousand megaxenos per second [520 MXe/s], which shows up as a bright spot on our cameras. Downregulation is a technique by which we decrease the capacity of a pup by about seven orders of magnitude using a thaumaturgic ritual that's classified even to me now. The downregulation triggers a reaction in the pup that converts it back down to an aver [sic].

> **[Interviewer]**: Sorry, a what? I would prefer if you used proper abbreviations for instances.

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: I-- A -β instance. Aver is short for average.

> **[Interviewer]**: I see. In your file, I remember seeing you were against the immediate termination of δ instances.

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: Yes. I was the one who got -δ instances removed from the kill-list. The Foundation can be a bit trigger-happy sometimes.

> **[Interviewer]**: I would think deltas would be the most dangerous.

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: They would be the most *useful* is what they would be. A created delta, by simulation, would immediately start teleporting all over the place. That's *why we have* the attraction telekill chamber, after all. To capture any deltas for research.

> **[Interviewer]**: So it wasn't a moral question, then?

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: ... No, I don't think so. Those people that turn into deltas and gammas are too far gone to qualify as human anymore. Nobody with that level of power over others can be seen as human.

> **[Interviewer]**: I find that quite interesting. Remind me, your file says you went to Deer College, correct?

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: Yes. I got my doctorate in thaumochemistry there. It was a relatively new field at the time; I specifically published the founding paper on resonance structure glyphs.

> **[Interviewer]**: How has your degree specifically helped with the development of the SCP-9202 protocols and the research that is still being performed?

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: I made the first xenoconversion lens on accident and published it to the Foundation Journal of Chemistry, give or take a year and a half ago? It was the first thing I did at the Foundation that really got attention. I was doing thaumochemistry research for SCP-0000 when I actually *made* the thing. It wasn't even supposed to be that thaumoactive; it was just for enhancing the impenetrability of the glass. Apparently that triggered the conversion effect, and for a while, *that* was the anomaly. Where is the energy coming from to make these photons? And then we figured out the thaumoactive chemicals in the human brain elsewhere, and the Foundation called on *me* to figure it out since I was the expert on thaumoactive resonance structures. I took a sample of the material as a curiosity (with permission, of course) and then realized, Oh, this is the same thing! which led to the discovery of the 9202 network. Then my *fucking sister* happened, and the ΨK, and now look where we are today.

> **[Interviewer]**: Your file says you also got a second degree while you were there.

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: I did get a degree in anart.

> **[Interviewer]**: If I remember correctly, one of the things taught in that class is "a strong moral compass", and you did get full marks, so how do you still justify that?

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: Like I said. Anyone who has that level of control over people, or who has committed grave crimes, like the D-Class, can't be considered human anymore.

> **[Interviewer]**: I see. And what was your experience during initial containment efforts of SCP-9202-Ψ?

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: Probably the worst part of my tenure at the Foundation.

> **[Interviewer]**: I doubt it was the worst --

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: Have *you* ever had to stay up to 3 A.M. making *anart* with an AWCY teenager because apparently your three-year-old degree in anart made you the most qualified person on the team, and *then* fall prey to your *own anart* and find yourself looking for fursuits on Etsy at four in the morning, on your fourth cup of double-shot espresso? No? I thought not.

> **[Interviewer]**: I would think the worst part of your employ was when Pearson --

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: My SISTER betrayed ALL OF HUMANITY for some *stupid reason* that I CANNOT COMPREHEND. But even that wasn't enough; she had to fucking make it my mess to clean up.  ALL OF THAT CLEANUP AND WORK WAS ASSOCIATED [with] HER. So yeah, you could say Pearson was the worst thing that ever happened to me. But I consider that second to the fact I HAD TO CLEAN IT ALL UP.

> **[Interviewer]**: ... I see. And finally, what was your experience as an affectee of SCP-9202-Ψ?

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: I... Emotional reactivity, lowered professionality, inability to focus, all of that. Is this a secret DFS visit?

> **[Interviewer]**: I assure you, if I was an agent of the Department of Fire Suppression, you would know. Finally, have the SCP-9202-Ψ procedures been effective for you?

> **[Dr. Atacama]**: ... I have experienced an uptick in my previous abilities since when I was not a part of the furry community.

> **[Interviewer]**: Alright, well that just about wraps it up for today. Just so you know, that specific SCP-9202-B instance has been being disseminated throughout the Foundation, so you should be able to talk without shouting at people again soon. Next set of interviews won't be until Monday.
