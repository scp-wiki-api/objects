## **Special Containment Procedures**

SCP-XXXX is contained within Containment Enclosure-XXXX-A at Site-██. The enclosure consists of a 120m × 120m artificial woodland ecosystem designed to replicate a stable temperate forest biome.

Containment is maintained through ecological regulation rather than physical restraint. SCP-XXXX is designated as a managed ecosystem-dependent anomaly.

The enclosure must include:

*Mixed deciduous canopy coverage (minimum 40%)

*Natural soil substrate integrated with fungal and root systems

*Artificial freshwater stream loop with continuous filtration

*Multiple low-disturbance zones for behavioral stabilization

*Subsurface containment mesh extending 15 m below ground level

*Reinforced perimeter composed of adaptive polymer containment glass

*Personnel are to treat SCP-XXXX containment as ecological maintenance rather than security  enforcement.

## **Description**

SCP-XXXX is a cervid-like anomalous entity approximately 2.1 meters in height at the shoulder. The entity exhibits morphological similarities to terrestrial deer species; however, no known biological classification matches its structure.

**SCP-XXXX exhibits the following anomalous properties:**

#### 1. Spatial Phasing and Displacement

SCP-XXXX is capable of passing through solid matter. During elevated stress states, it demonstrates short-range spatial displacement consistent with localized teleportation.

#### 2. Emotional Dampening Effect

Physical contact with SCP-XXXX induces measurable reductions in human aggression, anxiety, and physiological stress indicators.

#### 3. Dietary-Dependent Behavioral Shift

SCP-XXXX requires continuous intake of fresh fruit biomass. When adequately fed, SCP-XXXX remains docile and non-hostile.

When deprived of sufficient nourishment over extended periods, SCP-XXXX enters a deprivation state characterized by:

*increased territorial behavior

*phasing-based traversal of containment barriers

*hostile engagement with nearby personnel

*elevated lethality potential

*Extended deprivation (estimated ≥1 year without adequate feeding) results in sustained predatory behavior and significantly increased casualty potential.

SCP-XXXX consistently returns to baseline behavioral stability following refeeding.

### **Addendum XXXX-α: Behavioral Response Profile**

SCP-XXXX is non-hostile under normal conditions and does not initiate aggression unless provoked or physically harmed.

When injured or threatened, SCP-XXXX may perform a limited defensive bite response before de-escalating. Affected personnel typically experience complete tissue regeneration within approximately 10 minutes, consistent with anomalous salivary compounds.

When the anomaly is left without fruit biomass for extended periods of time, the object has been proved to become hostile after a suspected time of a few months. During this period of hostility, the object has been proven too severely injure, and sometimes [DATA EXPUNGED] personnel.

### **Addendum XXXX-β: Personnel Access Protocol**

SCP-XXXX is designated as a Low-Restriction Access Entity. Personnel with Level 1 clearance or higher are permitted to enter the enclosure during confirmed calm-state periods.

Approved activities include:

ecological maintenance

observational research

fruit distribution operations

Prohibited actions include:

aggressive movement toward SCP-XXXX

restraint attempts

interference with feeding systems

provocation during deprivation indicators

Repeated aggressive interaction increases SCP-XXXX stress response and accelerates transition into hostile behavioral states.

### **Addendum XXXX-γ: Nutritional Ecosystem Protocol (“Green Cycle Maintenance”)**

SCP-XXXX containment stability requires sustained provisioning of fruit biomass.

A minimum of 300–500 kg of mixed fruit per week must be introduced and distributed across the enclosure via ecological dispersal methods, including canopy placement, ground scatter zones, and concealed deposits.

Centralized feeding is discouraged, as SCP-XXXX exhibits stronger stability responses when food sources are distributed throughout the ecosystem rather than concentrated.

Failure to maintain feeding cycles may result in deprivation-state breach conditions.

### **Addendum XXXX-δ: Emergency Recontainment Protocol (“Bait and Stabilize Procedure”)**

In the event of containment breach or hostile state manifestation, personnel are authorized to initiate emergency recontainment procedures.

Authorized responders include Site security personnel, Mobile Task Forces, and approved D-Class personnel under supervision.

#### Procedure:

*Deploy fruit biomass dispersal units immediately

*Avoid hostile engagement or pursuit behavior

*Establish visible non-threatening food distribution patterns

A*llow SCP-XXXX to transition into feeding behavior voluntarily

*Initiate gradual return-to-containment once calm state resumes

*Armed presence is not effective in suppressing SCP-XXXX and may increase stress response  probability.

***D-Class personnel may be used as fruit carriers or dispersal agents but are not expected to engage SCP-XXXX directly.**

#### **Research Note**
