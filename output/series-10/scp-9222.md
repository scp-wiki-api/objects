= **Addendum 5:** Medical Report

# Medical Report

On June 4th, 2025, Department of Astronomy Researcher Dr. Adam Altair was found in his office, unconscious, slumped over his desk. He was holding the day's newspaper and was covered in his own blood. The following items were found in the room with him:

* One copy of 'The New Orleans Herald', headline: 'FAMED SINGER MISSING'.

* An uncut record.

 * Of note, no record players exist within the office.

* A game of settlers of Catan. Almost complete, however the 'Robber' piece is missing.

* A broken ruby wedding ring, smashed beyond repair.

* A teaspoon, with which Altair attempted to remove his own eyes.

 * This has been deemed the primary source of the blood.

Altair was quickly brought to the nearest Foundation medical facility and treated with epinephrine, opioids, and depressants. An invasive scan was performed on his brain which determined that while he was *not* under the influence of a cognitohazard, he *was* afflicted with a powerful antimeme, requiring a CRV of {{1929}} to resist (Altair did not possess this).

An untested treatment plan was proposed and performed in which Altair was continually sedated and exposed to increasing doses of mnestics. This plan persisted over nearly 2 weeks culminating in a dose of 3 parts Class-Y and 1 part Class-Z Mnestic. After this, and the stabilization of Altair's vitals, he was allowed to awaken. A trusted figure was brought in to interrogate him, his boss, Charlie Laplace, was selected.

![](mudroom.png)

I have entered the facility. I don't really know what to call the place I'm in now. @@@@

@@@@

I feel very strongly that I should go up, it is brighter, less damp, and I can escape through a window should the worst come to pass. This is the tactical choice, it is not the one I am making.

I will go down. I am searching for a secret, and those tend to be buried in the deepest possible places.

![](nursery.png)

I find myself at a nursery. This is not altogether unexpected. Large Foundation sites, which L13 certainly was, very commonly contain nurseries. Researchers live on site, have kids, stuff happens. The place stinks to high heaven, something is rotting but I cannot guess what. I cannot imagine why this place was created in its current state. It contains capacity for only 4 children, the average Foundation nursery should be able to hold over a hundred.

@@@@

@@@@

I pried further into the used bed. Didn't find much at first, until I checked under the pillow. There was a human eye, severed violently. I am leaving this room, something in my gut finds it deeply uncomfortable.

**UPDATE:** Once I closed the door, I heard the brief wail of a baby come from within. I did not hear this when I first approached. It was quickly silenced. I could have sworn I heard a 'shush'. I think I made a good choice by taking off my shoes.

![](armory.png)

I am in an armory. It is very standard, exactly as you'd expect. It's maybe the first thing I've seen that is as I'd expect. It is this prospect I find the most unsettling. It has all the standard munitions, though no records. I can't find any mission case files, nor any inkling of the men who used to use this room. In a place so otherwise untouched, it seems strange that the files specifically are what has been taken.

I am sorely tempted to use this opportunity to arm myself. I, of course, am armed with a standard Foundation handgun, though that is a small ordinance compared to what I find on these walls. I cannot tell if taking a gun would upset this place, or if it would simply be following the customs as I did in the mudroom. This is a dilemma, there is no right answer, but only in one answer do I get a gun, it hangs now from my belt.

@@@@

@@@@

In terms of oddities, there is one, the bullets. Almost all the heavy weapon ammo is gone (though mercifully not that of the high caliber pistol I selected), the casings remain, piled up in drawers, but the payloads are gone. It is like every bullet has been used.

Stranger still is what accompanies the casings. Crosses, Stars of David, and other assorted religious iconography, enormous amounts of it. It is like after the bullets ran out, they tried turning to these symbols as surrogates. It is possible that this explains the religious connotations of the sign outside, though I somewhat doubt it. My gut tells me that that sign warns of something I have not yet seen.

# Blacksite L-13 Investigation

by *Adam Altair*

![](tunnel.jpg)

I wish I'd kept my fucking shoes.

After the armory, I descended further, down the next staircase. I'm sure I'm missing huge swaths of the site, but my goal is at the bottom so that's where I'm going.

Eventually, that staircase dropped off, and here I am in this flooded tunnel. It is an undeniably oppressive atmosphere, but it also the first time that I don't feel watched since I entered the site. This has two disturbing implications. One: The feeling was something actually watching me, and it refused to follow me down here. Was it maybe too big? Or was it afraid of something else down here?

The second possibility is that I was being watched by the site itself, if that's somehow possible. This would explain why the gears stopped when I looked at them. If so, why'd it stop looking? Maybe I took a wrong turn, and left the site? I haven't seen any turnoffs though. Maybe I went the totally wrong direction, and it thinks I won't be able to make it back and find its secrets. I do not know, I am not sure I want to.

@@@@

@@@@

As for the hallway itself, it is extensive. I have easily been walking half a mile. It is dark, too, thank god for my flashlight. There is writing on the walls, I'll attach a list below, not much of it means anything, though of course the big question is there too.

What springs to mind the most is the source of the flooding. I can guess from the smell: its sewage, but why is it here? Why was that even a possibility? This wasn't meant to be a sewage tunnel, it was connected directly to the other floors, it was even listed on signs as 'path to bottom section'. Who builds their site connected to a sewer system?

Phrases on the walls:

> {{WHAT DID YOU DO TO DESERVE THIS?}}

> {{COULD YOU LOVE YOURSELF IF YOU HAD TO?}}

> {{WHAT HAPPENED TO ALICE STARR?}} 

![](boiler.png)

I have officially transferred from confused to baffled. I have found the boiler room. It is enormous, dozens and dozens of machines. One of them pumps water.

It pumps water. Into the tunnel. On purpose. That means they intentionally built the tunnel into the other half of the facility to be flooded with water.

Neat.

I'm way too scared to touch anything here, though I did find a map of the underground. Almost all the water is pumped through the tunnel into an absolutely enormous chamber about another quarter mile from here, but one single pipe shoots off into an area I've no idea how to access. It's labeled "Director's Office".

@@@@

@@@@

Now, I haven't seen any keycards anywhere, but I figured the director's office would probably be locked up, so I decided to check. I found a computer terminal and asked "is the directors office locked?"

It responded "Yes. Director Keycard Required."

Now, I haven't seen any bodies from which I could liberate a keycard, but in case it comes up, I wanted to know who to 'look for'. I asked "who's the director?"

"Adam Altair, Containment Specialist."

This is odd for several reasons. One, why am I the director of the site? And two, why am I a 'containment specialist?' I'm a researcher at the Department of Astronomy, I've never been on the containment team for an anomaly, and I'm certainly no director. It is deeply disconcerting.

At least the feeling of being watched hasn't returned.

![](containment.png)

I found what the water is going to, and I found out how L13 contained its anomalies.

It's basically a lazy river, huge in scope I'm sure. It must stretch for miles. Most of it not human accessible, or at least I couldn't find a way. Floating on the river is 360 (trust me, I counted) big glass tubes. They are a little bit shorter than the average person, but much wider. They're labeled "Generalized Containment Chamber".

We have our own version of these. We've been trying to make them work for a while now, and they do, but only a little. Our top-end ones are designed to handle virtually all Safe and Euclid anomalies, and some Keters, but not much else. These ones can handle seemingly anything, Safe, Euclid, Keter, Tiamat, and this one weird esoteric class called 'Salem'. I'm sure they can handle more than just these, but those are the ones explicitly listed.

I don't know where they got them but, judging from the size of the circuitry alone (via a quick estimate of the chip size and Moore's law), they're at least 200 years more advanced then our own.

@@@@

@@@@

This at least answers one of the most burning questions I had about this place. It bears all the signs of a research and containment facility, but seemingly lacked the actual containment part. There were so many corners that I should have turned and found myself staring at a immense steel door labeled 'Containment Zone', and instead met benches, potted plants, and, if I was lucky, a water cooler.

There are no signs of any anomalies ever actually being contained here, of course. No records, no refuse, no scratches. No dust. Nothing to indicate these machines aren't straight from the box, bar one: a small wooden piece in the corner of one of the tanks, it was pure luck I spotted it. I didn't get a great look, but I think it was a robber from Settlers of Catan, weird.

The only left for me to do now (without turning back of course), is to check out the director's office. Hopefully, the prior director kept good notes.

**METHOD:** Memetics discovered that the ideatic makeup of the message was directly interlaced with that of over 200 other anomalies. A site, L13, was formed for the comprehensive and express examination of the transmission (at this point officially classified as 'SCP-9222'). 9222 was found to be deeply conceptually related to the city of New Orleans, hence, this was chosen as the location for the site.

All related anomalies were relocated to L13 and research came into full force.

---

**FINDINGS:**

Memetics performed a biological analysis, and discovered that the root of the cognitohazard was in the human eye. From this they developed Protocol HOTSPOON in order to counteract it. Additionally, the primary vector for contamination was found, the sentence: '{{What happened to Alice Starr?}}'. This phrase was isolated and detoxified, these combined to form the primary containment method for SCP-9222.

The Department of Tactical Theology focused on the source of the transmission, a distant star, and were able to confirm that SCP-9222 was a quasi-god. While it did possess considerable influence, it was not actually sapient, and could only exert will in the form of repeated patterns of behavior.

The Department of Astronomy performed a historiographic analysis, comparing the 9222 signal to their own data. They found that the signal had been broadcast multiple times, most recently on May 20th, 1964, where it was mistaken as the 'Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation'.

They further broke down the signal, using increasingly sophisticated analysis techniques. They found that it was 98% comprised of human screams (primarily the voice of a man and a woman), and 2% comprised of a song. The song was isolated and found to have been a known recording from July 4th, 1929. They were unable to make further progress on the timeline of SCP-9222.

---

**END OF PROJECT:** Project ANEMOIA was never officially concluded, and continues in some form to this day. Its current priority is neutralizing a second 9222 related cognitohazard.

= **Addendum 16:** Containment Breach

**Individuals Present:** Mr. Altair, Alice Starr

**Date:** June 4th, 1929.

**Location:** The Rising Sun Opera House

*Altair is marching upstairs to the main floor of the house. He is muttering under his breath.*

**Altair:** Goddamn [indecipherable].

**Altair:** Whole fuckin' thing's a [indecipherable].

*Altair approaches Alice's door.*

*He presses a piece of paper onto the door, it sticks there, it reads 'you're fired.'*

*He clenches his fists, and then smashes his head into the door.*

*It opens.*

**Alice:** Is everything alright?

*Altair jumps back.*

**Altair:** You... what?

*She glances at the sign.*

**Alice:** You don't really mean that, do you?

*Altair shakes his head, still speechless.*

**Altair:** I.. uh.. I couldn't find you.

*She tilts her head.*

**Alice:** Really? I'm not exactly a hard girl to spot.

*She gestures to herself.*

*She is wearing an ruby dress, which shines painfully against the harsh lighting of the backstage.*

**Altair:** I looked everywhere. The bar, the woods, and further, couldn't find you.

**Alice:** Really? I've been here the whole time, rehearsing. Haven't left once tonight.

**Altair:** Bullshit. I must have knocked on that door a hundred times.

**Alice:** I don't know what to tell you...

*She smiles.*

**Alice:** Are you sure you were looking for *me*?

**Altair:** I--

**Alice:** Doesn't matter, the shows about to start! Wish me luck!

*He smiles, it does not cover his entire face.*

**Altair:** Luck.

**Alice:** And remember... you've worked really hard to get me here, you've earned this, you deserve it.

*She smiles before running onto stage.*
