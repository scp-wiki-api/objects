SCP-9227-1 is to be stored in a standard non-anomalous storage locker ID no.832 located in Site-4 Sector 2. Access to the locker requires Level 2 or higher keycard authorization. Inspections of SCP-9227-1 must be conducted every Tuesday to verify its condition and confirm that no unnatural deformations have occurred to SCP-9227-1.

SCP-9227-1 is a humanoid skull with empty tooth sockets. When a non-anomalous tooth is inserted into one of SCP-9227-1’s vacant sockets, it is transformed into an instance of SCP-9227-2.

Once removed from SCP-9227-1, instances of SCP-9227-2 begin to defy gravity. Rather than falling under their own weight, they remain suspended in place and can be freely repositioned within a three-dimensional environment, maintaining their location and orientation until moved by an external force.

Instances of SCP-9227-2 pose no significant threat under normal circumstances. Unless a individual flicks a instance of of SCP-9227-2 with a applied force of 27 mph (43 km/h). Contact below this threshold will merely cause the instance to drift a short distance in the direction of the applied force.

When an instance of SCP-9227-2 is successfully flicked, it will accelerate in the direction of the flick, reaching a velocity of approximately 2,700 mph (4,345 km/h). Upon impact with a solid object, it typically shatters. The kinetic force generated by the collision is sufficient to inflict severe injury or, in rare cases, death upon impact with unprotected personnel caution is advised. Testing has demonstrated that the projectile can be reliably stopped only by materials with exceptionally high shock-absorbing properties.

<summary>+ Addendum-9227-A Restoration Protocol Alexander</summary>

****Discovery (Cont.):****

**Protocol Alexander**, named in honor of Senior Researcher Alexander after he discovered this method during Experiment Log 9227-A12, is the standard restoration procedure for SCP-9227-1.

****Protocol Alexander (Procedure):****

In the event that SCP-9227-1 sustains cracks, fractures, or other structural damage, Restoration Protocol Alexander is to be initiated by filling all of its tooth sockets with human teeth. Teeth originating from non-human animals do not produce the same effect. However, teeth obtained from chimpanzees and other species closely related to the Hominidae family have been observed to function equivalently for the purposes of this protocol.

Once every socket has been filled with human teeth, SCP-9227-1 will begin vibrating at approximately 1,800 RPM. During this process, all damage to the skull is gradually repaired through currently unexplained means. Upon completion of the repair cycle, the inserted teeth will rapidly decay, detach from their sockets, and fall away.

Following the event, SCP-9227-1 will appear structurally intact and exhibit no visible signs of previous damage. Additionally, all dirt, debris, stains, and loose fragments present prior to the procedure will disappear, leaving SCP-9227-1 in an apparently pristine condition.

<summary>+ Addendum-9227-B Discovery Retriever Mission-B</summary>

**Date:** 4 June 2008

****Retriever Mission B (Discovery):****

The SCP Foundation MTF unit **Golden Retrievers** conducted a raid on the residence of millionaire Lin Huang following evidence of anomalous activity linked to his name.

The information originated from a separate Foundation operation referred to as **Retriever Mission-A**, targeting a recently leaked Chaos Insurgency facility known as **Cell-55**, during which recovered documents referenced Huang as a known associate and financial supporter of the Insurgency. These documents indicated that Huang had been providing resources in exchange for the acquisition of anomalous artifacts to be transferred to his estate, forming what he referred to as a personal "collection."

Based on this information, Foundation assets escalated the investigation, leading to the discovery of his hidden mansion and authorization of a secondary raid, designated **Retriever Mission-B**. During the search of Huang’s residence, primarily his personal office, additional evidence was recovered confirming his direct involvement with Chaos Insurgency operations.

Multiple anomalous objects were discovered on-site as part of his "collection," each displayed in his office and subsequently secured for Foundation containment. These items are believed to have been obtained through Insurgency channels in exchange for financial compensation and political influence.

At the time of this report, Lin Huang remains at large, believed to be evading Foundation capture with assistance from the Chaos Insurgency. The raid resulted in the seizure of his residential property, incriminating materials, and his personal collection. Huang himself was not apprehended.

<summary>+ Experiment Log 9227-A15</summary>

**Status:** Approved

**Experiment Log:** 9227-A15

**Object:** SCP-9227-1

**Lead Researchers:** Dr. Phil and Dr. Alexander

---

**Procedure:**

A set of 5 teeth with varying levels of anomalous properties was placed on a table alongside SCP-9227-1. Each tooth possessed a different anomalous effect, ranging from simple color-changing behavior to minor temporal distortion.

---

**Results:**

| Tooth | Anomalous Properties | Reaction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tooth A | Changes color when exposed to heat. | When Tooth A was disconnected from SCP-9227-1, it merely defied gravity and floated in place. However, after Dr. Phil flicked the tooth, it immediately shattered into fragments. |
| Tooth B | Minor teleportation abilities within a 1–5 meter radius when enclosed in a person's palm. | Similar to Tooth A, the instance floated after disconnection. When struck, it teleported directly in front of Dr. Alexander before instantly crumbling into dust. |
| Tooth C | Partially metal-infused tooth (provided by Dr. Phil). | No anomalous reaction observed. Upon disconnection, the tooth simply fell onto the table. |
| Tooth D | Artificial replacement tooth. | No anomalous reaction observed. The tooth detached and fell normally with no additional effects. |
| Tooth E | Chipped human tooth. | No anomalous reaction observed. The tooth dropped to the floor without incident. |
---

**Conclusion:**

Only teeth possessing active anomalous properties exhibited unusual reactions after being disconnected from SCP-9227-1. Non-anomalous or effectively inert samples displayed no observable effects and behaved as ordinary objects.

---

**Notes:**

> "Results vary."

> — Dr. Phil

> "I want to try my teeth."

> — Dr. Alexander

</details>

**Status:** Approved  

**Experiment Log:** 9227-A21  

**Object:** SCP-9227  

**Lead Researcher:** Dr. Phil  

---

**Procedure:**

Two instances of SCP-9227-2 were placed 4 feet above the ground and 5 feet apart. A D-Class was instructed to flick one instance into the other.

---

**Results:**

D-88811, a trained precision subject, successfully caused the two instances to collide. Upon impact, all of his teeth shattered violently.

---

**Conclusion:**

Collisions between two instances of SCP-9227-2 cause catastrophic dental failure in the initiating subject.

---

**Note:**

Don't do that again...

**Status:** Approved  

**Experiment Log:** 9227-A33  

**Object:** SCP-9227  

**Lead Researcher:** Dr. Phil  

---

**Procedure:**

A D-Class had an instance of SCP-9227-2 inserted as a replacement for a missing tooth. The D-Class was then instructed to attempt to flick the implanted tooth to observe the resulting effects.

---

**Results:**

D-72912 was flung across the room upon activation, resulting in multiple injuries. Repeated attempts confirmed that the direction of the flick correlates with the direction of the subject’s displacement.

---

**Conclusion:**

When an instance of SCP-9227-2 is implanted in the gums, the subject can be propelled by flicking the implanted instance while it remains in place.

**Note:**

<summary>+ Experiment Log 9227-A3</summary>

**Status:** Approved  

**Experiment Log:** 9227-A3  

**Object:** SCP-9227-2  

**Lead Researcher:** Dr. Phil  

---

**Procedure:**

A robotic arm was constructed to mimic a human index finger. Due to anomalous requirements, the system was infused with blood. An instance of SCP-9227-2 was placed in a 10 m × 10 m chamber for testing.

---

**Results:**

Upon contact, the robotic finger triggered a reverse reaction. Instead of moving in the direction of the flick, the instance moved in the opposite direction, destroying the robotic hand.

---

**Conclusion:**

Mechanical or artificial replication of the flicking motion causes inverse response behavior. Androids and cybernetically enhanced personnel are prohibited from interacting with SCP-9227-2.

---

**Note:**

what...  

- Dr. Phil (Partially Robotic)

<summary>+ Experiment Log 9227-A7</summary>

**Status:** Approved  

**Experiment Log:** 9227-A7  

**Object:** SCP-9227-2  

**Lead Researcher:** Dr. Alexander  

---

**Procedure:**

A severed D-9821 (from  previous experiment conducted by Dr. Alexander) arm was used to test whether a non-living hand could perform the flicking motion on SCP-9227-2.

---

**Results:**

Dr. Alexander entered the chamber with a cooler containing the arm. When the flicking motion was attempted, the hand exploded on contact, covering Dr. Alexander in blood. He remained unharmed and appeared satisfied. Later, the bone had reformed into a tooth-like structure resembling SCP-9227-2.

---

Dead humanoid tissue attempting the flick motion results in explosive failure and partial structural transformation into tooth-like material.

---

**Note:**

"Good for tooth replacement"

<summary>+ Experiment Log 9227-A12</summary>

**Status:** Approved  

**Experiment Log:** 9227-A12  

**Object:** SCP-9227-1, SCP-9227-2  

**Lead Researcher:** Dr. Alexander  

---

**Procedure:**

Dr. Alexander inserted 32 human teeth into SCP-9227-1 to test full-socket activation. Dr. Alexander  volunteered himself to insert each socket.

---

**Results:**

SCP-9227-1 began shaking violently. Dr. Alexander remained in contact with SCP-9227-1 flick its forehead several times because he thought it would act similarly too a instance of SCP-9227-2. Instead of responding to flicking, SCP-9227-1 initiated structural self-repair. All damage from previous experiments was restored, and the teeth decayed and detached afterward.

---

**Conclusion:**

SCP-9227-1 begins self-repair once all sockets are filled. This led to the creation of **Protocol Alexander**.

---

**Note:**

"Thank you for the recognition... I guess the results weren't what I expected, but this is good too."  

- Dr. Alexander
