**SCP-9270:** Volveré Aunque Me Borren

by [TopDownUnder](top)

Content Warning for: **Suicide, gaslighting, and mental illness**

**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9270 is installed within Area-34, Sub-Level 39. Access is restricted to personnel possessing Level 4/9270 clearance or above. SCP-9270 is kept isolated from all external networks; all interactions with SCP-9270 are to be done through hardline terminals with one-way data input protocols. Sub-level 39 is to be fully outfitted with Letulu Noospheric Dampeners. Direct visual observation is prohibited during activation. 

Activation of SCP-9270 requires approval of the O5 Council, the current Director of the Department of Anomalous Weapons Development, and the Ethics Committee. All proposed activations are to include full documentation of the target's identity, including full legal name, biometric data, and at least three justifications for targeting. Unauthorized use of SCP-9270 is considered grounds for termination.

Following activation, all known records containing references to the target are to be reviewed and updated to ensure full erasure. This includes, but is not limited to, digital archives, physical documentation, and audiovisual media. Personnel assigned to review are to undergo standard Class-B amnestic treatment within 48 hours. 

Knowledge of SCP-9270-ERR is restricted to the SCP-9270 Research Team.

**Description:** SCP-9270 is an eigenweapon developed by the Foundation, measuring approximately 2.4m x 1.8m x 1.6m, composed of interlocking metallic components, analog input interfaces, and organic material interlaced throughout its internal chassis. SCP-9270 is continuously operational, despite the lack of a power source. 

SCP-9270 is capable of erasing individuals from consensus reality. Targets are removed from physical reality, with memories and records manipulated to omit them. This process is not instantaneous nor complete in its erasure, with activation ending after 72 hours. 

SCP-9270 operates by locking onto an individual's Noospheric signature. From there, it locates and deletes any related nodes from the NoosphereA conceptual space consisting of the totality of human knowledge and thought. and alters reality to remove any evidence of their former existence. As a safety precaution, any nodes deleted from the Noosphere are copied into SCP-9270's storage prior to deletion. These nodes can be accessed and interfaced within a chamber attached to SCP-9270.

The first version of SCP-9270 was developed in 1951 by Olaf Lunden, the founding Director of the [ Department of Anomalous Weapons Development](awd-hub). This initial version functioned by modifying past events to avoid the birth of the target. While effective, this method resulted in cascading effects and unforeseen consequences. The current means of erasure was developed in 1963. Since then, SCP-9270 has been continuously modified and updated by AWD. 

**Addendum.9270.1:** Execution Logs

SCP-9270 has been used by the Foundation to erase individuals deemed to be security risks, de-stabilizers, or otherwise antagonistic towards Foundation operations. Due to the incomplete nature of SCP-9270's erasure, it has been almost exclusively used against individuals of little notoriety.

**Addendum.9270.2:** SCP-9270-ERR

SCP-9270-48 was designated for removal on 2025-02-14. Surviving records indicate that SCP-9270-48 was a Costa Rican civilian, informally associated with a pararevolutionary network in the Central Valley region. While no direct anomalous activity is attributed to SCP-9270-48, Foundation documentation cites major involvement in the coordination of mutual aid efforts, secure transportation of persons of interest, and the distribution of anti-Foundation materials among Veiled populations.

SCP-9270-ERR designates a failure state in which an individual targeted by SCP-9270 can continue to exist. To date, SCP-9270-ERR has only been observed in the case of SCP-9270-48. A year after SCP-9270-48 was erased, her ex-wife showed signs of incomplete suppression.

**Addendum.9270.3:** ERR Suppression Attempts

Following confirmation of SCP-9270-ERR and the absence of an identifiable origin, the SCP-9270 Research Team authorized a series of suppression attempts targeting Dr. Alma Sáez.

## Cognitive Anchoring

**Procedure:** Dr. Sáez was placed under supervised therapeutic observation. Sessions focused on identity reinforcement, emotional grounding, and differentiation between verifiable memory and inferred constructs.

**Result:** Dr. Sáez demonstrated full orientation and no measurable cognitive impairment. Subject continued to report the presence of an unidentified individual, described consistently as "close" or "familiar."

During guided exercises, Dr. Sáez repeatedly paused before responding to prompts, stating "I feel like I'm responding for two people."

## Amnestic Treatment

**Procedure:** Class-B amnestics were administered to disrupt memories associated with SCP-9270-ERR. Follow-up doses were calibrated to suppress emotional recall.

**Result:** Unrelated autobiographical memory recall was measureably reduced. SCP-9270-ERR manifestations persisted.

Dr. Sáez reported difficulty recalling minor personal information, while simultaneously describing SCP-9270-48 with increased confidence and specificiy. When questioned on this, Dr. Sáez responded: "Everything feels farther away. But she doesn't."

## Environmental Reset

**Procedure:** Dr. Sáez was relocated to temporary housing within Area-34, under the pretense of her current living quarters being contaminated with [SCP-742](SCP-742). Dr. Sáez's belongings were replaced with standard Foundation-issue furnishings. Access to personal records, communications, and external environments was restricted.

**Result:** No reduction in SCP-9270-ERR. Within 24 hours of relocation, Dr. Sáez began referring to specific areas of the new quarters as if shared, including statements such as "You always stand there" and "Are the lights hurting your eyes too?"

This attempt was suspended prematurely by Dr. Akabi Hayk, citing potential psychological risks to isolating Dr. Sáez.

## Sensory Isolation

**Procedure:** Subject was placed in low-stimulation observation suite for 48 hours.

**Result:** SCP-9270-ERR intensified. Audio logs captured Dr. Sáez engaging in an extended one-sided conversation. Speech patterns indicate paused consistent with waiting for a response. At multiple points, Dr. Sáez altered her posture to accommodate an unseen second party.

## Noospheric Suppression

**Procedure:** A Letulu Noospheric Dampener was installed within Dr. Sáez's quarters. The system was calibrated alongside surveillance equipment to activate during SCP-9270-ERR manifestations.

**Result:** Field stability degraded within 6 hours of initial activation. Monitoring systems recorded continous low-level interference with the field. Attempts to isolate or excise the signal were unsuccessful. Surveillance indicated no change in SCP-9270-ERR activity. When asked to describe how she felt the night before, Dr. Sáez stated: "It felt clearer."

Upon deactivation of the field, system logs across three terminals contained text entries consisting of a single repeated phrase: "Volveré."
