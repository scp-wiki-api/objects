**Item #:** SCP-9282

**Object Class:** Safe

**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9282 is kept within a storage locker in item storage Site-2090. Due to the limited quantity of SCP-9282 instances, Foundation personnel that wish to use an instance of SCP-9282 are required to submit a request to research lead Dr. Andrew Meyers. The request will then be forwarded to the Foundation’s Medical Resource Committee.

**Description:** SCP-9282 refers to a large box of adhesive fabric bandages. Upon discovery, the box contained 72 out of 110 listed items. Due to testing, only 66 currently remain. If placed upon an open wound, SCP-9282 instances will act as a non-anomalous standard bandage. SCP-9282’s anomalous properties manifest upon placing an instance on a disfigured or amputated limb, or an approximate location of a removed or altered organ.

Testing has revealed that SCP-9282 is capable of fully regenerating limbs or organs that have been amputated or disfigured. While SCP-9282 does not appear capable of fixing neurological damage, it can completely eradicate an illness or disease if it is the root cause of an amputation or disfigurement. SCP-9282’s full capabilities remain unknown due to limited quantity, but it is hypothesized that more extreme body modifications could be possible.

SCP-9282’s healing process will begin immediately upon placement of the instance; said instance does not need to remain on the affected area for the process to complete. ~24 hours upon placement, SCP-9282’s healing process will conclude.

All test subjects of SCP-9282 that were deemed successfulSee Test Report 1. reported positive psychological changes within a week of the completion of testing. It is unknown if this is an effect of SCP-9282 or simply a result of new-found confidence within the subjects.

**Test Report 1:** It has been reported that SCP-9282's affects failed to activate when an instance was placed upon the pelvis of D-21434, who lost her uterus due to uterine cancer several years prior. When questioned, D-21434 stated that she was happy with the fact that her uterus was gone. This has lead SCP-9282 researchers to hypothesize that SCP-9282's effects may be tied to self-image.

**Incident Report:** On 07/12/2024, it was reported that SCP-9282's storage locker was accessed without permission by Researcher Suzanne Kaneko. Notably, Dr. Kaneko had been absent from work for the past few days. The following information excerpt taken from Researcher Kaneko's personnel file has been deemed relevant:

Before her introduction to the Foundation on 02/09/2013, Reports indicate that Researcher Suzanne Kaneko survived a self inflicted bullet wound through her left eye. 

Despite this injury usually either being fatal or resulting in severely decreased quality of life, the Foundation was notified when it was reported that Researcher Kaneko had survived with the following conditions and symptoms:

> Mild Ataxia

> Hemiparesis

> Epilepsy

> Chronic pain

> Mood dysregulation

> Impaired depth perception

It was deemed that due to Researcher Kaneko’s intelligence and competence in human biology and medicine not being impaired, she would be offered a contract with The Foundation.

Researcher Kaneko has not been located. Upon discovery, she will still be reprimanded for theft of an anomaly.
