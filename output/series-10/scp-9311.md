SCP-9311 - A Series of Elaborate Precautions

**Special Containment Procedures:** Testing on SCP-9311 will continue until its purpose has been identified. All instances of SCP-9311 are to be stored within Warehouse 14-B, 14-C, and 14-D at Site-19. Further containment is pending.

**Description:** SCP-9311 is a collection of 455,807 artifacts produced by The Factory and various subsets. All instances of SCP-9311 are completely identical, constructed with a mixture of nickel-plated steel, vulcanized rubber, ceramic, glass, plastic, and a small quantity of an unidentified resinous material no longer in commercial use.

SCP-9311 is difficult to summarize without comparison. SCP-9311 resembles, in part:

* a laboratory instrument,

* a kitchen appliance attachment,

* an automotive component,

* a musical instrument,

* a surveying tool,

* a toy,

* a piece of medical hardware,

* and the upper half of a device whose lower half is missing.

The true purpose of SCP-9311 is unknown. Since the first instance was contained in 1989, and The Factory has continued to produce it without any signs of stopping, it has been determined that SCP-9311 must have a purpose of some kind.

Printed on the bottom of each SCP-9311 instance is a serial number. The serial numbers range from 0000002 to 35241119, though gaps exist. No two recovered instances share the same serial number.

SCP-9311 instances have been recovered at several dozen locations since it was first documented. SCP-9311 was first discovered following the demolition of the Meyer & Sons Agricultural Storage Annex outside Terre Haute, Indiana. The structure’s sub-basement contained 18,000 crated instances of SCP-9311, all packed in wax paper and straw, along with shipping manifests indicating transfers from six defunct shell companies linked to known Factory fronts. These instances were originally classified under the List of Anomalous Objects until more were recovered four months later.

The only clue leading to SCP-9311's purpose was a folder containing what is believed to be the original patent to SCP-9311, located within an abandoned puppy mill in Lowell, Massachusetts formerly owned by The Factory. The document is believed to have been created by several people with little contact between each other, leading to minor aesthetic differences. Most of the contents suffered severe water damage and were illegible.  The documents have been digitally transcribed below.

MODEL 88-C

 

PROPERTY OF THE FACTORY

 

FOR COMMERCIAL APPLICATIONS

 

PATENT PENDING
