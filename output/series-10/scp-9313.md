**Item #:** 9313

**Object Class:** Safe

**Special Containment Procedure:** SCP-9313 is held within Item Warehouse-71. No precautions are required when handling the item.

**Description:** SCP-9313 refers to a CRT television set that fails to display when powered on. Despite this, individuals who look upon the screen of SCP-9313 will claim the item does function, and is tuned to an out-of-service channel. This anomalous effect can be neutralized through resistance to psionic influence or by assuring oneself of their juvenescence.

**Discovery:** SCP-9313 was discovered on March 5, 2005, in Macon, Georgia. It was found among the possessions of 82-year-old Jane Austins by her son Steven Bodalec during the fulfillment of her will. While attempting to sell the item, Mr. Bodalec received several complaints from buyers that the television would only display one channel. This lead to an altercation and detainment by county sheriffs, alerting a Foundation agent embedded in the Georgia State Patrol.

When prompted on the item's origins. Mr. Bodalec stated the following:

* Ms. Austins lived alone.

* Ms. Austins was supposedly senile.

* Ms. Austins was estranged from most family.

* Ms. Austins did not leave her house, aside from purchasing groceries.

* Ms. Austins spent most days watching the news.

* Nothing is burnt into the TV.
