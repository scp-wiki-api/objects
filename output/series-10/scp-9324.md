**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9324 is kept in a secure standard anomalous items locker within the safe-class containment wing of Site-19. 

If any unusual sounds are heard from within or near SCP-9324's containment locker, the duration and nature of these sounds are to be logged and reported to Dr.Grace. The source of these sounds are currently being investigated.

~~Testing is only permitted with authorization from Dr.Grace.~~ Testing is suspended indefinitely.

**Description:** SCP-9324 is a clear acrylic photo-insert keychain measuring approximately 5.0 cm x 3.5 cm, attached to a standard steel split ring. The object shows minor structural wear consistent with domestic use, including light surface scratching. The central compartment can be pried open to allow the insertion of a small photograph.

The anomalous properties of SCP-9324 manifest when a human subject inserts a photograph of an individual with whom they share a significant emotional bond (e.g., a family member, romantic partner, or close friend) into the central compartment.

Upon closing the compartment, the subject experiences a profound sense of comfort regarding the individual in the photograph. Any grief, anxiety, or interpersonal stress associated with that person is entirely mitigated.

<summary>+ Experiment Logs</summary>

**Experiment-9324-1**

---

**Subject:** D-1910

**Input:** A physical photograph of D-1910’s mother, whom D-1910 identified as “Sarah Vance” and had expressed severe anxiety regarding her failing health prior to the test.

**Results:** D-1910 was instructed to place the photograph inside SCP-x and hold the object for sixty seconds. Upon contact, D-1910’s heart rate stabilized. The subject smiled and expressed an overwhelming sense of relief, stating, "I don't have to worry anymore."

**Post-test analysis:** Researchers attempted to access the medical and civil records of "Sarah Vance" to update D-1910’s psychological profile. Database queries returned zero matches. Cross-referencing D-1910’s birth certificate listed his maternal parent as "Eleanor Vance," who passed away during childbirth. When questioned, D-1910 expressed confusion, stating he had never known anyone named Sarah and had always been at peace with being an only child raised by his father.

**Researcher Notes:** Likely an administrative error during D-Class intake. The intake clerk probably recorded the wrong name during the initial interview, leading to the discrepancy. D-1910's sudden emotional stability is an effect of the anomaly.

**Experiment-9324-2**

---

**Subject:** D-8831

**Input:** A photograph of D-8832. D-8831 and D-8832 were assigned to the same cell and had developed a close relationship.

**Results:** D-8831 exhibited immediate relaxation. When asked how he felt about his companion, D-8831 stated he felt "entirely content" and "perfectly fine with being alone in the sector."

**Post-test analysis:** Research staff attempted to locate D-8832 for a follow-up interview. Internal Site-19 rosters indicated that the designation D-8832 did not exist. DNA databases, security footage, and housing logs showed no record of an individual matching D-8832's description ever being processed or residing at the site. Cell 404, previously recorded as a double-occupancy unit shared by the two D-Class, was found configured as a single-occupancy unit.

**Researcher Notes:** Site-19 has been experiencing bugs since the last server migration. The automated skipping of designation numbers is an annoying glitch, but it explains why the system thinks D-8832 never existed. I’ve submitted a ticket to IT. 

**Experiment-9324-3**

---

**Subject:** D-2911

**Input:** Photograph of biological daughter (Lily ██████).

**Results:* Subject's depression was completely eliminated. Subject smiled and reported a feeling of peace and safety regarding his family.

**Post-test analysis:** Standard background checks reveal D-2911 has no biological or adopted children and underwent a vasectomy at age ██. Medical staff concluded the subject was experiencing a stress-induced false memory.

**Researcher Notes:** D-2911 should’ve never been selected for this test. His declining mental health and his kidnapping charges all contributed to this. I’ll be more mindful when selecting test subjects. 

**Experiment-9324-4**

---

**Subject:** D-9021

**Input:** Photograph of fiancée.

**Results:** Subject stated that the weight of the "engagement" had been lifted and he felt "unburdened."

**Post-test analysis:** HR records confirm D-9021 has been legally single his entire life. An investigation into the "fiancée" led to a dead-end; no record exists in any federal, state, or social media registry.

**Researcher Notes:** We’ve really got to look into the massive amounts of missing and/or wrong data. If this keeps up, we might even need to overhaul all the site-19 records. Furthermore, we need more mentally stable D-class personnel. This batch keeps not having relations with any of the people in pictures placed within SCP-9324, not only that, those people don’t even exist! I’m taking this issue up to site command and suspending all testing.

**INCIDENT REPORT 9324-ALPHA 19/10/2011**

---

**Reporter:** Junior Researcher Boyd

**Description:** A persistent sound originating from the vicinity of SCP-x’s containment locker. Sounds resemble a rhythmic, muffled thumping or scratching, accompanied by faint, high-pitched sounds that almost sound like screaming. It's really distracting.

**Response:** Maintenance teams inspected local HVAC arrays, piping, and air vents. No source of the sounds were identified. Further investigations required.

**INCIDENT REPORT 9324-BETA 21/10/2011**

---

**Reporter:** Security Guard Miller

**Description:** During my night patrol, the vibrations from inside the locker were intense enough to cause the adjacent metal plating to rattle. It sounded like someone frantically hitting a hollow wall, maybe plastic or a similar material, with their fists. It stopped the moment I tapped on the locker door.

**Response:** Facilities and machines in the vicinity of the locker were checked thoroughly. No cause was found. Other anomalous objects housed in the same room are currently being tested for any unknown effects.

**INCIDENT REPORT 9324-GAMMA 24/10/2011**

---

**Reporter:** Doctor Grace

**Description:** It appears that all photographs use in SCP-9324 testing are gone. The drawer they were kept in is completely empty. Security camera footage and forensics have shown no signs of theft or trespassing. It also seems like they have been missing for a while, and we've just noticed.

**Response:** The entire research lab was searched thoroughly. No trace of the photographs have been found.

**Addendum.9324.1:** On 5/11/2011, during a routine inventory audit, Dr. Grace utilized a digital microscope to inspect the interior compartment of SCP-9324 for any physical degradation or residue left behind by previous inputs.

Upon magnifying the inner chamber of the plastic housing, Dr. Grace noted that the clear acrylic walls of the insert slot appeared heavily abraded. The abrasions formed dense clusters of microscopic, vertical striations in groups of 5 separated by █ millimeters.

Dr. Grace concluded the report by attributing the micro-abrasions to friction caused by the repeated insertion and removal of photographs during the initial tests. 

Furthermore, SCP-9324 has increased in mass by █ grams. It is unknown how or why this has occurred, and it is now SCP-9324’s research team’s top priority to understand and explain this phenomenon. Testing with human subjects is still suspended until further notice from high command.
