DESCRIPTION: SCP-9338 is, in its current form, an indestructible,Under the Sa-Pan definition, proposed in *Fundamental Properties in a Paratechnological Context* (Pan & Sa, 2013). static, cuboid block of wood, measuring 50cm x 80cm x 120cm.Most of the time. While impossible to sample, the majority of experts have agreed the wood belongs to the species *pinus tabuliformis* var. *tabuliformis*.Notably, this species is not native to Singapore, where SCP-9338 was discovered. Apart from its indestructibility, SCP-9338 exhibits identical physical traits to any other cuboid of wood of the same presumed species.

HISTORY: SCP-9338 was discovered on the 12th of March, 2020, at geographical location {{1°23'28.5"N 103°58'49.8"E}}. Its original form differed greatly from its current one:

DESCRIPTION: SCP-9338 is an anomalously, impenetrably dense forested area spanning an estimated 150 square meters. It comprises trees of the species *pinus tabuliformis* var. *tabuliformis*, but the exact number is impossible to ascertain; both visual and technological scans appear to "get lost" within the area. The trees, while not individually impervious to destruction, exhibit a collective resilience to eradication efforts; each time a tree is destroyed, cut down, or otherwise damaged, the area anomalously shifts to produce a new, whole tree to replace it.

Initially discovered by civilians ghost-hunting in the nearby Old Changi Hospital,Relevant video has been scrubbed from the internet by Site-512 IT. Relevant individuals have been amnesticised. **MTF-ꙟ-1** was deployed for the initial investigation, which preliminarily classified the anomaly as **SAFE**/**DARK**/**NOTICE**.Risk class was later changed to **CAUTION** by EL&R. A temporary cordon was placed around SCP-9338. For further information on the investigation history of SCP-9338, refer to Addendum 9338.I.

PROPOSED SPECIAL CONTAINMENT PROCEDURES: SCP-9338 is to be temporarily held by EL&R. It is to be kept away from other blocks of wood. It is to be kept near to a piece of pure jade at all times. There must be at least two specialists from EL&R on standby to respond to any incidents involving SCP-9338. It is to be stored in the centre of a sufficiently large space (at least 150 square meters).

SCP-9338 shall be treated as follows in different forms:

- When in the form of **wood**, it is to be treated as a low-threat anomaly, isolated in accordance with the above procedures.

- When in the form of **forest**, one tree is to be carefully and deliberately removed by one of the above trained specialists. This will regress it to the form of **wood**. The removed tree may be burned after.

- When in the form of **dense forest**, or its original form, one tree is to be carefully and deliberately removed by one of the trained specialists, prioritising whichever tree in reach is perceived as the highest. This will regress it to the form of **forest**. The removed tree may be burned after.

- When in the form of **forest** or **dense forest**, where the above attempts fail, the piece of pure jade is to be carefully and deliberately added by one of the trained specialists, inserted from the perceived left of the anomaly. If in the form of **dense forest**, specialists are advised to watch for falling wood. If this succeeds, it will enter the form of **beautiful jade**.

- If the above fails, or if it enters the form of **beautiful jade**, EL&R leadership is to be notified immediately. A designated verbivoreUndefined. is to be deployed.

Addendum 9338.IV: Proposed additional description for  SCP-9338Do not attempt to propose this again.

DESCRIPTION: [..]

SCP-9338 is, to be more precise, the Chinese bound morpheme 木 *mù*. In its base state, it is highly unstable, due to its lack of inherently stabilised meaning; fortunately (or unfortunately), it defaults to the generic understanding of 木块 (wooden block).Perhaps this has to do with the visual perception of the word 木 as something small and non-complex.

This high instability manifests in its propensity to collapse *upwards* into the simplest single-character morpheme that takes 木 as a root, 林 (forest). In the form of the forest, it is trivially reduced to its base state via figurative removal of the excess 木. The unstable faux-morpheme decays quickly into the significantly-stable meaning-base 树 (tree), and can be safely burned.The faux-morpheme's decay, as opposed to the pure morpheme's collapse, is best understood as a result of the pure morpheme's preference to stay in a stem most aligned with the root, disregarding the stability of the result.

In around 24.1% of cases,Empirically estimated. SCP-9338 is carried forward by semantic momentum and collapses further upwards into the next simplest single-character morpheme, 森 (dense forest). At this juncture, figurative removal of the excess 木 results in the production of two 树. Aiming for the perceptually "higher" one reduces the risk of crush.So far, only two incidents have occurred, both non-lethal. Of course, failing to remove the upper 木 still results in regression to 林, but instead of a clean un-densing, half of the morpheme collapses on top of you.

It is theorised that the careful application of jade (玉) will be sufficient to collapse SCP-9338 into beautiful jade (琳). This will only be applied as a last resort, as 琳 is a morpheme far less stable than the base 木;In modern Chinese, 琳 is found almost exclusively in names; its base meaning is rarely used. any collapse from it will have catastrophic results.Hence, deployment of the verbivore.
