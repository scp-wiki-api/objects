**Special Containment Procedures:**   The full known text of SCP-9355 is classified level 4. Verbal recitation experiments require written approval from the HMCL supervisor.

A class-2 Ontostatic FenceA network of linked Scranton Reality Anchors, used to isolate regions of anomalous ontology from the surrounding space. is to be maintained around both Area 9355-2 and 9355-3 until the anomalous spaces have fully disconnected from baseline reality.  Anchors in these fences are to be rotated regularly and closely monitored for symptoms of fivefold dissonance.  Mobile Task Force Mu-17 ("Iron Horses") is assigned to monitor the area and prevent civilian incursions into Area 9355-2 using cover story Epsilon 6 ("Gas Explosion").

Survivors of Incident 9355-3 are to be housed in the anomalies wing of Site-17's on-site orphanage pending further evaluation.

**Description:** SCP-9355 is an illustrated book of children's poetry titled *Asterisk Cookies and Other Ways to Unwind,* attributed to [Dennis Lee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dennis_Lee_(author)) in 1976.  When recited aloud, poems from SCP-9355 cause various anomalous phenomena.  The nature and intensity of such vary based on a poorly understood interaction of the following factors:

* The specific poem being recited.

* Whether the speaker has the poem they are reciting, and to a lesser extent other portions of SCP-9355, fully memorized.

* The age of the speaker and of any affected individuals.

* The context of the recitation.

* The speaker's degree of affiliation with GoI 05-30 (The Unwinding Circle) or related Fifthist heresies.

While the full text of SCP-9355 contains 25 poems, several remain completely unknown and the full text of most poems is not confirmed. Some poems contain content which can be interpreted as referring to classified information.

SCP-9355 does not exist; it was never written nor published.  No physical copies exist, and no references to SCP-9355 have been documented prior to April of 2020.

All information related to SCP-9355 comes from recordings, documents and testimony related to the Stellar Ecdysis Educational Collective (SEEC, pronounced 'seek'), a branch of GoI-05 (the Fifth Church) formerly based out of Anklebone, Saskatchewan.  It operated out of two facilities, the Fifth Chapel of the Stellar Carapace  (now designated Area 9355-1) and the Simon Grazeloae Memorial Academy (now designated Area 9355-2).

#### {{Gary Summers Witness Statement: April 7th, 2020}}

---

Okay, so first, the verschooling program, right?   Short for "Reverse Schooling."  It's this wild new education system where instead of *learning* things, you'd *remember* the truths that were already inside you.  Drawing on past lives, parallel worlds, quantum entanglement.  Completely the opposite of locking kids in a classroom and drumming facts into their heads, you know?  Very forward-thinking.  Really disrupting traditional education.

And they'd actually learn stuff!  They had this ten year old boy they showed around who could read, write, and talk in like twenty different languages including the Divine Tongue, and he knew all this weird math, it was really impressive.

So that's where SEEC came from.  The Church was already running a private elementary school in Anklebone so it was pretty easy to organize, and most of the local Fifthist community came around to the new sect.  Obviously everyone wanted their kid in the new thing, but it was pretty exclusive at first.  First year, just a couple of kids.  Next couple years, four or five get in.  This year is the first time they really expanded it out, pushed to get some of the newer converts involved?  So this year there's at least 30 new kids in the program.  And the Lowells, Jack and Michelle, just down the block from me, they get in too.

Anyway uh, the incident.  I was over at their place one afternoon, helping Jack and his oldest boy do some work on their car, right? So we were in the garage, and Michelle was in the living room doing the program with the younger one.  Three or four times a day, they read the kids these nonsense poems, right?  So she started on one of those and for some reason I could hear it so clearly, through a closed door from two rooms away.  It went like --

Ah, that's right.  Okay, I'll write it down.

**Warning: Do not read any part of the following aloud.**

---

//One year lost at sea with me.

I told you I must surely die

Without a bite to eat!

So I ate your legs with shells of eggs

and now your legs are plastic pegs!

And don't you think that's neat?//

//Two years lost at sea with me.

You told me you'd not make it through

without a little snack!

So you ate my hands with rubber bands

And on we sailed to unknown lands!

I'm never going back.//

//Three years lost at sea with me.

We told us we'd no more to see

on ocean or on shore!

So we ate our eyes with butterflies

And as for lies or hows or whys

We'd say that there's no more!//

//Four years lost at sea with me.

He said from sound to be unbound

before it's time to go.

So he ate our ears with pruning shears

And told us dears to face our fears

And know what we should know.//

After the first verse, I couldn't move my legs.  Jack got it worse, he was standing up and just toppled over.  Didn't make a sound, though.  Must have hurt, surely.  The older kid just wobbled a little.

Second verse, I dropped the wrench I was holding.  Arms went completely limp.  Couldn't move em, couldn't feel em, it was like they weren't even there.  Sounds scary, right?  But it wasn't, it felt natural.  Nobody said anything, we just listened.

After the third verse I went blind.  Total darkness, not the least impression of light, I'd never experienced that before.  It felt safe.

Then after the fourth verse, I couldn't hear anymore, and that was a problem because I couldn't hear the poem. I couldn't hear it!  There's another verse to the poem, I know there is.  It was maddening, knowing the fifth verse was happening and I was missing it.

Anyway, after a few hours my senses gradually started to come back and I could move around again.  Jack took half a day to recover, Michelle's still unconscious.  I'll have to ask her how the poem ends, if she ever wakes up.

Oh, the kids?  The older kid said he doesn't remember any of it.  Younger kid, the one in the program, he doesn't talk anymore.  I bet *he* knows the last verse.  I wish he'd tell me.

#### {{Supplemental Material: Home Verschooling Orientation Pamphlet}}

---

= **PLAYTIME**

---

After a healthy breakfast, it's essential that your instar starts their day on the right foot.  Begin the first period with a nonsense rhyme from one of our approved authors, then guide your instar through one hour of unstructured Playtime.  Signal the end of Playtime with a second rhyme.

Remember that human speech is strictly forbidden during Playtime!  If your instar engages in human speech during Playtime, you are to chide them in the Divine Tongue and use one of the approved disciplinary rituals to punish them before resuming unstructured play.  This is mandatory and essential to the verschooling process.

= **MOLTING**

---

After playtime, it's time to begin the daily molting ritual.  Have your instar sit crosslegged in the focus circle and activate it.  Don't worry if they struggle at first; they'll settle in soon enough.

Consult the attached calendar to determine which markings to apply, and where.  Upon completion of the markings, your instar will begin to molt.  This may appear uncomfortable, but it is essential to the verschooling process: do not interfere, and do not release your instar from the focus circle. The molting will usually take about half an hour; this will leave your instar with some amount of free-time before lunch.

= **REMEMBERING**

---

After lunch period and a second Playtime period, the Fifth period of your instar's day will be the Remembering period!  By this time, they should be pretty-well recovered from their previous molting and be once again covered by a coat of soft, healthy pink skin.  In the event that they haven't, it's okay to extend the second Playtime period, or even grant a few hours of free-time for rest before the Remembering period.

Pick out today's Sermon from the calendar and read it to your instar in the Divine Tongue.  If they can't sit still you may use the focus circle, but it's better not to.  Don't worry if they don't seem to understand.  You are speaking to the imago.

After the sermon, inscribe the mark of remembering on your instar's forehead and supervise their Remembering.  The Remembering will go on for ten to twenty minutes -- you'll know they're nearly done when the smoke starts to dissipate.  Do not allow them to leave the home during this time, but otherwise do not interfere with the imago's activities.

After that, schooltime  is done for the day! Your instar can take the rest of the day to play and relax.

She's not a *real* Fifthist, you know?  Wasn't even raised in the church.  She's one of those new converts who's into crystals and astrology and wants to "find herself."  Yeah yeah, she knows the books pretty well, and she's got the light in her, but no roots.  The decent families go back at least a couple generations these days, and of course the *real* families, we go back before there was such a thing as a Fifth Church.  The newcomers, they're just in it for themselves.  No respect for authority, that's why the devils get 'em.

Yeah, the chapel, I'm getting to it!  So the weird thing was it was actually Jeremy. Jeremy Sloane, the vice-principal at the Academy?  She must have done something to him.  He got up on the roof and read out one of those awful poems off of a piece of lined paper.  Yeah, I remember it, can't seem to get it out of my head.  I'll write it down.

**Warning: Do not read any part of the following out loud.**

---

//The first pair of socks is for dinner.

The twost pair of socks is for lunch.

The half pair of socks is for breakfast, you said, 

With cornflakes to give it a crunch.//

//The second pot holder's for dreaming.

The wecond's for when you're awake.

The onest is for anywhere anytime really,

But couldn't you leave me some cake?//

//The third paper boat is for leaving.

The word is you're not coming back.

The worst of it's sailing in circles forever,

How else will we get back on track?//

//A fourth of the moon is for lying.

A quarter's for telling the truth.

The northerly wind knows the answer, you said,

When called from a telephone booth.//

//A fifth of the blood is for bleeding.

A fifth of the bones are for glue.

A fifth of the way to the end of the world,

I won't be there waiting for you.//

It's a little hard to describe what actually happened?  At first it was kind of like an earthquake, but in a very small area, and pulsing, like a heartbeat. And right at the end, bones, from the ribcage, expanding out and closing over the building, and then there was a lot of blood everywhere.  Also corn flakes, which I thought was silly.  A *proper* working wouldn't have cornflakes.  Anyway, it was a weekday, so basically nobody was in the building.  I'm sure we'll have it fixed before long.  Too bad about Jeremy; it took them half the day to bend him back to approximately human shape, and he's really not the same.

Did I see anyone else?  Well, plenty of people gathering round to watch.  Plenty of the mundane folk running for their lives as well; I guess you people will take care of that for us?

Oh, I did see Crystal.  She was at the chapel before the incident, I guess, but she can't have been involved.  The Scolxns are as good a line as you get, they go way back, and Crystal's a fine church girl.  I admit, I did think she'd be more upset.  She had a thing for him, you know?  He was well out of her league, of course ~~ blood without smoke only gets you so far ~~ but she was pretty persistent about it for a while.  I guess she finally got over him. Probably for the best, under the circumstances.

#### {{[UNKNOWN] Journal Entry April 28, 2020}}

Dear Diary,

They just don't get it.  It's all very frustrating.  This is *it.*  This is how you get there, to the Fifth World. The unravelling path.  You can't take all of yourself, you've gotta hollow out first, but you get there.  With the Archons, you have to become *them* and then none of you gets in.  That's no good, and it's not fair to Moon either. I guess this next part won't be either.  I'll see if I can leave him a way out, if he likes.  I owe him that much.

It's so clear to me now.  How can our Deacon not see it?  I'll give it one more shot, see if I can't shine through the smoke.  A nice clean theological debate, just the two of us.

If that doesn't work, I'll have to be a little underhanded.  The Unwinding Circle let me have one of the tapes.  Just in case.  I won't let anyone stop me from going there.

#### {{Surveillance Footage: Subjective, Variable, Fifth of May (5.5)}}

---

**Foreword:**  The following footage was delivered to a Foundation front company on May 5, 2020, immediately following Incident 9355-3.  Package was unlabelled, but gift-wrapped in glossy white paper and tied with a pink ribbon, as is characteristic of GoI 05-30 (The Unwinding Circle).  Please be advised that while this content does not appear to be ontokinetically or memetically hazardous, it does trigger a state of abnormal subjectivity, and has mild mnestic effects.

---

<CONTENT BEGINS>

I don't like school.  I wish I could stay home like last month.  I thought we were going to stay home for longer, but the Church got the government people to let us go back early, which sucks and I don't like it.

I'd rather be at home with Mommy and Daddy, even if they're busy and we do mostly the same stuff as at school.  I guess Mommy's at school now, but she's different since the thing from somewhere else came to visit.

Really, I wish I could just do regular school.  The other kids get to learn things.  Special school is supposed to be better but I don't get how.  It makes you less, instead of more.  I'd rather just learn to read.

I think that's how the poems and stuff got to us.  The molting sort of peels you away so other things can reach you, and a bit of the light slipped in instead.  It likes it when things get peeled away.  Mommy got really excited about it, but I don't think she really got it.

I don't think the adults *can* get it.  There's too much of them.  Too dense.  It slows the light down, so it spends too long inside them.  Starts to take them apart.  If you're nearly transparent, like it is, then you can hear it, just a little.  Thoughts that disappear before you can really think them, and a ringing sound.  I think it wants someone to play with.

A bell rings, and something with my Mommy's face says words that crawl through the air, leaving slimy trails, like little snails.  I remember there was something she wanted me to do, once school started again.

I wait for the other kids to leave the room, then  I go over and tug at her skirt.  She smiles down at me with eyes that glint like hard plastic.  I repeat back the words she told me, before.

//"Up in a shoebox, up in the closet

Up where I just can't reach.

They think I don't know, they think I'm too slow,

They think they can fill up my skull up with cotton

Until I forget about what I've forgotten."//

Mommy's body goes stiff and shaky.  Smoke comes out of her ears. 

//"Everything scary, everything hairy

Everything they won't teach.

So what if they're crude, so what if they're rude,

So what if they're wicked and ugly and rotten?

I want them all back! I want myself back!"//

Mommy coughs up red stuff and grabs at her chest.  Things are moving, inside her throat, under her skin.  It must hurt.  I'm sorry, Mommy. 

//"Pile up the books and pile up the boxes,

Till I can barely reach.

There's nobody here, there's nothing to fear,

So pull down the shoebox and get with the gottin'

Of all of the things that I've ever forgotten."//

Mommy coughs up up a little thing made of flesh and wax.   She stomps on it till it turn to slime, and smiles at me.  Red and pink stuff drips from her chin, and from her eyes, and there's still something moving under her skin.  The dull plastic glint in her eye vanishes, replaced by a dangerous sparkle.

"Thank you, Moon," she says.  "You've been very brave.  Now, run.  Run straight home and find Daddy, okay?   Mommy has to take care of something."

Mommy hugs me, and I want to cry again but I don't.  I love Mommy, but she's also kind of scary.  I run away as fast as I can.

I go out to where the camera can't see me.  Mommy stays in the room and pulls a little black rectangle out of the bottom of her purse.

Someone else comes through the door.  A child-sized thing in an oversized black cloak.  A thing from somewhere else.  Smoke swirls around.  Everything it touches gets a little bit thin.

"[DATA LOST]," says the thing.

Mommy laughs.  "You really *don't* get it, do you?"

Skittering things close in on Mommy from all sides, from directions that shouldn't exist.  Too many to count.

She eats the little black rectangle.

The tape plays.  A light shines in the water.

There is a loose end.  Something pulls on it.  Mommy is gone.  The skittering things are gone.  The hooded figure is gone.  Other things are gone.

The camera stops recording.

<CONTENT ENDS>
