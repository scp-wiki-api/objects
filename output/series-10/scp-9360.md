> ++++ Special Containment Procedures:

>

> ~~-~~-

> 

> Weather conditions above 6,500 m (21,325 ft) in the Himalayan region are to be continuously monitored by the Foundation’s astronomical and meteorological forecasting offices. If visibility levels are deemed too low, all hiking exceeding said height is to be prohibited to the public through the use of fake weather warnings. To avoid SCP-9360 nesting areas, the Foundation has designed climbing routes for civilian climbers to minimise public contact with the anomaly. If any climber survives an encounter with an instance or witnesses an instance, they are to be administered Class B amnestics.

> 

> Current attempts to contain SCP-9360 instances are ongoing and to be performed by MTF-Epsilon-99 ("The Cold Eagles"). All captured SCP-9360 instances are to be contained at Site-36 in separate standard biological cells, which are modified to have temperatures of -35°C (-31°F) during the winter months and -15°C (5°F) during the spring/summer months. Transitions to different temperatures must be gradual over the span of several months to simulate realistic conditions and keep instances content. If an SCP-9360 instance lays eggs, a specially designed drone (Drone-9360-079) is to be used to extract all eggs, where samples of Substance-9360 are extracted, and the eggs are destroyed. Any handling of Substance-9360 requires Level 3 biohazard protection. Currently, there are five (5) live instances contained. If at any point an instance of SCP-9360 is to die whilst in containment, the corpse is to be transported to Site-12 for research purposes.

> ++++ Description:

> 

> ~~-~~-

> 

> SCP-9360 is a species of predatory bird that resides at altitudes of 6,500 m (21,325 ft) strictly in the Himalayas. Adults range in wingspans from 6 to 7 m (20 to 23 ft), vertical heights from 2 to 3 m (7 to 10 ft) and weigh 50 to 80 kg (110 to 176 lbs).

> 

> An SCP-9360 instance possesses wings covered in light grey feathers resembling the down feathers of an owl. Along the trailing edge of each wing are significantly sharper feathers, which function as cutting structures. Because the species lacks teeth, these feathers are used to slice prey into consumable pieces. When not in flight, the sharp feathers fold inward into the wing to prevent self-injury. Along the whole leading edge of each wing is a single appendage that is identical in shape to the forelimb of a praying mantis, which acts as an SCP-9360 instance's arm for tearing open prey and for building nests. These appendages are a much darker grey than an entity's feathers. An SCP-9360 instance's legs are a similar colour to their appendages and account for an average of 50% of its height.

> 

> The head of an SCP-9360 instance is the same shape as a praying mantis but proportioned to an instance's body size, with a head length of 40 to 60cm (15.7 to 23.6 inches). Each instance's eyes are a light blue, positioned similarly to a praying mantis's compound eyes, and are insect-like in appearance, but anatomically bird-like, with a panoramic vision capability. These eyes briefly flash when it detects potential prey to alert hunting partners. The cranial cavity is similar in size to that of a chimpanzee, with a brain size of 395 to 490 cc, which is consistent with the species' observed intelligence during hunting, nest construction, and maintenance of clear social structures. 

> 

> SCP-9360 instances have beaks that are 20 to 25 cm (7.8 to 9.8 inches) in length and have a shape similar to that of an eagle, being a darker grey than their legs and appendages. SCP-9360 instances' necks contain fourteen vertebrae, allowing them to rotate their heads up to 270° in any direction, with the motion visually resembling an unnatural twitch. These head rotations are believed to be performed to hide their glowing eyes from prey they are approaching. Despite numerous biological adaptations, the mechanism allowing SCP-9360 to remain active at extreme altitudes with minimal oxygen availability remains unknown.

> 

> SCP-9360 instances are capable of reaching speeds of 220 to 240 km/h (137 to 150 mph) when gliding and 50 to 65 km/h (30 to 40 mph) when soaring or cruising. When soaring or cruising, instances appear not to produce any detectable noises to human ears, but when gliding, detectable sounds are minimal. SCP-9360 instances primarily hunt during low visibility conditions, with it believed that this allows them to approach prey unnoticed, as they are believed to be able to see more clearly in these conditions, but this is unconfirmed. Hunting is mainly performed by two males who prefer opportunistic hunting, targeting exposed and vulnerable animals, which are commonly human hikers, but they have been observed to hunt other native animals of the Himalayas. 

> 

> An instance detects prey through highly acute directional hearing, with estimates suggesting it can hear a large majority of sounds within a 2 km (1.2 miles) radius, but exact numbers are unclear. SCP-9360 instances appear to preferentially investigate irregular or artificial sound sources such as metallic impacts, electronic signals, or animal vocalisations/other sounds. 

> 

> Once a hunting pair find a target, one instance will approach from in front of the target and distract using a 100 to 120 db screech that has been compared to a blend of human screams and radio static, with the other instance approaching from behind. Using their wings' sharp edges and appendages, they consume prey live but leave small portions that they sometimes take back to their nests to feed female entities and juvenile entities. Alongside using its wings and appendages, SCP-9360 instances have been observed to regurgitate an acidic digestive slurry, which partially digests their prey before eating. Prey that is not transported back to nests is typically buried beneath layers of snow by the hunting pair.

> 

> Nests of SCP-9360 instances are usually found on cliff edges across the Himalayan region in altitudes higher than 6,500 m (21,325 ft) and usually house two dominant males, three to four females and five to six juveniles. The nests are built with snow, ice and native metamorphic rocks by the females, who also care for juveniles, whilst males hunt and return food to the nest. Juveniles stay within their parent's nest for around five years before they leave, where males build their own nests whilst females search for a male's nest. When in nests, instances exhibit intermittent jerking motions similar to mantid pre-strike behaviour. As well as this, instances possess natural camouflage amongst surrounding rock formations, allowing them to avoid detection by animals they don't see as prey. 

> 

> The lifespan of an SCP-9360 instance is believed to be 20 to 30 years. Near the end of an instance's life, it appears to be able to sense it as it flies to higher altitudes to bury itself prior to death. All mature female instances will begin laying eggs at around eight to twelve years old and will usually lay one to three eggs in their lifetime. Incubation lasts approximately seven months, with females not leaving their nest at any point during incubation until the egg hatches. All SCP-9360 eggs are visually indistinguishable from surrounding metamorphic rock formations when coated in snow or frost. These eggs are covered in a substance known as Substance-9360. 

> 

> Substance-9360 is a transparent, viscous substance. Whilst the freezing point of this substance is unknown, the substance rapidly sublimates at temperatures above 0°C (32°F). Contact with this substance can cause a necrotising infection with primary symptoms being vomiting, diarrhoea and paralysis. This can cause death within one to two weeks of exposure, with the only known treatment being amputation of the affected tissue within 24 hours of initial exposure. All SCP-9360 instances have an immunity to this substance and its effects, with the substance being found in the female reproductive tract. It is believed that there are 500 to 600 live instances in the wild at any point; however, exact numbers are unconfirmed.

> ++++ Discovery:

> 

> ~~-~~-

> 

> Encounters with SCP-9360 instances may date back to 29 May 1953, when Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay completed the first confirmed ascent of Mount Everest. During their summit attempt, they reported observing flashing pale blue dots and very faint gliding noises while they were approaching the "death zone". The anomaly's existence was confirmed after Foundation personnel recovered recording equipment from a deceased hiker near the summit of K2 on ██/██/20██. The following audio log is a transcription recovered from the device.

> ++++ Addendum

> 

> ~~-~~-

> 

> Fossils of SCP-9360 have been discovered across multiple altitudes of the Himalayas, ranging from 1,500 to 6,500 m (4,921 to 21,325 ft). These fossils' ages range from within the past few years to three million years ago. Across these three million years, various physical changes have occurred in SCP-9360 instances that are believed to allow them to adapt to higher altitudes of the Himalayas. The key difference in old fossils compared to modern instances is the size, with the oldest fossils showing a wingspan of 2 to 3 m (7 to 10 ft) and a vertical height of 1 to 1.5 m (3 to 5 ft). The diets of ancient instances of SCP-9360 are unknown; it is believed they were similar to their current diets. 

> 

> It is unknown which species SCP-9360 evolved from, as its DNA doesn't match any known animal genus. The closest living relatives to SCP-9360 are the Himalayan Griffon Vulture and praying mantis; however, they aren't considered close enough to be a part of the same genus. Research into SCP-9360's evolutionary history is currently ongoing as various Foundation-employed archaeologists are deployed and working to find more SCP-9360 fossils within and around the Himalayan region; however, no fossils have been discovered outside of the Himalayan region. Traces of Substance-9360 have been found on fossils dating back to 1.5 million years ago, suggesting instances didn't always produce the substance, with it being unclear why they began doing so.

> ++++ Update - 2024-3-24

> 

> ~~-~~-

> 

> 

> A recent incident involving SCP-9360 resulted in the deaths of seven civilian climbers during an ascent of Mount Everest at an altitude of 5,300 m (17,388 ft). Foundation personnel were able to recover five bodies, with the other two believed to have been brought to the instances' nest. This is the first known instance of an SCP-9360 attack below an altitude of 6,500 m (21,325 ft), with it currently unclear whether this was an isolated event or a sign of SCP-9360 evolving to hunt at lower and unmonitored altitudes. Furthermore, the nest of the instances believed to have performed the assault was located at an altitude of 5,600 m (18,373 ft) and was removed by MTF-Epsilon-99.

> 

> Personnel recovered a bone tool carved from a human radius; however, what it was used for is unknown. No records of tool usage by SCP-9360 instances have been recorded prior to this discovery. Additionally, personnel documented a triangular arrangement of various animal skulls near the nest perimeter. Several skulls displayed precise scoring marks inconsistent with feeding behaviour. Monitoring of SCP-9360 nesting habits is now considered the highest priority for the SCP-9360 research team personnel.
