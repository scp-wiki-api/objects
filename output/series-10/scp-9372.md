**Special Containment Procedures:** As SCP-9372's location effectively shields the general populace from its existence, its containment is technically unneccessary. However, to facilitate better containment, research is nevertheless currently ongoing to ascertain whether SCP-9372 is an anomalous technical error, the signs of former or ongoing third-party interference in the SCiPNET database, or a [nomenclative hazard](scp-4000). 

To this end, all files containing SCP-9372 manifestations have been separated from the general SCiPNET network and remain isolated on a separate server for analysis. Until research can conclusively ascertain the nature, origins, and possible elimination methods applicable in regards to SCP-9372, Foundation personnel are instructed to not engage with any digital errors similar to the anomaly that may yet occur in non-quarantined files.

**Description:** SCP-9372 is a phenomenon due to which at least 127 personnel formerly employed by the Foundation are not recognized by the SCiPNET database as extant human beings.

SCP-9372 presents itself as a technical error occurring within the digital file archive globally employed by the Foundation. The accounts of afflicted personnel remain uncompromised, but do not display any actual name when interacted with. Instead, they respond with a 403 Forbidden error that cannot be changed through manual account override, loading in higher clearance credentials, or by using backdoor server editions. In spite of this, the actual non-nominative ID numbers that are assigned to each account remain unchanged and thus the account themselves *can* be interacted with. However, each account itself alongside all files within the SCiPNET database that mention them refuse to display any actual names, instead greeting the viewer with a blank block of text that similarly cannot be interacted with or changed.

Notably, all pictures loaded into the SCiPNET database which ~~ by their description ~~ would depict the aforementioned employed individuals in physical form remain similarly inaccessible. Whether or not this phenomenon is connected to SCP-9372 itself remains under research.

In summary, accounts afflicted by SCP-9372:

* Do not possess any extant names, instead linking to a singular, erroneous nominative designation which does not exist, which presents itself as a blank block of text, and which appears to be the same inaccessible designation across all manifestations;

* Cannot be interacted with in any meaningful capacity;

* Can only be "seen" through interacting with text and files that either reference the accounts themselves or are attributed to them;

* Nevertheless, as per other files within the Foundation's archives Which have been located and read by similarly "reading around the blanks" of the missing names.   have all once belonged to actual personnel that were once factually employed by the Foundation.

As of the time of writing, no attempts have been made to contact individuals affected by SCP-9372, as ~~ per Foundation records ~~ almost all of them already perished due to unrelated reasons.
