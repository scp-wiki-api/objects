SCP-9373

---

## * SPECIAL CONTAINMENT PROCEDURES

---

SCP-9373 is currently kept in the main lobby of Site-01. Personnel are allowed and encouraged to freely use SCP-9373 at their leisure. Cleaning and upkeep of SCP-9373's optical and mechanical components are to be performed as needed.

---

## * DESCRIPTION

---

SCP-9373 is a 19th-century refracting telescope, constructed primarily of brass and teak wood. Despite its age, the lens and internal optics of SCP-9373 show no signs of deterioration and remain in pristine functional condition. Engraved along the barrel of SCP-9373 is the following inscription:

*We can see the beauty of the world, even if it can't see ours.*

![](AW-signature.png)

When an individual looks through the eyepiece of SCP-9373, the expected image is replaced by a view of the planet Earth as seen from the surface of the Moon. The precise vantage point on the lunar surface varies between sessions, with no point having been repeated to date.

The image produced by SCP-9373 is stable for the duration of each session. Repositioning or reorienting the telescope has no effect on the observed image.

SCP-9373 was originally constructed by [Professor A██████ W█████](https://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/system:page-tags/tag/professor-aw#pages) in the 1890s for her wife, Nora W█████. Following the Professor's death in 1914, the object passed into the private collection of one of the Foundation's founding members. SCP-9373 remained in personal custody for several decades thereafter, until it was donated to the Foundation in 1969.
