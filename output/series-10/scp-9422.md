Item #: SCP-9422

Object Class: Keter

Special Containment Procedures:

SCP-9422 Is to Be Contained in a Localized Facility Room surrounded by Reinforced Soundproof Steel Walls to prevent unauthorized access or contact with SCP-9422

Access to SCP-9422 is to be given to Researchers from A Level-3 Research Personal or Higher. Personnel are prohibited from speaking or making Requests to SCP-9422 without approval.

Description: SCP-9422 is an Ancient Stoned Statue Head measuring approximately 40 centimeters in height and weight approximately 15-18 kg. The object is to be descoped as a humanoid Statue head covering from head to upper torso, with its face displaying closed eyes and emotionless expression

Observations:

 It's concluded when speaking a wish to SCP-9422 The once shut eyes will briefly open for 3 seconds before closing. During this time, Lights and Electronics will briefly flicker or static for seconds. However, SCP-9422 does not seem to understand or observe the actual intention of wishes demanded by persona, studies shown it seems to intercept the wish and completely ignores the true tone and meaning of it. 

This frequently causes unexpected events or harmful outcomes to occur.

Discovery:

SCP-9422 was discovered in abandoned underground temple after reports of archeologists reported missing after entering the temple.

Inside the temple, Foundation Agents discovered SCP-9422 sitting on a stone pedestal surrounded by multiple humanoid stoned statues.

The anomaly seems to predate somewhere around Egyptian time or centuries before then.

Further testing revealed that stone statues where once's humans who had interacted with SCP-9422

Testing Log SCP-9422-01

Subject: D-9422-01

Request: "I wish I had Unlimited Money"

Result:

The Subject's Bank Account had displayed nearly unlimited money

However do to SCP-9422's intermate of human language, the currency was traced back to thousands of individuals banks that were robbed worldwide.

Conclusion:

SCP-9422 had fulfilled the users' requests but ignored consequences

Testing Log SCP-9422-02

Subject: D-9422-02

Request: "I wish I was Immortal"

Result:

The subject was placed in observance room for 6 months; at time no sign of biological decay or ageing had occurred.

However, the user had experienced lack of regenerative tissue, causing permanent damage.

Conclusion:

The subjects wish had been fulfilled ageing, decay and no sign of weakness had happened in span of 6-month observations, but user had experienced rapid regenerative tissue causing infections than death.

Final Notes:

As of now SCP-9422 is still being observed and contained at Site-137 Directed by Dr. Jacob Howards

Any further tests have been canceled, SCP-9422 conversations are to be stopped for Foundations Containment Risk.

Until more information is discovered, all requests made to SCP-9422 are considered a potential containment risk.
