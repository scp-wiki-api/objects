**Item #:** SCP-9463

**Object Class:** Euclid

**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9463 is to be secured within Site-74's Hazardous Materials sector, at standard temperature and pressure (i.e. 273.15K, 1 atm). Under no circumstances are any personnel to be allowed within the containment chamber of SCP-9463 without explicit authorization from an individual with Level II Clearance or above. All staff are to utilize hazmat gear when conducting experiments on SCP-9463. Levels of hydroxide are to be monitored daily through the testing of pH levels. In the event that the calculated concentration of hydroxide ions in SCP-9463 exceeds 4.0M, or the solution rises above a pH of 14.60, the site is to be evacuated and the area flushed with acetic acid (or any other weak acid) until bubbles are observed to form at the surface of the anomaly. Mobile Task Force β-7 ("Maz Hatters") will then be summoned to perform an inspection and cleanup. Entry into the containment area is deemed safe after the unit departs.

**Description:** SCP-9463 is a circular lake surrounded by dirt with a radius of approximately seventeen (17) meters and a depth of three (3) fathoms. Although the composition of the liquid present in the lake was initially unknown, research has revealed that the liquid contains distilled water, potassium ions (K^^+^^), and hydroxide ions (OH^^-^^), indicating the former presence of potassium hydroxide (KOH). The solution has been observed to “attempt” to preserve the exact proportion of contents in the body at the same ratio as it was at the time of discovery: 3.0M. The removal or addition of solution fails to disrupt the so-called “homeostasis” of SCP-9463; the exact mechanism by which the system replenishes and removes materials is currently not understood.

The primary lake maintains a physical connection with a comparably minuscule pond, designated SCP-9463-1. Said area was discovered on ██ June 2009, during a routine test. The pond has been noted to comprise water, solid zinc, and dissociated manganese dioxide (MnO,,2,,). The bodies of water exhibit anomalous properties pertaining to electrical conduction. Each time the pH tester is inserted into the larger reservoir, the bodies behave reminiscent of a galvanic cell, with an unexpectedly high potential of 7.84 volts. Foundation researchers theorize that the aforementioned underground connection, whose composition has not been confirmed, serves as the wire connecting the anode (zinc) and the cathode (currently unknown), and that the dirt separating each liquid acts as a salt bridge. The reason for the activation of this phenomenon has yet to be explained, along with the abnormal voltage of the cell. Otherwise, SCP-9463-1 displays no unusual activity.

SCP-9463’s more obvious anomalous effects manifest when living human matter touches the liquid inside of the reservoir. If the object in question is placed in and subsequently withdrawn from the solution, it will sustain severe burns and will often ignite within three (3) seconds of contact. If the object, even if dead, were to be submerged in the fluid of SCP-9463, it would immediately sink to the bottom of the lake, and it should be deemed unrecoverable. See Addendum 9463-B.  ██ personnel have been lost in this manner. Experimentation reveals that animals, plants, and any organism besides humans are impervious to the effects of the strong base, but not that of the flame; however, the flame does not cause any harm. The significance of this is unknown.

On exceedingly rare occasions, SCP-9463’s solution will experience a rapid increase in pH until reaching a level of 14.60. Over a period of twenty-four (24) hours, beginning on the eighth hour after pH begins to change, the temperature in the containment chamber will increase to approximately ███K. If the pool is not neutralized within the next sixteen (16) hours, an explosion will engulf all life within a radius of two (2) kilometers before returning to its previous state.

It has also been noted that the liquid of SCP-9463 does not retain its anomalous properties when removed from the pool.

**Addenda:** 

**Recovery**

On ██ December 2007, an elderly man was reported missing in the town of [REDACTED]. Agent ██████, who resides in the area, coincidentally discovered the person sitting on a bench directly across from SCP-9463 in a remote, wooded area, located approximately seven (7) kilometers from the town. The man did not respond to any inquiries posed by Agent ██████. After five minutes of silence, the individual rose and walked out of the forest, later discovered to have returned to his home. Shortly following the departure of the man, Agent ██████ observed a water strider attempting to interact with SCP-9463, resulting in the insect transforming into a pillar of flames and prompting the agent to contact the Foundation for further investigation. 

Upon arrival, the immediate region of the anomaly was promptly secured by a unit of the Mobile Task Force. Civilians approaching the area were requested to leave the area at once; failure to comply resulted in the detainment of the violators and the administration of Class A Amnestics.

**Incident 9463-1**

*Notice: The majority of the following information was gathered through security camera footage.

On 07 November 2010, at approximately 13:42, a small group of researchers were conversing in the observation deck above SCP-9463's chamber when the stairwell entry doorway was automatically and unexpectedly locked. This was accompanied by a series of strongly audible sounds resembling gunshots emitted by the room's intercom speaker, measuring at a consistent 152 dB; the noise was not reported at any other locations in the facility.

The individual nearest to the glass, later identified as Doctor ███, suffered cardiac arrest and collapsed forward into the glass window, causing it to shatter. She was the only researcher remaining in the room upon discovery. The person closest to the exit donned ear protection, removed a pistol from his lab coat, and began firing in the direction of each researcher, incapacitating them. All retained consciousness during the event. The assailant then began to lift each body (aside from the corpse of Doctor ███) and drop them into SCP-9463. After completing his task, the individual dropped his weapon onto the platform and dove head-first into the lake. He was later recognized to be Junior Researcher █████, who was formerly employed at Site-19; his motivations are currently not understood, but a lead was given in the form of [DATA EXPUNGED]. All staff involved in this event are presumed dead.

**Incident 9463-2**

On 01 August 2013, SCP-9463's containment chamber experienced a significant upward deviation in temperature by about sixteen (16) Kelvins, as reported by Agent Kasparov. Attempts by researchers to artificially cool the area utilizing internal fans and liquid nitrogen, but to no avail; temperatures continued to rise, also affecting other regions of Site-74. Levels of the solution's pH were measured to increase to 14.60, and the liquid inside the lake retained a constant temperature of 273.15K. An evacuation of the facility was ordered immediately; all essential workers and anomalous objects were secured. Logistical staff determined that ██ Class-D personnel would not be able to be transported out of the compound. Upon return, all of their bodies were discovered outside the facility, presumably in attempts to escape. Analysis reveals the cause of death was intense and repeated burns. 

Site-74 resumed operations shortly after, and a procedure was developed based on the hypothesis of Doctor Totaka that neutralizing the solution of SCP-9463 would result in the cessation of the devastating phenomenon. He was proven correct when a similar incident, designated Incident 9463-3, was ended due to the flushing of the chamber with acetic acid, At Site-74, acetic acid exists in surplus due to its necessity for the containment of several other anomalous objects in the facility. which was met with the bubbling of the surface of the lake and the return of the containment area to normal conditions. MTF β-7 was then asked to perform a thorough examination of the chamber and to clean any chemical waste (primarily in the form of KOH) produced during the incident.

Special containment procedures have been updated in face of this cataclysm. Since the event transpired, episodes of this style of abnormality have occurred four (4) times.

A terrible array of cuts and gashes and bites and slashes on them. Leaking arteries and veins. Slits on necks, spitting out their elixir like an inverted waterfall. Blood and shit and piss and spit and I don't even know what, everywhere. Gorescarred, empty vessels.
