**Somnial Containment Procedures:** Observational sleepers embedded in external collectives are to covertly proliferate the idea that oneiric geocaching (colloquially known as "egocaching") is dangerous, embarrassing, or otherwise unpleasant. Superliminal micromemes to this effect have been seeded in various forms of popular dream-based entertainment, including *Teeth Falling Out*, *Didn't Study For A Test*, *Scary Ways To Die*, *Naked In Public*, and *[ Iron Chef](scp-4497 )*.

In the event that egocaching's popularity continues to increase, the governing bodies of [Sweeter Dreams Inc.](Sweeter Dreams Inc.), Oneiroi West, and the [ ☽☽☽ Collective](three-moons-initiative-hub ) have agreed to place immigration restrictions on SCP-9481-related travel.

**Description:** SCP-9481 is an immobile accumulation of discarded thought-stuff known as an "egocache." Though SCP-9481 instances do not appear to possess any abnormal qualities, the increasing prevalence of oneiric geocaching has made them a subject of interest.**Eparch:** Item is non-anomalous but related to the anomalous.

**peaksm1th** - IT Department - *Hayes, Kansas*

Has anyone else heard of this egocaching thing?

I found out about it a few nights ago, when I was looking for a geocache in a giant McDonalds playplace. In case you don't know what geocaching is, it's sort of a treasure hunt game. You take a portable GPS, a set of coordinates, and maybe some clues, and you use them to seek out a hidden box or capsule or whatever. Geocaching has been a waking hobby of mine for years, so it was only a matter of time that I'd start dreaming about  it.

It wasn't easy getting to the cache, since the playplace was like 3 miles tall and I kept having to fight off these giant wasps that wanted me to read their mediocre poetry (long story), but I eventually found it in an upside-down planetarium on the lowest level. When I opened it up to write my name in the logbook, I found a hyperlink to a... website? Smart phone app? Shared delusion? I'm still not sure what we call these things, but suffice to say it turns out there's an underground community of people hiding and hunting down "egocaches" in our dreams. It's wild.

**Topic:** Egocaching

= {{**✹ BEGIN RECALL ✹**}}

**Static:** Good evening, good morning, and good luck to everyone still trying to wake up. You’re listening to *Dead Air for the Newborn Lucid*, the only radio program that caters to our freshly awakened dreamers, and to you (yes, *you*, specifically). I’m the illustrious June Static, and tonight I am joined by the *alleged* creator of egocaching.soma.

**Guest:** Alleged by whom?

**Static:** By egocachers, primarily.

**Guest:** You shouldn't believe everything you read online. Or, anything you read online, these days.

**Static:** Too true. So, do you deny these allegations? The first listed cache *is* under one of your known aliases...

**Guest:** No. I’m just not agreeing with them, either. Does it really matter?

**Static:** When you’re talking about the single most popular dreamtime activity across the Lucidverse, it matters a heck of a lot!

**Guest:** Agree to disagree.

**Static:** What should we call you, by the way?

**Guest:** Oh, I never [ take calls](scp-1498 ) anymore, no one should. Too dangerous.

**Static:** Fair enough! So, if you won’t tell us the “who” of egocaching’s creation, can you at least let our lovely listeners know if the website came first, or the sport? My sources are awfully uncertain on that front.

**Guest:** Dreams and dreamers, chickens and eggs. Does anything really come before anything else, June?

**Static:** Typically, yeah. Are you always this evasive?

**Guest:** Most people consider me to be very straightforward, actually. *<Pause.>* That was a joke.

**Static:** *<Laughter.>*

**Guest:** I’ll try to be more direct, in spite of my nature: the website was simply easier for people to notice.

**Static:** Easier than the sport?

**Guest:** Easier than the pressure.

**Static:** What pressure?

**Guest:** The pressure underneath awareness, created by truth as it boils to the surface.

**Static:** Direct, you said? One hell of a line to drop right before our first commercial break.

**Guest:** I said I’d try. And you did ask where it began.

**Static:** So, did egocaches start appearing with the website? Or were people already hiding these things before it was created?

**Guest:** People have always hidden things in dreams. Feelings. Names. Faces of dead parents. The missing sentences of conversations one is not brave enough to have.

**Static:** Indeed they have.

**Guest:** Dreams are still life, moments captured in effigy. A light switch that never works never leaves a room dark. A clock that insists it is still your birthday, or your sister's funeral, or last night, is those things, forever, and whether we like it or not.

**Static:** Reality doesn’t accept refunds.

**Guest:** And while the dream world is far more symbolic about how it portrays its truth, it is still truth. One just has to be able to read its language.

= {{**✹ END RECALL ✹**}}
