**Special Containment Procedures:**  Foundation webcrawler I/O LORENZ is to monitor all commercial sportsbooking and prediction marketplaces for newly created accounts consistent with SCP-9518 patterns. Accounts tagged are to be cross-referenced with the Foundation’s databases of living, deceased, missing, and nonexistent persons. Accounts with a high probability of being SCP-9518 instances are to be deleted, along with any bets or markets the account created.

If an SCP-9518 event is suspected to occur, I/O LORENZ is to trigger an emergency response and delete all broadcasts and social media posts related to the affected game. Agents inoculated with Class W antimemetic drugs and equipped with portable SRAs are to be dispatched to the affected game venue, and are to organize containment efforts. This includes the amnesitization and evacuation of the civilian population from within the venue, the publishing of a cover story to justify the evacuation, and assisting with any necessary cleanup.

**Description:** SCP-9518 is a phenomenon that occurs within commercial sportsbooking and prediction marketplace platforms, wherein bets placed will alter reality in such a way that the bets placed become successful.

SCP-9518 events begin one day prior to the affected sporting event. Between ten and fifty sports betting accounts will simultaneously manifest and place bets on existing positions. Depending on the platform, they may manifest alongside and bet on a custom market. If these bets remain up, a number of anomalous events will occur during the affected game that invariably lead to the serious injury or fatality of multiple players or audience members.

SCP-9518 events emit a passive memetic effect that causes witnesses to treat them as natural parts of the game, and will react as though the events are in no way unusual. If an SCP-9518 event disrupts the game to the point that it cannot be completed traditionally, the remaining players will play towards an alternate win condition.

Several patterns regarding SCP-9518 manifestations have been identified:

* SCP-9518 only affects basketball games.

* SCP-9518 primarily affects high-profile telecast games.

* SCP-9518 does not manifest within physical sportsbook venues.

Due to the nature of its manifestations, the lead hypothesis on SCP-9518 is that it is sentient and suffers from ludomania.Gambling addiction.

**Discovery:** SCP-9518 first came to the Foundation’s attention in 2018 following a string of continual manifestations. Following these incidents, Foundation researchers identified the link between SCP-9518 and sportsbooking, and the current containment procedures were implemented. 

Further analysis has shown that SCP-9518 has manifested as far back as 1997. Due to the large variance in SCP-9518 events, these incidents were mistakenly documented separately under the Logs of Extranormal Events or Unexplained Locations.

**Update to Containment Procedures:** Thirty prototype Scranton Reality Suppression and Response (SRSR) Systems have been deployed at basketball courts around the world. If an increase in baseline hume levels consistent with SCP-9518 patterns are detected, SRSR Systems will stabilize local reality for nineteen minutes. SRSR Systems will then automatically trace the source of ontokinetic energy, and will transmit a series of files to the source both electronically and psionically. Files transmitted by the SRSRS system will include:

* The Foundation International Addiction Helpline number and email.

* Recordings of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy sessions about gambling addiction.

* PDFs of various SMART Recovery Handbook editions.

* Links to various online self-help seminars.

The Foundation is currently working with multiple national governments and Groups of Interests to regularly inspect major intranet networks for SCP-9518 manifestations.

Basketball is banned at Foundation Sites that lack an operational SRA. Sites with basketball courts are to decommission those courts before conducting SRA maintenance. 

**Update (27/01/2030):** SCP-9518 manifestations have dropped by 75% from peak rates. The Temporal Anomalies Department has been instructed to open a rift to 2007 and to have Caitlin Clark throw a basketball through it to prevent a temporal paradox from occurring.
