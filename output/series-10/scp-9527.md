**Item #:** SCP-9527

**Object Class:** Safe

**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9527 has been relocated to Site-45. The ice cavity from which it was retrieved is to be preserved in its original state. Unauthorized extraction of water from the cavity, or any form of sonar survey, is prohibited.

**Description:** SCP-9527 was discovered by a Foundation expedition beneath the Antarctic ice sheet, within a permanently enclosed saltwater lake cavity formed by a geological anomaly. No visible entrance to the cavity exists.

The primary object is a small vessel approximately 24.8 cm in length. Its hull is crafted from an unknown composite of deep-sea bone and coral, intricately built into a streamlined form. No propulsion mechanism is present, and the interior bears no trace of life. Along its flank, minute shells have been inlaid to form a name, which translates to The Nevermelting.

Within its compartments, the following were found:

* Approximately 120 intact carapaces of Antarctic krill, arranged in neat rows inside the cargo hold.

* A star chart assembled from diatom frustules and the fossils of unidentified creatures, whose patterns correspond precisely with deep-sea current data from a specific historical period in Antarctic waters.

* A tiny magnetite crystal suspended in the pilot house, encased in a bead of transparent biological gel.

* A scroll woven from baleen fibers and inscribed with pigment derived from the eyes of krill. Its writing system is highly complex. The final passage, as deciphered, reads:

**Log Cycle 7, Epoch 441:**

> The songs drifting down from the upper currents have grown shrill and thin. An acrid sourness is spreading inward from the edges of the world, slow as fire. We have charted every known warm current and cold, calculated one thousand and seven hundred migration routes.

> 

> Nowhere to go. We have nowhere left to migrate.

> 

> This place is the last, warm grave.

> 

> Here the flagship The Nevermelting comes to rest. We will broadcast our coordinates and our complete voyage logs into the deep in every direction, until this carrier decays.

> 

> If there are those who come after, may your seas still be cool.

> 

Testing has revealed that the water within the saltwater lake cavity possesses a pH level significantly lower than that of ordinary seawater.
