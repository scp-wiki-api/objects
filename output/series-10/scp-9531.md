**SCP-9531 — We Come with the Rain**

**Author:** 

**Articles you may enjoy:**

**[The Immortal Empire](The Immortal Empire)** — *The Empire's diplomacy is thus simple but effective: Join us and enjoy a golden age everlasting. Refuse us and see what horrors lurk in the void of space.*

**[Shiloh A. Wrun](Shiloh A. Wrun)** — *In light of the gravity of these crimes, we sentence Shiloh A. Wrun to the most severe punishment the Wanderers' Library can deliver: Exile of body, of soul and of memory.*

**Item #:** SCP-9531

**Object Class:** Euclid

**Special Containment Procedures:**

SCP-9531-1 and SCP-9531-2 will remain buried together, their exact location known solely to the inhabitants of San Miguel Coatlinchán per the terms of their agreement with the Foundation. Non-anomalous replicas of both items have been provided to Mexican authorities to conceal the removal of the originals. A disinformation campaign is in place to dismiss further sightings of SCP-9531-3 as hoaxes. Additional anomalous weather is to be monitored and evaluated for possible connections to SCP-9531.

**Description:**

SCP-9531 collectively refers to the anomalies involving the monolithic statues of the Nahua rain deity Tláloc (SCP-9531-1) and his wife—the water deity Chalchiuhtlicue Whereas Tláloc is the deity charged with creating and distributing rain, Chalchiuhtlicue rules over surface and underground bodies of water, including rivers, oceans, lakes and subterranean reservoirs.  (SCP-9531-2). SCP-9531-1 is composed of basalt stone, measures 7 meters in height and weighs approximately 168 tons. SCP-9531-2 is composed of andesite, measures 3.19 meters in height and weighs approximately 16.3 tons. 

SCP-9531’s primary effect is triggered when SCP-9531-1 and SCP-9531-2 are physically separated by a distance greater than one kilometer, with each of the items serving as the origin point of a localized anomalous field of influence that generates a distinct set of meteorological phenomena; the effect progressively subsides when the objects are reunited. 

SCP-9531-1 generates the following phenomena:

* Immediate formation of stationary thunderstorms which remain fixed over the item’s location and migrate accordingly upon its relocation. During these events, rainfall initially exceeds 20–30 mm/h, with the precipitation rate increasing as the anomaly’s field of influence expands.

* Cloud-to-ground electrical discharges occurring simultaneously and forming what observers describe as “vaguely humanoid figures.”

* Hailstones exhibiting unusual sharpness and hardness, able to easily penetrate human skin.

* Dense fog formation during periods of maximum ambient temperature; witnesses have reported hearing voices of weeping children emerging from the fog.

Conversely, SCP-9531-2’s effects include the following:

* Accelerated reductions in soil moisture with no proportional increases in evaporation.

* Surface water bodies experiencing measurable volume loss without evidence of seepage, extraction, or evaporation; the rate of depletion increases the longer the anomaly remains separated from its counterpart. 

* Ground fissures developing along mapped shorelines of former lacustrine systems.

* Irrigated vegetation displaying advanced symptoms of hydric stress despite continuous water availability. 

* Infiltration rates approaching zero despite unchanged soil composition and porosity.

* Auditory hallucinations manifesting amongst individuals in close proximity to underground water bodies in the anomaly’s area of effect, with subjects exhibiting a growing compulsion to dig until they’ve reached the subterranean reservoirs; those who succeed immediately submerge themselves to the point of drowning. Survivors have consistently described the phenomenon as a sensation of being invited, welcomed, or called by the water itself.

The severity of these anomalies correlates positively with the distance separating both objects; its effects initially remain bound to a one kilometer radius from the items’ present location before expanding until the boundaries of both fields come into contact, at which point SCP-9531-3 begins manifesting along the zone of convergence. 

SCP-9531-3 designates four distinct male entities (individually designated SCP-9531-3A to SCP-9531-3D) superficially resembling human youths of indeterminate age, dressed in garments resembling those of the priestly class of the Mexica empire. Each SCP-9531-3 instance wields a wooden club carved with pluvial iconography and carries a clay pot upon their back; for the duration of each manifestation, they will playfully chase each other around, with the winner being the first to shatter someone else’s pot. Thus far, no SCP-9531-3 instance has won.

Despite being jovial and friendly when approached, SCP-9531-3 instances quickly lose interest in conversation with Foundation personnel unless they are provided with ceremonial offerings, and express a desire to continue their game uninterrupted (see *Video Log Transcript SCP-9531-3*). Upon SCP-9531-1 and SCP-9531-2 being reunited, SCP-9531-3 will instantly demanifest.

**Discovery:**

SCP-9531-1 was first described by Mexican anthropologists in the mid-1800s, although its existence was previously widely known by the Nahua-speaking inhabitants of San Miguel Coatlinchán—a settlement originally founded by the Alcohua ethnic group in what is now the State of Mexico—who attributed spiritual and mystical properties to the monolith. According to local folklore, the monolith was buried by the devotees of Tláloc at the height of the Spanish conquest to prevent its destruction; only the tip of its tongue protruded from the ground and marked its place of burial, eventually becoming known as the *piedra de los tecomates* (“stone of the gourds,” for the natives would fill their gourds with the rain water that collected at the site). The statue’s hiding spot was named was named *Barranca del Agua* (“Ravine of Water”) and remained a place of holy significance for the people of San Miguel Coatlinchán, who offered tributes to the buried idol in exchange for rain and abundant harvests.

Despite these religious practices, SCP-9531-1 did not initially manifest any anomalous characteristics, even after it was partially disinterred by an archeological expedition led by Leonardo Bartres in 1903 and identified as an idol of Tláloc. It was not until 1963, when the Mexican government announced the monolith would be moved to Mexico City for the inauguration of the National Museum of Anthropology, that the first abnormal phenomena was registered. Besides meeting fierce opposition from the inhabitants of San Miguel Coatlinchán which culminated with the suppression of protests by the Mexican army, efforts to remove the monolith were hampered by torrential rains and thunderstorms considered abnormal for the region. These climatological phenomena followed the statue during transit, and subsided only when it arrived at the National Museum of Anthropology, where SCP-9531-2 was already housed.

SCP-9531-2 was uncovered in the Mesoamerican archaeological site of Teotihuacan  Nahuatl for *City of the Gods*.   in 1836 and was transported to Mexico City in 1890. Like its counterpart, it exhibited no anomalous behavior until 1963, when its presence is presumed to have abated the effects of SCP-9531-1. 

Up until February 2028, the Foundation remained unaware of the anomalies surrounding both monoliths, only being alerted about their nature when, during major reparations at the National Museum of Anthropology following the devastating [ June 2026 earthquake](https://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/scp-8795 ), SCP-9531-2 was transported to a temporary storage fifteen kilometers outside of museum premises, triggering its and SCP-9531-1’s effects.   

The initial effects of SCP-9531 quickly worsened as the distance between the statues increased, with one side of Mexico City experiencing sudden flash-floods while the other underwent dryness leading to several cases of severe dehydration amongst the population not unlike those present during a heatwave. For the next ten hours, the rest of the anomalies’ effects began manifesting, leading to the city government declaring a state of emergency and culminating with the appearance of SCP-9531-3 in the overlap between the two climatological fields. At this point, the Foundation intervened, cordoning off the area where SCP-9531-3 had manifested and sending a team led by Field Agent Olivia Cuamatzi to mediate with the anomalies.

Initial contact bore little results. Although SCP-9531-3 acknowledged Agent Cuamatzi and her team, they refused to interrupt their game and provide any information about the abnormal weather phenomena, which was fastly approaching catastrophic levels. Upon reviewing the live footage transmitted by Agent Cuamatzi, Foundation experts observed that the vestments worn by SCP-9531-3 were linked to the prehispanic cult of Tláloc, allowing them to provide the field agents with appropriate offerings of corn, tobacco, copal incense and pulque. Agent Cuamatzi approached the anomaly once again and delivered the tribute to SCP-9531-3. The following is a transcription of the ensuing conversation.

**Date:** 16/02/2028

**Note:** Translated from Nahuatl. 

**<Begin Log>**

*Camera turns on. Visibility is greatly reduced due to fog and the volume of rain. The silhouettes of SCP-9531-3 continue to chase each other as Agent Cuamatzi approaches. She lays down the offerings, which causes all instances of SCP-9531-3 to notice and approach her.* 

**SCP-9531-3A:** Ah, she returns! Look, Nappatecuhtli! She brings tribute this time!

**SCP-9531-3C:** Then I guess she deserves some answers, Yauhqueme. What do you say, Tomiyauhtecuhtli? And you, Opocheli?

**SCP-9531-3D:** Yes, yes, let her ask again the questions her heart holds!

**SCP-9531-3B:** We will answer truthfully! The sooner her curiosity is quenched, the sooner we can go back to our game! 

**Agent Cuamatzi:** I am glad to see these offerings please you. I have questions about all this rain and the… uh… dryness, and how it’s related to the game you’re playing.

**SCP-9531-3C:** Yes, the rain. We come with it. We master and distribute it, letting it fall little by little or all at once as our Father commands. 

**SCP-9531-3D:** *Drip drip drip* the rain goes down. *Tap tap tap* it falls into our Mother’s lap, for the earth to be replenished and the thirst extinguished. We are the drums that set its rhythm.

**SCP-9531-3A:** Even [ our flayed uncle](https://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/scp-8562 ) needs water for his withered skin. We are north and south, east and west, and our work never ends.

**SCP-9531-3B:** No, never! We are always working, always toiling to keep the balance of the heavens and the earth. There is rarely any time left to play!

**Agent Cuamatzi:** But now there is time, no? Is that why the climate is… well, like this?

**SCP-9531-3A:** Mother and Father are looking elsewhere, yearning for their precious idols of stone. What was once sundered was reunited and then sundered again! 

**SCP-9531-3D:** They will not notice if we make a little mischief. No one will pull us by our ears now! “Don’t break your pots,” they say, yet they both did that long ago. Why shouldn’t we want to do the same?

**SCP-9531-3B:** Father’s wrath rains down elsewhere as it did when he was first ripped from his people! Oh, how he howled as his loyal ones wept for his stolen image, how he thundered! Only Mother could ever calm him down, yet now they both clamor with indignation!

**SCP-9531-3C:** Ah, but the laws of heaven are clear as water, and Mother and Father gave their word: no more turning people into fish!

**All instances simultaneously:** But we didn’t agree to abstain!

**Agent Cuamatzi:** I see. And now that you’re free to play your game while your parents are busy, what happens when one of you wins? 

**SCP-9531-3D:** Oh, the prize is very simple! Whoever wins gets to end the world.

**<End Log>**

**Afterword:** Following this conversation, the Foundation alerted the Mexican government of its findings, leading to SCP-9531-2 being hastily moved back into the National Anthropology Museum, abating the crisis and causing SCP-9531-3 to dissipate. 

After consultating with CALMECAC, *Comandancia de Armamento y Logística Mexicana para el Control de Amenazas Contranaturales*. (Mexican Armament and Logistic Command for the Control of Unnatural Menaces). Anomalous branch of *Secretaría de la Defensa Nacional*, SEDENA (Secretariat of National Defense).  it has been determined that the best way to avoid repetition of this incident and minimize the risks that the anomaly entails is to return SCP-9531-1 to the people of San Miguel Coatlinchán. Any latent properties of the anomaly will remain concealed by local folklore and customs, and the native population have agreed not to disclose the return of the statue to outsiders. Since renewed separation of the monoliths risks breaching containment, SCP-9531-2 will be buried next to its counterpart, thus negating any further anomalous phenomena or manifestations of SCP-9531-3.
