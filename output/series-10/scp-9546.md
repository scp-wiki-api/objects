**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9546 and SCP-9546-1 are contained in a standard humanoid containment chamber in Research Sector-07 of Site-529. Upon request, books and approved writing materials are provided to SCP-9546-1 in order to reduce stress. All testing of SCP-9546 must be approved by Head Researcher Madeline Thorson. SCP-9546-1 must be sedated before any attempts to transport SCP-9546. During transport, SCP-9546 must be secured within a Class I Restriction Harness and monitored by no fewer than two armed guards.

**Description:** SCP-9546 resembles a worn sock puppet with brown button eyes and a crudely stitched mouth. Numerous sections of the fabric have been repaired with mismatched patchwork. SCP-9546 wears a miniature jester costume and emits a persistent smell of decomposition. Unidentified stains are present along the lower portion of the object.

SCP-9546-1 is an 8 year old boy named Evan Martinez. SCP-9546-1 displays no anomalous properties independent of its connection to SCP-9546. Attempts to remove SCP-9546 have resulted in severe pain and extensive tissue damage localized to SCP-9546-1's left hand and wrist. Further separation attempts have been suspended due to the risk of a degloving injury. 

**Discovery:** On February 5th, 2001, a Foundation agent embedded within the Seattle Police Department took SCP-9546 and SCP-9546-1 into custody following the reports of a young boy wandering the streets alone for several days. Witnesses described the boy as pale, exhausted, and abnormally protective of SCP-9546. When attempts were made to remove SCP-9546 from SCP-9546-1, he attempted to flee, screaming, "Leave me and Lewy alone!". Additional attempts at separation resulted in SCP-9546-1 suffering sudden cardiac arrest and loss of consciousness.

**Addendum 9546.3:** 02/16/2001 Follow-Up Medical Exam

= **Site-529 Follow-Up Medical Report**

---

**Patient:** SCP-9546-1 (Evan Martinez)

**Examining Physician:** Dr. Amelia Strong

**Condition Summary:**

* Previous hairline fractures of the lower left ribs no longer detectable

* Abdominal contusions fully resolved with no residual bruising or tenderness

* Gangrenous tissue previously observed at the junction between SCP-9546-1's wrist and SCP-9546 no longer present

* Scratch marks along the left forearm healed without visible scarring

**Physician's Notes:** Follow-Up examination conducted seven days after initial intake. The purpose of the examination was to assess recovery progress following the extensive injuries documented during SCP-9546-1's arrival at Site-529.

CT imaging revealed no evidence of the previously documented rib fractures. Bone structure appeared intact with no indications of recent healing, callus formation, or prior trauma. Medical staff initially suspected an imaging error, however, repeated scans conducted using separate equipment yielded identical results. Physical examination of the abdomen also failed to identify any signs of injury. Bruising observed during intake has completely disappeared. SCP-9546-1 reported no pain when pressure was applied to the affected regions.

The tissue necrosis and gangrene surrounding the attachment point between SCP-9546-1's left wrist and SCP-9546 was no longer there. Tissue previously described as extensively damaged now appears healthy. No evidence of infection, scarring, or tissue regeneration was present. Medical staff were unable to identify the reason for the restoration. Scratch marks observed along the left forearm have likewise healed completely. No scar tissue was detected.

Throughout the examination SCP-9546-1 remained cooperative and displayed no signs of discomfort. During examination, SCP-9546 repeatedly interrupted personnel and objected to continued medical evaluation of SCP-9546-1. 

When informed that all previously documented injuries had disappeared, SCP-9546 responded with the statement: "Told ya he'd be alright. The kid always bounces back." No further explanation was provided. Recommendation has been made for continued weekly examinations to monitor for recurrence of symptoms or additional anomalous healing events.

**Addendum 9546.9:** Researcher's Note 09/30/2001

**Researcher:** Dr. Madeline Thorson

**Note:** On 09/10/2001, SCP-9546 entered an inactive state during a scheduled recreational period with SCP-9546-1. Unlike previous recorded events, SCP-9546 did not resume activity. Dr. Strong maintained continuous observation for five days. No movement, vocalization, or other anomalous activity was observed. Repeated attempts to elicit a response were unsuccessful.

At 08:14 on 09/16/2001, SCP-9546 detached from SCP-9546-1’s hand and fell to the floor of the containment chamber. The separation occurred without injury to SCP-9546-1. A comprehensive medical examination was conducted immediately. The examination found no remaining anomalous connection between the two subjects. SCP-9546-1 displayed no anomalous properties requiring continued containment.

Following review by Site administration, SCP-9546-1 was approved for Class-B amnestic treatment and released into a Foundation-operated foster placement under long-term observation.

Prior to amnestic administration, SCP-9546-1 requested supervised access to SCP-9546. The request was approved. SCP-9546-1 departed Site-529 on 09/29/2001. SCP-9546 remains secured in archival containment. No further anomalous activity has been recorded. 

By recommendation of Head Researcher Madeline Thorson SCP-9546 is pending reclassification from Euclid to Neutralized.
