]]

]

]

SCP-9593 Promotional Poster.

**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9593 has concluded, and a standard Foundation media blackout has been initiated. Foundation Web Crawlers have scrubbed all references to SCP-9593 from social media websites, entertainment forums, and printed media. Two copies of *Prestige TeeVee* are stored in Site-322's anomalous media archive. Viewers of the show must have a memetic resistance score (MRS) in the 93rd percentile or above in order to conduct research.

**Description:** SCP-9593 is *Prestige TeeVee*, a limited television series produced by the [Totleighsoft Entertainment Division](https://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/scp-2803) and written by Daniel Enforcement. 

The show's six episodes follow Dennis Keller, an insurance broker who has been defrauding elderly clients for the past 4 years, and his family and friends. Episodes range from 55 to 70 minutes in length and premiered simultaneously on every streaming service at 10 PM every Monday night from March 4th, 2018, to April 8, 2018. 

Any individual who views more than 15 minutes of any episode of SCP-9593 will become convinced that *Prestige TeeVee* is one of the greatest artistic achievements in the history of the medium. The show ostensibly contains no meaningful character development, no discernible thematic content, and no narrative tension.

Individual subjects experiencing this anomaly invariably disagree on what the show is about, describing it as fundamentally concerned with masculinity, capitalism, grief, and the American family. When subjects are shown other interpretations, they will agree that those readings are also correct. 

**Addendum 9593.1:** Character Synoposis

**DENNIS KELLER** — played by Kyle Chandler

A 52-year-old from Columbus, Ohio, and regional manager of a mid-sized insurance company. For four years, he has been subtly defrauding elderly clients by enrolling them in unnecessary policies and pocketing the difference. In addition to enjoying grilling and collecting old baseball cards, he drives a three-year-old Lexus.

**CAROL KELLER** — played by Melissa Leo

Dennis Keller's wife. She has not inquired despite being aware of the unexplained income. She keeps a vegetable garden, loves her book club, and is characterized by her neighbors as kind and trustworthy. By most quantifiable standards, her marriage to Dennis is stable.

**RYAN KELLER** — played by Lucas Hedges

The seventeen-year-old son of Dennis and Carol. Throughout the series, he is gloomy and reclusive. 

**PHIL ACCARDO** — played by Michael Shannon

Dennis's associate, hired to handle circumstances in which clients who have been defrauded become liabilities. Throughout the series, it is implied that he has the capacity for violence. Dennis is personally uninteresting to him and professionally competent.

**ARTHUR BEEM** — played by Bob Odenkirk

Dennis Keller's direct boss at the brokerage. Despite working with Dennis for 9 years, Beem is unaware of, or shows no interest in, Dennis's activities outside quarterly performance metrics, where Dennis routinely places in the top 3. Every other Friday, the two go out to lunch and discuss football. Affected viewers refer to Beem as "the moral center of the show." 

**NEIL KESSLER** — played by Shea Whigham

Dennis Keller's closest friend for 30 years. Kessler has no connection to Dennis's fraudulent activities and works in commercial HVAC installation. About twice a month, the two go to a public golf course to play golf. 

**GARY** — played by Tim Blake Nelson

Next-door neighbor to Dennis Keller, who is never given a last name. He appears in four episodes, but only briefly, with no dialogue beyond small talk. His Weber Summit series grill is worth about $1,400 more than Dennis's current model. This is the only matter to have elicited a discernible emotional response from Dennis throughout the entirety of SCP-9593. 

**TOMMY ACCARDO** — played by Mahershala Ali

Phil Accardo's younger brother, who appears in a singular scene in the second episode, eats a sandwich while Phil and Dennis talk in a diner. He doesn't speak, look either man in the eye, or respond to what they say. He finishes his sandwich and walks out of the frame. 

As of the date of this filing, users on a variety of television/entertainment-focused message boards have made over 43,000 posts about how Tommy Accardo is the last truly innocent person in the world of the show.

**PATRICIA WOHL** — played by Judith Ivey

A 71-year-old retired schoolteacher who has been a client of Dennis Keller for over a decade. Over the course of four years, she signed up for six unnecessary insurance policies, generating approximately $34,000 in fake commissions. She is financially stable because she receives a state pension and hasn't noticed the difference.

**Addendum 9593.2:** Truncated Episode Descriptions

**EPISODE 1 — "MARGIN"**

The episode begins with an aerial shot of Columbus, Ohio, at dawn. Twelve minutes are dedicated to showing Dennis's morning routine. He makes coffee, checks his email, and drives to work on I-71 while a loud orchestral score plays throughout the entire segment.

The episode's central sequence shows Dennis meeting with Patricia Wohl, a 74-year-old widow, in his office. During a 44-minute one-shot conversation, he signs her up for three insurance policies that she doesn't need and will never use. The camera films this from a distance, through glass, framed like a security camera. 

The last scene, a 4-minute, 11-second shot, shows Dennis standing by his grill in the backyard at night. The score reaches a climax. He is likely considering whether or not to get a new grill, eventually deciding it doesn't need to be replaced before going inside. The end credits roll over a still shot of the grill in the dark.

**Addendum 9593.3:** Foundation Intervention

The Foundation was made aware of SCP-9593 following the airing of its first episode. Multiple attempts were made to intervene and cut off SCP-9593 broadcasts each week, but viewers could still access illicit streaming websites to continue viewing new episodes.

Instead, the Foundation opted to continue with the show's removal on mainstream streaming services and placed pixel trackers on 3,500,000 URLs associated with illegal video streaming, with the goal of amnesticizing viewers and scrubbing the internet of references to it following the airing of the final episode. This was successful, and SCP-9593 was fully contained on April 11th, 2018. Interviews with the cast were held, and none had any recollection of filming the show. 

**Addendum 9593.4:** Daniel Enforcement
