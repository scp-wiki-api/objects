The proposed eigenmachine will operate via a temporally retroductive thaumaturgical effect. Through careful manipulation of conditions in a controlled environment, trapping relevant data in anomalous energy pulses, and injection of ambichronological materials at controlled intervals, a ritual may be produced such that the only conditions that could result in the present state are equal to conditions that have already sent a warning (with all necessary data) to adjacent timeplanes, thus averting this disaster before it began. It is our hope that careful study of this iteration's historical logs may allow other iterations to circumvent the anomaly.

ADDENDUM 9630.III: Additional speculation regarding SCP-9630

It is one day before the activation, but my concerns have driven me to edit this file.

Already, there are holes in this file, emptiness we cannot parse or understand. The world is wavering, but we do not notice, save an odd feeling that something has gone wrong. Even as I type this, I feel part of me itching to insert a word that no longer exists, a turn of phrase rendered meaningless by the anomaly, like a tongue finding an unexpected gap in teeth.

It is, perhaps, hubristic to think we can worm our way out of this mess, be it in this iteration or any other. But what I am even more concerned by is SCP-9630 itself. Most agree it is the construct of non-existence, but none can explain how it arose, or its seeming intelligence. Nor why it chose our iteration to target.

It is my fear that SCP-9630 is, essentially, a parasite, hopping along the tracks made when concepts diffuse outwards and inwards. If so, how much danger might our attempt cause? Are we doing the right thing? Is the anomaly preventing our completion, or chasing us towards it?

It is impossible to halt it now. There is still a chance this attempt works; abandoning it will only lead to further despair in these last days. But what if this dooms us all? What if the only reason our attempt has not failed is because the anomaly wills it?

I enclose this at the end of the file, which was, is, and will be the last of the innumerable dispersed in our attempt, such that whoever finds this knows: we tried our best to evade the hunter, but we have only made it angry. We hope this will aid your own attempt at escape.

I convey this iteration's deepest apologies if we are in error.

- **O5-4**

SCP-9630 was first identified by the Department of Disappearances, via observation of an absent intuitive well-ordering of emotions, despite circumstantial evidence pointing to its existence. Further investigation revealed a dramatic decrease in universal conceptual complexity, at which point the issue was forwarded to O5 Command. The Foundation observed several further related incidents, leading to the current situation.

Additional historical information and details of **PROJECT** **ECHO** are found in the ADDENDA.

# PROJECT ECHO

### HQ, SITE-512

###### COMMISSIONED BY: **O5 Council**

###### SUPERVISED BY: **O5-4**

17/02/23

Bruno van Rijn

Director, Site-512

---

**Overview**: PROJECT ECHO is the proposed final action of this iteration of the Foundation. Employees without sufficient clearance have been informed this project aims to counteract the phenomenon caused by SCP-9630; however, given the evident inevitability of the collapse of the global conceptosphere, it is necessary to avoid further attempts at preserving this iteration, and instead aim to forewarn other iterations. 

As such, the end goal of this project is the successful construction of an eigenmachine capable of propagating the documentation this iteration has collected to any potential extradimensional or extrauniversal entities. This will be delivered via a "Temporal Reboot"-standard ritual.

The proposed eigenmachine will operate via a temporally retroductive thaumaturgical effect. Through careful manipulation of conditions in a controlled environment, trapping relevant data in anomalous energy pulses, and injection of ambichronological materials at controlled intervals, a ritual may be produced such that the only conditions that could result in the present state are equal to conditions that have already sent a warning (with all necessary data) to adjacent timeplanes, thus averting this disaster before it began. It is our hope that careful study of this iteration's historical logs may allow other iterations to circumvent the anomaly.

The activation date is scheduled for 27/02/23. The signal will have triggered on 13/02/23. The resultant retrocausal quanta will build up and result in a ZK-Class temporal paradox on 13/03/23, and hence can be disregarded.

ADDENDUM 9630.III: Additional speculation regarding SCP-9630

It is one day before the activation, but my concerns have driven me to edit this file.

Already, there are holes in this file, emptiness we cannot parse or understand. The world is wavering, but we do not notice, save an odd feeling that something has gone wrong. Even as I type this, I feel part of me itching to insert a word that no longer exists, a turn of phrase rendered meaningless by the anomaly, like a tongue finding an unexpected gap in teeth.

It is, perhaps, hubristic to think we can worm our way out of this mess, be it in this iteration or any other. But what I am even more concerned by is SCP-9630 itself. Most agree it is the construct of non-existence, but none can explain how it arose, or its seeming intelligence. Nor why it chose our iteration to target.

It is my fear that SCP-9630 is, essentially, a parasite, hopping along the tracks made when concepts diffuse outwards and inwards. If so, how much danger might our attempt cause? Are we doing the right thing? Is the anomaly preventing our completion, or chasing us towards it?

It is impossible to halt it now. There is still a chance this attempt works; abandoning it will only lead to further despair in these last days. But what if this dooms us all? What if the only reason our attempt has not failed is because the anomaly wills it?

I enclose this at the end of the file, which was, is, and will be the last of the innumerable dispersed in our attempt, such that whoever finds this knows: we tried our best to evade the hunter, but we have only made it angry. We hope this will aid your own attempt at escape.

I convey this iteration's deepest apologies if we are in error.

- **O5-4**
