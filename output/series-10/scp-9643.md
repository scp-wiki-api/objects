EXT. FOREST BY THE LICKING RIVER - MORNING.

A man crouches down, silhouetted by fog, shadows slashing him as trees billow in a bitter wind. He examines the body with calculated detachment, his eyes as cold and dark as the corpse laying before him. Slowly, he rises. A flicker of fire briefly illuminates his face as a cigarette is lit, and he idly caressing a scar on his chin, ruminating stoically. The man is hard and handsome, like a stubborn tree that refuses to be cut down, its roots clung deep into the earth, but one that you can still scratch the name of your sweetheart onto. He stares at the body as wisps of smoke curl and caress him like the ghost of a long-forgotten lover.

= **NARRATOR**

Life is funny sometimes. Just when you think you can relax, you get called out to the back end of Nowhere by a man you've never met, to solve a case you know nothing about, only to be greeted by a sorry stiff and a dreary grey sky. But that's the kind of life I lead. You see...

His voice is a harsh growl, like he had visited Hell and dined upon the brimstone, the grit and granules sticking to his throat until he exhales sulfur and fire. The man looks up, staring out into the forest, the sky above roiling with the promise of a distant storm. 

= **NARRATOR**

I'm the guy you call when everything that could go wrong, has, and since I'm here, that can only mean one thing:

The man drops his cigarette and snuffs it out with his shoe, grinding it into the forest floor.

= **NARRATOR**

That I and everyone in a twenty-mile radius was sitting pretty on a powder keg that was about to blow.

He is MURPHY LAW, and he is also our NARRATOR. He is poised and sharp, ready to give anybody, man or monster, dead or alive, a bit of the business.

= **THOMAS**

Wait, what the hell was that? What just happened? Why did everything turn black and white? Did we--

= **HUNTER**

What in God's name is going on here? How did you do that?!

HUNTER coughs, fanning away the smoke from MURPHY'S cigarette.

= **THOMAS**

Hey! Hey, can I get some answers? Sir?

MURPHY ignores them.

= **NARRATOR**

A couple of bewildered hikers stooping over a body like vultures, demanding answers. Heh, you and me both. The stiff had obviously been eighty-sixed and dragged out here, but by who, and why? Of course, it was a lot more complicated than that, seeing as how--

= **MURPHY**

Hmm? No, I'm a private investigator. I got called out here by a Mr. Wilson Dane. Never met the guy, but based on the description he gave me, uh...

Is that who that is? Uh, well, uh ... I don't know anything about it, sir. We just found him like this -- my brother and me.

Dane had called me outta the blue on the phone a few days ago, saying he had a big job for me, then told me to meet him in a town called Sadiesville -- but he never showed up, so I had to ask around. Got a hot tip about someone matching Dane's description being thrown onto a truck headed for the river. I drove out to the bank and spotted a boat with four people in it, one of them slumped over dead or drunk, then tailed it out here. That's when I came across the body.

= **THOMAS**

What did he want with you?

= **MURPHY**

No idea, but I got a pretty good feeling it's got something to do with him getting knocked off. Either of you fellas familiar with Sadiesville?

= **THOMAS and HUNTER**

No sir. We're from Morehead.

= **MURPHY**

Hmm. Guess I'll have to do things the hard way, then. You two fellas run along now. I'll deal with the cops.

= **THOMAS**

Y-you sure?

MURPHY ignores them as he lights another cigarette and stares into the distance.

= **MURPHY**

Probably be better if you do. I got a feeling it's gonna be one of those days...

= **TITLE SPLASH**

= **Murphy Law in... Skip 9643 - ANOMALIES WITH DIRTY FACES!**

EXT. STREETS OF SADIESVILLE - LATE MORNING

MURPHY LAW is walking into town, his eyes darting around, studying his surroundings carefully. The sky is a pale, soupy grey, and although the trees and distant mountains are bursting with color, the town itself seems washed-out and dim. He notices a barn in the distance constructed of rotting wood, an adjacent silo half-claimed by rust, and a neighboring house with a large satellite dish in the yard, most of its metal stripped. In town, some of the buildings are boarded up and crumbling, the grass surrounding them is overgrown and tangled, and the streets are in severe disrepair.  As he pauses, a dog trots across the street in the opposite direction, a severed human hand in its mouth.

= **NARRATOR**

When I first came here, I heard some of the locals call this place "Satansville". Hmph, can't imagine why. 

MURPHY shoves his hands in his pockets and continues walking. He sees cars from three decades ago sputtering past, their chassis eaten away by rust, their bumpers torn off. He sees panhandlers on street corners, their faces misshapen by abuse, starvation, and desperation. He sees traffic lights flickering eternally from their gallows. He sees graffiti on billboards, condemned buildings, and discarded automobiles. He sees factories contributing to the slate sky with smog. He sees rust, mold, soot, decay, poverty.

Something catches his eye as he crosses a street: cars, signs, fences, street lamps, even the sides of buildings, are all caked with an unusual grime. MURPHY closely examines the grime on one of the fences, scratching at it with his fingernail. Even with the general noise of the town and the grumbling sky above, he thinks he hears a faint sound coming from it. He leans in closely, but a voice suddenly calls out to him.

= **VOICE**

Ah, I see you've discovered the local oddity.

Startled, MURPHY jerks around, seeing a disheveled grey-haired middle-aged man wearing a rumpled overcoat. The man raises his hands, indicating he means no harm. This is WESSON.

= **WESSON**

Sorry, didn't mean to startle ya. I just figured you were new in town since you seem to take an interest in that stuff. It's called "whiskey fungus"; it's a strain that feeds off the angel's share of the bourbon -- the stuff that evaporates during the aging process. It'll stick to just about anything, including cars and trees. Apparently it's harmless to humans.

MURPHY studies the man for a few more seconds. He notices a sidearm hidden under the overcoat.

= **MURPHY**

Is that so? Are you from around here?

= **WESSON**

Nah, not really. I'm just poking around a bit.

= **MURPHY**

Hmm, so you wouldn't know who Wilson Dane is, by any chance?

= **WESSON**

Sorry, doesn't ring a bell. Giacomo would be the one to ask, maybe.

= **MURPHY**

Who?

= **WESSON**

Nelson Giacomo. Owns a distillery around here, plus a nightclub, a few restaurants, and a handful of other businesses. What's your connection to this Dane fellow?

= **MURPHY**

I'm a private investigator; he called me out here on a case. The problem is, he bit the big one before I could figure out what he wanted, and now I'm running around trying to pick up the pieces.

WESSON peers at MURPHY carefully before pointing at him. He then grins.

= **WESSON**

Are you Murphy Law?

= **MURPHY**

You've heard of me?

= **WESSON**

Yeah, some of the people I work with won't shut up about you. Special Agent Raven Wesson, [Unusual Incidents Unit](https://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/unusual-incidents-unit-hub).

WESSON presents his federal ID and chuckles.

= **WESSON**

Never thought I'd actually meet Murphy Law in person.

= **MURPHY**

Yeah, small world. What's the UIU doing out here? You got an angle on Dane, too?

= **WESSON**

Nah, local law enforcement'll handle that. The higher-ups usually throw one or two of us out here to check up on local cryptids and such -- you know, dog-men, cat-women, gorilla-bears, devil-monkeys. Heh, supposedly there's a half-eel, half-pig out by Herrington Lake.

= **MURPHY**

Sounds like people drink too much moonshine. You ever get anything on this Giacomo fella?

= **WESSON**

No, he's legit. Been that way for years. He used to be part of the [Chicago Spirit](https://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/chicago-spirit), but all those dues were paid long before he came here. Opened up that bourbon distillery of his and expanded from there. It's actually pretty good stuff, if you're into that sort of thing.

= **MURPHY**

How do I get in touch with him?

WESSON sighs, idly kicking at grass and gravel.

= **WESSON**

Well, he's sort of a big deal in this town, so it's more than likely HE'LL get in touch with YOU. He has a girlfriend who sings at this nightclub, Monroe's.

MURPHY stares at WESSON incredulously.

= **MURPHY**

A nightclub, in this hayseed town?

= **WESSON**

Like I said, Nelson's a big deal. Apparently he wants to reinvigorate life into this old burg. Local scuttlebutt's not my strong suit, though, so who knows.

MURPHY nods his head.

= **WESSON**

Hey, if you plan on poking around, just be careful which logs you overturn. Giacomo's got a pair of nasty bodyguards on his payroll, high-end hitmen he lifted off of Marshall, Carter, & Dark. You'll know em' when you see em'. 

= **NARRATOR**

I thanked him for the warning, and he told me where he was staying in case I wanted to keep in touch. I've made it a habit not to bother feds or the police or anyone with a badge any more than I have to. We tend to step on each other's toes a lot. The nightclub wasn't going to be open for hours, so I figured I'd grab a bite and settle in. Wesson told me of a place that served the best hot browns he'd ever eaten, and that was enough to stoke my curiosity. 

MURPHY heads in the direction of a local diner. Thunder rumbles in the distance, and a rain like pale iron begins to fall.

FADE OUT.

I had my pick of the litter of cheap hotels, so I set up a base, then swung by a store and bought a bottle of Giacomo's bourbon, called "Foster's Own". Nothing seemed out of the ordinary, but I didn't trust this town enough to guzzle it, even if I did have a bit of a hankering.

Again?

And a few other places. That's how I know Willie. He was a manager or something over there.

EXT. SADIESVILLE LUMBER MILL - EARLY MORNING.

MURPHY is standing outside his car, staring at the derelict mill as a steady rain pours.

= **NARRATOR**

I wanted to hit that distillery, since I figured it was my best shot at cracking this case, but first I decided to check out the lumber mill Holiday mentioned, just to indulge my curiosity. It was even more run-down than the rest of this sorry town. In all likelihood I was just wasting my time, but in my line of work, even the smallest rocks might have something interesting to hide if you take the time to turn them over.

MURPHY approaches the mill, his footsteps sloshing on the wet, rocky surface. There is not a soul to be found, and except for the pattering of rain, it is completely silent. As MURPHY approaches, he notices patches of whiskey fungus clinging to various surfaces. He takes four steps before hearing a creak. He stops and carefully looks around. Slowly, he pulls out his magnum, keeping it level. He resumes his approach.

Although the front door is boarded and labeled as CONDEMNED, the rotted wood falls apart easily. MURPHY enters the mill, shining a flashlight, and picks his way through carefully. Various sections of the roof have caved in, allowing the rain to pour in. Other parts of the structure are rusted beyond recognition. Under MURPHY'S light, the rust vaguely resembles blood. He proceeds further, alerted to any sound or movement. His heart races as his foot suddenly bursts through the floor, breaking apart the moldy wooden surface, and he curses. His sour mood changes as he hears the sound of distant laughter. MURPHY shines his flashlight in the direction he thinks the sound came from, but sees nothing. Carefully, he pulls his foot out of the floor and continues.

A broken window, partially caked with dust and whiskey fungus, draws his attention. MURPHY cautiously approaches it and peers out. Close-up on his eyes as they widen in surprise. He sees distant shapes in the rain, moving faintly. MURPHY watches silently, noticing a creature resembling a panther emerge from the woods. His hair stands on end as the creature rears up on its hind legs, and begins to walk.

One of the shapes stands and turns. It is small, pale, with dark eyes and a scraggly beard. MURPHY ducks down as its gaze turns to the mill. He peeks out after waiting for a minute and sees nothing. He sighs, but holds his breath as he hears something whistling in the distance. MURPHY decides to withdraw and leaves the mill. Before getting into his car, he turns around one more time, scanning the area, but sees nothing. A vague whistling is heard before he manages to close the car door and drive off.

= **NARRATOR**

No comment.

FADE OUT.

INT. BLUE MOON DINER - MORNING.

MURPHY LAW is seated at the counter.

= **NARRATOR**

Before I set off for the distillery, I slipped into a local diner for some grub. Say what you will about this run-down burg, but they didn't screw around with their food. Before I had taken two sips outta my coffee, though, two well-dressed men around my age barged through the door. They looked like they had stepped in from two centuries ago, and carried themselves with a kind of polite, restrained arrogance. I ignored them until they sat down and flanked me.

= **MAN ON LEFT**

Morning, Lou.

The attendant at the counter smiles anxiously.

= **LOU**

[Hi, Chris. Hi, Beryl](https://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/scp-4450). Wh-what can I get for you gentlemen?

CHRIS points to MURPHY.

= **CHRIS**

Our meal's on 'is tab. The usual if you don't mind.

= **MURPHY**

Like hell it is!

MURPHY growls but is restrained as CHRIS places a hand over his wrist. The grip is firm and irresistible. LOU stiffens, watching them nervously as he writes the order down and sends it off. CHRIS smiles at MURPHY.

= **CHRIS**

No need to get excited, dutch. We just wanna make sure our guests are eatin' good n' proppa. What would you recommend for em', Beryl?

= **BERYL**

I'm thinkin' oatmeal, Chris.

= **CHRIS**

Ah, that's a fine way to begin the morning, eh? Tidy as china. But let's give 'im a bit o' flavor, eh? I'm thinkin' blueberries.

= **BERYL**

Naw, blackberries. Perfect time o' year for em'.

CHRIS grins, patting MURPHY'S hand.

= **CHRIS**

Right you are, Beryl. Oatmeal and blackberries for Mr. Law. Nix the java there, Lou.

MURPHY glowers as LOU carefully removes his coffee. It is substituted with a bowl of oatmeal with blackberries.

= **BERYL**

Much nicer, innit? Easy on the belly and gives ya tons of energy.

MURPHY grumbles. BERYL stands up, examining the wall. A rusty cast-iron skillet is hanging nearby. He removes it and smiles at LOU.

= **BERYL**

Bah, this old thing's gone to pot. Ere', lemme dispose of it for ya.

BERYL crumples the skillet in his bare hands as if it's made of tissue paper. He then tosses it into a nearby wastebasket. His brother laughs and rubs MURPHY'S back.

= **CHRIS**

Now don't get all touched, dutch:  we just want you to have a swell time in Sadiesville. Innit that right, Beryl?

= **BERYL**

Sure we do. This town's got lots o' charm. Lots of it.

BERYL removes one of the blackberries and eats it.

= **BERYL**

Be a real shame if something UNFAVORABLE happened while you was here.

= **CHRIS**

We reckon you got your business here, whatever that is, and we're just here to make sure you go about it unmolested.

= **BERYL**

Right, right. Then once you're done, you tip your hat, say farewell, and go back where you was from. Nice and peaceful like.

= **CHRIS**

Cuz it'd be a shame if you couldn't do that.

CHRIS picks up a nearby steak knife and squeezes it by the edge. The metal warps as easily as melted butter. He puts the knife back, chuckles, and slaps MURPHY on the back, almost shoving him into the counter.

Oh, just toddling around on vacation. I could ask you the same thing, Murph! You out here on a case?

MURPHY scoffs.

= **MURPHY**

You don't know the half of it, Fred.

= **FRED**

Oh? Well, maybe you can tell us over lunch.

= **MURPHY**

Us?

MURPHY peers around, seeing a young woman standing shyly behind FRED. She is wearing a yellow summer dress and has long golden-brown hair pulled back into a ponytail. FRED looks back at her sheepishly.

= **FRED**

Oh yeah, this is [Cassy](https://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/scp-085). She's a ... uh, a very close friend.

CASSY smiles and gently holds FRED'S hand. MURPHY notices and mirrors her expression. He removes his hat.

= **MURPHY**

'Zat so? Well, it's a pleasure to meet you, Miss Cassy.

= **CASSY**

Likewise, Mr. Law. Freddie's told me a little about you.

= **MURPHY**

Uh, not too many crazy stories, I hope.

CASSY laughs softly.

= **CASSY**

That's a matter of opinion and perception, Mr. Law. Maybe you could offer yours over that lunch Fred mentioned?

MURPHY smiles.

= **MURPHY**

I'd be honored, ma'am -- uh, my treat, of course.

= **FRED**

Murphy, please! I insist! After all, I think I owe you a favor or two!

MURPHY gazes somberly at FRED'S cane.

= **MURPHY**

All right, Fred: your treat. You ever tried burgoo?

= **FRED**

I don't even know what that is.

MURPHY chuckles and leads the way, putting his hat back on.

= **MURPHY**

Local specialty. Southern comfort food. I'll drive.

CASSY and FRED chatter as they walk off. MURPHY half-listens for a moment but pauses as he catches movement out of the corner of his eye. He turns quickly, but the vague shape hides before he can get a better look.

= **NARRATOR**

Yeah, there's definitely something odd about this part of the woods. I dunno, maybe it's something in the water.

Thunder is heard overhead, and a gentle rain begins to sprinkle. Murphy looks up, into a dismal grey sky.

= **NARRATOR**

The rain in Appalachia is the worst. It'll slash and sting you, rip you to shreds, chill you to the bone, drown you. It comes and goes when it pleases, makes the sky dreary, drains all the color outta the world. They say the sun shines bright on the old Kentucky home, and maybe sometimes it does. 

But not today.

MURPHY solemnly tips his hat and turns around to rejoin the others. He walks off into the distance, humming quietly to himself.

SLOW FADE OUT.
