##grey|**ITEM #:**##

**9701**

##grey|**CONTAINMENT CLASS:**##

**KETER**

**Special Containment Procedures:** The entity is currently uncontained. Public sightings should be covered up as per standard procedure. 

**Description:** SCP-9701 is an anomalous member of the *Leporidae* familiy The familiy of rabbits and hares. Its exact genus is debated due to multiple contradictory reports. that is capable of Level 3 reality bending. The only confirmed physical details are that the entity has white fur and red eyes. SCP-9701 migrates between the Earth and the Moon regularly. This behavior is unexplained. 

Researchers have identified 118 likely SCP-9701 sightings since 2000, with 71 of those being confirmed.  Of the 71 confirmed SCP-9701 sightings, 34 of them occurred in proximity to a natural disaster, 47 were in rural areas, and at least 20 involved direct interactions with humans. Sightings have decreased steadily since 2000, with a particularly steep decline since the start of the Foundation's investigation, indicating that SCP-9701 may be aware of its observation.

In addition to contemporary accounts, mythical accounts throughout the world match the description of SCP-9701. The veracity of these accounts, however, cannot be confirmed; they should be considered coincidental for the time being.

#### Addendum-9701-1: Abridged Incident Log For the full log, contact Dr. Huffard. 

---

| Date | Location | Event | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 28, Dec, 2004 Two days after the Sumatra–Andaman earthquake  | Meulaboh, Indonesia | Locals from the city outskirts reported speaking with a white hare that left a basket near their camp. Humanitarian workers initially dismissed this as a post-traumatic hallucination until one of them captured video of the entity mid-flight. | Witnesses refused to provide specifics about SCP-9701 when interrogated by the Foundation. Class B amnestics were administered. |
| 30, August, 2005 A day after Hurricane Katrina made landfall.  | Louisiana Bayou, U.S. | One self-identified disaster volunteer made a social media post about a rabbit being spotted conversing with locals. The entity fled when the worker attempted to record SCP-9701 with his phone. | The worker promptly mounted a two-day hunt for the rabbit with a group of followers; all involved were lost in the Bayou, presumed dead. No cover-up procedures were required. |
| 9, Nov, 2005 | Baltimore, U.S. | West Baltimore residents began trading a suspicious and unseasonal amount of chocolate eggs. CCTV footage spotted a rabbit resembling SCP-9701 in the area. | All eggs were confiscated for review. No anomalous details noted. |
| 2, June, 2008 | Chirripó, Costa Rica | Field Agents in San Jose reported that a group of hikers befriended a rabbit on *Cerro Chirripó*. Apart from its presence at the summit of a mountain to which it is not indigenous, no anomalous characteristics were noted. One hiker said that the rabbit was "strangely thoughtful; I think he enjoys the sunset." | Field agents refused to provide the names of the hikers. The agents were accordingly demoted. |

#### Addendum-9701-2: Interview Log

---

> **Foreword:** SCP-9701 has recently been captured by Mobile Task Force Gamma-4 during a Lunar expedition. The following is a transcript of Dr. Huffard's interview with SCP-9701.

> ~~-~~-

> **[BEGIN]**

> @@ @@

> *Dr. Huffard clears his throat as Agent Cortez sets SCP-9701 down. The entity sniffs the table.*

>  

> **Dr. Huffard:** It is, um, 1/9/2009. I am Dr. Huffard, and the interviewee is SCP-9701. Now, SCP-9701, could you please begin by stating your name?

>  

> *SCP-9701 looks at Dr. Huffard, blinks, and turns around. He sighs.*

> 

> **Dr. Huffard:** We are aware that you can speak. Please answer the question. It is in your best interest to cooperate. Next question: Why do you move between Earth and the Moon?

>  

> *SCP-9701 wiggles its nose and stares at the table. Dr. Huffard stays silent. The entity jumps off the table.*

> 

> **Dr. Huffard:** (*whispered*) Are we, uh, sure we got the right rabbit?

>   

> **Agent Cortez:** Is there more than one rabbit on the Moon?

> 

> *Huffard and Cortez stare at the rabbit in silence.*

> 

> **Dr. Huffard:** Fair point. 

> @@ @@

> *SCP-9701 defecates on the floor. Huffard nods his head slowly.*

>  

> **Dr. Huffard:** I think we can end this interview. 

> @@ @@

> **[END]**

#### Addendum-9701-3: Dr. Huffard's Status Report

---

> +++++ Dr. Huffard 5/5/09

>  Since the capture of the suspected SCP-9701, no further sightings have been reported, indicating that this is the true SCP-9701. To confirm, we let SCP-9701 loose outside the facility for a few minutes yesterday (with Scranton reality anchors on stand-by). Nothing happened. It did not flee, it did not change behavior, it is just a rabbit. Whatever the reason, SCP-9701 is no longer an anomaly. To all tests and all observations, SCP-9701 is an 8-year-old, 47-centimeter, 2-kilogram, white Japanese hare (*Lepus brachyurus*) with red eyes under Foundation custody. The research team is trying to uncover the cause of this change. Personally, I wonder if, perhaps, it’s our obsession with categories and boxes that has somehow turned the anomalous mundane...

>    

> I'll update the entry once my team decides how to move forward,

> -Dr. Huffard
