**Item Number:** SCP-9706

**Object Class:** Safe

**Special Containment Procedures:** Item is to be monitored bi-weekly by personnel with extrasensory perception. As the item is immobile and its anomalous effects are entirely telepathic in nature, it poses no threat to the Veil of Normalcy. Transcriptions of communications are available to personnel with Level-3 clearance.

**Description:** SCP-9706 refers to two defunct railroad crossing signs (SCP-9706-1 and SCP-9706-2) located in rural Niedersachsen, Germany. The signs are sentient and are capable of telepathic communication. While psychically-sensitive individuals are able to receive this exchange, the anomalies are unable to perceive any communication outside of their own. The gates of both signals are locked in a closed position and are pointing in the same direction, presumably through automation error. They were first discovered in 1953, when a member of the Imperial German Anomalous Matters Examination Agency (IGAMEA) vacationed in the area. Containment and observation of the anomalies continued as IGAMEA was absorbed into the SCP Foundation.

**Addendum:** Various transcriptions of telepathic exchanges made between the anomalies.

**SCP-9706-2:** Don't you think it's strange?

**SCP-9706-1:**  What?

**SCP-9706-2:** That we're both facing the same direction.

**SCP-9706-1:**  What's so strange about that?

**SCP-9706-2:** Well, we're supposed to be facing opposite directions. That way, the smaller metal things will stop moving so they don't get hit by the big metal thing.

**SCP-9706-1:**  But I haven't seen the small metal things or the big metal thing in a very long time. Those huge trees you see over there were just little saplings the last time I did. I'm pretty sure we have nothing to worry about.

**SCP-9706-2:** But what if one of those metal things does come by?

**SCP-9706-1:**  Don't worry, I'll stop it all by myself. You can just stay there and relax.

**SCP-9706-2:** So I'm useless, then.

**SCP-9706-1:**  Of course not. Don't be silly. You're the most useful thing I know of.

**SCP-9706-2:**  How so?

**SCP-9706-1:** Well, who else is around for me to talk to?

**SCP-9706-2:** You know, I've been thinking. Why do you think we both point in the same direction?

**SCP-9706-1:**  I've been thinking, too. Do we have names?

**SCP-9706-2:** Oh, that is a good question. I've known you for a long time but I've never known what to call you. I just look over and see you there. I never assumed I needed a name. 

**SCP-9706-1:**  I think that having one would be pleasant. 

**SCP-9706-2:** Yes, but how do we go about getting one?

**SCP-9706-1:**  Well, let's make up names for each other. You have remarkable red and white stripes all along your gate, so I'm going to call you "Streifen".

(*silence*)

**SCP-9706-1:**  Do you not like it?

**Streifen:**  No...I'm just not good at making up names, or anything else, really. All I'm good at is pointing in the same direction as you.

**SCP-9706-1:**  Why not do what I did, and find a characteristic about me that you like?

(*silence*)

**Streifen:** You like my stripes?

**SCP-9706-1:**  I do. They're your best feature.

(*silence*)

**Streifen:**  At night, when it's dark and quiet, and I think I'm all alone, sometimes I look over and see a little red light blinking, and it puts me at ease. I guess that means that you're "Blinken" now.

**Blinken:**  (*laughs*) I like that. Blinken. And you're Streifen.

**Streifen:**  Hello, Blinken.

**Blinken:** Hello, Streifen.

**Streifen:**  Blinken?

**Blinken:**  Yes?

**Streifen:**  Why do you think we both point in the same direction?

**Blinken:** I don't know. I never concerned myself with it before.

**Streifen:**  Blinken, do you think I'm strange?

**Blinken:**  No, why you do you say that?

**Streifen:**  Because I'm pointing in the same direction as you.

**Blinken:**  So? You've been that way for as long as I can remember.

**Streifen:**  But you're there at the crossing between the road and the rail. If a metal thing came our way, you'd be able to stop one. I'd just look silly standing here.

(*silence*)

**Blinken:**  Well, maybe you would, but you're the only other one I've ever known. Who's to say that anyone else would be silly or strange if they pointed in the opposite direction?

**Streifen:**  Surely, at some point, I must have been pointing in the right direction. Then an accident happened, and now I'm like this. Or maybe one of those zweibeinig Presumably a human.  maliciously moved me like this, and now I'm stuck this way.

**Blinken:**  Possibly. I honestly don't know. You were the first thing I ever saw, and when I saw you, you looked like that. As far as I'm concerned, this is normal.

(*silence*)

**Streifen:**  You really think I'm normal?

**Blinken:**  What else could you be?

**Streifen:**  Sometimes, I think I was meant to point in the opposite direction. Like I was made to be something else, and I ended up like this instead. As an accident. Or out of malice.

**Blinken:**  I don't think either of those are true, Streifen, and even if they are, this is who you are now.

(*silence*)

**Blinken:**  You know what I think? If you were to suddenly change, would you really be "you" anymore?

(*silence*)

**Streifen:**  I'm not sure what that means, Blinken. I need to think about it.

**Streifen:**  Blinken, if you could change something about yourself, what would it be?

**Blinken:**  Oh, that's easy: I'd shine a yellow light instead of a red one.

**Streifen:**  (*laughs*) Why is that?

**Blinken:**  Because yellow's my favorite color. Red just looks strange to me. Yellow reminds me of that bright thing above us.

(*silence*)

**Blinken:**  Streifen, would you change the direction you're pointed in?

(*silence*)

**Streifen:**  I don't know. I thought about what you said earlier, about me being "me". Would you like me if I pointed in the opposite direction?

(*silence*)

**Blinken:**  If that was the only thing that changed ~~ if you stayed exactly the same, the same old Streifen I know ~~ I probably wouldn't mind. If that's how you were originally, I think I could like it. But if you changed, even just that little bit of you...

(*silence*)

**Blinken:**  I don't really think that you would be Streifen anymore. Only Streifen points the same way that I do.

(*silence*)

**Blinken:**  Streifen? Are you okay?

**Streifen:**  I was just thinking about what I'd change about myself. I think I'd change my stripes.

**Blinken:**  (*laughs*) But then you'd *really* not be Streifen!

**Streifen:**  No, silly! I'd made them look black and yellow, and they'd be going up my body instead of across my gate. I think I'd look very fashionable. Besides, you think my red stripes look strange.

**Blinken:**  (*laughs*) No I don't! I think my red *light* looks strange. Red is very fetching on you.

**Streifen:**  Oh, what would you know about fashion? You're pointed in the wrong direction.

(*they both laugh*)

@@ @@

**Blinken:**  Streifen, do you ever think it's strange that we're here, *just* here, and we never move, or never see anyone like us?

**Streifen:**  We're here to stop the small metal things from hitting the big metal thing.

(*silence*)

**Blinken:**  Do you want to know what I honestly think? I think only I was meant to do that.

**Streifen:**  You mean I'm not even supposed to be here?

**Blinken:**  That's not what I meant. Only I'm supposed to stop the metal things. You have a different purpose.

**Streifen:**  What's that?

**Blinken:**  You're here to keep me company. That's why I can talk to you, and nothing else. And only Streifen can talk to me.

**Streifen:**  Maybe it's because we're both pointed in the same direction.

**Blinken:**  I think it's because we are what we are, and that's why we can understand each other. Nothing else can hear us or talk to us except ourselves.

**Streifen:**  So we're special.

**Blinken:**  Yes, exactly!

(*silence*)

**Streifen:**  That makes me very happy, Blinken. "Only we can understand each other". I think...

(*silence*)

**Blinken:**  Streifen?

**Streifen:**  No: I'm *glad* I am the way I am. I'd never be able to talk to you if I weren't. That's my purpose.

**Blinken:**  Oh, Streifen, I'm so happy.

@@ @@

@@ @@

@@ @@

**Streifen:**  Hey, Blinken, do you know what a carriage is?

**Blinken:**  I think so. It's similar to one of those metal things I'm supposed to stop, only it's made out of wood.

**Streifen:**  I remember one of the zweibeinig mentioning it. They said they wanted to have a carriage with someone. I'm not sure what they meant, but when they said that, the other zweibeinig looked very happy.

**Blinken:**  It must be something good. What else do you remember?

**Streifen:**  They said that it was something with holes in it, but they'd be joined together.

**Blinken:**  That doesn't make sense.

**Streifen:**  I know, but they were both very happy about it. The "joining" part in particular is what interested me. They said it means they'd always be together.

**Blinken:**  Just like us.

**Streifen:**  Yes.

(*silence*)

**Streifen:**  Blinken, do you think we could have a carriage, too?

**Blinken:**  Well, I don't care to have any holes in me, but the joining part certainly sounds wonderful.

(*silence*)

**Blinken:**  Yes, I'd love to have a carriage with you, Streifen, if you'll agree to watch over me so I don't get any holes.

**Streifen:**  Of course, as long as you agree that we'll always be together.

**Blinken:**  I'd like that. So now we have a carriage.

(*silence*)

**Blinken:**  It really doesn't feel any different from before, does it?

**Streifen:**  I wouldn't say that. I can't explain it, but I feel a bit happier now.

**Blinken:**  So do I, but then again, you've always made me happy, so I'm not sure what's so different.

(*silence*)

**Streifen:**  Blinken?

**Blinken:**  Yes, Streifen?

**Streifen:**  You've always made me happy, too.
