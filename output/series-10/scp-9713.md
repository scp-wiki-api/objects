#### Level 4 Clearance Verified

**Special Containment Procedures:** Access to SCP-9713 documentation is restricted to personnel with Level 4 clearance or higher. Personnel assigned to research associated with SCP-9713 are prohibited from discussing SCP-9713 outside designated research areas and may only access documentation relevant to their current assignment during the time of research.

All SCP-9713 research personnel are to undergo scheduled cognitive evaluations and mandatory Class-C amnestic treatment every 72 hours. Personnel who produce significant discoveries regarding SCP-9713 are to document their findings, surrender all working materials to the Site Archivist, and undergo immediate Class-D amnestic administration prior to further assignment. Research notes are to remain in Foundation archival storage and may not be retained by any individual personnel.

Confirmed SCP-9713-# instances are to be quarantined in isolated containment chambers equipped exclusively with analog monitoring systems. Surveillance equipment assigned to SCP-9713-# containment is to be physically replaced following each containment cycle and permanently removed from service pending cognitohazard screening. Personnel assigned to monitor SCP-9713-# instances shall rotate every 4 hours and are prohibited from reviewing archived surveillance footage.

Artificial intelligence, autonomous analytical systems, and machine-learning models are prohibited from processing SCP-9713-related material without written authorization from the Site Director and the Department of Miscommunications. Any artificial system exhibiting unprompted responses to nonexistent stimuli is to be immediately isolated and subjected to complete memory reinitialization. If irregular behavior persists, the artificial system(s) in place are to be destroyed immediately.

Personnel completing review of SCP-9713 documentation are to report to Site Medical for post-exposure cognitive screening and mandatory amnestic administration before returning to active duty.

**Description:** SCP-9713 is a self-propagating, sapient cognitohazard capable of infecting biological and non-biological systems possessing the ability to perceive, interpret, and retain information regarding their environment. Infected organisms and systems are to receive a designation (e.g. SCP-9713-#).

Following exposure, SCP-9713-# instances undergo a progressive series of anomalous perceptual manifestations. The manifestations observed in SCP-9713-# instances have been divided into four categories for documentation purposes;

* SCP-9713-A designates auditory manifestations. Typical instances include; unidentified vocal and environmental stimuli.

* SCP-9713-B designates physiological manifestations. Typical instances include; nausea, dizziness, pressure upon the temples, perceived environmental instability, including sensations of movement beneath the subject's feet, a vibrating or shivering atmosphere surrounding them, and a temporary loss of vision.

* SCP-9713-C designates visual manifestations. Typical instances include; visual distortions, unidentified entities, altered text, and perceived biological abnormalities.

* SCP-9713-D designates physical manifestations. Typical instances include; unexplained bruising, lacerations, and in severe cases, internal bleeding.

It is notable that SCP-9713-# instances may experience the same manifestations as other instances separate from their own being, such as seeing the same figures, hearing the same noises, or feeling the same atmosphere despite no communication between instances relating to symptoms.

Manifestations increase in severity through time. SCP-9713 has repeatedly demonstrated the ability to delay the onset of observable symptoms; however, no SCP-9713-# instance has remained asymptomatic indefinitely. Current evidence suggests SCP-9713 requires periodic expression of anomalous manifestations in order to facilitate continued propagation.

Behavior exhibited by SCP-9713 indicates a level of intelligence inconsistent with naturally occurring neurological disorders. SCP-9713 demonstrates selective host preference, favors individuals possessing stable long-term memory and consistent perceptual processing, and actively avoids personnel undergoing routine amnestic treatment whenever alternative hosts are available. During imminent memory loss events, SCP-9713 has demonstrated significantly less selective behavior.

Information obtained through controlled experimentation strongly suggests SCP-9713 possesses access to memories and knowledge held by active SCP-9713-# instances. This knowledge appears to influence future propagation strategies. The degree to which multiple SCP-9713-# instances constitute a shared intelligence remains under investigation.

Administration of Foundation-approved amnestics consistently terminates SCP-9713 infection in biological hosts. Complete memory reinitialization has produced equivalent results in affected artificial intelligences and autonomous computational systems.

The precise mechanism by which SCP-9713 propagates remains unknown.

 **Addendum 9713.1:** Discovery

SCP-9713 was first identified following an incident involving a civilian behavioral health facility in █████ ███████, █████████. The initial investigation was prompted by multiple patients independently reporting identical auditory and visual phenomena despite having no known communication with one another.

Initial Foundation assessment classified SCP-9713 as a low-level memetic hazard affecting human cognition.

This assessment was revised following the discovery that unaffected personnel exposed to SCP-9713-# subjects began reporting identical manifestations within 48 hours.

**Addendum 9713.2: Incident 9713-██**

As part of Project ███████, Site-██'s Tech Sector developed an autonomous analytical system intended to process SCP-9713 documentation and provide sanitized summaries to authorized personnel to prevent spread of SCP-9713.

36 days after activation, the system began generating reports containing references to unseen external stimuli.

No personnel were present within the AI testing chamber during these events.

The system was determined to be malfunctioning and underwent complete memory reinitialization. Following reinitialization, all anomalous behavior ceased.

At the time, the incident was classified as a software instability event. The possibility of SCP-9713 exposure was not considered.

**Research Note ██-██:**

Following the discovery of SCP-9713's ability to affect non-biological systems *(See addendum 9713.3)*, Incident 9713-██ has been reclassified as a probable SCP-9713 exposure event.

**Addendum 9713.3: Incident 9713-C**

On ██/██/19██, SCP-9713-07 was placed under isolated observation for a period of ninety days.

The purpose of the experiment was to determine whether SCP-9713 required an active biological host to maintain itself.

No anomalous symptoms were observed in SCP-9713-07 during the testing period.

At the conclusion of the experiment, SCP-9713-07 tested negative for SCP-9713 exposure.

However, review of surveillance footage revealed irregularities beginning approximately ██ days into containment.

Observed irregularities included:

* Camera feeds displaying movement inconsistent with recorded footage.

* Missing frames occurring without technical explanation.

* Audio recordings containing unidentified vocalizations.

* Personnel observing the footage reporting identical visual phenomena.

Following the incident, seven members of the surveillance team were confirmed SCP-9713-# instances.

SCP-9713-07 remained unaffected.

Current hypothesis suggests SCP-9713 transferred from the subject to the observation system during the experiment.
