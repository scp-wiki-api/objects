**Description:** SCP-9719 is a valley with an area of approximately 160 km^^2^^ located in Kermanshah Province, Iran. As of December 2025, it has a population of 11,942, who reside in three main villages and numerous smaller settlements. 62.5% of the population speak Southern Kurdish (Xwarin) as their first language, with the remainder speaking a dialect of Persian. The vast majority of the population are bilingual. Locals report that Assyrian and Jewish communities were present during the 20th century, but had fully emigrated by the 1980s.

SCP-9719-1 is a sandstone stele located on a hill at the centre of SCP-9719. The stele is embedded into the soil, but moves vertically at a rate of approximately 1.8 cm per day, rising to a height of 2.55 m above the ground and then receding again. At no point has the stele been observed to be fully submerged or fully exposed. Due to the similarity of the stone used with that of the surrounding mountains, it is believed to have been carved near to its current location.

The area of effect of SCP-9719-1 expands and contracts in proportion to the height of the stele above ground: at maximum height, its effects are experienced through the entire valley, and at minimum, it affects an area around 100 m in diameter. While within its area of influence, all affected individuals will refer to themselves and other individuals within SCP-9719 exclusively by substitute names.

The names of individuals born within SCP-9719, or who have resided in SCP-9719 for more than 5 years, will be substituted by names from an unknown Hurro-UrartianAn extinct language family of the Ancient Near East consisting of Hurrian and Urartian, in addition to the aforementioned language. Urartian, the last surviving member of this language family, is believed to have gone extinct around the 6th century BCE. language. The names of individuals who were not born within SCP-9719 and have resided there for less than 5 years will be substituted by names in AkkadianAn East Semitic language which was the dominant language of Mesopotamia from the mid-third millennium BCE to around the 8th century BCE, and included dialects such as Assyrian and Babylonian.. Subjects are unable to recall their original names or the original names of other affected individuals unless shown physical evidence of their original name; this will be forgotten shortly after exposure. 

Following departure from SCP-9719-1's area of influence, all names return to their normal state.

## **Audio Log 9719-013**

**Dr. Golshani:** All of them, I suppose.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Iran's not what I expected. Well, maybe it's because we're up here in the mountains. It's a big country.

**Dr. Golshani:** You should come to Shiraz someday. And you have to see Isfahan. Maybe even Tehran, if you like big cities and can stand the air.

**Dr. Kazemi:** I'd love to, but...

**Dr. Golshani:** You feel nervous being here, right?

**Dr. Kazemi:** Yeah.

**Dr. Golshani:** I know that you're from the Foundation.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Which foundation?

**Dr. Golshani:** I think the colonel knows, too.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Hm. Are you from ORIA? 

**Dr. Golshani:** Yes, but it's not like I had any choice if I wanted permission to work here. This place is my passion, Shirin. I think there's something incredible about it. We know a lot about the Elamites, but the further north you go, the later the earliest historical records. Our only window into the life of the people who lived in this part of Iran four thousand years ago is right here, in a collection of names. I analyse them as best as I can. Would the Foundation be interested in my work?

**Dr. Kazemi:** I would, certainly.

**Dr. Golshani:** Well, the ORIA doesn't care much about ancient onomastics, so I think I can share a little with you. Things of purely academic interest.

**Dr. Kazemi:** What have you learned?

**Dr. Golshani:** Here's one thing. When Naram-Sin attacked, there were no soldiers here to face him.

**Dr. Kazemi:** How do you know?

**Dr. Golshani:** Many of the names here are professional names. Ewri-Šarruma the innkeeper, Urhi-Tešob the butcher, Puẖiya the weaver. But even men here who served in the war against Saddam never receive the name 'soldier'.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Surely that's because the Lullubi were a decentralised society, without a standing army. They must have relied on some sort of levy when war broke out.

**Dr. Golshani:** Elam had professional soldiers, and the Hurrians and Urartians had warrior aristocracies. There's no sign of either here. Maybe they did levy the farmers and the potters, but no-one would have stood a chance against the might of the Akkadian army. If there was a fight@@~~which, knowing the boasts of the Sargonic kings, there probably was~~@@it must have been a massacre.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Is that bitterness I hear in your voice?

**Dr. Golshani:** I don't deny it. In my heart, I feel for these people, even though they died so long ago. In a small way, the stele brings them back to life. Shirin, do you want to know? Do you want to find out your name?

The inscription on SCP-9719-1 depicts the Akkadian king Naram-Sin (c. 2255-2218 BCE), who is followed by a line of armoured spearmen, standing victorious over a mixture of naked and half-naked figures. *[Dr. Kazemi does not reply.]*

**Colonel Rohham:** Still being awkward, are we? 

**Dr. Kazemi:** I would like to leave.

**Colonel Rohham:** Doctor, I imagine that you have already formed some opinions about the work we're doing here. I suspect that they are affected by 'westoxification'. A fog has built up in your mind and muddles your thinking.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Thank you for your honest diagnosis.

**Colonel Rohham:** I am not trying to be clever with you. I'm just trying to be honest. Let me explain the situation that we find ourselves in: four thousand years ago, the king of Agade marched into this valley, slaughtered its people, and left behind a device to mark them forever as the humiliated victims of his empire. 

**Colonel Rohham:** He was not the last. Let's recall the twentieth century, when foreign diplomats carved up the Middle East with new borders and new names to keep us divided. They set up puppet kings, armed them to the teeth, and let them indulge their most evil desires, directing them both at their neighbours and their own populations. Divided and oppressed, we could do nothing to fight back against them. The British and the Russians split our country in two to suit their own interests. After the Second World War, when we tried to stand up for ourselves and take control of our own oil, the West... well, you already know that story.

**Colonel Rohham:** The Islamic Republic is the only government that has been able to stand up their influence. That's why they hate and fear us. They will keep attacking us on every front until we have the means to guarantee our own sovereignty. This project can bring an end to our oppression.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Do you honestly think you can weaponise the anomaly?

**Colonel Rohham:** 'Weaponise' is the wrong word. This is our deterrent. It's a shield against imperialism.

**Dr. Kazemi:** I don't see how changing peoples' names is going to save Iran from imperialism, colonel.

**Colonel Rohham:** Names are the anchor for the self. I cannot tell you more unless I am sure that you're on our side.

**Dr. Kazemi:** How am I supposed to feel enthusiasm for any of this when I'm being treated like a hostage?

**Colonel Rohham:** We are sequestering you for security reasons, but the way you choose to live here is entirely up to you. Look at Dr. Golshani, for example. She understands the privilege of this work. If you are not co-operative, life will be harder for you. If you help us, however, all sorts of doors will open up to you.

## **Log 9719-031**

**Personal Recording Device of Dr. Shirin Kazemi**

**Dr. Kazemi:** I don't, colonel.

**Colonel Rohham:** Psychological terror. 'Shock and awe', they call it. They strike at the people's minds, not just their bodies, with irresistible force. They try to break our resistance by overwhelming our senses and breaking our hope. But what if we could render that powerless?

*[Dr. Kazemi looks searchingly at Dr. Golshani, who smiles nervously back.]*

**Colonel Rohham:** Marzbani, report.

**Lt. Marzbani:** Telemetry looks good, sir. We're ready to receive the payload.

**Colonel Rohham:** Good. It's entering the workshop now. Prepare for anomalous effect.

## **Log 9719-032**

**Personal Recording Device of Dr. Shirin Kazemi**

*[Dr. Golshani is sitting on her bed, fiddling with her nails. Dr. Kazemi paces from the bathroom to the bedroom and back.]*

**Dr. Kazemi:** There's something I haven't been told, isn't there?

**Dr. Golshani:** Well, yes. Lots of things. What in particular are you thinking about?

**Dr. Kazemi:** I thought that this anomaly had been well-characterised. We know what it does, how its area of influence works, all of that. It just changes names. It shouldn't make any changes to our personalities. Earlier, it was like a switch was flipped when the casing came online.

**Dr. Golshani:** Okay, Shirin. I suppose you should know this. Some times ago, we wanted to check if there was any sort of technology inside the stele that might explain its effects. Metal, electronics, you know. So we did a CT scan and used ultrasonics to try and look inside, too. It's almost all solid sandstone, as you might expect from a stele, but there are two spherical voids of lower density within it.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Voids? What do you mean?

**Dr. Golshani:** They contain something that might be organic material. But the weird thing is that in the ultrasonics test, we could see that the two spheres were moving. They rotated around and tracked the movement of people nearby. We haven't been able to prove this, but I think these are the two eyes that Naram-Sin mentioned in the inscription.

**Dr. Kazemi:** *(Sitting down hard on her bed.)* God. So the protector is still alive?

**Dr. Golshani:** Its eyes are, at least. One of them must be the 'tender eye', which made the people of the valley obedient and docile. The other one would be the 'flaming eye', which took away the fear of those Lullubi and made them determined to protect their home. They ran right onto the spears of the veteran Akkadian army.

**Dr. Kazemi:** I don't like the sound of this. The casing is blocking the signal of the first eye and broadcasting the second, right? You literally just reminded us that this didn't save the Lullubi at all. How many would have died if they had just negotiated with Naram-Sin or if they had fled and hidden in the mountains? It's the same story across history! Fighting to the death doesn't win you wars. Thermopylae didn't stop Xerxes, Ariobarzanes didn't save Persepolis, and the US drove the Japanese out of the Pacific one island at a time.

**Dr. Golshani:** It forces the enemy to invest much more to destroy you. The calculus changes.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Because you're demanding they destroy you utterly. What kind of future would our country have after that?

**Dr. Golshani:** *(Sighing.)* I'm scared you might be right, but we've already made the plans to deploy relays that will allow this device to affect cities across Iran.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Please help me, Kasra. We have to convince them not to do this. It would be mass suicide.

**Dr. Golshani:** I'm sorry, Shirin, but it's too late. We just have to hope that the Colonel is right.

## **Log 9719-035**

**Personal Recording Device of Dr. Shirin Kazemi**

*[Colonel Rohham stands in the viewing gallery, finishing a speech. Dr. Golshani, Dr. Kazemi, and many of the facility's senior staff sit in metal chairs facing the window. The colonel raises his cup of tea in salute to his colleagues, who cheer.]*

**Colonel Rohham:** As the relays come online, the effects of our work will propagate out in stages. Kermanshah will be first, then Tehran, and then every other city in our nation. Our enemies' attacks will become utterly futile.

*[There are cries of 'Ya Hossein' from the audience.]*

**Colonel Rohham:** Every one of you has contributed to this project. You are the heroes of our country. You are keeping the child and his mother safe, the cleric and the youth, the grandparent and--

*[The emergency klaxon begins to blare. The facility staff stand and mutter amongst themselves. A soldier bursts in through the rear door.]*

**Soldier:** Colonel!

**Colonel Rohham:** What's our status?

**Soldier:** We're under attack, sir. The barracks have been hit by heavy ordnance. There's a hostile unit approaching the facility on foot.

**Colonel Rohham:** The Zionists are here. Everyone to defensive positions! I will take charge at once.

*[The rest of the officers hurry out the door. Before exiting the room, the colonel looks back towards Drs. Golshani and Kazemi.]*

**Colonel Rohham:** You will remain here. Protect the device with your lives if it comes to it. Understood?

**Dr. Golshani:** Yes, sir.

*[He turns to the four guards remaining in the room.]*

**Colonel Rohham:** Nobody enters the room with the artifact. You are our last line of defence.

**Guard:** We won't let you down, sir.

**Colonel Rohham:** I know you won't, brother. 

*[Moments after he departs, a tremor passes through the facility. Dr. Golshani grabs Dr. Kazemi's wrist.]*

**Dr. Golshani:** We could hide.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Kasra... can you tell me exactly where eyes are located inside the stele?

**Dr. Golshani:** The eyes? Oh. Yes. They're behind the symbols of Sin and Ishtar, above Naram-Sin's head. But--

**Dr. Kazemi:** Would you agree with me that the flaming eye is probably behind Ishtar's symbol?

**Dr. Golshani:** Well, it seems more probable than the other way around...

**Dr. Kazemi:** I need to get into the workroom. 

**Dr. Golshani:** Okay. Okay. I just need a moment.

*[She breathes out slowly.]*

**Dr. Golshani:** Okay.

*[They take the steel stairs down from the viewing gallery to the workroom door. Two of the guards are stationed in front of it.]*

**Dr. Golshani:** Excuse me.

**Guard:** Yes, doctor?

**Dr. Golshani:** We've noticed some calibration errors coming from the artifact. We think that the tremors might have dislodged the interface. One of us needs to go in to manually realign it.

*[The guard taps on his radio.]*

**Guard:** Colonel, come in.

*[He listens to the static for a few seconds.]*

**Guard:** Colonel, we have a query at the laboratory.

*[There is only static.]*

**Guard:** Damn things barely work down here. Okay, but do it quickly. The enemy could be here any second.

*[Dr. Golshani nods. The guard opens the door and allows Dr. Kazemi to enter while Dr. Golshani returns to the viewing gallery.]*

*[Dr. Kazemi steps into the workroom. The door is sealed behind her. She stands staring at the stele for a moment, balling her hands into fists. Dr. Golshani has reached the viewing gallery and begins communicating via the PA.]*

**Dr. Golshani:** I'm here.

**Dr. Kazemi:** Good. I'm glad.

**Dr. Golshani:** Is the anomaly affecting you?

**Dr. Ahmadi:** You'll have to tell me. I feel a bit strange. I'm Leyla Ahmadi, right? Is that my name?

**Dr. Golshani:** For now, yes.

**Dr. Ahmadi:** So I'm a martyr now. I don't feel like much of a hero.

**Dr. Golshani:** There's a rotary hammer drill on the trolley to your left. They used it to create the sockets in the casing for the steel bolts.

*[Dr. Ahmadi retrieves the drill and steps towards the casing.]*

**Dr. Golshani:** Shirin... Leyla?

**Dr. Ahmadi:** Yes?

**Dr. Golshani:** I'm glad to be serving my country with you.

**Dr. Ahmadi:** Likewise.

**Dr. Golshani:** The symbol of Ishtar is below the word 'وطن'Farsi: *vatan*, "homeland". on the casing.

*[Dr. Ahmadi reaches up to touch the word; she cannot reach it comfortably. She goes to the side of the room and comes back with a crate. Standing upon it, she begins to drill into the casing. The force of the drill's motion almost knocks her off the crate, but she is able to recover.]*

**Dr. Golshani:** I was just looking up your namesake, Leyla. She was a nuclear physicist at Imam Hussein University. They got her with an airstrike last year.

**Dr. Ahmadi:** *(Shouting over the sound of the drill.)* I'll try not to let them kill me again.

**Dr. Golshani:** I'll keep an eye out for you.

*[As Dr. Ahmadi drills, her body suddenly seizes and she falls backwards off the crate.]*

**Dr. Golshani:** Shirin! What happened?

*[Dr. Ahmadi gets slowly back up onto her feet, grimacing with pain.]*

**Dr. Ahmadi:** I'm alright. I think I'm through the casing.

**Dr. Golshani:** What's your name?

**Innasḫira:** Innasḫira of Agade.

**Dr. Golshani:** Okay, yes, you must be at Naram-Sin's stele. Let's make a hole in this irreplaceable piece of our national heritage.

 **Innasḫira:** On it.

*[The drill bit is still embedded deep into the stone. Innasḫira climbs back up onto the crate to resume her drilling.]*

**Dr. Golshani:** Wait! The colonel is coming back!

*[The door slides open. Colonel Rohham, bleeding from his face and ear and with his uniform covered in patches of dust and ash, enters the room. He is flanked by two guards.]*

**Colonel Rohham:** What are you doing, doctor?

*[Innasḫira does not turn around or reply.]*

**Colonel Rohham:** Dr. Golshani. Talk to her. Stop her!

**Dr. Golshani:** Sorry, sir. I couldn't stop her even if I wanted to.

*[The colonel draws his pistol and aims at the back of Innasḫira's head.]*

**Colonel Rohham:** I don't want to do this, but you are a traitor to your country.

*[The door explodes inwards. An IDF Special Forces team rushes in, neutralising the two IRGC guards and apprehending the stunned Colonel.]*

**Operator #1:** Target three secured!

**Operator #2:** *(Sweeping his weapon towards the stele.)* Targets one and two located!

*[The Liaison, a man wearing a full-face visor and a long coat with a Foundation logo enters the room from the smoke-filled hallway. He regards the scene intently. Two more operators break into the viewing gallery and apprehend Dr. Golshani. The Liaison addresses Innasḫira.]*

**Liaison:** Doctor. It's good to see you well. I'm Foundation, too. We're here. You're safe. You can stop now.

*[Innasḫira continues drilling.]*

**Liaison:** We have a containment team on its way down into the facility. Don't worry. We'll take it from here.

*[Innasḫira does not reply.]*

**Liaison:** Doctor, I understand what you're trying to do, but if you damage the anomaly, we lose everything we could learn from it. It has incredible research value. You are a capable scientist. You could lead the team assigned to it!

*[Innasḫira turns to face the Liaison.]*

**Innasḫira:** I am a scientist, yes. I'm a Doctor of the Foundation. But I'm other things too.

*[The drill lurches forward. A trickle of blood emerges from the stele where the drill bit entered, and drips down the handle of the drill, and along Innasḫira's arm, constantly increasing in flow. The trickle becomes a stream, and it begins saturating Innasḫira's clothes and boots and pooling on the crate and floor around her.]*

**Innasḫira?:** [UNINTELLIGIBLE MIXTURE OF LANGUAGES]

*[Every person in the room slumps down, unconscious.]*

## **Interview Log**

**Interviewer:** Dr. Yael Sommer, senior psychiatrist

**Interviewed:** Subject 9719-1

**Dr. Sommer:** You can sit down, Dr. Kazemi.

*[Subject continues to stare into the mirror.]*

**Dr. Sommer:** Shirin?

**Subject:** You keep calling me that.

**Dr. Sommer:** It's your name, dear.

**Subject:** There are thousands of names pent up inside me. I feel like a pressure cooker. 

**Dr. Sommer:** Are you fighting the names?

**Subject:** Not really. Can I see Dr. Golshani?

**Dr. Sommer:** I'm afraid not. She's still in custody. She asked after you, but of course, we can't tell her anything.

**Subject:** Is she being co-operative?

**Dr. Sommer:** As I understand it, yes. Perhaps we'll be able to find a place for her.

**Subject:** She deserves better.

**Dr. Sommer:** We've made a note of your recommendation.

**Subject:** Good. Did you see my request?

**Dr. Sommer:** Yes, I did. Unfortunately, we can't approve the use of amnestics until we have a clearer idea of what's happened to you. This doesn't seem to be a purely memetic, or even memory-related, phenomenon.

**Subject:** And they want to learn what I know about the entity.

**Dr. Sommer:** You should co-operate with them. It would make them more likely to grant your requests.

**Subject:** Of course you'd say that.

*[Subject turns back to the mirror.]*

**Dr. Sommer:** Can I ask you something, off the record?

**Subject:** Nothing here is off the record.

**Dr. Sommer:** Very well, for the record. Why did you disobey your superior and damage the anomaly? What was it that you put ahead of your service to the Foundation?

**Subject:** I had the means and opportunity to neutralise a threat.

**Dr. Sommer:** Don't make this situation more difficult than it needs to be.

**Subject:** Your liaison came in with the same speech that Colonel Rohham had given us. We need to use this anomaly to defend our country, or to study the anomalous, or whatever. Join us and doors will open to you. You can be important if you help us achieve our goals. It wasn't right. Something was missing.

**Dr. Sommer:** Oh?

**Subject:** I kept thinking about the way that the people of that valley have lived under dozens of empires, being stripped of their names and languages and culture over and over again. Their god gave them a little protection, but the empire of Naram-Sin was too much for it. Its protection was turned into their humiliation.

**Dr. Sommer:** And you wanted to protect them?

**Subject:** It's more than that. That story has played out all over the world. We've been the empire in that story, and we've been the victims of empire, too. We were knocked down many times, and we always managed to get back up and resume where we left off. The humiliation of being beaten seems to make us crueler when it's our turn to do the same.

**Dr. Sommer:** Well, doctor, I am afraid that your actions have not ended the cycles of history.

**Subject:** I just wanted to take away a way to be cruel. That artifact itself is cruel. The entity watched over the valley for thousands of years until a prideful man decided he wanted to dismember its body to make a point. That meant four millennia of being stuck inside the stone, under constant torture, seeing its home suffer and change without it. I thought that if I killed it, I could give it peace.

**Dr. Sommer:** And did you kill it?

*[Subject touches her reflection in the mirror with two fingers.]*

**Subject:** I don't think it was the kind of thing that dies.
