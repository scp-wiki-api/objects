**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9732 is currently stored in standard anomalous items storage at Site-17, Locker 9732-A. Because of its declassified nature, employees at Site-17 are permitted to requisition SCP-9732 for rhetoric practice, debate preparation, or psychological stress testing upon request of the current Level 3 Research Director.

Personnel who have any contact with the object must create a strict time limit (no more than two hours), using a clock since those affected by the object will not give up the argument on their own. SCP-9732 is never to be used at any administrative planning or ethics committee meetings.

**Description:** SCP-9732 is an antique apothecary scale constructed primarily from tarnished brass and copper, which have oxidized to a tealish-brown color. SCP-9732 is 32 centimeters tall. There are no manufacturer labels, serial numbers, or brandings on SCP-9732. However, metallurgical analysis places its construction in mid-19th century London.

The anomalous properties of SCP-9732 begin once a human comes into contact with the scale around its central fulcrum. Once in contact, the individual becomes an infallible and mandatory “devil’s advocate.” Anytime a person within 5 meters of the individual expresses a definitive statement, the individual must argue, passionately and logically, against the very same statement. This process takes place regardless of whether or not the statement is universally accepted, the individual’s personal belief in it, and the detrimental effects that the opposition of the statement may have on the individual personally.

In such discussions, the physical pans of SCP-9732 will tilt depending on the “weight” or persuasive power of the arguments made inside the room. The subject retains all their prior knowledge, memories, and vocabulary, weaponizing their own expertise to defend their position. The compulsion anomaly in question will end instantly once SCP-9732 is separated from the possession of the subject in question, leaving them confused or embarrassed about their previous arguments.

**Addendum 9732.1:** Recovery Log

SCP-9732 was retrieved on 14 October 2016 from the estate sale of Arthur Pendleton, a well-known defense lawyer from Boston, Massachusetts. Following Pendleton's death, members of his family attempted to sell his office furniture through an auction. The auctioneer inadvertently activated SCP-9732 while giving a description of a set of old books. This led to a three-hour argument between the auctioneer and other participants during which the auctioneer described reading as a “plague upon the human intuition.” All involved participants were given class A amnestics.

**Addendum 9732.2:** Experiment Log 9732-A

> **Lead Researcher:** Dr. Demi N. Shen

> **Subject:** Junior Researcher Hopper

>

> **[BEGIN LOG]**

>

>

> **Lead Researcher:** Dr. Demi N. Shen

> **Subject:** Dr. Aris Thorne (Site-17 Lead IT Developer)

>

> **[BEGIN LOG]**

>

> **Dr. Shen:** "Python is a highly efficient language for directory automation and metadata parsing."

The subject justified the need to use raw Assembly language and manually edit codes in hex to sort computer files on the grounds that the Global Interpreter Lock in Python and its memory usage is “a crutch for the lazy,” which has disconnected modern programmers from the beauty of hardware.

> **Subject:** Junior Researcher Calsane

>

> **[BEGIN LOG]**

>

> **Dr. Shen:** "When performing logarithmic differentiation, dropping a negative sign makes the entire derivative mathematically incorrect."

>

Jr. Researcher Calsane then delivered a forty-five minute filibuster stating that the missing negative sign was not a mistake, but rather a brilliant theoretical negation of the growth rate, which supposedly leads to entirely new and better paths of mathematics. The right hand of SCP-9732 hit the table as a consequence of the passion and loudness of the lecture.

**Addendum 9732.2:** Experiment Log 9732-A

> **Date:** 18 June 2019

> **Lead Researcher:** Dr. Demi N. Shen

> **Subject:** D-8432 (Subject instructed to hold the object and wait for a prompt)

>

> **[BEGIN LOG]**

>

> **Dr. Shen:** "You are currently holding a brass scale."

>

> **Subject Response:** D-8432 suddenly stopped and became engrossed in the scale he held in his hand, immediately getting into an intense debate about the meaning of the word "holding." The subject argued that, on the subatomic level, the electrostatic force of repulsion prevents any kind of interaction between human skin and solid objects, so the hand does not touch the brass but just hovers above it, so it is holding nothing. The subject moved on to discussing the problem in terms of the Ship of Theseus paradox.

>

> **[END LOG]**

**Addendum 9732.2:** Experiment Log 9732-A

> **Date:** 10 January 2022

> **Lead Researcher:** Dr. Demi N. Shen

> **Subject:** Site Director Thomas Graham

>

> **[BEGIN LOG]**

>

> **Dr. Shen:** "The Foundation contains anomalies to protect humanity."

>

> **Subject Response:** Site Director Thomas Graham took two hours and twelve minutes to thoroughly dissect the mission statement of the Foundation. The subject argued that “humanity” is an evolutionary standstill and stated that the Foundation, by being a repository of anomalies, is impeding the natural and chaotic evolution of the universe. The subject called for the release of all entities of Keter classification to “stimulate global character development.”

> **Note:** Testing was halted and SCP-9732 was forcibly removed from Director Graham's hand after he reached for his security override keycard. Director Graham approved the item's Safe-class designation and declassification the following morning, citing a "fascinating, albeit terrifying, mental clarity."
