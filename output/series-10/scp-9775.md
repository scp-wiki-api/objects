**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9775 spores, except those slated for preservation, are contained within Baseline Cold Storage Unit (bCSU) 013-C in Site-103. The SCP-9775-A research project is currently housed within Variable-Environ Isolated Containment (VEIC) greenhouse 0420-B in Site-103, which has been modified as follows:

* Addition of adjoint laboratory space.

* Hermetic sealing across the VEIC-laboratory complex.

* Implementation of acroamatic autoclavation functionality.

Prior to personnel evacuation from, acroamatic abatement of, or introduction of new SCP-9775-A candidates to VEIC 0420-B, all SCP-9775 spores must be collected, counted, verified, and returned to bCSU 013-C. Furthermore, to reduce risk of cross-contact, VEIC 0420-B's sterilization cycle must be run between introductions of anomalous SCP-9775-A candidates.

VEIC 0420-B is currently configured to model a tropical moist broadleaf forest habitat, as found in the Guayanan Highlands. Testing of SCP-9775-A candidates must continue until SCP-9775-A is found. Under no circumstances may SCP-9775 be permitted extinction.

Immunodeficient or hypothermic staff must avoid proximity to SCP-9775 at all times.

**Description:** SCP-9775 is the fungus *Sclerocystis perobscura**Eukaryota Fungi Mucoromycota Glomeromycetes Glomerales Sclerocystaceae Sclerocystis perobscura* (formerly *Glomus perobscurum**Eukaryota Fungi Glomeromycota Glomeromycetes Glomerales Glomeraceae Glomus perobscurum*), a species unknown to the superveil scientific community. SCP-9775 is an obligate-symbiont arbuscular mycorrhizal fungus with close phylogenetic relation to other such fungi,Especially the *Sclerocystis* type-species, *Sclerocystis coremioides* with its plant symbionts collectively designated SCP-9775-A. SCP-9775 is hypothesized to produce a novel antimemetic compound, designated SCP-9775-B, when in mycorrhizal mutualism. While the effect of this compound in nature is mild, concentrated solutions are able to increase its effect to human scales. Preliminary research on SCP-9775-B has revealed multiple promising potential uses of the compound, including antimemetic masking of Foundation assets or use as a new Class A "General Retrograde" Amnestic.

No instances of SCP-9775-A are currently known to the Foundation, either in the wild or in captivity. As a result, every attempt at germinating SCP-9775 has failed, and the species is considered extinct in the wild.

## Addendum 9775.1

Discovery

SCP-9775 was initially discovered in 1946 in land now constituting Saikai National Park in Japan. In the wake of the atomic bombing of Nagasaki, Foundation agents were deployed to the area in order to ensure that the event had not precipitated the creation of additional anomalous objects.

A previously-unknown antimemetic effect was discovered by Researcher Douglas Helmann, having been prescribed a taper dose of mnestics, during a game of "Eye Spy" while on a morning walk with Agent Akira Locke. Following his field report, a team was sent out to perform further analysis. SCP-9775 spores, still carrying a weak antimemetic effect from residiual SCP-9775-B, were recovered from the soil through wet-sieving and decanting before being brought back to Site-19 for controlled testing.

At the time of discovery, mycorrhizal fungi were still poorly understood. Lack of foresight prevented the isolation or recovery of any instances of SCP-9775-A, leading to a complete failure of spore germination in the lab. The project was eventually abandoned and the spores consigned to cold storage, until their recent rediscovery during a routine storage audit.

## Addendum 9775.2

Unbalanced Exchange Hypothesis

Analysis of a soil sample sent alongside the spores was able to provide insight into the evolutionary benefit of SCP-9775-B and its mechanism of operation.

Current consensus among the SCP-9775 research team holds that SCP-9775-B was evolved in order to facilitate mycorrhizal parasitism in the form of trade extortion on the part of SCP-9775. Mycorrhizal fungi typically engage in trade with their host, providing soil nutrients in exchange for energy and carbon. Introduction of SCP-9775-B to existing mycorrhizal mutualisms, however, disrupts this pattern. Uptake by either side disrupts their ability to track trades, resulting in oversupply of their respective commodity. It is hypothesized that SCP-9775-B is a secondary metabolite, produced and selectively applied by SCP-9775 in order to extract more nutrients and energy from SCP-9775-A than it would otherwise be able to, in effect facilitating a transition from mutualism to biotrophic parasitism.

Due to SCP-9775-B's ability to affect all three major eukaryotic kingdoms of life (animal, plant, and fungi), the mechanism through which it operates is currently thought to be abstract in nature, acting on the concept of information and entropy rather than any specific information-storing medium.

## Addendum 9775.3

Every new recruit asks the question at some point. Why do we contain? If these objects must not be left to the public's eye, would it not be safer and cheaper to destroy them outright? Don't the Global Occult Coalition have a point?

By now, you all have heard countless examples of an anomaly neutralized, only to come back twice as dangerous. I won't waste your time regaling you with more of the same. What I am trying to convey, here, is something a lot more subtle, that will hopefully convince the stragglers among you. It is for that reason I petitioned for the declassification of this file, and encouraged the team to go ahead with Rhizoquest: to show you the other side of containment, what I see as one of the Foundation's most noble persuits.

The loss of a species is something inconcievably vast. Millions of years of specialization, an entirely unique kind of being, unlike any other, robbed of its future. Every life it would have lived, every one of its marvels to behold, all the good it could have done...

It saddens me to even think about.

Despite this, however, you would be hard-pressed to find anybody willing to fight to rescue a fungus from the brink. Some conservationists talk of the unloved animals, who quietly pass on away from the public eye, as if the problem was unique. As if any species deserves to live over any other, just because we see ourselves more in them.

It's evident here more than anywhere: containment is the urge to preserve, not to sequester. This desire to rescue, to hold and nurture and ensure survival: that is what separates us from the GOC. They are the hunters and poachers of the vulnerable behind the veil, but we are the zoos and nature preserves. Without the Foundation, *Sclerocystis perobscura* and many, many others would have died in the dark. One day, by our efforts, they will once again live in the light.

Metaphorically speaking, of course. It lives in the dirt.

Dr. Alexander Penelope Handsdirty

Director of Parabiological Sciences

## Addendum 9775.6

Early-Stage SCP-9775 Symptoms

The early stage of an SCP-9775 infection is defined as the period between spore germination and first sporulation. As such, early stage SCP-9775 is localized (due to inability to metastatize) and primarily marked by accumulation. The following symptoms were witnessed by SCP-9775-A-1/1:

* Fever.Due to the only example of SCP-9775 infection starting on an extremity, it is unknown whether elevated body temperatures would, in fact, kill or otherwise halt SCP-9775.

* Chronic pruritus.Itchiness.

* Inflammation of infection site.

* Painful rash.

* Skin lesions.

* Increased appetite.

* Minor atrophy of non-infected areas of the body.

* Exhaustion.

## Addendum 9775.8

VEIC 0420-B Revised Biosafety Protocol

On 2026-03-09, the VEIC 0420-B Biosafety Protocol was revised by VEIC Oversight in order to provide more comprehensive warning of the dangers posed to containment personnel. The following notice must be posted to all enterances to VEIC 0420-B and its attached lab:

# Biological Hazard

## "Geas"-Class Personnel Danger

Full-body PPE (including N95 respirator) required for entry.

Decontamination required for egress.

**Immunocompromised and hypothermic individuals may not enter.**

Enterance exposes you to poorly-understood **parabiological and antimemetic hazards**.

**Caution saves lives.**

| Advisory Contact | Researcher Violet Noma-Anradh |
| --- | --- |
| Emergency Contact | Liason Demetrius Khan |
**Lab under quarantine. Do not enter without express authorization.**

In addition, novel SCP-9775 activity has necessitated greater measures in protecting research staff and preventing containment breach. The following articles have been added to the full procedure manual, available in the VEIC 0420-B attached lab or upon request:

##### Entry Restriction:

Access to {{Premises}}, including by members of the SCP-9775 research team, is restricted to those expressly cleared for entry. All entry clearances given prior to 2026-03-09 are invalid. Solely the following individuals are permitted enterance:

* Researcher Violet Noma-Anradh

{{Premises}} has, additionally, been placed under biological quarantine. Entry or exit, including by janitorial staff or in the case of emergency, is expressly prohibited. Removal of any parabiological matter from {{Premises}}, without exception, constitutes a containment breach.

##### Biological Materials Handling:

Spore retention policies instituted by the SCP-9775 containment procedures have proved infeasible, necessitating spore control through biological quarantine. {{Premises}} is assumed to carry enough biohazardous material to pose a risk of to any connected biospheres.

The total breadth of SCP-9775 infection vectors is currently unknown. Respiratory isolation and full-body PPE are currently highly recommended in order to prevent inadvertant infection through hyponychial contact, respiratory inhalation, bodily fluid transfer, or prolonged dermal contact.

Due to the risks involved in regular crossing of biological material from {{Premises}} to the wider site, a working supply of SCP-9775 spores may be kept within {{Premises}} itself.

##### Biohazardous Residue:

Biohazardous residue has been discovered in and around {{Premises}}, consisting of high quantities of SCP-9775 spores suspended in deoxygenated blood. Any such residue discovered must be immediately cleaned and disinfected, no matter its size, age, or origin.

Out of an abundance of precaution, all unknown biological matter must be destroyed.

## Addendum 9775.11

Late-Stage SCP-9775 Symptoms

SCP-9775 infection commences its late-stage by the production of dermal fruiting bodies. Commonly identified as mushrooms, these structures result in countless sensitive bumps across the skin of the infected. Physical trauma to these areas, including as mild as itching, scratching, or bumping, can cause a skin rupture, taking a mix of spores and blood up to the surface.

Around the time of this development, multiple other symptoms become apparent due to the maturation of the SCP-9775 mycelia within a host. The most notable among them include the following, extrapolated from SCP-9775-A-1/1:

* Widespread papula across skin of infected areas due to growth of fruiting bodies.

* Occasional thick, bulbous "hairs" of chained fruiting bodies emerging from infected areas.

* Amelioration of early-stage symptoms due to amnestic production preventing proper immune function.

* Short-term memory loss, particularly exacerbated by manual articulation of infected areas.

* Intense dissociation from infected area.

* Convulsion and numbness in infected areas.

* Discontinuous spread of infection sites due to haematogenous metastasis.

Due to SCP-9775's ability to metastatize through haematogenous spore dispersal, late-stage SCP-9775 infection is thought to be incurable.

## Addendum 9775.12

*Urtica flectens* (Homo Group) 'Bleeding Violet'

On 2026-05-11, the *Urtica flectens* specimen provided to the SCP-9775 research team by Site-103 reached its mature phase of development, and began to flower. As the specimen had been predominantly inoculated by SCP-9775-A-1/1 genetic material, and appears to exsanguinate upon contact, the resulting strain was named the "Bleeding Violet".

The "Bleeding Violet" nettle flowers through the formation of small, human arm-like appendages, which branch from the stem such that palm and fingertip equivalents point towards the plant's primary source of light during growth. As these structures prove to be viable hosts to SCP-9775 under hyponychial spore application, the "Bleeding Violet" has been designated SCP-9775-A-2.

Since differentiated *Urtica flectens* do not produce seeds, all individuals of SCP-9775-A-2 are budded clones. The only known successful method of budding involves the amputation of one flowering structure, which may then be planted in soil. The resulting SCP-9775-A-2 instance will skip its undifferentiated growth stage during development, resulting in another viable host for SCP-9775 within approximately one month.

Additional research on SCP-9775-A-2 and the extraction of SCP-9775-B is currently ongoing.
