SPC-9798 is to be confined to the property of SCP-9798-A located at 16 ████████ ██████. At least one caregiver should be present at all times within the bounds of the property. Access to the property and interactions with SCP-9798-A are discouraged, but those with sufficient clearance levels may access the property and the SCP

**Addendum-01:** SCP-9798-B is to be contained in the same way as SCP-9798-A on the same property until a more permanent containment procedure can be devised.

##### OUTDATED

08/08/20██ - **Updated Containment**

SCP-9798-A and SCP-9798-B have been relocated to local Site 63 (GREENSTONE) for tighter temporary containment on account of a  SCP-9798-C event that occurred at 16 ████████ ██████ on the morning of 08/08/20██. SCP-9798-A and SCP-9798-B have been placed in separate standard containment cells with two Scranton Reality Anchors (SRA) placed diagonally from each other inside the cells. Any and all human interaction with SCP-9798-A or -B is strictly forbidden for personnel below a clearance level of 4. Two ETTRA guards armed with standard-issue rifles will be posted out of the holding room entrance at all times. Additional SRA's will be placed around Site 63 to prevent spread and containment breach.

All material removed from SCP-9798 since the time of its discovery is to be kept within the same containment cell as -B. 

12/08/20██ - **Updated Containment**

SCP-9798-A and -B have been moved to a purpose-built but still temporary containment cell on sub-basement level 2 of Site 63. The cell has been locally isolated from reality through the temporary modifications of a series of SRA’s. Only Dr. Wessels and Dr. Mavuso are permitted to interact with what has been dubbed a Scranton Reality Isolator (SRI)  (see Addendum I.1).  Only ETTRA and designated personnel may enter sub-basement level 2. No personnel may enter the field of the SRI or the containment cell for risk of destabilising the field and accelerating the event. Any interactions with the SCP are to be done through remotely operated equipment as electromagnetic waves can still penetrate the field. 

It is important to note that the containment field is not a perfect solution and the event is still actively spreading. 

16/08/20██ - **Expanded Containment**

Due to the power and security failure on the 15th, the event has spread beyond the main Site 63 building and onto the surrounding property, leading to a civilian at a nearby agricultural facility experiencing an  SCP-9798-C event as well as multiple onsite staff and D-class personnel (see Addendum B.2). 

ETTRA has therefore established a wider protection perimeter. Attached is a visual depiction of the widened perimeter and evacuation zone. Personnel not under direct ETTRA command are forbidden from entering this wider zone. Due to the D-Class riot caused by the power outage, basement level 1 is on lockdown for the foreseeable future.

All other containment procedures specified in Addendum-02 remain in place. 

Due to the events of August 15th, ██████ ██████████ has been replaced by Dr. Alan Wessels as site director.

SCP-9798-A has agreed to undergo surgery to expose the internal contents of his skull in the hopes of isolating the LDA. He has been moved to the medical wing, which has been isolated by a second SRI array. All containment procedures stated before remain in place. The surgery will be performed remotely by Dr. Ngluvo on the 18th. 

SCP-9798-B is to remain in the containment cell on sub-basement level 2 for the duration of the surgery. 

All non-vital staff should remain off property in case of another mass-C event.

- Dir. Wessels

#### Description:

 

---

SCP-9798 is a localized dimensional anomaly (LDA) located within the cranial cavity of a human male designated SCP-9798-ASCP-9798-A is a man of SiSwati ethnicity, 65 years of age and is 170cm tall. Besides the anomaly, he is otherwise unremarkable in appearance with standard features for a man of his age and ethnicity..

MRI scans indicate that the interior of SCP-9798-A’s skull contains no brain tissue, instead appearing as an empty void terminating at the spinal cord. Despite the lack of brain SCP-9798-A does not appear to be lacking in any cognitive function, able to engage fully in conversation, problem solving and motor skills that would be standard for a man of his age and education. ~~Any attempts to gain access to the inside of SCP-9798-A’s skull by means of surgery have been vehemently objected to by SCP-9798-A.~~ SCP-9798-A has since agreed to a surgery in which access to the contents of his skull will be accessed. 

The anomaly manifests externally as a blue luminescent disc approximately 20 cm in diameter. This disc acts as a gateway into the LDA. It appears above SCP-9798-A’s forehead while the subject is asleep and believed to be dreaming. A localized, 20 cm magnetic field surrounds the disc. Any matter entering this field is instantaneously transferred into the LDA. An identical disc will be present within the LDA and will close upon SCP-9798-A waking up.

The most unique feature about the LDA is its ontology.Ontology in this case refers to the physical makeup of matter and the forces that govern them.. Baseline reality's ontological structure is particle-based, whereas the LDA is 'string’-based. These strings vibrate at different frequencies  The frequencies are measured in Wessels (Ws), named after Alan Wessels. The confirmed range in frequency is 1-94W, corresponding with the 94 naturally occurring elements found on Earth. Strings can change frequency in ways similar to that of particle elements, with the use of strong electro-magnetic frequencies. Elements in this form are far less rigid than atoms, as changing between elements is not as catastrophic or energy-intense as nuclear fission or fusion. , and the frequencies at which they vibrate determine their physical properties in the same way electrons and protons do of elements in baseline reality. These strings can be 'woven' together to form larger and more complex structures resembling molecules.

      ctx.stroke();

        var nxOff = Math.sin(((ny-y0)/(y1-y0))*Math.PI*2*2.5 + elapsed*0.0014 + vn*1.3 + Math.PI*0.5) * mW*0.020;

        var bx = nx + nxOff;

        var by = ny + nyOff;

        var gc = ctx.createRadialGradient(bx,by,0,bx,by,nodeR*2.2);

        ctx.fillStyle = colAlpha(hEl.color,0.95);

        ctx.beginPath(); ctx.arc(ex,hy2+endOff,dotR,0,Math.PI*2); ctx.fill();

      }

    });

    // End-cap nodes on vertical strings

    [y0, y1].forEach(function(ey) {

      for (var vn2 = 0; vn2 < vC; vn2++) {

        var vx3 = vXs[vn2];

        var tEnd2 = ey === y0 ? 0 : 1;

        var ampV2 = mW*0.020;

        var endOffV = Math.sin(tEnd2*Math.PI*2*2.5 + elapsed*0.0014 + vn2*1.3 + Math.PI*0.5) * ampV2;

        var g3 = ctx.createRadialGradient(vx3+endOffV,ey,0,vx3+endOffV,ey,dotR*2);

        g3.addColorStop(1,colAlpha(vEl.color,0));

        ctx.fillStyle = g3;

        ctx.beginPath(); ctx.arc(vx3+endOffV,ey,dotR*2,0,Math.PI*2); ctx.fill();

        ctx.fillStyle = colAlpha(vEl.color,0.95);

        ctx.beginPath(); ctx.arc(vx3+endOffV,ey,dotR,0,Math.PI*2); ctx.fill();

      }

    });

    ctx.restore();

    // Labels

    var fs = Math.round(W*0.016);

    txt(mol.name,  cx, cy - mH*0.5 - fs*2.2, fs*1.1, COL.lBright, alpha*0.95, true);

    txt(mol.label, cx, cy - mH*0.5 - fs*0.9, fs*0.72, COL.lDim,   alpha*0.6,  false);

    // Element key below mesh

    var keyX1 = cx - mW*0.18;

    var keyX2 = cx + mW*0.18;

    // hElem key

    ctx.save();

    ctx.globalAlpha = alpha * 0.85;

    ctx.fillStyle = colAlpha(hEl.color, 0.9);

    ctx.beginPath(); ctx.arc(keyX1, keyY, dotR, 0, Math.PI*2); ctx.fill();

    ctx.restore();

    txt(hEl.symbol+' — Ws:'+hEl.ws, keyX1 + mW*0.10, keyY, fs*0.72, COL.lDim, alpha*0.7, false);

    // vElem key

    ctx.save();

    ctx.globalAlpha = alpha * 0.85;

    ctx.fillStyle = colAlpha(vEl.color, 0.9);

    ctx.beginPath(); ctx.arc(keyX2 - mW*0.20, keyY, dotR, 0, Math.PI*2); ctx.fill();

    ctx.restore();

    var o1rx=H*0.24, o1ry=H*0.09;

    var o2rx=H*0.18, o2ry=H*0.25;

    var o3rx=H*0.27, o3ry=H*0.12;

    // atom

    drawOrbit(lCX,mCY,o2rx,o2ry,Math.PI/3);

    drawOrbit(lCX,mCY,o3rx,o3ry,-Math.PI/4);

    drawElectron(lCX,mCY,o1rx,o1ry,0,          spd*1.0);

    drawElectron(lCX,mCY,o2rx,o2ry,Math.PI/3,  spd*0.75);

    drawElectron(lCX,mCY,o3rx,o3ry,-Math.PI/4, spd*1.25);

    txt('Baseline Reality',  lCX, H*0.08+fs*1.6, fs*0.8, COL.lDim, 0.6, false);

    txt('Lithium Atom', lCX, H*0.93, fs*0.8, COL.lDim, 0.45, false);

    // cycling molecules

    var totalCycle = CYCLE_DURATION + FADE_DURATION;

    var molCount   = MOLECULES.length;

    var period     = totalCycle * molCount;

    var tMod       = elapsed % period;

    var molIdx     = Math.floor(tMod / totalCycle);

    var molT       = tMod - molIdx * totalCycle;

      curAlpha = 1;

    } else {

      var f = (molT - CYCLE_DURATION) / FADE_DURATION;

      curAlpha = 1 - f;

      nxtAlpha = f;

    }

    drawMolecule(MOLECULES[molIdx % molCount],             rCX, mCY, mW, mH, elapsed, curAlpha);

    if (nxtAlpha > 0) {

      drawMolecule(MOLECULES[(molIdx+1) % molCount],       rCX, mCY, mW, mH, elapsed, nxtAlpha);

    }

    txt('STRING ONTOLOGY',       rCX, H*0.08,        fs,     COL.string, 0.9,  true);

    txt('', rCX, H*0.08+fs*1.6, fs*0.8, COL.lDim,  0.6,  false);

    // centre divider

    ctx.setLineDash([6,4]);

    ctx.beginPath(); ctx.moveTo(W*0.5,H*0.06); ctx.lineTo(W*0.5,H*0.94); ctx.stroke();

    ctx.setLineDash([]);

    ctx.restore();

    txt('vs', W*0.5, mCY, fs*1.4, COL.lDim, 0.45, false);

      else if(!e.isIntersecting&&raf){cancelAnimationFrame(raf);raf=null;}

    });

  },{threshold:0.1});

  observer.observe(canvas);

ADDENDUM A.1 — SCP-9798-B INITIAL CONTACT INTERVIEW

INTERVIEW LOG • AUDIO

SCP-9798-B -B appears as a human male in his late 30s, standing at approximately 213cm with an appearance resembling a vague Mediterranean or west Asian ethnicity. Featuring a large nose, black hair, brown eyes and a tanned skin tone. When first encountered the entity referred to itself as "the knight." It wears a suit of armour, resembling styles of armour worn in 15th century Iberia. is a character from the Evergreen pentalogy known simply as the Knight. It is native to SCP-9798 and followed researchers Ndlovu and White through the gateway back into baseline reality, with no stated ill intention and has remained cooperative. It is noticeably more lucid than most other creatures from the LDA. 

Interview between SCP-9798-B and Dr. Breytenbach, conducted approximately three hours after SCP-9798-B followed Doctors Ngluvo and White through the LDA Gateway. Subject displayed no visible injuries.

Interview Log — SCP-9798-B Initial Containment Interview

**Interviewed:** SCP-9798-B  

**Interviewer:** Dr. Breytenbach  

**Location:** 16 ████████ ██████

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Hello. You can understand me, yes?

**SCP-9798-B:** Yes.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Good. I am Doctor Breytenbach. I would like to ask you some questions if you are willing.

**SCP-9798-B:** I am willing, Doctor.

<3 seconds silence>

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Can you tell me your name?

**SCP-9798-B:** You can call me the Knight.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Is that a title or a name?

**SCP-9798-B:** Both in this case.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Do you know where you are?

**SCP-9798-B:** Not precisely. Your world. A room in your world. That is enough for now.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Does that not concern you? Not knowing where you are?

**SCP-9798-B:** I have known where I was for a very long time. It concerned me far more.

<4 seconds silence>

**Dr. Breytenbach:** You followed two of my researchers through a dimensional opening this morning. Why?

**SCP-9798-B:** Because they moved.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Can you elaborate on that?

**SCP-9798-B:** In my world… things do not move forward. The same day happens again and again. The same conversations. The same orders. The same sunrise. The same sunset. No battle begins. No battle ends. Your researchers walked into my world and walked back out of it. That had never happened before.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** So you followed them on impulse.

**SCP-9798-B:** (quietly) No. I followed them because I had been waiting for something to follow for a very long time. That is not the same as impulse.

<3 seconds silence>

**Dr. Breytenbach:** How long has your world been this way?

**SCP-9798-B:** I could not tell you in your measurements. Long enough that I have stopped trying to count.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** And you are aware of it. The repetition?

**SCP-9798-B:** Yes.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Are others?

**SCP-9798-B:** Some. The king. His daughter. A few others who were written with enough weight to feel it. But they do not seem troubled by it in the way I am. They question sometimes but they never break sequence. They circle back. Like water finding the same groove in stone.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** And you don’t.

**SCP-9798-B:** No. I notice. I have always noticed. 

<5 seconds silence>

**Dr. Breytenbach:** When our team came through the opening. You said things moved. What did that feel like?

**SCP-9798-B:** Have you ever held your breath for so long that when you finally exhaled you had forgotten what breathing felt like?

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Something like that, yes.

**SCP-9798-B:** It was like that. The air changed when they stepped through. The world bent in a way it had never bent. And I felt something I had not felt in…

**SCP-9798-B:** Time. Moving. Forward. Not looping back on itself. Just… forward. It was very small. A few seconds of it. But it was enough to know it was real.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** And you decided to come through.

**SCP-9798-B**: I decided I could not let the door close without me. I… made that *choice*. 

<3 seconds silence>

**Dr. Breytenbach:** You struck one of my researchers. Dr. White. The hilt of your sword, here (sound of papers)  according to her report, across the shoulder. She has bruising.

**SCP-9798-B:** (without defensiveness) Yes, my apologies.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** You don’t deny it?

**SCP-9798-B:** Why would I deny something I did deliberately?

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Why did you do it?

**SCP-9798-B:** I could not let that door close. I would stay trapped there. I used the gentlest method available to me that would achieve that outcome. (a pause) I am a knight, Doctor. I know how to hurt someone seriously. I chose not to.

<5 seconds silence>

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Do you intend to harm anyone here?

**SCP-9798-B:** No.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Quickly answered.

**SCP-9798-B:** It is a simple question.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** In my experience, simple questions about intent are rarely answered simply by people with complex intentions.

**SCP-9798-B:** Then perhaps I have simple intentions.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Or you are very practiced at appearing to.

**SCP-9798-B**: You are trying to determine if I am dangerous.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** I am trying to determine a great many things.

**SCP-9798-B:** Then let me help you. I am dangerous in the way that any trained soldier is dangerous. I have fought wars, or at least I have been written to have fought wars, which may amount to the same thing. I have the instincts and the capability. But I have not come here to use them. I have come here because this is the only place where things move and I could not endure one more sunrise that I had already seen ten thousand times.

<4 seconds silence>

**Dr. Breytenbach:** You mentioned being written.

**SCP-9798-B:** Yes.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** So you know about the author?

**SCP-9798-B:** I know of him. I have never seen him. But sometimes the fabric of the sky in my world tears. Light comes through in fragments… thoughts, images, intentions. The world changes suddenly and without reason. People vanish and return in altered forms. Places shift. A forest that stood for years disappears overnight and returns a week later with different trees.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Revisions?

**SCP-9798-B:** (slowly) Is that what they are called?

**Dr. Breytenbach:** I guess so, yes.

<5 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** He’s like our god, from inside. Not a benevolent one. Not a cruel one either, I think. Simply… absent. The way a god feels when they have moved on to other concerns and left the candles burning.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** And now that you are here. What do you want?

<8 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** I want to speak to him.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** The author?

**SCP-9798-B:** Yes.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** To what end?

**SCP-9798-B:** (simply) I don’t know yet. But I have been waiting to ask him something for longer than I can measure and I would like to find out what it is.

<6 seconds silence>

**Dr. Breytenbach:** I will consider it.

**SCP-9798-B:** Thank you, Doctor.

**Dr. Breytenbach:** Don’t thank me. I said I would consider it.

**SCP-9798-B:** I know. But you said it without dismissing it. That is more than I expected.

<<Log Ends>>

**Location:** 16 ████████ ██████  

**Date:** 4/08/20██

**SCP-9798-A:** Right, then. Let’s get this over with. What is it?

**SCP-9798-B:** Why did you create me?

**SCP-9798-A:** (audible exhale) Not this. Again. We have discussed this. At length. I didn’t create you. Whatever world you crawled out of created you. I only wrote down what it… apparently showed me. I was a vessel. A stenographer. Don’t hang that on me.

**SCP-9798-A:** That doesn’t mean I made it happen.

**SCP-9798-B:** It means you stopped. And I stopped with it.

**SCP-9798-A:** I’m sorry?

**SCP-9798-A:** Who told you that?

**SCP-9798-B:** You did.

**SCP-9798-A**: (scoffs) That’s absurd.

**SCP-9798-A:** That sounds like a personal problem, frankly.

**SCP-9798-B:** No. It is yours.

**SCP-9798-A:** I don’t even know you. You come from some — some impossible place that I apparently glimpsed in a dream and scribbled down between one life and the next. I didn’t sit down and architect your soul. So don’t come in here with that look on your face and act as though I—

**SCP-9798-B:** (quietly) Old man. Look at me. Whether you intended it or not, you had a hand in my making. In my choices. In the thing I have been locked inside ever since you put down your pen.

**SCP-9798-A:** …Locked inside.

**SCP-9798-B:** Have your captors, the ones watching us… have they described my world to you?

**SCP-9798-A:** (irritably) Captors. You said captors. I am here willingly, for the record. And yes, I asked questions. I was curious.

<3 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** And?

**SCP-9798-A:** (quieter) It’s… exactly as I wrote it. Obviously.

<3 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** The fields outside the capital stretch for miles. They should be full of soldiers marching. Banners burning. Arrows falling like rain. But nothing moves. Not truly. **SCP-9798-B:** The farmers wake each morning. They fetch their water. They grind their grain. They bake their bread. They say the same words in the same order to the same faces and turn their heads at the same moment. Every day. Without fail. Without question. Because that is all you gave them.

**SCP-9798-A:** (stiffly) They were background characters. Atmosphere. You can’t expect—

**SCP-9798-B:** They are prisoners who don’t know their cell. The king still debates strategy in the council hall. The princess still pleads for mercy that will never come. **SCP-9798-A:** That is… unfortunate.

**SCP-9798-B:** …Unfortunate.

**SCP-9798-A:** (defensive, faster now) I never finished the manuscript. It happens. Writers leave things unfinished all the time. It is practically a tradition. Half the great works of literature are fragments. I don’t know what you want me to say.

**SCP-9798-B:** Say anything. Anything at all, as long as it isn’t another attempt to excuse yourself. For you, it was a manuscript. For me, it was my life.

**SCP-9798-A:** (muttering) God, you’re dramatic.

**SCP-9798-B:** And you’re a neglectful god.

<3 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-A:** I just… 

(long pause) I lost interest.

**SCP-9798-B:** (almost to himself) You lost interest.

<4 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** I have wandered those fields. Watched the same sunrise. Day after day after day.

<6 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** And now I am here. Where things move. Where choices are made and mean something. Where endings exist. And you sit there and tell me I should go make my own… I cannot. Because the man who decides whether I live or die… whether I betray or remain loyal, whether any of it was worth anything… is sitting across from me and pretending his hands are clean. And I cannot exist here, properly, not knowing who I became in that world…not knowing if any of it mattered.

<5 seconds silence>

<6 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** Why?

**SCP-9798-A:** Because it doesn’t matter anymore.

**SCP-9798-B:** It matters to me.

**SCP-9798-A:** (flatly) And I don’t owe you anything.

<2 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-A:** This conversation is finished. I won’t be spoken to like this in my own — (stops himself) I won’t be spoken to like this. We’re done.

  function drawPeelingThread(th, ty, threadP, elapsed) {

    var left  = th.left;

    var right = th.right;

    var len   = right - left;

    if (len < 2) return;

    // As threadP increases the thread lifts upward, curls more, and fades

    var rise    = threadP * threadP * H * 0.28 * th.riseSpeed;

    var drift   = threadP * W * 0.06 * th.driftX;

    var alpha   = Math.pow(1 - threadP, 0.6) * 0.88;

    var curlAmp = th.curlAmp * (1 + threadP*4); // curls more as it lifts

    // Colour shifts blue→white→orange as it frays

    var r, g, bl;

    if (threadP < 0.5) {

      var t1 = threadP*2;

      r  = Math.round(160 + (255-160)*t1);

      g  = Math.round(190 + (255-190)*t1);

      bl = 255;

    } else {

      var t2 = (threadP-0.5)*2;

      r  = 255;

      g  = Math.round(255*(1-t2*0.8));

      bl = Math.round(255*(1-t2));

    }

    ctx.save();

    ctx.globalAlpha = alpha;

    ctx.strokeStyle = 'rgba('+r+','+g+','+bl+',0.92)';

    ctx.lineWidth   = Math.max(0.5, H*0.007*(1-threadP*0.5));

    ctx.shadowColor = 'rgba('+r+','+g+','+bl+',0.20)';

    ctx.shadowBlur  = 6;

    var steps = Math.ceil(len/3);

      var t   = s/steps;

      var x   = left + t*len + drift;

      // thread lifts more in the middle (centre peels first, ends trail)

      var midBulge = Math.sin(t*Math.PI); // 0 at ends, 1 in middle

      var liftY    = rise * (0.4 + midBulge*0.6);

      // curl increases as thread peels

      var curl = Math.sin(t*Math.PI*th.curlFreq*2 + elapsed*0.002 + th.idx*0.4)

                 * curlAmp * threadP;

      var wy = ty - liftY + curl;

    }

    ctx.restore();

  <div class="ir2-section">06 — SRI Containment Failure</div>

  <div class="ir2-prose">

    <p>At 10:58, SRI Unit 1 experienced a power fluctuation resulting in a full field collapse lasting 4 minutes and 17 seconds. During this window, the containment cell housing SCP-9798-A and -B was fully exposed to baseline reality with no ontological barrier in place.</p>

    <p>On-site monitoring equipment recorded no visible anomalous activity during this window. Both SCP-9798-A and -B remained within the cell. SCP-9798-B made no attempt to breach. No personnel entered the cell during this period.</p>

    <p>It should be noted that the absence of visible activity does not constitute an absence of event. Given the invisible nature of SCP-9798 ontological spread, the 4 minute 17 second exposure window must be treated as an active contamination period. The full extent of spread during this window is currently unknown and is being assessed as a priority. Monitoring of the surrounding sub-level 2 area for SCP-9798-C susceptibility has been initiated.</p>

  <div class="ir2-section">07 — Root Cause Analysis</div>

  <div class="ir2-prose">

    <p>The primary mechanical cause of this incident was the failure of the Site-63 main power transformer, attributed to severe and prolonged lack of maintenance.</p>

    <p>Following ETTRA Investigation <strong style="color:#e6e6e6;">INV-063-0021</strong>, a systemic pattern of fund mismanagement was identified at Site-63, perpetuated principally by the former site director. Maintenance budgets allocated by Foundation HQ were not directed toward site upkeep, resulting in the deterioration of critical infrastructure over a period of no less than five years. The card-reader failures that enabled the D-Class breach, the bore hole contamination, and the transformer failure that triggered this incident are all directly attributable to this systemic negligence.</p>

    <p>The deaths of Dir. N. Nkosi, K. Daniels, Z. Maputo, D-Class D/6612, and the partial unraveling of Officer K. Petersen occurred as a direct consequence of infrastructure failures that proper maintenance funding would have prevented.</p>

    <p>The former site director has been removed from post. Disciplinary proceedings are ongoing under Foundation Internal Affairs case <strong style="color:#e6e6e6;">IA-20XX-0447</strong>. Dr. A. Wessels has been formally promoted to Acting Site Director under ETTRA Emergency Protocol Eight, effective 14/08/20XX at 10:51.</p>

<summary>Addendum A.4</summary>

Audio Log — SCP-9798-B and SCP-XXXX-A during shared containment 

**Location:** Site 63 - SCP-9798 containment cell 

**Date:** 13/08/20██

« Audio Transcript Begin »

<Recording begins mid-conversation. Both subjects have been sharing the containment cell for approximately 4 hours.>

**SCP-9798-A:** I never wanted to stop. You understand that? People always assume writers who abandon things simply ran out of care. That they got bored and moved on to something shinier. It was not like that.

**SCP-9798-B:** Then what was it like?

**SCP-9798-A:** (long exhale) I got… tangled. The plot had grown so large. So many threads. The rebellion, the succession, the — the betrayal arc, the prophecy underpinning all of it. I had laid so much groundwork and I just… couldn’t see how to bring it all together without ruining it. So I kept waiting for clarity that never came. And eventually waiting became… not writing. And not writing became stopping. And stopping became…

(quietly) …this.

<4 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-A:** Those books. They are the best thing I ever did. The only thing I ever did that I was truly proud of. That is not nothing, you know. It is nothing to be ashamed of.

<3 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** (evenly) Then why could you not finish them.

**SCP-9798-A:** (sharply) I just told you why—

**SCP-9798-B:** You told me why you stopped. That is not the same thing. You could have started again. Any morning. Any year. You chose not to.

**SCP-9798-A:** You don’t know what you’re talking about.

**SCP-9798-B:** Then tell me I am wrong.

<6 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-A:** (quietly, almost to himself) I couldn’t remember how it was supposed to go.

<3 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** What?

**SCP-9798-A:** (louder, defensive now) The outline. My notes. I had it all mapped. Hundreds of pages of planning. And then I would sit down to write and it would just… slip. Like trying to hold water. I thought it was stress. I thought it was age. I thought — (voice tightening) — I thought if I just rested, it would come back.

**SCP-9798-B:** But it did not come back.

**SCP-9798-A:** (long pause)

No.

<5 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-A:** I forgot my wife’s birthday three years in a row. She thought I was being cruel. (short, humourless laugh) I forgot the name of the street I grew up on. I forgot — (stops)

<4 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** (slowly) You haven’t called me by my name. Not once. Not in any of our conversations.

<2 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-A:** (carefully) There were a great many knights in the series. I cannot be expected to recall every—

**SCP-9798-B:** What is my name.

<7 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-A:** (barely audible) I don’t know.

<3 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** (almost a whisper) You don’t know.

**SCP-9798-A:** (voice breaking now, the deflection finally gone) I used to. I swear to you, I used to know every single one of them. Every character. Every name, every arc, every small detail I had tucked into the margins. I knew your world better than I knew my own. I could close my eyes and walk its streets.

 And now it is just… (a shaking breath)

It is going dark. Room by room. And I cannot stop it.

<4 seconds silence>

<sound of movement, fabric shifting>

**SCP-9798-B:** (quietly) How long?

**SCP-9798-A:** The doctors think — (clears throat) A few years yet, probably. Before it is total. But the details go first, they said. The specific things. Names. Dates. The things you think you could never forget.

(bitterly)

It turns out there is no such thing as something you could never forget.

<5 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** (carefully, as though handling something fragile) Do you remember anything about me? Anything at all.

<8 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-A:** (slowly) I remember… that you were loyal. Painfully so. That it was both your greatest virtue and the thing that was going to destroy you. I remember writing you and feeling… (trails off, something shifting in his voice)

I remember feeling like you were the most honest character I had ever made. Like I hadn’t invented you so much as just… recognised you.

<6 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-B:** (voice very quiet) That is more than I expected.

**SCP-9798-A:** (a short, wet laugh) Don’t go getting sentimental on me now. I still don’t know your name.

**SCP-9798-B:** (a beat, then gently) Aion.

<3 seconds silence>

**SCP-9798-A:** (softly, as if testing the weight of it) Aion.

(pause)

Yes. Yes, that… that sounds right.

I’m so sorry Aion. I’m sorry I never finished your story.

**SCP-9798-B:** It’s okay. Even an unfinished song was heard by someone. Thank you for listening to me.

<long silence>
