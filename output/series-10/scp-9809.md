Item #: SCP-9809

Object Class: Euclid

Special Containment Procedures:

SCP-9809 is contained within a 16 km² artificial wildlife reserve maintained by Foundation Outpost-9809 in the southern region of the Carpathian Mountains.

The reserve is disguised as a government-sponsored ecological rehabilitation project administered jointly with the Romanian Ministry of Environment. Access by civilians is prohibited.

Motion sensors, thermal cameras, and autonomous observation drones are to monitor SCP-9809 continuously. Personnel are forbidden from addressing SCP-9809 verbally after sunset.

No reflective surfaces larger than 10 cm² are permitted within 500 meters of SCP-9809's location.

In the event that SCP-9809 vocalizes human speech, all nearby personnel are to immediately withdraw and initiate Protocol SILENCE-FALL.

Individuals exposed to SCP-9809's vocalizations for longer than three minutes are to undergo psychiatric evaluation and Class-B amnestic treatment.

Description

SCP-9809 is an adult male brown bear (Ursus arctos) estimated to be approximately 3.2 meters in length and weighing 640 kg.

Genetic analysis confirms SCP-9809 belongs to no known bear population currently inhabiting Romania.

The anomaly manifests through SCP-9809's apparent ability to acquire and retain memories belonging to deceased humans.

The mechanism by which these memories are acquired remains unknown.

When SCP-9809 enters a dormant state, typically between 23:00 and 04:00, it frequently vocalizes in Romanian, English, Russian, German, and occasionally languages that have not yet been identified.

The speech consists of personal memories, conversations, confessions, and descriptions of events that investigation later confirms occurred in the lives of deceased individuals.

Approximately 83% of identified memories originate from persons who died within a 50 km radius of SCP-9809's territory.

The remaining 17% belong to individuals with no known connection to Romania.

Discovery

SCP-9809 came to Foundation attention in 2018 following reports from shepherds near the village of Poiana Mărului.

Witnesses reported encountering a bear that repeatedly addressed them by name and referenced private events from their childhoods.

Initial investigation suggested a coordinated hoax.

Three days later, local police discovered a recording in which an unidentified bear-like voice accurately described the contents of a sealed murder investigation from 1994.

Foundation assets embedded within Romanian law enforcement initiated containment operations.

Addendum 9809-1

Initial Interview

The following transcript was recorded on 14 October 2018.

Interviewer: Dr. Ionescu

Subject: SCP-9809

Dr. Ionescu: Can you understand me?

(SCP-9809 remains motionless.)

Dr. Ionescu: If you understand me, raise your head.

(SCP-9809 complies.)

Dr. Ionescu: What are you?

(35-second silence.)

SCP-9809: I am hungry.

Dr. Ionescu: Hungry for food?

SCP-9809: No.

Dr. Ionescu: Then for what?

(SCP-9809 appears agitated.)

SCP-9809: For names.

Interview terminated.

Addendum 9809-2

Incident SILENCE-FALL

On 22 January 2020, Researcher M. Pavel ignored standing procedures and attempted extended communication with SCP-9809 after sunset.

The following recording was recovered from his body camera.

22:43

Pavel: How many memories do you have?

SCP-9809: Millions.

Pavel: That's impossible.

SCP-9809: So is death.

Pavel: What happens to people when they die?

(SCP-9809 turns toward the camera.)

SCP-9809: They arrive.

At this point all electronic equipment within a 200-meter radius ceased functioning.

When power was restored 14 minutes later, Researcher Pavel was missing.

No remains have been recovered.

Over the following month, SCP-9809 began repeating fragments of Pavel's personal memories despite the fact that he had not been declared deceased.

Researcher Pavel remains officially listed as Missing.

Addendum 9809-3

The Reflection Event

On 9 September 2023, a maintenance vehicle carrying a polished steel panel entered SCP-9809's enclosure.

Multiple observers reported SCP-9809 approaching the reflective surface.

The bear reportedly stared into its reflection for approximately 11 minutes.

Audio recordings captured the following statement:

"There were two of us."

Immediately afterward, SCP-9809 exhibited severe distress and attempted to destroy all nearby reflective objects.

Subsequent analysis revealed an unidentified humanoid figure standing beside SCP-9809 within the reflection.

The figure was not visible to observers.

No explanation has been found.

Addendum 9809-4

Recovered Memory

During a vocalization event on 13 March 2025, SCP-9809 recited the following statement repeatedly for six hours:

"We were not made to remember the dead. We were made to carry them."

Linguistic analysis determined the phrase was spoken in an archaic dialect predating modern Romanian.

The dialect is believed to have disappeared between the 12th and 14th centuries.

No historical source containing the phrase has been located.

Research Note

The prevailing theory among Foundation researchers is that SCP-9809 is not an anomalous bear.

Evidence increasingly suggests that the organism currently observed as SCP-9809 may merely be a physical manifestation of a much older entity associated with human death and memory throughout the Carpathian region.

Whether SCP-9809 is collecting memories, preserving them, or consuming them remains unknown.

As of this writing, personnel assigned to SCP-9809 have begun reporting recurring dreams involving vast forests filled with thousands of silent human voices.

No causal connection has yet been established.

Final Foundation Assessment:

SCP-9809 is dangerous not because it attacks people.

SCP-9809 is dangerous because it appears to know what becomes of them after they disappear.
