**Item #:** SCP-9941

**Object Class:** Keter

**Special Containment Procedures:** A live record of future and current Noseworking competitions should be held within Reliquary Area-27. As SCP-9941 events are both random and self containing, focus should be placed on excavating dig sites and cataloguing relevant objects.

**Description:** SCP-9941 is a recurrent theological phenomena centered around Noseworking competitions. This event occurs in upwards of 0.17% of worldwide instances. 

During an SCP-9941 event, dogs will significantly diverge from typical behavior. These diversions typically take the form of following nonexistent scents, digging at the ground around the location, and barking at strange intervals or directions. This will eventually accelerate into each dog at the event digging a hole in its spiritual center.

Digging will continue for three days and three nights, at which point all worship will cease. Simultaneously, all people present at the event will leave the congregation with no knowledge of the occurrence. 

Individuals will have false memories of their dogs suffering from typical deaths, illnesses, or disappearances. Public record of the sacrament existing, including photos or videos of the miracle itself, exit consensus reality entirely unless within an anti-Akiva field or containment structure.

Churches dug by SCP-9941 miracles typically contain relics of minor to major significance. It is not known if these sacramentals are directly tied to occurring miracles, or if they materialize post determination. Idols exhibit consistent spiritual abnormalities. 

The worldwide spread of SCP-9941 ceremonies has been continually shrinking since it was first witnessed. SCP-9941 miracles appear to be moving towards a singular center point. The exact goal of this pilgrimage is currently undetermined.
