**Item Number:** 9943

**Object Class:** Euclid

**Special Containment Procedures:** Foundation teams have constructed local outposts for the purpose of containing SCP-9943-1 instances. All sharp objects have been removed from the immediate vicinity of each SCP-9943-1 instance, and typical locations of impact have been covered with cushioning material. Personnel are to remain at least 20 meters from an SCP-9943-1 instance. If someone enters an SCP-9943-1 instance, medical professionals trained in blunt force trauma are to be mobilized. Spectral-class specialists are on standby by in case of a SEVERING event.

**Description:** SCP-9943 is the collective designation given to a series of 35 craters, each designated SCP-9943-1. These take the form of a ring loosely distributed throughout the city center of Amritsar, Punjab, India. Each instance has the same 10-meter radius and a variable depth. Upon entering SCP-9943-1, the subject will be immediately propelled outward by an unknown force away from the center of the crater, reaching significant, non-lethal speeds. Thaumaturgic traces suggest an artificial origin of SCP-9943, though this remains uncertain. 

---

**DISCOVERY**

---

@@@@

GoI-1984, The House of The Naga, brought SCP-9943 to the Foundation's attention following a series of tourists being maimed and hospitalized upon inadvertently entering an SCP-9943-1 instance. Locals reported these events to the Amritsar branch of GoI-1984, wherein they conducted an initial sweep. Due to the presence of trace EVE particles, as well as the potential for deepening of relations, Foundation personnel experienced with GoI-1984 suggested a delegation of the investigation to Site-93.

SCP-9943 was given its designation upon reception of documentation from GoI-1984. This followed an agreement wherein GoI-1984 would provide historical records and Foundation personnel would carry out much of the on-site research. GoI-1984's ongoing re-organization has created minor delays in navigating their archives. Received information has been digitized, and is attached below along with Foundation files.

**BY ORDER OF THE CROWN, HIS MAJESTY, KING GEORGE V**

---

By his grace did the King permit Lord Chelmsford, Viceroy of India, to delegate these tasks to I, Lieutenant Governor of the Punjab, Michael O'Dwyer:

* The collection of information pertaining to the events of the Bagh in 1919.

* A re-examination of the facts of the matter such as to quiet Parliament.

* A defense of my actions, and Colonel Dyer's as well, that day.

As a part of the investigation, it was determined that we should re-visit the site. It brings me no joy, though it should, to recall that time, just a few short months ago. The blood has long since been washed away by rains or mourners. No, what bothers me is something my visceral senses cannot detect -- that my position is teetering on the edge, at the whims of whatever decision appears most palatable. 

There are talks of a convening of Parliament for a special session, or at least for some form of debate. There has been plenty talk from Churchill and Secretary Montagu, plenty disdain from both Country and some of the popular figures here ~~ yet, I remain Lieutenant Governor. It would not do to simply lie around ~~ if this office offers me some lonesome means by which I may clear my name, I had ought to seize it.

This is a most frustrating turn of events -- yet I will take it on the chin as a proper gentleman should. It is not my quarry that the barbarous folks resist governance. Rebellion was due, and it was my understanding that should  a spark potentially present itself, it is my responsibility to stamp it out. Are we to be surprised? These are a people that consider the swinging of a saber a *dance.*

Dyer still holds his role yet. If he remains unsullied in professional terms, then it may be possible to deliver me some hope. I am thankful that I was not the one in the plaza that day -- I have only the grace of God to thank for this.

In our preliminary examination of the nearest SCP-9943-1 instance to us, we selected a side street off of Sultanwind Gate Road. Said street had a peculiarly high collection of instances -- nearly three to four in a single abandoned, overgrown lot. 

We had been lucky -- the person who was struck (and the resulting incident that alerted us, or the House, rather) had lived, and only wandered through *one* of the instances, to our knowledge. Had they gone through such a force again, their organs would be a slurry across all the weeds. The House members that had been waiting for us there wanted to run point, originally. I had to say no, or at least let them know that it might have been unsafe for them too. It would be easy enough with the instrumentation that we had brought to demarcate where each instance was. Levels, hyper-precise sonar to determine elevation, things like that. 

That was, if they hadn't already made some crude marks of where they thought it was -- stones, some spray paint, stuff they brought from one of the *mandis* down the road. The borders they had circled were about *three times as large* as we expected. We thought it'd help us narrow it down, at first, and then we realized that we'd hardly be able to get *near* it. Which was fine, really, all we needed to do after all was just know the general area where they were to establish a containment zone.

Then as we asked, it turns out they had done this with every single other instance. Kind of a blow to the ego there. At least they were handy enough to make a map. Nothing too out of the ordinary, other than each instance making a general circle around one of the memorial areas. First, though, we've been asked to test what it responds to. Hypotheses were *any* living thing, but the amount of plant growth we observed (and the insects and birds within) disproved that rather immediately. 

We've set up a rather small forward base there, and elected to keep the House's delineations. Saves us work, and they're not impossible to notice. Tests about the exact borders'll come later. Hopefully, it doesn't need us to throw any of us at the problem to hash out the specifics. Most of us saw the poor sap who got whacked last time -- and it was nothing pretty. Straight into a concrete wall at, well, I don't even want to know what speed. He's still unconscious in the hospital, and it's been three or so weeks.

It is a most frustrating thing, some of these laws we have attempted to put into place. Though they were not carried out by me, one of the ones we have encountered the most resistance regarding, even from our Sikh Sepoys, is that of disarmament. They are a martial peoples, and we have decreed such that they lay down their Kerpans, and such a simple request (easily allayed by the use of a smaller blade or ceremonial necklace, no-more than a mere finger in size) has historically been met with difficulties.

In a similar vein, my predecessors have time and time again attempted to outlaw that practice of Shaster Vidya, or Jhatka-Gatka, that weapon-toting art that they carry out in their jungles and Guru-dwaras. It is a most terrifying thing to witness, though we understand it carries a certain weight for their peoples. As such, while we know it is still practiced with those replacement implements, we allow it to continue. We are not blind, though we are merciful.

Mercy it may be, though I find my patience running increasingly thin. Whether it is made of metal or not, their muscles will remember how to swing the sword and spin their terrible whirling Chakkars -- a matter of time is what I see it as, most definitely.

I have written to both Sirs O'Dwyer and Montagu regarding this concern of mine, yet have been met with little more than silence. If I receive a response, it is naught more than a perpetual delay, with their more pressing concerns taking a greater precedence. They have their worries, and I have mine -- I am a military man, and thus, perhaps see what they do not. It has been a while since they have taken up bayonets and rifles, and in this atrophy, their gaze has softened.

Colonel R. Dyer

Agent Caesar is currently recovering in Foundation care. After two weeks, he was deemed medically able to be interviewed by Site-93 staff, at which point he notified staff of having experienced psychic event as a result of impact. Given the difficulty in parsing his speech due to a healing jaw, GoI-1984 psychics conveyed a mental re-enactment of what Caesar perceived during this event. Below is a transcription of what was verbally described.

Agent Caesar is floating behind himself. His hands are frosty windows into the ground below, as he sees through them and all of himself. It is a new experience for him, and not a new experience for what is causing it.

It has done this many times before, to many like him, who have dared to enter its ring, its zone, its home. It has many such homes, and many such selves, who all guard their territory like the most fearsome of lions.

His former body is crumpled on the ground, being lifted by the few other personnel there at this hour. Gingerly, he reaches a hand forward to try to re-enter his body. It does not work, and he hangs his head in disappointment.

"This is a problem," he says.

"Why am I here? When will I go back? Am I safe?" he asks.

All very good, and very pertinent questions, were he asking them to anyone or anything else. Though it is not as if he had the choice.

That which brought him here reassures him that this is only a temporary state. Not reassures, rather, but begrudgingly states so. It wishes there were another way to do so, but its expertise is violence. Death would be preferable, but in that situation, he would be truly unable to actually *see.*

"See what?" He wonders what is so important to know and to see, that it has been deemed worth what he estimates to be at least six months of physical therapy, once he returns to his body.

What brought him here finds this to be a great offense. In the past, only the most vile and terrible thoughts and feelings would eat at it and bring it to this point. This is a new(ish) sensitivity it is not expecting. Perhaps the Foundation is reminding it of what has gotten it so incensed. Caesar apologizes. Here he is only Caesar.

Here he is less than that. He is skin. He is bone. He is sinew. He is none of these things, and a being of light and smoke. And now that he understands this, and understands what his role and responsibility is, it shows it to him.

There is a FATHER and there is a CHILD.

There is a VILLAGE and there is the OUTSKIRTS of the village, away from the prying EYES.

He is brought closer, and he sees the faces -- one bearded, one peach-fuzzed. Both are dressed in white, with saffron shawls tied around their bodies like bandoliers.

The father was the sort of person who you could tell knew what he was doing, even if he did not at that time. There was a convincing look in his eyes, weary and brown, and a countenance that suggested he had no awareness that his face looked so heavy. Steady hands carved wood as his child looked onward, a precision that people would have typically lost if they were being watched so intently.

The child was baby-faced, not a hint of cheekbone visible, a chipped tooth in the front that had refused to come out just yet, even as all his others had fallen out and been tossed to the crows to pick up and incorporate in their nests. He was a rambunctious one, and if he could, would have tussled with those fiends to get it back. Sentimentality was always a weakness of his.

“This is a gatka.”

“And why’s it called that? I thought we were going to *do* gatka?”

“Yes, *with* a gatka. This stick—” he sighs, holding up the half-carved baton, “is going to be what we start you off with.”

“And I don’t get the cool stuff first?”

“No, not yet. In due time, child.”

He nods. He’s learned that complaining too much only annoys his father further, which is not what the either of them need right now. His father DOES have a large blade in his hand after all.

“Perhaps once you’re older,” his father starts, “we’ll get you a chakkar.”

His son cannot recall the last time his father was so serious. Everybody he could think of that did not live in his village seemed to treat the whole affair rather positively. His father told him that there were complex reasons for this tolerating, sweet-talking, and even dying, all for those who descended like a star from the sky. Centuries-old sentiments and frustrations being brought back up and utilized, forgotten scabs picked at and wounds reopened. 

While the child churns these thoughts in his head, his father has already returned to whittling the last bit of the branch into an appropriately-sized *soti*, no larger than his forearm. He knew his father was as confident as he was with the knife because of his training that the men of the village did every few days. Quiet as mice, they were, the only hint they existed in their front yard the quiet whooshes of air as deep and heavy swings were made, and the sharp crack of wooden swords on shields. "Is what we're doing alright?"

"Meaning?"

"Why do we need to hide it?"

"Trouble, child."

"To put down my toys."

He takes a few, small swings. Overhead, side-strikes, other bits of simple motions that he's picked up from watching from the stairs while pretending to have long since been asleep.

"Of course."

"And we would win? Like the stories."

His father does not answer.

Caesar is confused. Why stop it here? Why is this all he gets to see? 

Because it is not only *him* who needs to see it. He understands this, and feels his vision cloud before he takes a long, sharp blink. The voice in his brain has quieted down, and he feels a strange pull, as if he is being vacuumed towards his body speeding away in a whining ambulance.

With a start, as if he was born anew to blinding lights and cacophonous noise, he opens his eyes. A sharp pain is in his chest, a different sort than the sensations from his bones stabbing into places they shouldn't. There is a sinking feeling there too, as through the haze of drugs and endorphins, he realizes that he is not the *only* person that will be forced to see the rest. 

He cries, not because of all of that knowledge or because of his body laying in a crumpled mess, but because of what else the rest will be witnesses for.
