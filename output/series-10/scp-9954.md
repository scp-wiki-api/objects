### Special Containment Procedures:

---

SCP-9954 was considered neutralized following the failure of Project TRYST. However, evidence suggests that at least some of its remains may persist on the surface of Saturn's moon, Phoebe. Thus, Foundation efforts should prioritize the restriction of interplanetary spaceflight to and from Phoebe, and an exclusion zone should be designated up to 15km around SCP-9954's impact crater. A temporary lunar research station, designated SCP-9954 Proximal Research Station (PRS-9954), should be established within the exclusion zone to facilitate the search for and study of SCP-9954 samples. Should such samples be discovered, they will remain at the established Phoebe research station for an interim storage period before being transported off-site for further research at a dedicated Foundation facility on Earth.

### Description:

---

SCP-9954 was initially determined to be a centaurA small Solar System body that orbits the Sun between Jupiter and Neptune. displaying characteristics of both an asteroid and a comet, but was producing a comaAn envelope that forms around the comet's nucleus when it passes close to the Sun and its nucleus begins to sublimate. and an infant bow shockShock waves produced when the conditions of density and pressure change dramatically due to interactions with stellar wind. at the time of its discovery and was thus classified as an interstellar rogue comet, 54X/2286 K1 (Lily). Spectrographic analysis revealed that SCP-9954's nucleus exhibited an unremarkable composition, containing an amalgamation of rock, dust, water ice, frozen carbon dioxide, carbon monoxide, methane, ammonia, and a variety of organic compounds, such as methanol, hydrogen cyanide, formaldehyde, ethanol, ethane, and various hydrocarbons and amino acids. However, the overwhelming, uncharacteristic prevalence of these organic compounds, far exceeding levels on 99.997% of similar small Solar System objects, implies the building blocks of DNA and RNA, especially nucleotides such as adenine and guanine, likely formed on its surface, potentially allowing for complex prebiotic evolution.

While SCP-9954 was otherwise non-anomalous in nature, it was observed not to be confined to or impacted by gravitational forces. Its trajectory was static and rectilinear -- strictly one-dimensional, defying known laws of motion and gravitation, suggesting an unknown force fueled its anomalous movement. SCP-9954 traveled with a relatively low velocity as it entered the outer solar system, and was estimated to reach a non-orbiting perihelionThe position in an object's orbit around the sun that is closest to its surface. within 56 years, and was expected to exit the solar system within 112 years of its discovery.

### Addendum SCP-9954-1: Project TRYST

---

The Foundation prioritized SCP-9954 research due to its potential insight into the origins of organic life and to uncover the unidentified force behind its anomalous movement. Project TRYST was later established with the intent to intercept and sample SCP-9954 as it reached non-orbiting periapsisThe position in an orbit closest to the body that is being orbited. of Phoebe in 2302. In 2288, the construction of the Advanced Comet Research and Sampling Station (ACRSS) began in orbit above Phoebe. Simultaneously, the Artificial Intelligence Applications Division (AIAD) began development on a Class-IV General Intelligence Artificially Intelligent Conscript (.aic), Sequitur.aic, to assist in the management of the ACRSS's Computer Command and Data Handling subsystem (CCDHS)The CCDHS supports navigational control of the station and the handling of data transmitted to or received from various other systems, including, but not limited to the guidance, mechanical, power, propulsion, software, and communication subsystems.. Sequitur.aic's early trial results promised above-average efficiency and unprecedented accuracy in its problem-solving, decision-making, adaptability, and learning-ability, and, in 2298, Sequitur.aic was fully deployed to the completed ACRSS, making it humanity's first .aic-supported orbital research station. The ACRSS was fully operational by 2300 and was staffed by more than 75 individuals sourced from Foundation sites on both Earth and Mars.

On March 5th, 2302, SCP-9954 made its approach on Phoebe. The station would, with the support of Sequitur.aic, move into position to intercept the comet, matching its velocity and vector, traveling parallel to it for several days to facilitate the retrieval of several samples. However, on March 10th, 2302, Mars Orbital Site-054 intercepted a high-priority distress signal originating from the ACRSS's calculated orbital intercept vectorDue to propagation delay, it was presumed that this incident occurred approximately one hour and ten minutes prior to the delivery of the distress signal.. While Sequitur.aic should have been able to calculate the intercept flawlessly, SCP-9954 ultimately collided with the ACRSS. The station suffered catastrophic damage and was sent, with SCP-9954, careening to the surface of Phoebe.

It was presumed that SCP-9954 had disintegrated upon impact and that no Foundation personnel within the ACRSS had survived. All project assets were considered lost, and Project TRYST was deemed a failure. The Foundation Internal Review Committee (IRC) opened an investigation of the AIAD to determine the cause of the collision, in which the IT Department's Director, Hange Roa, denied all responsibility, citing Sequitur.aic's assessments and trials to certify its functionality and operability.

## Subject:

### **On the Implication of the AIAD in the ACRSS Incident**

Sequitur.aic only builds upon its predecessors, outperforming the very foundations it was built upon. 200 simulated mission control scenarios with zero errors. 25 pre-deployment trials where perfect operational efficiency was maintained across all subsystems, where complex, multi-step protocols were executed with unparalleled precision. Throughout its 10-year development lifecycle, dozens of external auditors within the IRC itself have attested to its accuracy. Hell, even Dr. Cross, Chief Engineer of the ACRSS, testified that Sequitur.aic's fault tolerance was unprecedented. Sequitur has proven to us time and time again that it is simply incapable of making mistakes.

Alexander Pope in the 1700s famously said, "To err is human," not algorithmic. If a mistake was made, it was made by a human. The final clause of that quote is "To forgive, divine." The Foundation will have to find within itself the power to forgive whoever caused such a tragic loss of life, but Sequitur is not to blame.

Hange Roa,

Director

IT Department

Due to widespread corruption of ACRSS CCDHS logs and records following the collision, a multi-source reconstruction of events was conducted, and a timeline was assembled with 92% confidence, which meets IRC's tolerable margin of error.

### Addendum SCP-9954-5: ACRSS Incident Reconstruction - Section D

##### Section D - ACRSS Communication Re-Established

---

**Timestamp:** +120h to +750h Post-Impact

**Summary:** Following the interception of the high-priority distress signal 68 hours post-impact and Sequitur's Module 17 diagnostic log, a single transmission was received, indicating that Module 17's O2 supply was depleted. No further communications were transmitted from the ACRSS CCDHS. After a period of dead air that persisted for more than 48 hours, Mars Orbital Site-054 observational staff concluded that any remaining survivors had since expired. As per standard operational protocol, all dedicated communication channels with the ACRSS remained open for the full duration of the 168-hour mandatory signal retention window. Approximately 120 hours post-impact, communication with the ACRSS was unexpectedly re-established, and Mars Orbital Site-054 received another high-priority distress signal originating from Module 17.

---

**Timestamp:** +920h to +934h Post-Impact

**Summary:** The retrieval team entered Phoebe's orbit on schedule and descended to the surface of the moon, arriving at the ACRSS crash-site nearly four hours later. It should be noted that, at this point, no further communications had been received from Lydia Wells or Sequitur.aic. Despite radio silence for more than 30 consecutive days, Project TRYST asset recovery was to continue as planned.

ACRSS Exterior Survey:

**Timestamp:** +920h Post-Impact

> **Report:** Visual confirmation of the ACRSS wreckage. The exterior hull exhibits signs of catastrophic damage to several modules, resulting from both a collision with SCP-9954 and an impact with Phoebe. A total of 34 bodies were identified outside numerous module airlocks. Observed bodies exhibit abnormal discoloration inconsistent with vacuum exposure. Patterns consistent with foot traffic are visible in the surrounding regolith, extending between airlocks and away from the wreckage: irregular stride lengths, inconsistent depths, overlapping and partial prints. Thermal imaging indicates active heat signatures on Phoebe's surface, branching away from the ACRSS in a lattice-like formation. Source remains unidentified.

ACRSS Interior Survey:

**Timestamp:** +921h Post-Impact

> **Report:** Interior access achieved via Module 01. Auxiliary power remains active in limited capacity. Primary systems offline. While Sequitur.aic's logic core did sustain minor damage in the collision with SCP-9954, it should be noted that the logic core exhibits additional localized damage inconsistent with calculated impact vectors from the ACRSS-SCP-9954 collision event. Instead, evidence suggests intentional tampering post-impact, resulting in the total shutdown of Sequitur.aic subsystems. Data recovery achieved. Transmitting to Mars Orbital Site-054.

> 

> All 18 of the ACRSS modules show signs of rapid decompression. Life support subsystems nonfunctional. No survivors were identified.

> 

> Unlike other ejected survivors, Lydia Wells was discovered within Module 17, deceased. Discoloration of tissue consistent with other survivors. Did not seem to possess any SCP-9954 sample. PDA discovered intact, albeit damaged, in her possession. It appears repairs were made to the device, as a toolkit and various electrical components were located adjacent to Wells' body. Internal storage contained corrupted audiovisual data and partially overwritten personal correspondence addressed to her partner, Cassidy Rae.

SCP-9954 Impact Crater:

**Timestamp:** +934h Post-Impact

> **Report:** Thermal imaging indicates active heat signatures in the surrounding regolith, not dissimilar from those found at the ACRSS crash site, extending from the center outward. Source remains unidentifiable. Surface sample retrieved for further analysis. The crater's diameter is approximately 27km -- too large for the recovery team to survey completely. Establishing a temporary exclusion zone and ground station is recommended to facilitate further SCP-9954 sample search and retrieval.

### Addendum SCP-9954-7: Post-Recovery Biological Assessment

---

Preliminary examination of recovered regolith samples confirmed the presence of a previously unidentified extremophilic microorganism capable of sustaining metabolic function in the absence of atmospheric oxygen, sustained vacuum exposure, and extreme thermal conditions exceeding previously documented biological tolerance thresholds. Isotopic analysis and organic residue profiling indicate a composition broadly consistent with material detected within SCP-9954’s nucleus, suggesting a shared origin.

Current observations indicate a marked increase in cellular activity in samples exposed to high-energy shock events consistent with SCP-9954’s impact with the ACRSS, though causality has not been established.

Morphological and genetic analysis indicate no known terrestrial or Solar System analogues. In accordance with standard designation protocol for unidentified extraterrestrial biological agents, the organism has been assigned the provisional classification SCP-████.

Parallel analysis of SCP-████ samples is currently ongoing at the newly established PRS-9954 and at Mars Orbital Site-054.

### Addendum SCP-9954-8: Extrasolar Orbital Survey

---

Orbital Site-054's deep-space tracking arrays identified a small Solar System body breaking through the Oort cloud, designated 6X/2305 S1 (Rae). While spectral resolution remains insufficient for full compositional matching, preliminary analysis indicated photometric similarity to previously catalogued cometary data, particularly regarding particulate dispersion under solar stress.

Of particular note is the object's rectilinear trajectory.

Kinetic modeling suggests a persistent, linear vector unaffected by all known measurable perturbative forces from nearby stellar and planetary masses. This behavior is consistent with prior reconstruction models applied retroactively to SCP-9954 during pre-impact orbital assessments.

With its current trajectory and velocity, comet 6X/2305 S1 is projected to enter Mars' atmosphere in approximately 12 years. However, consistent acceleration persists outside modeled variance, increasing as heliocentric distance decreases. Thus, confidence in long-range projection remains below acceptable predictive thresholds.

Further comparative analysis between SCP-9954 and comet 6X/2305 S1 is ongoing. It is currently undetermined whether SCP-9954 represents an isolated instance or a segment within a larger, uncharacterized population of similarly behaving objects.
