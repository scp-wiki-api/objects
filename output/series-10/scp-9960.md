**Item #:** SCP-9960

**Object Class:** Ticonderoga

**Special Containment Procedures:** Due to SCP-9960's non-physical nature and unknown transmission mechanics, direct containment has proven impossible. Foundation efforts are currently focused on early identification of affected Individuals through Front Organization ████ ████████████████████, financial surveillance systems, behavioral analysis and embedded informants within psychiatric and neurological institutions worldwide.

Individuals displaying symptoms associated with SCP-9960 are to be discreetly detained under psychiatric emergency protocols and transferred to Foundation custody for observation.

Foundation personnel are prohibited from engaging SCP-9960 in unscripted conversation without Level 4 authorization.

**Description:** SCP-9960 is a parasitic cognitive entity capable of gradually inhabiting the consciousness of human subjects. SCP-9960 demonstrates a preference for individuals occupying positions of significant influence.

Observed hosts include: Heads of State, Corporate Executives, Military Officials, Celebrities, Religious Leaders, Financial Authorities.

SCP-9960 appears capable of inhabiting only one individual at a time. Habitation periods range between 3 and 5 months before spontaneous departure occurs. The criteria governing host selection and departure, and its mechanisms, remain unknown.

Early-stage infection is typically asymptomatic, as habitation progresses, subjects begin exhibiting substantial behavioral and neurological abnormalities. 

**Documented Symptoms include:**

* Emotional Flattening

* Dissociation

* Compulsive or irrational decision making

* Insomnia

* Altered Speech Patterns

* Changes in brain wave patterns

* Sleepwalking

* Ideological reversals

* Abnormal increase in memory retention and reasoning capabilities

* Possession of classified or otherwise inaccessible information

* Inappropriate emotional responses

SCP-9960 demonstrates apparent telepathic capabilities during active habitation events. The entity has repeatedly responded to questions prior to verbalization and has demonstrated awareness of information known exclusively to nearby personnel and/or interviewers.

SCP-9960's habitation is currently divided into five phases:

Phases

Symptoms

Phase I

No overt symptoms, subjects occasionally report vivid dreams as well as sleepwalking. Occasional mentions of feeling "watched".

Phase II

Behavioral irregularities begin to emerge alongside measurable increases in cognitive performance and emotional detachment. Subjects in this stage consistently outperform baseline cognitive testing by 200-600%

Phase III

SCP-9960 begins intermittently taking control of Subjects, outside of unconscious states. Knowledge anomalies become increasingly severe.

Phase IV

Not all Subjects reach this Stage as SCP-9960 appears to occasionally exit Hosts after reaching Phase III. In cases where progression continues, SCP-9960 exhibits full bodily control overlap with the subject, most Hosts exhibit no measurable continuity with pre-infection personality structure at this stage.

Phase V

SCP-9960 vacates the host abruptly, resulting in extensive neurological damage of varying severity. Departure occurs without warning and is not accompanied by any consistent physiological trigger. Duration of departure typically lasts between 10-15 seconds, during which subjects remain fully unresponsive, displaying complete cessation of voluntary movement and failure to respond to external stimuli.

SCP-9960 is not considered absent following departure events, only non-interactive. In all confirmed cases, SCP-9960 does not reappear in previously vacated hosts.

**Classification Submission Log**

**Case File:** MH-1986-VH

**Date:** ██/██/1987

**Subject:** Michael van Hober

**Proposed Classification**: Ticonderoga

**Proposed Item Number:** SCP-9960

**Team Lead:** Dr. habil. ████

**Team Members:** ███ ███████,█████ ███████████, Dr. ███████, ████ █████ ██████

---

[BEGIN LOG]

Subject Michael van Hober was admitted to ████ ████████████████████ on ██/██/1986 following reported progressive behavioral and cognitive deviations consistent with previously catalogued "Executive Cognitive Dissociation" cases.

Initial examination revealed severe affective flattening, dissociative speech patterns and inconsistent autobiographical recall. Subject demonstrated no apparent distress regarding significant financial and social irregularities reported by family members, including complete liquidation of assets and reassignment of corporate authority to non-affiliated personnel. 

Standard neurological imaging was conducted. Initial scalp EEG readings indicated an anomalous secondary brain wave pattern present both during wakefulness and induced sleep states. These patterns remained constant regardless of external sensory suppression or pharmacological intervention.

Subject was subsequently transferred to full monitoring under controlled anesthetic induction to isolate neurological baseline activity. At approximately T+16 minutes following induction, subject exhibited sudden return to full motor function despite confirmed anesthetic levels consistent with surgical unconsciousness, subject sat upright without external stimulation.

At this time, attending anesthesiologist Dr. ███████ noted internally that the subject appeared "not fully under" and began preparing to verbalize concern to the surgical team.

Subject then addressed attending anesthesiology personnel with the statement:

> Ahhh, you got me!

Subject then laughed for approximately 11 seconds.

Immediately following restraint protocol intervention, subject demonstrated unsolicited verbal response directed toward anesthesiologist Dr. ███████, accurately reproducing a private conversation conducted earlier the same day between said researcher and a non-Foundation civilian. No recording devices were present at said exchange.

Subject abruptly ceased speech mid-sentence whilst reciting the referenced private conversation for approximately 8 seconds, during which subject remained motionless and unresponsive. 

Subject then exhibited rapid cognitive regression and confusion. Subject repeatedly inquired regarding identity of medical personnel and purpose of restraints. 

Scalp EEG readings returned to baseline wakeful activity patterns, with no secondary or anomalous brainwave activity detected post-event. 

Immediate "post-departure" effects include:

* Retrograde amnesia spanning confirmed pre-admission baseline by approximately 6 years

* Severe disorientation and incoherent speech patterns

* Loss of situational awareness

* Identity Destabilization

Subject was unable to provide coherent responses for approximately 43 minutes following event termination. Further neurological testing is requested to determine correlation between cessation of anomalous activity and observed cognitive degradation.

Based on observed multi-layer cognitive activity, information access anomalies and apparent non-local awareness of personnel intent, Dr. habil. ████ recommends immediate classification of the phenomenon as SCP-9960 under Ticonderoga designation pending full containment protocol development.

[END LOG]

**Case File:** AV-1993-V

**Subject:** Aleksandar Vuković

**Team Lead:** Dr. habil. ████

**[BEGIN LOG]**

Following the classification of SCP-9960 after incident MH-1986-VH, Foundation intelligence divisions initiated analysis of unresolved executive instability events matching observed behavioral markers.

On ██/██/1993, embedded Foundation assets within the Serbian Parliamentary Oversight Committee (SPOC) flagged ongoing review proceedings concerning Prime Minister Aleksandar Vuković following  detection  of multiple behavioral correlations consistent with the SCP-9960 habitation patterns. 

The following document was obtained through embedded parliamentary intelligence assets during active no-confidence proceedings and represents the first confirmed large-scale governmental manifestation believed to be associated with SCP-9960.

**Serbian Parliamentary Oversight Committee (SPOC) DOC-█████**

**Motion Support File - Vote of No Confidence (Executive Office of the Prime Minister)**

**Compiled Extract (Abbreviated for Parliamentary Review)**

**Source Status: PARTIAL RETRIEVAL / MULTI-VOLUME ARCHIVE / EXCERPT OF BRIEFING**

**Date:** ██/██/1993

---

@@ @@

**[BEGIN EXCERPT]**

@@ @@

**0. Executive Summary**

@@ @@

This document consolidates findings from SPOC Case Files 19-B through 44-F regarding sustained executive continuity failure within the Office of the Prime Minister (hereafter EOPM).

@@ @@

Across a review period of approximately ██ months, the EOPM has demonstrated repeated issuance of procedurally valid governance actions that are mutually contradictory, non-continious across time intervals, or structurally misaligned with both governing party doctrine and legislative mandate.

@@ @@

Despite full activation of coalition correction mechanisms, internal party governance enforcement and prior SPOC advisories, no sustained stabilization of executive policy  continuity has been achieved.

@@ @@

A brief overview of the covered topics within this document include:

* Repeated appointment of non-affiliated civilian personnel to national defense advisory roles without clearance compliance

* Systematic restructuring of ministerial bodies outside parliamentary authorization pathways

* Unauthorized delegation of classified state authorities to unvetted individuals

* Multiple incidents of classified intelligence disclosure during public executive communications

* Issuance of legally valid but operationally non-functional executive directives

* Failure of all existing political correction mechanisms under conditions of maintained parliamentary majority support

@@ @@

SPOC assesses the current condition as a systematic failure of executive continuity mechanisms not previously recorded in post-constitutional governance history.

@@ @@

Full evidentiary record exceeds 1,400 pages across classified annexes A-███ and is available upon parliamentary security clearance request.

@@ @@

**0.1 Operational Risk Assessment**

@@ @@

Recent consolidated review of executive activity patterns indicates progressive degradation in the separation between procedural governance output and intended legislative function.

@@ @@

While all recorded actions remain formally compliant within executive issuance frameworks, SPOC identifies an increasing frequency of governance outputs that lack interpretable policy continuity, stable administrative intent, or predictable institutional consequence.

@@ @@

At current trajectory, SPOC assessment projects the following within the next governance cycle:

* Complete breakdown of executive-legislative interpretability

* Inability of parliamentary systems to distinguish valid policy from non-functional directive output

* Collapse of coalition enforcement mechanisms under internally inconsistent executive justification patterns

@@ @@

SPOC therefore classifies the current condition as **non-stabilizable** under existing constitutional correction frameworks and formally recommends immediate initiation of a vote of no confidence against the Executive Office of the Prime Minister.

@@ @@

Failure to enact transition procedures within the current legislative window is assessed as likely to result in irreversible degradation of executive governance coherence, with cascading effects across ministerial function, security oversight, and our democracy as a whole.

@@ @@

**[END EXCERPT]**

Observed patterns displayed by Prime Minister Aleksander Vuković are consistent with prior habitation events recorded in Subject MH-1986-VH, most notably anomalous information access events; altered linguistic construction; progressive degradation of interpersonal continuity and compulsive and irrational decision output. 

Foundation analysis has thus fair failed to identify any coherent strategic objective behind the majority of executive actions observed during the Serbian Executive Incident. Several actions appear mutually contradictory even within short operational windows.

Despite SCP-9960 demonstrating the capacity to access and manipulate high-level administrative systems through infected hosts, current evidence does not suggest these systems constitute the anomalys primary focus. Instead, Foundation researchers increasingly suspect that resulting institutional destabilization may represent secondary effects of behavior not intended toward any identifiable strategic outcome.

Recorded incidents continue to support the conclusion that SCP-9960 possesses access to information unavailable through conventional sensory means, including internally held thoughts, emotional associations and non-verbalized memory content in individuals.

Following the Serbian Executive Incident, authorization was granted for controlled direct-interaction procedures with active SCP-9960 hosts in an attempt to determine: its extent of cognitive penetration capabilities; degree of intentionality behind observed behavior; possibility of predictive modeling and criteria governing host selection and abandonment events.

Interview authorization approved under Protocol ████████████

██ days following authorization, SCP-9960 presenting as Aleksandar Vuković was detained under medical pretenses and transferred to Site-██ for interview procedures.

**[END LOG]**
