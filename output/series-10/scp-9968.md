**Object Class:** SkótosSkótos is defined as limited or ongoing research in an object.

**Special Containment Procedures:** All instances of SCP-9968 have been removed from the wild and split between Site-1912,Locally named "Cypress." Site-1907,Locally named "Grouse." and Site-1908.Locally named "Seymour." No further instances are to be sent to Wilson’s Wildlife Solutions under any circumstances.

Unless specified, SCP-9968 instances are to be placed within a containment cell with acrylic viewing windows. Plastic materials other than acrylic should not be used for containing SCP-9968.

SCP-9968-A-64 is to be deployed to select sites within the Canadian West Coast to serve as plastic waste and trash disposal.

SCP-9968-B-1’s containment is to resemble the natural habitat of the Great Bear Rainforest as much as possible in its hybrid containment cell. Once a month, SCP-9968-B-1 is to be fed plastic waste from accumulated trash collected at Site-1907.

Select SCP-9968-D-20 and SCP-9968-D-21 instances are currently on loan to Sites 1912 and 1908 in a special pride theme exhibit.

SCP-9968-E-150 has been handed over to WWS as compensation for the damage caused by SCP-9968-B-1. Additional select SCP-9968-A-64, SCP-9968-D-20 and SCP-9968-D-21 instances have been sent to WWS for further compensation.

**Description:** SCP-9968 refers to species of animals consisting of anomalous edible plastic. Any shed fragments of SCP-9968 take twice as long as their non-anomalous counterparts to degrade in the environment.

SCP-9968-E-150

[[include <a href="http://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/component:responsive-tables-source">:scp-wiki:component:responsive-tables-source</a> |cell-end= --] ]]

[[include <a href="http://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/component:responsive-tables-source">:scp-wiki:component:responsive-tables-source</a> |cell-start= --] |cell-header=Species ]]

Canada Goose (*Branta canadensis*) 

[[include <a href="http://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/component:responsive-tables-source">:scp-wiki:component:responsive-tables-source</a> |cell-end= --] ]]

[[include <a href="http://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/component:responsive-tables-source">:scp-wiki:component:responsive-tables-source</a> |cell-start= --] |cell-header=Description ]]

SCP-9968-E-150 are capable of consuming plastic and converting it into an eco-friendly material that is harvested from laid goose eggs. 450 contained instances.

[[include <a href="http://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/component:responsive-tables-source">:scp-wiki:component:responsive-tables-source</a> |cell-end= --] ]]

[[include <a href="http://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/component:responsive-tables-source">:scp-wiki:component:responsive-tables-source</a> |row-end= --] ]]

[[include <a href="http://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/component:responsive-tables-source">:scp-wiki:component:responsive-tables-source</a> |responsive-table-end= --] ]]

**Discovery:** SCP-9968 was initially discovered on 12/26/1991 by Site-1912 personnel in the northern portion of the Great Bear Rainforest near Prince Rupert, BC. At the time, it was presumed that animals were repurposing plastic for their own use until a sample test of shed skin from a SCP-9968 instance found its anomalous properties. An SCP designation soon followed after a search of Hecate Strait found multiple SCP-9968-A instances.

From 01/01/1992 to 06/01/2007, Foundation vessels from Site-64 and vessels backed by Site-17 and Site-19 captured and secured every SCP-9968 instance. All instances were transferred to Sites 1912, 1907 and 1908. The last SCP-9968 instance was contained on 06/01/2007.

Since 2008, Wilson's Wildlife Solutions has repeatedly called for the aforementioned sites to improve the care of SCP-9968 instances or send them over to WWS. Between 2008 and 2019, money was raised and construction was conducted to expand WWS Center to take in the SCP-9968 instances. By 04/01/2019, WWS was capable of housing all SCP-9968 instances at Sites 1912, 1907 and 1908.

**Incident-9968-WWS:** On 05/01/2019, a call was received by Site-1907 in regards to health concerns about SCP-9968-B-1. At the time of the call, SCP-9968-B-1 was contained at that site.

**Call_Log_05012019**

**Caller:** Wilson's Wildlife Solutions Representative Sera (On behalf of Tim Wilson)

**Receiver:** Site-1907 Board Member Kira, Site-1907 Director Min

Begin Log…

**Sera:** Hello?

**Kira:** Identify yourself.

**Sera:** Tch… I’m calling you on behalf of Wilson’s Wildlife Solutions. We’ve been made aware of the concerning treatment of SCP-9968 instances.

**Kira:** Hang on… I’ll connect you to our Site Director.

**Sera:** Say again?

*No response recorded for 120s.*

**Sera:** Hello?

**Min:** This is Site-1907’s Site Director. What are your intentions with our SCP-9968 instances? And why call us specifically?

**Sera:** Wilson has requested for the instances to be moved to WWS. Via the Boring Agreement,Currently disputed by Cypress, Grouse and Seymour on WWS operational range limits since the 2017 Boring Agreement Amendment. you are required to do so given that SCP-9968Instances held at Cypress, Grouse and Seymour did not have names at the time. can be raised here at WWS without holding back on your resources.

**Min:** Who told you about this?

**Sera:** Site-64 personnel.

*No response is heard for 30s.*

**Min:** Sera. We do not have an obligation to give you every animal anomaly that can be safely raised by your organization. You do not have jurisdiction over our anomalous animals.

**Sera:** Director, I am simply relaying the orders here. The Boring Agreement-

**Min:** I am aware of that agreement. You had to amend it with Site-64 to cover our province and expect us to follow that shit. We were never consulted, nor did we even sign it.

**Sera:** Director. We’re just worried about your SCP-9968 instances being mistreated and abused. I get that you don’t like the agreement, but you have an obligation to follow it.

**Min:** I am not going to repeat this again. Our SCP-9968 instances are being properly cared for and we are not going to let your Boring Agreement dictate our site’s choices. Tell Wilson that he has no jurisdiction in the Province of British Columbia, alright?

**Sera:** Director. Deflecting is not going to-

*Min abruptly hangs up the call.*

End Log.

**Followup:** Following the call, multiple calls were made to Sites 1912, 1907 and 1908 to force SCP-9968-B-1 to be transferred to WWS. Mediation efforts were conducted by Sites 17, 19 and 64 to enforce the Boring Agreement.

A deal was reached on 05/25/2019 between Site-1907 and WWS. In the deal, Site-1907 will hand over select SCP-9968 instances to WWS under the supervision of Site-64, Site-17 and Site-19 personnel assigned to oversee the transfer process. Site-1912 and Site-1908 pointed out that Site-1907 was effectively forced to sign the deal under the threat of losing all SCP-9968 instances to WWS.

**SCP-9968 under WWS care:**

Select SCP-9968 instances were sent to WWS in the days following the deal, including SCP-9968-B-1.

**Receiver:** Site-1907 Director Min

**Wilson:** Director. I can’t handle Eden anymore. It’s breaking out every single day and it’s stressing out our critters. We’re at our limit with our patience with this bear.

**Min:**  Funny for you to say that. Wilson. You really thought that a large plastic bear was something that you could handle? Let alone a polar bear?The [https:*scp-wiki.wikidot.com/critter-profile-the-polar-bear Ursus Maritimus] [https:*scp-wiki.wikidot.com/uiu-file-2008-21 Incident] of 2008 where an anomalous polar bear wrecked havoc on an entire town. **Min:** And who started that deal? Wilson?

**Wilson:** You do not have to remind me. Director. Remember the Boring Agreement before you attempt to capture another animal anomaly, alright?

**Min:** Assuming we ever decide to come to an agreement on the treaty boundaries. Wilson.

**Wilson:** That discussion will be for another time. Director. Right now, all I ask is whether you are willing to take Eden back to Grouse.

**Min:** You will break the deal if you do so.

**Wilson:** I’m serious. Director. The deal is being broken for the safety of everyone here at WWS. What do you want us to do when Site-64 and us are at our breaking point with Eden?

**Min:** . . . Wilson. I told your rep that you had no jurisdiction in British Columbia and yet you and your American Foundation reps had to shoehorn a deal in my face to make it like that. Critter Profile: **Eden**

Overview!

@@ @@

**Name:** Eden

**Species:** Spirit Bear (*Ursus americanus kermodei*)

**Primary Caretaker:** Site-1907 SCP-9968-B-1 Containment Team

**Diet:** Plastic Waste

**Housed:** Site-1907's SCP-9968 Containment Facility

@@ @@

Creature Features!

SCP-9968-B-1, aka Eden, has a niche for being a very mischievous escape artist. SCP-9968-B-1 will escape its containment cell, seek the nearest trash can for trash consumption, and then return as if nothing happened.

Aside from its plastic composition, no additional creature features were of note for documentation.

**History!**

*SCP-9968-B-1 prior to the issues at WWS*

SCP-9968-B-1 is one of many SCP-9968 instances captured by the Foundation between 1992 and 2007. Initially, SCP-9968-B-1 was kept at Site-1907 until 2019 when it was briefly moved to WWS. It was kept there from 05/26/2019 to 06/13/2019, when it was moved back to Site-1907.

Special Needs and Accommodations!

As of 01/01/2020, please refer to the posted containment protocols outside of SCP-9968-B-1's cell. Currently, SCP-9968-B-1 has no additional accommodation aside from the need for plastic waste in its meals.

SCP-9968-B-1 has a dedicated hibernation cave built into its containment cell during its hibernation period.  During that time, personnel spend their time cleaning and maintaining the containment cell until SCP-9968 awakens from hibernation.Length of hibernation is approximately 5-7 months between October and April.

Notes about Eden!

Since 07/01/2019, SCP-9968-B-1 was deemed safe for personnel interaction. SCP-9968-B-1 enjoys the frequent back scratches and petting from personnel assigned to it.

Prior to 07/04/2019 SCP-9968-B-1's biological sex was considered indeterminable. On 07/04/2019, a thorough medical checkup confirmed SCP-9968-B-1 as hermaphroditic. A subsequent medical exam has found that SCP-9968-B-1 prefers a male appearance.
