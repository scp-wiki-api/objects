**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-9971-1 should be kept in a twenty-by-twenty meter item containment unit located at Site-██. The containment cell is shielded with thirty millimeters of lead plating to prevent radiation leakage. It is mandatory for all personnel to don hazardous material equipment before entering its containment unit. The level of radiation emitted by SCP-9971-1 is monitored regularly, with any deviation of its typical emission pattern to be reported. In addition, SCP-9971-2 must be left in a safe-class item locker outside of its cell. A backup of its contents is kept on a hard-drive in the Site's electronic archives. SCP-9971-2's contents should be frequently monitored for rewrites and be subsequently documented.

**Description:** The object designated as SCP-9971-1 is a seemingly perfect replica of the Voyager II space probe launched by NASANational Aeronautic and Space Administration in 1977. In August ██, 20██, SCP-9971-1 was discovered by the CNEOSNASA's Center for Near Earth Object Studies Sentry program moving on a trajectory to intercept Earth. It was identified as Voyager II by NASA due to the radio transmissions originating from the object being identical to Voyager II's original transmission. Despite this, Voyager II was confirmed to still be in contact and on its original course. Agents embedded within NASA relayed this information to the Foundation, leading to an independent survey and retrieval of the probe before it entered Earth's atmosphere. After the object was brought into Foundation custody, it was taken to Site-██ to be examined. 

SCP-9971-1 is completely identical to the Voyager II probe. The replication is one-to-one, down to the object's internal components and the materials used to construct them. However, upon isotopical analysis of the object, it was discovered that the isotopic signatures of the alloys present indicate that the metals used within the probe do not originate from Earth. 

SCP-9971-1 emits a constantly shifting amount of cosmic radiation. The amount of which changes unpredictably but has not been documented to exceed 80 rems of radiation, with the lowest known level being 25 rems of radiation. Only SCP-9971-1's outer chassis exhibits this effect, with its internal components and SCP-9971-2 being completely unaffected.

A recreation of the Golden RecordThe golden disc found inside both Voyager probes, designated SCP-9971-2,was found inside of a compartment within SCP-9971-1 during the probe's examination. SCP-9971-2 itself is identical in make and contains the same contents of the original. Most notably, the pulsar map that resides on the bottom of the record is not the same as the one on the original. The pulsars depicted on the chart do not intersect at a known star, implying that the probe does not originate from any existing solar system in the galaxy. 

The contents of SCP-9971-2 change periodically, despite containing the original information from the Golden Record. This phenomenon was dubbed "SCP-9971-2 rewrite events" by research staff. It replaces random imagery and audio within the record's gallery with media not originally stored in the Gold Record. The exact cause of these changes is currently unknown, with no consistent interval being recorded.

### Incident Log [9971-2/2a]

On ██/1/20/██, researchers identified multiple overwritten images within SCP-9971-2. These photos lasted several weeks before restoration. A previously unseen anomalous phenomenon was recognized regarding the overwritten images, leading to the pictures being designated as SCP-9971-3. Exposure to SCP-9971-3 was observed to cause an irrational feeling of anxiety within individuals who viewed them.

##transparent|why are you afraid##

 This symptom was coupled with a feeling of physical sickness that began after looking at SCP-9971-3 for an extended period of time. The effects caused from SCP-9971-3, although temporary, would cause vomiting and general nausea; lasting around half an hour before dissipating. The effects were proved superficial and generally safe to continue studying. SCP-9971-2 rewrite event frequency began to increase after the incident, with its behaviour changing drastically as well. Rewrite events began affecting other electronics, overwriting its backup and multiple hard drives in a nearby archival room. The images appearing in the rewrites have been exclusively observed to be more instances of SCP-9971-3.

The extent of SCP-9971-2's information tampering capabilities are not yet known. However, all observed post-incident rewrite events seem to affect documents containing information regarding SCP-9971. An escalation in the object's disruption class and containment procedures is currently pending.

#### Addendum [9971-1/1a]

<details>
<summary>+ Research Log</summary>

After SCP-9711-2's last known rewrite event, further study was conducted to figure out the origins of SCP-9971-1. A research project led by Senior Researcher Doctor ████ J. was authorized, beginning sampling and investigation of SCP-997-1's chassis. Radiometric analysis of the materials residing in the plating of SCP-9971-1 revealed that isotopes within the probe's composition were dated to be over 15 billion years old.

</details>
