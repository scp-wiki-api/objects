---

**Special Containment Procedures:** Except during testing procedures, SCP-3798 is to be kept in a secure storage locker. Testing is to be conducted in a standard 3x3 meter containment unit with electromagnetic shielding. SCP-3798 will be inserted into a compatible VCR connected to a television set and power supply. Due to radiation hazards, staff members are not permitted to enter the containment unit during testing. A surveillance camera will be placed in front of the television set to monitor for changes in the contents of SCP-3798.

A microwave receiver will be placed adjacent to SCP-3798 during testing. All signals received from SCP-3798 will be translated from Morse code into the ISO basic Latin alphabet and transcribed for analysis.

**Description:** SCP-3798 is a VHS copy of *Baby Mozart: Music Festival,* a children's educational film released in 1998. The anomalous effect of SCP-3798 occurs when it is inserted into a compatible VCR or videocassette player connected to a CRT television set. At this point, rather than playing the original contents of the tape, a black screen will be displayed. The screen is occasionally interrupted by still images which appear to be from the tape, though some images have appeared which do not originate from *Baby Mozart.* 

SCP-3798 will also occasionally emit bursts of microwave radiation at a frequency of 1420.406 MHz.This frequency is equivalent to the spectral line that is created by a change in the energy state of neutral hydrogen atoms. It is observed frequently in radio astronomy, since radio waves of this frequency can penetrate opaque interstellar dust clouds. These emissions have only been detected while SCP-3798 is displaying a still image.

The source of the emissions of radiation by SCP-3798 appears to be an undefined two-dimensional space that materializes between the front plate glass and the dielectric layer of the TV set. This space only appears when the aforementioned conditions have been met. The source of this space is unclear, and it appears unresponsive to stimuli. Signals emitted by SCP-3798 are relayed in short bursts of 0.25 seconds and 1.02 seconds, which together correspond to letters and numbers in Morse code. These codes have been shown to translate to English language sentences.

A full transcript of images displayed and text received from SCP-3798 is listed in Addendum I.
