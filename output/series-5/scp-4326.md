**SPECIAL CONTAINMENT PROCEDURES:** Due to its location, SCP-4326 cannot be closed or displaced. Instead, a façade resembling the anomaly has been built around it, allowing for it to be used as a wedding venue without activating its effects.

~~All testing requests must be directed to Dr. Isadore Dubois at Site-666.~~ Experimentation with SCP-4326 may not be performed without direct approval from a member of the O5 Council.

**DESCRIPTION:** SCP-4326 is a private wedding chapel inside the Wynn, a luxury resort and casino on the Las Vegas Strip. A stained glass interior window depicting a traditional Fourth Circle marriage ritual (SCP-4326-A) is set directly behind its altar. In the event that a wedding ceremony is performed within the anomaly's boundaries, SCP-4326-A will glow, indicating that a [ bureaucratohazardous](scp-4703 ) effect has made the associated marriage legally binding. SCP-4326 does not appear to be limited by lawfulness, being able to facilitate same-sex marriage, polygamous marriage, human-demon marriage, and marriage to inanimate or conceptual objects.

---

**ADDENDUM 4326-1: INCIDENT LOG** 

On February 15th, 1995, Director House attempted a thaumaturgic ritual within SCP-4326 to reverse its effects, intending to immediately complete his pending divorce with {{[REDACTED]}} and avoid being assigned alimony. Though the ritual failed, Dr. Dubois later uncovered evidence of Dir. House's actions. In exchange for not being reported to the Ethics Committee, Dir. House allowed Dr. Dubois to use SCP-4326 to marry his longterm romantic partner, Agent Wes Knox.

Dr. Dubois and Agent Knox's wedding occurred on March 3rd, 1995. At an indeterminate point beforehand, the officiant's bible was swapped with a Foundation handbook (possibly as a joke, as the only attendees were other Site-666 staff members). The officiant claims that she did not notice the switch until after SCP-4326-A's activation.

Dr. Dubois, Agent Knox, and the SCP Foundation are currently undergoing divorce proceedings.
