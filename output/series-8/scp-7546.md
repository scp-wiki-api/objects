**Item #:** SCP-7546

**Object Class:** Safe

**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-7546 is to be contained in a standard cell within Site-15. The cell must be furnished with a table, a CRT television and an electrical outlet.

**Description:** SCP-7546 is an unauthorized clone of an Atari 2600 video game console. During its operation, gameplay is interrupted by various audiovisual anomalies, often forming coherent messages, imagery, and sounds. SCP-7546 will attempt to communicate with the user through these methods. Evidence suggests it is capable of listening to its surroundings and reacting accordingly.

**Addendum 7546-1:** The following is a collection of instances demonstrating SCP-7546's properties.

| **Game** | **Date** | **Note** | **Imagery seen** | **Text displayed** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| None | 2017/01/03 | None | A man rising from a bed, apparently confused. | "WHAT IS THIS?" |
| Space Invaders | 2017/01/03 | The laser cannon was shot by an enemy, resulting in a distorted tone of varying pitch. | The background began to rapidly alter in color. | "WHY DOES THIS HURT." |

For a complete list of records, please consult the Site-15 database.

**Addendum 7546-2:** It has been noted that SCP-7546 exhibits a pattern of fondness towards certain games, yielding previously unknown information.

Researchers analyzing this data have identified and connected SCP-7546 to █████ Street 32, San Jose, California. ~~An investigation into this location has been initiated. Results are currently pending.~~

The investigation has concluded with the discovery of an actively decomposing body with no discernable cause of death, identified as a man named Thomas Klein, alongside a piece of paper. Its contents are as follows:

> If anyone is here for any reason and reading this, please, do not worry about me. All my friends have gone away. Everyone else I know is more successful than I have ever been and would've been. Video games are the only thing I live for now. But don't worry about me; I'm going to a better place.

> 

> We're all better off this way.
