It is impossible to transport the SCP to another location, and every attempt to slow progression has stalled.

Thus, the Foundation is still actively exploring other avenues to contain this anomaly and prevent a possible ZK-Class Reality Failure Scenario.^^Edited^^

If, for any reason, a Foundation agent is dispatched to the anomaly's location, they are under **strict instructions** not to enter the distorted area. Expeditions into SCP-8175 are restricted to D-Class Personnel only.

**Description:** SCP-8175 is an area near Kayenta, Arizona that is currently expanding^^Edited^^, and appears to distort space and time within itself, creating an apparent "Vanishing Point" from all visible angles.

Originally stated to be "of ever-expanding size".

To the human eye, the area appears as if it extends forever, but increased study has led Foundation staff to believe this distortion occurs around a single point, which has steadily grown to encompass more space. As more of the town is enveloped, the buildings fold inwards to create the appearance of a tunnel and to faciliate the "vanishing" effect.

As one moves into the distorted space, the area seems to "fold" around them from their point of view, creating the illusion that they walk toward the horizon. However, the time they spend and distance increases exponentially the further they walk, though appearing normally to the person inside the anomaly. Walking for a few minutes will result in a several hour trek to return to the beginning. While able to move to their relative left and right, those who have entered the anomaly consistently report an endless, empty desert. They are able to walk in these directions, but they never seem to make any progress, remaining directly in front of the Vanishing Point.

Through extensive testing and experimentation, researchers have determined that the ratio of perceived seconds outside the anomaly to seconds inside steadily increases the further one moves toward the horizon. If one is motionless or moving back toward the entrance, the dilation is removed entirely, as communication is possible through radio technology only while subjects are stationary or actively returning to the beginning of the anomaly.

An emotional sensation has also been described upon entering the anomaly, subtly compelling those who have entered to continue forward until reaching the Vanishing Point. This sensation appears to grow stronger the further one ventures, but does not diminish upon returning from the anomaly itself.

Descriptions of the exact feeling, voice, or sensation tend to differ greatly upon separate accounts, but ultimately, all who enter the anomaly eventually succumb to the need to reach the Vanishing Point.^^Edited^^

Sentence has been cut to end after "accounts".

Outside the space, the figure grows smaller and smaller until they eventually disappear at the perceived point in space. No one has ever returned after reaching this point, seemingly becoming lost within the anomaly. Video and audio surveillance technology remains fully operational, but the distortion of time makes communication difficult. Additionally, the Foundation is unsure how radio technology is able to operate through the warped space, and transmit sound waves without issue.

**Discovery:** In early 1931, two Foundation agents were dispatched to a neighborhood outside Kayenta, Arizona, after reports of a "space hole" to local authorities were intercepted, detailing a distorted area in space that showed the horizon far away from them. Both agents verified the existence of the hole, requesting further aid from the Foundation to contain its existence and the anomaly itself.

At the time, the hole was no more than 1 centimeter in diameter, and the space distorted inside it was barely visible. Even so, the neighborhood was swiftly evacuated, and every citizen was administered Class-G amnestics to ensure there would be no stray memory of this anomaly.

Site-111 was established several thousand meters away from SCP-8175, utilizing the foundation of several existing buildings in the town. Its primary purpose was to monitor and contain SCP-8175, but was known to house several other smaller-scale SCPs, all of which are now "trapped" within SCP-8175.

On specially-approved expeditions to recover important documents from within the site, these anomalies' continued existence was confirmed, as well their relative containment methods to continue to be a success. However, after consulting the O5 Council, it was determined that these anomalies be left inside Site-111, which is estimated to pass the Vanishing Point in early 2021.

**Update- 4/18/2022:** Site-111 has passed the Vanishing Point. All anomalies previously observed in the facility are to be marked "neutralized".

***Expedition Log 8175.32.1 from Dr. Ethel Hollis on August 27th, 1932***

= **Expedition Form**

---

= **Reporting Agent: Ethel Hollis**

***Preparation:*** SCP-8175 has expanded to the size of an average doorway (approximately 2m in height), and has thus been deemed prepared for expeditions utilizing D-Class Personnel by the O5 Council.

D-Class Personnel #2230 was equipped with a backpack containing: 1 mechanically powered flashlight, approximately 2 day's worth rations, and a camera to take photos of the inside of the anomaly. Level 5 Researcher Dr. Ethel Hollis monitored the expedition from the safety of Site-111's observatory.

***Execution:*** 

= *The following is a transcript of the audio captured from D-Class Personnel #2230 and Dr. Ethel Hollis as they entered the anomaly for the first time.*

---

= **<Begin Log>**

**Dr. Hollis:** Hello, #2230: can you hear me? Is the earpiece working?

**#2230:** #2230? What does that...number mean?

**Dr. Hollis:** Oh, right. My apologies- you said you preferred to be called..."Marvin", was it?

**#2230:** Marvin. Yeah. Definitely preferable.

**Dr. Hollis:** Very well. Have you arrived at the, uh- gate yet?

**#2230:** You said it was just past the diner, right?

**Dr. Hollis:** That is correct, yes.

(A bit of shuffling can be heard on the tape, as #2230 approaches the entrance to SCP-8175. Suddenly, they come to a stop.)

**#2230:** Holy h- What in the- What is *that*?!

**Dr. Hollis:** #22- Marvin. We explained all of this to you before you were dispatched-

**#2230:** Yeah, yeah, you said it was an "area that distorts the space inside". I don't know if that was- I mean, it's accurate, but-

**Dr. Hollis:** Please just walk forward into the gateway.

**#2230:** I'm...not sure that I-

**Dr. Hollis:** -Marvin. I'm telling you this for your own good: walking through this is better than the alternative.

(A long pause. #2230 sighs.)

**#2230:** Fine, yeah. I get the deal.

(The sound of boots crunching against the sand relays for a few seconds, then comes to an sudden stop.)

**#2230:** Okay, I'm-

(#2230 abruptly pauses.)

**#2230:** Oh- Sorry, I, uh...I'm inside.

**Dr. Hollis:** Are you experiencing any physical pain?

**#2230:** Uh, no.

**Dr. Hollis:** Do you remember my name?

**#2230:** Ethel Hollis.

**Dr. Hollis:** And the date?

**#2230:** It's uh...August 27th, right?

(Dr. Hollis sighs in relief.)

**Dr. Hollis:** Yes. It is. Marvin, is there *anything* you can report? What do you see around you? Is it the same?

**#2230:** I mean, it- It looks mostly the same, yeah. But when I look past the diner on my right...there's nothing there.

**Dr. Hollis:** What do you mean "nothing"?

**#2230:** Well, the diner's still there. But there was a road behind it, and houses, buildings in the distance. Now, it just- It's just a long, empty desert with this one road down the middle. It looks...fake.

(The sound of a pencil striking paper can be heard, as Dr. Hollis records their conversation.)

**Dr. Hollis:** Hm...Interesting. Anything else of note?

**#2230:** Uh...y-yeah. Is there- Did you send other people in here?

**Dr. Hollis:** You are the first person to ever enter this anomaly. I can assure you of that.

**#2230:** But there's...some sort of voice. Or there's- Yeah, there's voices. They're faint, but I can definitely hear them.

**Dr. Hollis:** What are they saying?

**#2230:** It's not close enough to hear. I can barely even tell they're there. Should I...

(#2230 trails off. A short pause.)

**#2230:** Sorry. Do I have to keep moving forward?

**Dr. Hollis:** Yes. Please continue walking forward, and report anything notable you see along the way. I'll be standing by.

**#2230:** And...when can I stop?

**Dr. Hollis:** (Sigh) If nothing happens at all, you can return after 30 minutes. You understand?

**#2230:** I guess so. 

(As #2230's boot can be heard striking the dirt, a slight scratching sound cuts through the tape.)

**Dr. Hollis:** Wh- Marvin? Hello? Are you- Are you still there?

(Silence.)

**Dr. Hollis:** (Sigh) Ah, *shit.*

(Dr. Hollis tries in vain to "fix" the radio she speaks into for 3 hours, but eventually gives up and takes a short rest on the nearby seating in the observatory. 4 more hours later, the radio crackles to life.)

**#2230:** -ell is going on with you, Hollis? ...HOLLIS!

(A loud thud can be heard, followed by rapid footsteps, as Dr. Hollis rushes back to the microphone.)

**Dr. Hollis:** Hello- Hello? Marvin? Is that you?

**#2230:** YES! Where the hell have you been?!

**Dr. Hollis:** Oh, thank god. I've been waiting for you to respond for *seven hours*!

**#2230:** Se- That's...not possible. I've only been walking for about 20 minutes. And I've been *trying* to talk to you the whole time! You weren't answering me! It's the **least** you could do-

**Dr. Hollis:** -I'm sorry, I didn't hear you, or...*anything.* I thought-

(Dr. Hollis takes a deep breath.)

**Dr. Hollis:** How are you feeling?

**#2230:** I'm, uh- I want to get the hell out of here. That's how I'm feeling. This is all...too strange.

**Dr. Hollis:** What exactly is "strange"?

**#2230:** Those voices I told you about earlier! They're- They're louder now, too.

**Dr. Hollis:** Can you make out what they're saying?

**#2230:** It's- I don't really... (Clears throat) Listen, Hollis: I need to get out of here-

**Dr. Hollis:** -What are they saying, Marvin?

**#2230:** Well, I think they're telling me to keep moving forward. They're...begging, almost. It's still really hard to make any of it out, but I- I don't know if-

**Dr. Hollis:** -Who is "they"? Can you make out their approximate age? Sex?

**#2230:** It kinda sounds- I- I...don't know. But, I can't- I can't stay in here any longer, can I *please* come back now? It's been c-close enough to 30 minutes, right?

**Dr. Hollis:** For you, I suppose. Yes, you can leave. Please return to the base and I will examine you before you return to your containment area.

**#2230:** Oh, thank the *lord*. I can't bear to hear these-

(#2230 abruptly stops. A short pause.)

**Dr. Hollis:** Marvin? Are you alright?

**#2230:** Hollis...it's going to take me a *long time* to get back from all the way out here.

= **<End Log>**

It also seems to attempt to draw those inside toward the center, calling out in a supposed chorus of voices, though the reason is still currently unknown.

**Dr. DeMartin:** You could go, but I have a funny feeling you wouldn't stay away. Besides...I think you could be extremely helpful to us.

(Dr. DeMartin turns back to #4505.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** (Chuckle) And, quite honestly, it's- (Sigh) It's just *really* nice to have you back. Let us know if there's any way we can make you more comfortable.

(Smiling sincerely, he shuts and locks the door behind him, as he exits for the night.)

= **<End Log>**

***Interview Log 8175.64.12 from Level 5 Researcher Anthony DeMartin on May 29th, 1964***

**Interviewed**: D-Class Personnel #4505

**Interviewer**: Dr. Anthony DeMartin

**Foreword**: After extensively describing every bit of knowledge they possessed regarding the effects of SCP-8175, D-Class Personnel #4505 expressed interest in participating in expeditions. Following discussion between Dr. DeMartin and other psychological counselors, it was determined that #4505's unique sensation could be beneficial in understanding and potentially containing SCP-8175.

They were not given any special equipment to place, and were simply told to journey into the anomaly for five hours, then return to the Site. #4505 expressed discomfort at turning around, but was able to be convinced, and eventually returned to their containment area.

= **<Begin Log>**

(D-Class Personnel #4505 stands, staring at the door, waiting for Dr. DeMartin to enter. The latch unlocks with a loud click, as he opens the door and steps in eagerly.)

**Dr. Anthony DeMartin:** Hello, ma'am. Good to see you again!

(The creak of the metal folding chair can be heard as Dr. DeMartin places it opposite #4505's bed.)

**D-Class Personnel #4505:** Doctor, I- I could've kept going. You should've let me keep walking!

**Dr. DeMartin:** I understand, but we didn't want the effects to overwhelm you. You know th- ...We've made mistakes in the past, that's all.

**#4505:** But I can figure this out! I can! I can feel it, just beyond that threshold.

**Dr. DeMartin:** Feel what, exactly? Has this pull you've described, maybe, deepened?

(#4505 sits down, dejected.)

**#4505:** It's not a pull. It's *not.* It's- It's really there. The answer to all of this. I can figure it out, Anthony. I **can!**

(A short pause.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** **Anthony?** How did y- I never told you...Do you know who that is?

**#4505:** What? Who- Oh, that just popped out. I just- I'm comprehending more and more, *learning* information I never could've known, and if you let me walk further, I would've been able to figure all of it out!

**Dr. DeMartin:** Right. Of course. And, um- Why do you want to "figure this out" so badly?

**#4505:** I don't really know. But there's this- This feeling telling me there's something more here, it- it's *epoch-making*. I just know that...this is where I need to be. I can't just ignore something like this.

(Dr. DeMartin sits opposite #4505.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** Neither can I, ma'am. (Clears throat) So you're telling me that the further you go, the more you understand?

**#4505:** Precisely, yes! Goodness, you're a fast one.

(Dr. DeMartin chuckles a bit, his smile then quickly fading.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** Right, um- But do you feel unsettled, perhaps? If you don't go back into SC- the anomaly, will you be able to rest? Get a good night's sleep?

**#4505:** I mean, I don't think we should wait much longer. It's expanding, you know. About a centimeter every hour.

**Dr. DeMartin:** You can tell how fast it's growing?

**#4505:** I *told you* to let me keep walking forward! I could've gotten even more than that from this expedition!

**Dr. DeMartin:** Well, I- I apologize. Next time, we will certainly allow you to journey further. But we'll take a few days' rest for now. I don't want to overload you with work-

**#4505:** -I said I'm fine. I can go back in now, if you're ready.

**Dr. DeMartin:** I know you can, but we can't be too careful here. I want you to be able to work with us for a- well, a very long time, ma'am, if you're willing. Until, perhaps, we can figure this out...*together.*

**#4505:** You know, it's strange, but I- I just have this feeling that we will. And one day, we'll both be able to understand.

(Dr. DeMartin smiles broadly.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** You have **no idea** how happy I am to hear you say that.

(He stands up, heading for the door to their chamber.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** Tomorrow, we have some technical training for you to do. Get you ba- um...up to speed. But for now, just rest. You've earned it, ma'am.

(Dr. DeMartin knocks on the door, signalling the guards to unlock the latch.)

**#4505:** Have I earned a salary? I've got medical bills to pay for, you know.

**Dr. DeMartin:** (Chuckles) Well, we can discuss that tomorrow, as well,

Goodnight, ma'am.

(The door opens, as Dr. DeMartin exits for the night.)

***Interview Log 8175.68.11 from Level 5 Researcher Anthony DeMartin on February 14th, 1968***

**Interviewed**: D-Class Personnel #4505

**Interviewer**: Dr. Anthony DeMartin

**Foreword**: D-Class Personnel #4505, after voluntarily travelling into SCP-8175 over 20 separate times, has developed a hostile attitude toward Foundation staff. They refuse to enter the anomaly again, unless they're permitted to cross the Vanishing Point.

= **<Begin Log>**

(D-Class Personnel #4505 stands, staring at the door, waiting for Dr. DeMartin to enter. The latch unlocks as he pushes it open, stepping inside impatiently, greeting #4505 with an affable smile. #4505 does not move or respond.)

**Dr. Anthony DeMartin:** Hello, ma'am. Good to see you again. It's been a few days, so I'd like to-

**D-Class Personnel #4505:** -Let me back in there. Now.

**Dr. DeMartin:** (Sigh) Yes, I- I was told that you've been giving Agent Báez a few problems after the past few expeditions. But I'm here to hopefully-

**#4505:** -He won't- He won't let me in!

**Dr. DeMartin:** Well, you're very valuable to us, here. Any further on your last trip and you would've walked past the threshold-

**#4505:** -I don't give a shit! I **need** to figure this out. Just a few more steps...a few more steps and I'll get it *all*.

**Dr. DeMartin:** I...understand. But taking those steps would prevent you from providing that information for the rest of the Foundation. To me. You don't want that anymore? What about- What about Anthony?

**#4505:** He- He'd want me to- to find the answer. That's- That's all we wanted: the answer, and- and it doesn't matter what we do to get it! He knew that! With the- ...thethe- The D-Class, they-

**Dr. DeMartin:** Now, calm down, ma'am. Everything's alright. You're safe.

**#4505:** Nonono, you don't **get it**! No one is! No one's safe! Not until I can get in there and decipher this! Please, I know- I know you, and- I know you want to figure this out too! And- we can! We're finally there, after all these years of trying!

(Dr. DeMartin elicits a small gasp.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** ..."All these years"?

(He steps toward them.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** E- Ethel? Do you- ...Do you remember me?

**#4505:** I don't- I don't know *you*, but I feel- I've always felt what you mean to me, I think. I can- I can tell that- that the answer will help you, just as much as me, that- that just beyond that door, there's...*peace* for us.

**Dr. DeMartin:** Then, if you know who I am, you have to understand why I cannot allow you to walk into 8175. You're...indispensable. Without you, there isn't anything left for us, here.

**#4505:** But, you aren't *sure* I won't come back from there. You don't know! Even I don't know! And I- I can sense that I'll be able to understand everything, so *why not*? Why not let me through?! I'd come back! I would!

(A short pause.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** (Sigh) I really want to believe you, but no one *ever does*. I can't risk that. Not with you.

**#4505:** I could be different, right? Right?! You said- you said that I have a "unique experience", m- maybe that means it works differently than it does for the others! Please, *please*, I- I'm sorry, but I can't be out here, anymore. I **need** to make sense of this! It's- It's all I can think about, anymore!

**Dr. DeMartin:** But that's no different than before, right? This is...all we ever *wanted* to think about. And we have this chance to- to start over, you know? To keep working until we solve this- *from the outside*. Ethel, it's been- God, it's been such a gift having you back. Please don't- don't give up on all of this, now. Not now-

**#4505:** -It's not that! It's not giving up! That's not-

(#4505 groans in frustration, slumping down onto their bed and gripping their hair with their hands.)

**#4505:** No one who's- who's walked in there has *ever* given up. They just- They just recognized the- the futility of it all!

**Dr. DeMartin:** What "futility"?

**#4505:** Of this! **All** of this! What you're doing, what I- what I did...*God*, I- I can almost remember, but it's still- it's locked behind **there!**

**Dr. DeMartin:** There's nothing in there, E-

**#4505:** -Yes, there is! I'm telling you there is! Why won't you- Please, you have to- you have to believe me! **Please!** There's nothing more for us to- to *do* here!

(Dr. DeMartin crouches down to meet their eye level.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** I do believe you, okay? I do. But I'm struggling to- to fully grasp it all, like you can. I need you to help me here, okay? I need you to stay here and **help me**.

**#4505:** No. Nono. I can't help you anymore, u- unless I can go back in. Just *please* let me in there. I need to be with- I need to know what this is! I need to solve this for him!

(Dr. DeMartin sighs. A long pause, as he looks directly into their eyes.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** Ma'am...Look at me. I- I need you to listen to me: I've been trying to- to keep going without you, but I just- I'm *scared*, Ethel. I am fucking **terrified**. And I- I *need you*. Is that what you need to hear? I **need** you back with me. Working with me. You promised me- You *promised* we would figure this all out together. You always reassured me of that. You took- You took me from **nothing** and gave me everything I could've ever wanted. Gave me hope. And then you just...*abandoned me*!

(#4505 remains silent. Tears begin to drip down Dr. DeMartin's cheek.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** I know that- that you're starting to remember how things used to be between us, the incredible work you did here at the Site, and I just- I really want to believe that you'll be back. We- We were *so close* to having you back. And I know that this is really difficult, but I feel like this could be a gift. For- for both of us. This could be the **best thing that ever could have happened.** So, can't you just- Can't you just keep going? For me? Just so- Just so we can deal with all of this together? So I don't have to- So I'm not *alone*. Can you please do that for me?

**#4505:** I- ...

(A short pause. #4505 begins tearing up, as well.)

**#4505:** I'm s- I'm sorry, Anthony. But there's nothing left for me here anymore. Or- Or you. Or *anyone*. And you can't- you can't just distract yourself from that by u-using me. And you can't keep *me* from it, either. You can't. All you can do is embrace it: that's it. There's no other choices here.

(Dr. DeMartin sinks his head down, tears pouring down his face.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** No. This- This can't be you. Please, Ethel. You were always...blazing ahead, no matter what you saw or what you felt, no matter if *anyone* was trying to dissuade you. **You were unstoppable.** I know that woman is still in there, still fighting to work things out. That person who- who *needs* to help others. Who can help *me*- You can't just turn away from that, can you? That's **you.**

(A short pause.)

**#4505:** But I'm not. I'm not. Because...there's nothing to turn from. Only something to turn towards, and if you just let me back inside there, I can- I can help you- I really think I can. **Please.** I'll do anything. There's just...nothing left for me out here.

(Dr. DeMartin lets out a sigh, as he stands up, turning to leave the containment chamber.)

**#4505:** No! ...NO! Please, I have to go in there! Anthony! ANTHONY! Please!!

(He shuts his eyes, blocking out #4505's screams as best he can, refusing to turn and face her. Exiting the chamber, Dr. DeMartin speaks to the attending agent: Severino Báez, who has been monitoring the entire interview.)

**Dr. DeMartin:** (Wiping away tears) Can we- Can we give her something? Start over, maybe?

**Agent Báez:** I don't think so, sir. With the effect of 8175, wouldn't it just be the same? They'd still feel-

**Dr. DeMartin:** -But there's- There has to be some type of amnestic we could use. Something that just- It removes the-

**Agent Báez:** -*And* I should remind you that the Site Amnestics Division reported that future use could be dangerous for, well, what's left of their mind. I'm- I think this is the end of the line for them.

(A long pause. Dr. DeMartin looks back toward #4505.)

**Agent Báez:** Sir?

**Dr. DeMartin:** (Deep breath) Alright, then. Let's- ...Let's send her to the beach.
