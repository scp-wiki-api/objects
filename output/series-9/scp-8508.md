SCP-8505: **Blood Money**

**Author:** 

⚠️ **Content warning:** This article contains depictions of murder and the death of children, self-harm and mentions of gore.

--]

=====

=====

**Item:** SCP-8508

**Class:** Safe

**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-8508 is held in a locked box in a secure locker in Area-57. Access is strictly forbidden except for testing purposes. All tests must be recorded via both reports and video. All personnel are to be informed before testing that attempting to steal, withhold or keep any of SCP-8508-1 for personal gain is strictly prohibited. All instances of SCP-8508-1 must be returned to SCP-8508 after the conclusion of the tests except when the test differs from the standard procedure. Should any instance of SCP-8508-1 originate from a country where the primary language is not English or where there are multiple primary languages, testing can only commence if the D-class involved speak the primary or most common languages in that country. D-class are not to be used for more than one test consecutively. The potential usage and accepted uses of SCP-8508-1 are currently under discussion by the Ethics Committee.

**Description:** SCP-8508 is a small wooden box resembling a jewellery or trinket box. The words ‘1 Timothy 6:10’A Bible verse translated in modern times as ‘For the love of money is the root of all kinds of evil: which while some coveted after, they have erred from the faith, and pierced themselves through with many sorrows’. Commonly truncated into ‘Money is the root of all evil’.  have been carved into the back of the lid. There are no other marks on SCP-8508’s surface, or any lock or catch. SCP-8508 was found by police in an abandoned house in [REDACTED], New South Wales, along with a number of projects in various states of completion. After being brought to the Foundation's attention by police liaison, SCP-8508 was transferred to Area-57. Analysis of the available security camera footage of the area around the house suggests that the person responsible for SCP-8508's creation was A█████ Dawson, an anartist with suspected ties to GoI-5869 ("Gamers Against Weed") and GoI-0267 ("Are We Cool Yet?"). Dawson's present location is unknown.

SCP-8508’s interior consists of a single compartment; whenever SCP-8508 is opened, this compartment will contain a quantity of currency (henceforth SCP-8508-1). Both the amount and type of currency changes each time SCP-8508 is opened. The inner compartment possesses spatial warping properties, as it is able to contain amounts of SCP-8508-1 that are larger than SCP-8508 itself. If the current instance of SCP-8508-1 is left within SCP-8508 and the lid is closed, it will dematerialise; all attempts to utilise tracking devices in conjunction with this effect have failed.

All recorded instances of SCP-8508-1 remained inert while within SCP-8508, with their anomalous effect only activating when used in an attempt to make a purchase. Tests have shown that all instances of SCP-8508-1 are legitimate currency; however, when any instance of SCP-8508-1 is utilised as payment for any good or service, all parties involved in the transaction undergo a parapsychic hallucination or vision wherein they witness a death linked to that instance of SCP-8508-1.

Analysis of the collected data from the SCP-8508 tests has established that SCP-8508-1’s anomalous effect activates as long as at least one instance of SCP-8508-1 is used in a transaction, regardless of whether any person involved is aware of the origin of the money, if all of the singular SCP-8508-1 instance is used or only part, if SCP-8508-1's effect has already activated, or if the transaction is legitimate or only for testing purposes. However, the effect does not activate if the SCP-8508-1 instance is given away, donated to a charitable cause, or deposited in a bank account and then used to make the transaction, implying that SCP-8508-1’s effect is proximity-based and applies only to transactions.

In addition, analysis of post-test statements given by test subjects has established that all persons involved in a transaction utilising SCP-8508-1 experience a psychic vision from a third-person experience, showing the circumstances of the death linked to the instance of SCP-8508-1. Afterwards, all test subjects will perceive SCP-8508-1 as being soaked in the victim’s blood, no matter the circumstances of the death. While the statements have been used to support police cases (and have led to multiple convictions), Area-57’s staff are investigating potential methods of empathic shielding in conjunction with the continued tests, as all test subjects reported feeling the emotions and physical sensations of all parties shown in the visions until their conclusion.

**Addendum.8508.1:**

When SCP-8508 was first brought into Foundation custody, initial tests determined that each instance of SCP-8508-1 consisted of valid currency; some instances had fingerprints, but no complete prints were able to be obtained. All successful investigations into SCP-8508-1 instances have confirmed that the instances are the same banknotes used in the original incidents; however, there remains a number of instances where the origin of the notes is unknown. While these instances are still believed to be valid currency, attempts to trace the serial numbers have universally failed. Traces of various bodily fluids, bacteria and recreational drugs were found on the majority of each instance at levels consistent with non-anomalous equivalents. 

The fifth test resulted in the inadvertent discovery of SCP-8508-1's anomalous effect when part of the SCP-8508-1 was used in an experimental purchase. **Addendum 8508.2:**

In June 2025, an SCP-8508.1 test led to the discovery of an incident of serious malfeasance by a Foundation official. The transcript of the test is provided below.

**Date:** 12/06/2025

**Location:** Area-57

**Personnel:** Dr. Kade Nova (he/she), D-61042, D-39175

---

**<Begin Log>**

*<D-61042 and D-39175 are seated at a table in one of Area-57’s testing chambers, with Dr. Nova standing next to the table. SCP-8508 has been placed on the table between the two D-Class, next to the object provided for the transaction, a bottle of water from a nearby vending machine.>*

**Dr. Nova:** This is Doctor Kade Nova, supervising a test of SCP-8508 at Area-57 on the 12th of November, 2015. D-61042 and D-39175 will be conducting the test. For the record, could one of you please repeat the instructions I gave you?

**D-61042:** When we open the box, there’s gonna be money inside. You want us to open the box, take out the money, tell you what kind it is, count it and see if there’s anything weird about it. Then we use it to buy something, and then we get to see some poor guy get killed and we tell you all about it.

**Dr. Nova:** That’s right, thank you. Please commence the test.

*<D-61042 opens the box and stops, surprised.>*

**D-61042:** Holy shit, that’s a ton of money.

*<With D-39175's help, he withdraws SCP-8508-1, a very large, unbound pile of banknotes, and places them on the table next to the box. Several fall from the pile as he puts them down; D-39175 adds them back to the pile.>*

**D-61042:** OK, so, this is all US dollars, from what I can see. Right?

*<D-39175 nods, picking up a few notes and examining them.>*

**D-61042:** I just can’t say how much right now, ‘cause their money’s weird and this all looks the same.Australian paper money consists of five denominations, all of which differ in colour and size. In contrast, all denominations of American paper money are the same size, and until 2004, consistently had the same shade of green on at least one side. Later variations differ in colour.

**Dr. Nova:** Understood. Go ahead and count it, please.

*<Several minutes pass as the two D-Class carefully count the notes, separating them into groups and conferring with each other. Once they are finished, they double-check each group.>*

**D-39175:** Got it. There’s fifty thousand US dollars here, Doc.

**Dr. Nova**: Exactly fifty thousand?

**D-39175:** Yeah, from what we can tell. All $100 notes.

**Dr. Nova:** Anything off about any of it? Rips in the notes, marks or writing on them, any of it feel different?

**D-61042:** No rips, nothing felt different. Didn’t see any marks, but I guess I could have missed them.

*<D-39175 shakes his head in agreement.>*

**Dr. Nova:** OK, just to check, could you try this out on a few of them?

*<Doctor Nova gives D-61042 a Foundation-made variant of a counterfeit money detection pen. D-61042 uses it to test several of the notes, all of which appear to be genuine.>*

**Dr. Nova:** Always good to make sure. Well, time for the last part, if you would.

*<Dr. Nova picks up a notepad and pen from the table in preparation. D-39175 sighs heavily and picks up an SCP-8508-1 note.>*

**D-39175:** I'll give you one of these notes for the water bottle. Deal?

*<D-61042 grimaces, pauses and then finally speaks.>*

**D-61042:** Deal.

**D-39175:** Done.

*<Both D-Class freeze in place for several seconds. D-61042 doubles over, screaming, and then collapses to the ground. D-39175 drops the SCP-8508-1 note, vomits onto the floor, eyes streaming with tears, and clutches his face with his hands. Doctor Nova recoils and then goes to D-61042, attempting to help him up. D-61042 continues to scream and continually grasps his limbs and torso wildly.>*

**Dr. Nova:** What happened? What did you see?

*<D-61042 takes heaving breaths, attempting to speak, slowly returning to a stable state. D-39175 grips the table, his hand inadvertently coming into contact with the SCP-8508-1 note, and lets out a loud exclamation of disgust, shoving the note away from him and frantically wiping his hand on his uniform.>*

**D-61042:** You fucking motherfuckers!

**Dr. Nova:** What?

**D-61042:** Fucking liars, you killed him!

**Dr. Nova:** What are you talking about? What happened?

**D-39175:** His fucking *guts* were hanging out!

*<D-39175 retches, covering his mouth with his arm. D-61042 grasps the bottle of water from the table, opens it, drinks several mouthfuls and pours the rest of the bottle over his head, becoming visibly calmer as a result.>*

**D-61042:** That fucking arsehole took all the money for himself, and then the guy... he...

**D-39175:** He fell off the roof. He fell off the roof and landed on the fence.

**Dr. Nova:** You said 'us'...?

**D-61042:** I mean *you.* The Foundation. Your bunch of fucking arseholes.

*<Doctor Nova looks at both D-Class and nods slowly.>*

**Dr. Nova:** You're absolutely sure it was the Foundation?

**D-61042:** Fuck you, I *know* what I saw.

**Dr. Nova:** I didn't see what you saw. Did you get any names, or a location?

**D-39175:** Ramon? Or Ramos. One of them. That was the guy who took the money. I think it said Director?

**Dr. Nova**: ...*great.*

**D-61042:** It said Site-337 on the paperwork.

*<Doctor Nova exhales deeply, and then writes quickly on the notepad. She tears two sheets of paper from the notepad and sets one in front of each D-Class, before passing his pen to D-61042.>*

**Dr. Nova:** All right, you two take all the time you need and then when you're up to it, start writing down what you saw, every detail. We're going to need all the information we can get if we're going to do this right. I'm going to go make some calls.

*<Both D-Class nod, though D-31975 is hesitant, and D-61042 begins to write as Doctor Nova steps out of the room, pulling out her phone.>*

**<End Log>**

---

**Note:** Further investigation found that in 2001, Site-337 technician Damian Chambers was performing routine maintenance tasks on the outside of Site-337's main building when his safety tether snapped. Chambers fell several feet before landing on the adjacent fence, which impaled his body in several places. None of the injuries were immediately fatal, and Chambers inadvertently worsened his condition by attempting to free himself in a panicked state. Chambers was freed from the fence and taken to the closest hospital, but ultimately died from blood loss. Chambers' death was declared a tragic accident by Site-337's Director, Simon Ramon. 

The SCP-8508-1 test prompted a thorough investigation of Ramon and Site-337, which found that Ramon had repeatedly embezzled funds from Site-337, most notably from those earmarked for safety equipment, such as the $50,000 seen in the above test. As a result, Site-337's employees were often forced to use equipment that was damaged, outdated or otherwise flawed and had not been replaced or repaired, as in Chambers' case. Ramon was relieved of his position and taken into custody for embezzlement, manslaughter and gross misconduct. Further investigation is ongoing, particularly with regard to other injuries and deaths that occurred as a result of Ramon's embezzlement.

**Addendum 8508.3:**

In January 2026, Area-57 analysts conducted a detailed analysis of the results of every SCP-8508 test, excepting those wherein the anomalous effect of SCP-8508-1 were not triggered. While the majority of categories (location, nationalities of those involved, age of victims, method of death, reason for the death) showed no noticeable recurrences, there were two exceptions: first, the form of currency that appeared most frequently in the tests was the US dollarLikely due to its status as the world's reserve currency. In addition, the US dollar is freely accepted as tender in several countries, such as Costa Rica, Honduras, Cambodia and parts of Mexico, Peru and Canada., and second, in contrast to several of the tests shown in Addendum.8508.2, the majority of SCP-8508-1 instances came to less than $50 USD in value.
