**Object Class:** Safe

**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-8639 is stored in a glass display case at Site-138 and remains on display in Sector I's main lobby. No containment measures beyond baseline site security protocols are required. Removal of SCP-8639 from Site-138 is prohibited.

SCP-8639-1B instances are subject to a persistent normalisation effect and do not require containment. Any employed by the Foundation need this distinction noted on their personnel record.

**Description:** SCP-8639 is an 83.5 kg rounded granite stone, bearing the engraving “1805.” It produces a continuous cognitohazardous field of an approximate radius of 110 km, designated SCP-8639-1A. It additionally emits a faint rhythmic sound resembling a heartbeat, audible only under sufficiently quiet conditions and close proximity.

Individuals who have resided within SCP-8639-1A for a minimum of 10 years prior to the age of 16 are classified as SCP-8639-1B instances.

SCP-8639-1B instances demonstrate a persistent emotional attachment to SCP-8639, regardless of direct exposure. Instances are capable of subconsciously identifying one another, with observed reductions in stress indicators and neural activity associated with group cohesion when in proximity. Despite significant linguistic, religious, and regional variation, behavioural patterns among instances consistently converge toward Helvetic cultural norms.

At intervals of approximately 12 years, an 8639-Unspunnen event occurs. During an 8639-Unspunnen event, SCP-8639-1B instances residing within SCP-8639-1A will congregate in large numbers and attempt to locate SCP-8639. Upon retrieval, instances organise and participate in a steinstossenSport involing the throwing of a heavy stone overhead using both arms for the longest distance. tournament involving SCP-8639. Participation is associated with disproportionately high levels of psychological investment relative to the activity performed. 8639-Unspunnen events last approximately 20 hours.

**Discovery:** With the advent of portable thaumaturgic radiation detection equipment at Site-138, field operatives were tasked with surveying Switzerland for previously undetected anomalous objects. Significant readings were almost immediately noticed emanating from the 1976 UnspunnenfestNow designated the 1976 8639-Unspunnen event. during which SCP-8639 was actively in use. Preliminary contact was established with event organisers under the pretext of cultural acquisition; however, all attempts to secure SCP-8639 through formal negotiation were denied.

Subsequently, an undercover retrieval operation was authorised. On 03/06/1984, SCP-8639 was successfully extracted and contained in a thaumic-insulated locker. The operation was conducted under the guise of an action by a Jura separatist organisation, taking advantage of ongoing tensions between Jura and Bern.

**Addendum.01:** Secondary effects were observed within the next 3 years of SCP-8639’s containment. Although the fabricated attribution was intended to localise unrest to the Jura–Bern region, the response extended across almost the entirety of Switzerland. Civilian populationsLater discovered to comprise 70% SCP-8639-1B. exhibited elevated psychological distress indicators, including a 23% increase in anxiety-related consultations, a 17% rise in sleep disturbances, and statistically significant deviations in baseline affect (p < 0.01) across all monitored cantons.

In 1987, large numbers of SCP-8639-1B instances began coordinated movement toward Site-138. Concurrently, on-site SCP-8639-1B personnel reported a progressively intensifying compulsion to access Sector II containment areas, with several attempts to bypass security protocols recorded.

Given the mass civilian convergence and the associated risk of exposure to additional anomalous objects housed at Site-138, Site command authorised a controlled containment breach of SCP-8639. The object was transported approximately 10 km from Site-138 and deposited in a rural area on the outskirts of Interlaken.

Following relocation, all monitored SCP-8639-1B instances altered course toward the new position of SCP-8639. A full 8639-Unspunnen event subsequently manifested at the location, with no further attempts to approach Site-138.

**Addendum.02:** In an effort to better understand SCP-8639’s effects, Foundation historians and anthropological specialists conducted a retrospective review of historical records associated with the object. The following document is an English translation of a chronicle authored by Swiss bourgeois Balz Lamprecht in 1803, representing the earliest currently known reference to SCP-8639:

The most singular occurrence has taken place this day, which I commit to paper lest its impression fade with time.

@@@@

Though the [*https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Act_of_Mediation Act of Mediation] has, in the judgement of statesmen, restored order to our Confederation, it has not so readily reconciled the hearts of men. Between our cantons there remains a distance not traced upon any map, but borne of grievance and memory too fresh to be forgotten. And yet, by what persuasion I cannot say, I found myself drawn thither to Wilderwil, under the pretext of curiosity, though accompanied by some quieter feeling, be it guilt, longing, or unease.

@@@@

My astonishment was not that I had walked so far to come, but that so many others had done the same.

@@@@

Men of every town and village stood assembled upon the same ground, each as surprised as the next. We regarded one another as in a mirror: alike in dress, in custom, and yet held apart by an invisible reserve. It seemed as though some unspoken accord had guided us there.

@@@@

At first, no one spoke. A silence prevailed, of such a nature that one feared a single ill-chosen word might undo whatever fragile circumstance had brought us together. Yet, in time, voices arose, cautious and measured: a greeting here, a remark there, though minds still held them in check.

@@@@

It was then that a party of young men, from Bern and the Oberland alike, drew attention by their exertions. Having found a large stone, they began to cast it, each group striving to outdo the other. Their remarks, rivaling at first, soon rang more freely.

@@@@

As I observed them, I found myself reminded of the contests I had witnessed in younger days at the Schützenfests, to which my late father would sometimes bring me before the troubles befell our nation. Yet these young men could scarcely have remembered such games themselves, many being but children when the French crossed our borders. And still, without prompting or agreement, they had returned to it as though recalling something long familiar.

@@@@

Nor, it seemed, was I alone in being so reminded, as men of more advanced years, unwilling to be outmatched, stepped forward in turn, to the great amusement of all present. Thus, what might have borne the character of division was transformed into a friendly trial, and soon into simple merriment. I made the attempt myself to raise the stone; and though I lacked the strength to do so, I could not but feel a certain warmth in having touched it, in having taken part.

@@@@

More and more descended upon the place, until the strip of land between the two lakes seemed scarcely able to contain the multitude; yet there was no disorder. The tension of the morning gave way, and men cheered one another without regard to origin, as though they had returned, if only briefly, to their untroubled days of youth.

@@@@

Music was soon heard, and the air grew lively with song. Tables were laid, provisions shared, and laughter rose on every side. I cannot recall in these recent years so great a gathering marked by such harmony.

@@@@

As evening drew on, those same young men who had first set the stone in motion carved the year into its surface, that it might stand in remembrance of this day.

@@@@

I would not presume to name the designs of Providence; yet I confess it is difficult not to perceive in this occurrence something of a blessing. If HelvetiaThe female national personification of Switzerland. had long been wearied by discord, then today it seemed she had gathered her children once more into her embrace.

@@@@

May it not be the last such day.

**Addendum.03:** Following SCP-8639’s initial breach, controlled observations were conducted by embedded field researchers while the object remained under civilian oversight at the Victoria-Jungfrau Grand Hotel. Results indicated that distress responses in SCP-8639-1B instances correlate to uncertainty regarding SCP-8639’s status, location, and physical integrity.

Using this data, a new containment plan was formed and on 20/08/2005, SCP-8639 was once again extracted and re-contained at Site-138. During transport, an increase in the frequency of SCP-8639’s rhythmic emissions was noted, a phenomenon previously documented but disregarded during the 1984 containment operation. Upon arrival, Site Director Elise Kurmann conducted a standardised interaction interview with SCP-8639.

= **Interview 8639.01**

---

**Date:** 20/08/2005

**Interviewed:** SCP-8639

**Interviewer:** Site-138 Director Elise Kurmann.

**Foreword:** N/A

---

[BEGIN LOG]

[Kurmann is seated. SCP-8639 rests on the table. No movement or sound is recorded for approximately 1 minute. Kurmann sighs and adjusts her seated posture, placing both hands upon her knees.]

**Kurmann:** I know exactly how this looks. *[Pause]* I have redirected personnel, resources, and containment infrastructure to support a proposal I cannot properly justify in satisfying terminology. *[Short exhale]* The Overseers will never understand this. They will reduce it to inefficiency. Misallocation. A Site Director entertaining irrationalities. *[Short laugh]* They may not even be entirely wrong.

[SCP-8639 does not respond.]

**Kurmann:** But the existing model is incomplete. 

[Kurmann briefly reviews several documents positioned on the table.]

**Kurmann:** If SCP-8639 were merely a cognitohazard, then containment should have resolved the issue. Insulation should have weakened the effect. Civilian unrest should have remained localised. Instead, it propagated across nearly the entire country. *[Pause]* Not panic, not hysteria, but unease. And *that* distinction matters.

[SCP-8639 does not respond.]

**Kurmann:** They were not attempting to retrieve an object. They were attempting to confirm its condition. Was it damaged? Had it been altered? *[Pause]* We classified the effect as compulsive convergence behaviour: A group cohesion of low-level noospheric reinforcement. *[Short pause]* Interpretations which assume SCP-8639 is exerting influence over the population. 

[Kurmann leans forward toward SCP-8639.]

**Kurmann:** But now, I believe they are incorrect. I believe we misunderstood your nature. *[Pause]* Because, you don't draw them together. You are what they gather *around*.

[SCP-8639 does not respond.]

**Kurmann:** It's not about the land, or the festivities, or even the sport itself. It's about belonging... isn't it? *[Pause]* I do not know what you are. But, I think I understand it. You are part of them, and they carry part of you.

[Kurmann places her hand on SCP-8639.]

**Kurmann:** My site is not apart from you. It is within you. We are within you. *[Pauses]* These people- my personnel- they are yours as much as any beyond these walls. *[Long pause]* You have nothing to fear from us. Let me prove it to you.

[SCP-8639 does not respond.]

[END LOG]

On the following day, SCP-8639 was temporarily placed within the Site-138 cafeteria. Personnel, composed of 65% SCP-8639-1B,  were permitted to interact with the object during a social gathering incorporating traditional attire, food, music, and recreational activities. During the event, SCP-8639’s rhythmic emissions gradually returned to baseline frequency.

Though the Site Director’s methods were questioned by her international peers, during the subsequent 2011 8639-Unspunnen event, civilian SCP-8639-1B instances congregated in Interlaken and conducted expected festivities. SCP-8639 was not present; instead, a non-anomalous stone of comparable size and mass was utilised. No attempts were made to locate SCP-8639 or approach Site-138.

*Note: During the same event period, Site-138's SCP-8639-1B personnel independently gathered within the site's garden to participate in a small-scale informal steinstossen match and apéroSmall informal gathering usually accompanied of snacks and wine., making use of SCP-8639.*
