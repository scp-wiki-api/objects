**SCP-8665:** Null Wake

**Author:** 

**Read More:** [ Personnel File](sr-researcher-james-a-harkness-personnel-file)

**Item #:** SCP-8665

**Object Class:** Keter

**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-8665 is to remain undisturbed at its present location approximately 3.6 km offshore from Paamiut, Greenland. Maritime traffic is to be diverted via a standing exclusion zone, maintained under the guise of glacial shelf instability and environmental monitoring.

Any vessel approaching the area is to be intercepted and rerouted by Foundation assets operating under Greenlandic Survey Authority cover. Civilian exposure to SCP-8665 broadcasts must be suppressed via immediate signal jamming and, if necessary, Class-C amnestics.

No experimentation or exploratory missions are authorized aboard SCP-8665 at this time.

**Description:** SCP-8665 refers to the wreck of an unmarked early 20th-century fishing trawler found partially submerged in coastal waters off Paamiut, Greenland. The vessel exhibits extreme degradation inconsistent with its visible condition; hull corrosion resembles patterns seen in long-submerged shipwrecks, yet the ship remains partially afloat and periodically shifts its position against prevailing current models.

No crew has been observed aboard, though infrared imaging and sonar occasionally register indistinct movement within the hold and beneath the surface of the water nearby.

The anomaly is subject to increased activity during spring tide phenomena,e.g. full moons and new moons. *See more: [https:*en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tide#:~:text=called%20the%20spring%20tide spring tide].// as well as periods of heightened auroral intensity (KP-index ≥ 6).

During these intervals, SCP-8665 emits low-band VHF and Morse broadcasts. The transmissions are semi-coherent, featuring distorted Greenlandic dialects interwoven with archaic maritime terms and anomalous phonetic structures not found in any known Inuit language.

Common patterns in the broadcasts include:

* Repetition of ritual phrases associated with oceanic appeasement rites.

* Distorted confessions and lamentations by multiple voices, often layered or spoken in chorus.

* Warnings not to "follow the tide" or "call [upon] the shore."

* Direct references to Sedna (Inuit sea goddess), though in tones that suggest fear or shame rather than reverence.

Personnel observing SCP-8665 during active phases have reported sudden feelings of guilt, salt-bitter tastes, hallucinatory phenomena,Most commonly involving the sudden appearance of dense fog and marine animal carcasses. and false memories of being lost at sea and/or drowning.

**Addendum 8665.1** — Transcript of Recovered Broadcast

Captured during full moon and KP-7 auroral event. Phonetic elements indicate distress layered with ritual language. Translations are approximate; some segments retain direct Greenlandic due to unknown context.

> = **BEGIN LOG**

> 

> **22:42 UTC:** [Static, followed by erratic tapping and asemic Morse code.]

> 

> **Voice A:** (older male, rough) Our keel was not broken. The land refused us. We cast our hooks into her mouth, and she bit down.

> 

> **Voice B:** (feminine, chanting in fragments) Nerivakka. Nerivakka. She has devoured us. We sail the skin beneath the sea. The ice does not crack, nor close, nor cease. 

> 

> **Voice C:** (haggard, suggestive of liquid in lungs) I still see the snow on the cliffs. But my hands are water now. My breath is tide. We drip into each other. Drip. Drip. Drip.

> 

> **Voice D:** (whispered, distorted) Do not name her. Do not look in the wake. If you speak her name, she comes aboard, hair full of hooks.

> 

> **23:01 UTC:** [Metallic shearing. Indistinct screams. Broadcast ends.]

> 

> = **END LOG**

Phrases align with mourning chants and taboo invocations in pre-Christian Kalaallit oral traditions, particularly those involving Sedna's betrayal and subsequent dominion over drowned souls.

**Addendum 8665.2** — Fragmented Hymn Reconstruction 

Reconstructed from layered audio samples and phonetic alignment. Fragment appears to be a liminal chant, neither prayer nor lament, performed by those aboard SCP-8665.

> We sow the sea with our voice,

> But she speaks in forgetting.

> A net cast of silence,

> Drawn tighter, water pours inside.

> 

> She keeps no names in her belly,

> Only marrow, song, and bone.

> The ones who turned from shore,

> Now churn beneath her tongue.

> 

> Tikeqattaaq… Tikeqattaaq… Tikeqattaaq… Tikeqattaaq: Rooted in Kalaallisut terms for “coiling” or “spiraling,” possibly a metaphor for spiritual dissolution or recursive death. 

> 

> There is a black wave with no crest,

> And the wind that walks behind it has no name.

> We placed hooks in the mouths of the sea,

> But the sea remembered our face.

> 

> The flesh that sails cannot moor in memory,

> Nor dream in driftwood bones.

> O Sedna, whose hair ties the stars to the fjord,

> Let us forget your eyes.

> 

> Let us drift, unwatched,

> Until the lights open our mouth again.

= {{END OF FILE}}
