**Object Class:** Euclid

**Special Containment Procedures:** A barbed wire fence has been erected around the parameter of SCP-8687, while the area has been designated as a United States Army property. At least 5 armed guards disguised as millitary personnel are to patrol the fence, while searches for any smuggling are to take place in the nearby countryside on a bi-weekly basis. Any SCP-8687 inhabitants attempting to breach containment are to be subdued and returned to SCP-8687, with lethal force being authorized only in cases of immediate danger to containment personnel.

Foundation personnel are not to interfere with the affairs of SCP-8687 aside from food and supply deliveries. The treatment of the local population in Foundation-operated hospitals has been deemed unnecessary.

**Description:** SCP-8687 refers to the phenomena related to the town of Winding Falls approx. 1,421 inhabitants as of 2020., West Virginia. The majority of individuals present in the town have been exposed to major health hazards, including lead poisoning, drug use, carbon monoxide poisoning, major surgical malpractice and anomalous bodily alterations involving various pieces of technology. Despite this, the town's inhabitants have been found to be able to survive for much longer than would be expected, while still suffering extensive health problems. The reason for this is currently unknown, but is theorized to be related to some form of medical thaumaturgy undertaken during bodily alterations.

All inhabitants of Winding Falls are members of a previously undiscovered denomination of the [Church of the Broken God](https://scp-wiki.wikidot.com/church-of-the-broken-god-hub), a group of parareligions worshipping a deity known as "Mekhane", associated with technology and scientific progress. The specific denomination practiced by the community is thought to have branched off from the Cogwork Orthodox Church The most conservative branch of mainline Broken God worship, utilizing mainly outdated 1850s to early 1900s technology in their bodily alterations and worship. in the late 1880s, although the specifics are still under investigation.

**Discovery:** SCP-8687 first came into the attention of the SCP Foundation following the publishing of a student film project from the West Virginia University, entitled "Promised Land". The film was an hour-long documentary detailing different parts of the life and culture of the town of Winding Falls, with occasional commentary from its residents. Five students were involved in the making of the film, all of which had no prior ties to the anomalous community. Following questioning, all were amnesticized and released, while all copies of the movie have been destroyed. The film was officially labeled as a pseudo-documentary A piece of media depicting fictional events while pretending to be a factual documentary., with its more drastic and/or anomalous scenes alleged to be practical effects.

**Updated (04/09/2014):** Due to the declining population of SCP-8687, the prevalence of infections among its inhabitants and the continued refusal to receive Foundation-sponsored medical attention, the current containment procedures are undergoing revisions in line with the future predictions regarding the community. It is estimated that by May of 2030, active containment will no longer be necessary.
