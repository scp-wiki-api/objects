**Item #:** SCP-8693

**Object Class:** Euclid

**Special Containment Procedures:** Keep the seeds cold with six vacuum-insulated boxes that store at Site-42 Biological Cold Storage Euclid-7, -18°C for storage. They are passive when frozen below -10°C.

If growing them, use the soundproofed negative-pressure greenhouses in Euclid-7. Wear full-coverage sound-isolation helmets, NRR 30 dB minimum, plus standard Level C hazmat. No exceptions. I don’t care if you’re just checking the soil moisture. Helmet on.

Harvesting is by remote manipulator arms only. If it needs to go in manually—and you need a damn good reason—operators need sedation first. Midazolam 0.05 mg/kg IM. Also, a Cognitive Resistance Index of 65 or higher. And two security guards. And a signed waiver from the Site Director. It’s a pain for a reason.

Until the tubers hit 100°C core temperature, treat them as active hostile anomalies. Not “maybe anomalous.” Hostile. Burn everything unused—plant matter, soil, leftover seeds—within 24 hours.

The SCP-8693-2-A crystals are over at Site-42 Anomalous Materials Lab now. Lead-glass shielded boxes. Keep ambient noise under 40 dB. Doesn’t sound like much, but it matters.

**Description:** SCP-8693 is 180 seeds. They look like normal potato seeds of Solanum tuberosum. We found them in an abandoned cold-storage warehouse in ████, New York. Six standard insulated shipping boxes with 30 seeds each. Handwritten labels: “beef,” “pork,” “chicken,” “freshwater,” “saltwater,” and “luncheon.” Someone wrote them in a ballpoint pen. The handwriting is messy and weird.

Grow them like normal potatoes with the same cycle, same soil, same water. But the tubers underground aren’t starch. They’re made of protein and meat. Muscle protein and adipose tissue, corresponding to the label on the box. Beef batch grows beef. Pork grows pork. Chicken is chicken. The fish ones actually have fish protein sequences. Nutritional density is 2–3 times the natural equivalent, and fat content is way lower. And while the plant is alive, the tubers stay completely sterile. No refrigeration needed. Once the plant dies or you cut the tuber off, that stops. 24 hours later, it’s just rotting meat.

The plants can feel things. Or something close to feeling. Roots move through soil, slowly. About 2–4 cm per day. Sometimes less. They orient toward water and away from shadows, vibrations, body heat. We stuck electromyographic probes at the root tips and stem cuts. Got action potentials, peaking near 40 mV. Looks like vertebrate neuron activity. Not exactly the same, but close enough to be disturbing.

When you try to harvest them or damage them, they fight back. Two ways.

First: chemical. Cutaneous glands release an aerosolized compound, SCP-8693-1. Glycoalkaloid skeleton, similar to solanine, but modified. Unknown groups attached. It irritates skin—itching, redness, and minor toxicity. Nothing lethal. But at 0.05 ppm it kills bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasite eggs. 99.9% in under two minutes. That’s why the tubers stay sterile while alive. The plant is constantly secreting trace amounts.

Second: sound. There’s a crystalline organelle at the tuber-root junction. SCP-8693-2-A. Generates high-intensity acoustic emissions when the plant is stressed or harmed. 115 to 125 dB. However, it’s not just loud, though. It carries a cognito hazard. People exposed without hearing protection report guilt, Nausea, and deep regret. They say it feels like “killing someone that they can feel it.” One D-Class described it as similar to hearing the plant scream in a language he didn’t know but in some way understood.

At concentrations above 50 ppm, SCP-8693-1 gets into the bloodstream through mucous membranes. Causes Identity Substitution Syndrome (IS-8693). Subject believes they are one of the six meat-potato varieties. Acts accordingly. Severity depends on how stable they were to begin with. Low CRI means a bad trip. High CRI means they shake it off.

Here’s the part that makes this complicated: cook it to 100°C internally and everything stops. The meat protein denatures into conventional plant protein and amino acids. SCP-8693-1 breaks down into inert metabolites. The crystals collapse. After cooking, it’s just food. Tastes like the corresponding meat. No side effects, no toxicity, no cognitive hazards. We fed cooked samples to six D-Class. They asked for seconds. Protein absorption was 18% higher than real beef.

So it’s a potato that grows meat, screams when you hurt it, and is perfectly safe if you boil it first. Simple enough i guess(is it really just potatoes that grow meat？)

**Addendum 8693-2:** Biochemical and Thermal Deactivation Testing

**Lead:** Dr. ████

**Date:** 20██/██/██

0.05 ppm of SCP-8693-1. Tested against E. coli, MRSA, C. albicans, H1N1, Ascaris ova. All dead in 30–120 seconds. This stuff is better than anything in our medical stores and supplies. I’m not exaggerating. We should run more test on people.

Then we boiled it. Five minutes at 100°C. Tested again. Nothing. Zero antimicrobial activity. LC-MS showed it broke down into regular solanidine metabolites. Harmless.

Same thing with whole tubers. Cook to 100°C core temp. The meat becomes plant protein. Tastes like steak. Tastes like chicken. Tastes like fish. D-9081 through D-9086 ate all six batches. No vomiting. No hallucinations. Nobody thought they were a potato. Bloodwork showed 18% better protein uptake than beef control.

If I didn’t know what these things do while alive, I’d already have requisitioned them for the Site-42 cafeteria. I’m serious.

> **Note:** Surface searing is NOT enough. Core temp must hit 100°C. Don’t get clever with sous vide or light grilling. Boil it, bake it, pressure-cook it. Don’t half-ass this.

**Addendum 8693-3:** SCP-8693-2-A Crystalline Analysis

**Lead:** Dr. ████

These Crystal lattices are not any terrestrial silicate we know(at least for now). Resonance testing shows that they activate at >40 dB ambient noise. Full cognitohazard output triggers when harvest vibrations couple with microcavitation bubbles in the xylem.(This has potential industrial function:)

Thermal test: stable at 98°C. Collapses at 101°C. Irreversible.

This isn’t a defense organ. It’s a translator. Something built these plants to scream in a way that WE understand. And they built the kill-switch exactly at the boiling point of water.

Think about that. Not 90°C. Not 110°C. 100°C. The temperature at which humans cook food(it may be just a random temperature, actually)

Research continues. Do NOT hook these crystals up to any electronic or neural hardware. I don’t want to know what happens if we give them a louder voice.
