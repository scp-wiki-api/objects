**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-8734 is stored in Furniture Containment Locker 12, Site-77, within a sealed case constructed of ¾-inch plywood lined with matte black felt. The case must remain closed at all times when SCP-8734 is not undergoing scheduled testing. Personnel are prohibited from sitting in SCP-8734 without Level 3 clearance and the explicit written approval of the assigned Site Director.

Any individual who has made contact with SCP-8734 (hereafter "affected subject") is tagged in the Foundation's Personnel Continuity Database and subjected to a continuity audit every 72 hours, cross-referencing reported social history against archival records, tax filings, and school enrollment data predating contact.

Should an audit reveal a discrepancy consistent with SCP-8734's anomalous effect, the individual retroactively removed from the historical record is to be located and, if possible, retrieved. Retrieved individuals are administered Class-A amnestics and released with a cover memory appropriate to their apparent absence. Fourteen of nineteen documented removed individuals have been successfully located; the remaining five are presumed to persist in an undocumented state (see Addendum 8734-3).

Following Incident 8734-Kilo, a standing order prohibits SCP-8734 from being placed within visual range of more than one (1) person at any time during testing.

**Description:** SCP-8734 is a hand-carved hardwood armchair upholstered in worn floral brocade, consistent with furniture mass-produced for portrait-studio use between approximately 1890 and 1930. Material analysis returns no anomalous readings.

SCP-8734's anomalous property manifests when a subject sits in the chair for at least four (4) minutes and thirty (30) seconds while at least one witness is present. At the end of this interval, every witness present spontaneously acquires a false memory in which the seated subject has always occupied a role in their personal history — a sibling, a childhood friend, a decades-long coworker — regardless of whether that role is logically consistent with the witness's actual biography. This alone would place SCP-8734 within a familiar class of memory-altering anomalies. A second, more severe effect accompanies it.

For every witness who gains the seated subject, exactly one (1) person already occupying a comparable role in that witness's life is entirely erased — not killed, not relocated, but retroactively un-remembered by everyone present, and removed from every record any present witness has access to or could reasonably consult. Foundation researchers refer to the displaced party as the "unwritten." Unwritten individuals do not stop existing. They simply stop being real to anyone who was in the room — and, subsequently, to anyone who later checks a record that was in the room's reach.

The unwritten retain their own memories in full but lose the ability to substantiate them to anyone whose own knowledge of them runs through an altered record — a coworker who'd need to check a personnel file to vouch for them, a bank that only knows them by an account number. Memory held independently by a third party with no such record dependency, such as a childhood friend outside the witnessing group, is not reliably affected; the Foundation has documented several unwritten individuals who retained exactly one working relationship of this kind, which is how fourteen of nineteen were successfully relocated at all. Why some independent memories survive and others do not remains unexplained.

The seated subject experiences none of this as an intrusion. They report a complete, lifelong memory of relationships that came into existence four minutes and thirty seconds prior — and they are, without exception, entirely convinced they are telling the truth.

Physical media is altered by a mechanism the Foundation has not identified; SCP-8734 does not leave containment, emit measurable energy, or otherwise interact with anything beyond a 2-meter radius during testing. Though photographic analysis of affected documents has occasionally recorded an unidentified shape near the point of alteration (see Addendum 8734-1).

**Addendum 8734-1: Discovery.** SCP-8734 was recovered in 2003 from an estate sale in ██████████, Ohio, following a Foundation web-crawler flag on a probate dispute in which seven siblings gave contradictory sworn testimony regarding the number of children their deceased mother had raised. Investigation determined that an eighth "sibling," Mr. Harold ████████, had no birth certificate, school records prior to age thirty-four, or biological relationship to the family, despite being remembered by all parties as having been raised alongside them since infancy. SCP-8734 was recovered from the family's dining room and had reportedly been in continuous use for at least eleven years prior to acquisition.

Wear patterns on the chair's upholstery and legs indicate substantially more use than eleven years in a single household would produce. Foundation genealogical research has linked SCP-8734's carving pattern to at least eleven (11) separate photography studios operating in the American Midwest between 1895 and 1941, none of which remain in business — a provenance history difficult to reconcile with the item's uninterrupted presence in the ██████ family dining room. The Foundation has not resolved this discrepancy and considers it likely, though unconfirmed, that SCP-8734 itself is capable of falsifying its own provenance records by the same mechanism responsible for Addendum 8734-2 and related incidents. This hypothesis remains under active review.

A follow-up genealogical audit (Agent T. Okonkwo, 2019) attempted to trace the proprietors of all eleven studios individually. Business licenses, obituaries, or probate records were located for nine of the eleven. In seven of those nine cases, the listed proprietor's recorded date of death falls within eighteen months of the studio's closure, with cause of death listed as natural in every instance and no two proprietors sharing a physician, city, or decade. Agent Okonkwo's report notes the pattern without drawing a conclusion; the audit was suspended before the remaining two studios could be traced, and has not been resumed.

Image analysis of Figure 1, conducted in 2020 at the request of a junior researcher, flagged an indistinct shape near the doorframe at image left, partially obscured by the visible wall bracket. Three analysts independently attributed the shape to shadow and film grain. A fourth annotation, initialed but not signed, reads only: 'check the other ten photos for the same thing before you rule it out.' No further action was logged.

A subsequent, informal cross-reference (unofficial; not entered into the primary case file) compared the shape against other photographs known to have been altered by SCP-8734 outside of Foundation testing. Family portraits, studio archives, and similar civilian-origin material caught by the object's uncontrolled spread prior to acquisition. The same indistinct shape, or one closely resembling it, was noted in three additional images from this category. It has not been observed in any photograph or recording produced under controlled Foundation testing conditions, including all documentation from Incident 8734-Kilo. The distinction has not been formally investigated

**Addendum 8734-2: Interview.** The following interview was conducted with D-9114 approximately six hours after Incident 8734-Kilo, prior to psychiatric evaluation.

Interviewer:

Dr. Lindqvist, Head Researcher

Interviewee:

D-9114

Date:

██/██/2019

---

[BEGIN LOG]

Dr. Lindqvist

Can you tell me your name?

D-9114

Give me a second. I keep — there's six of them. Six answers. I can feel all six wanting to come out at once.

Dr. Lindqvist

Take your time.

D-9114

One of them had a daughter. I remember teaching her to ride a bike. I remember her falling and I remember the exact sound she made. That never happened to me. I know that never happened to me. I still remember it happening to me.

Dr. Lindqvist

Do the six memories conflict with each other?

D-9114

*[subject is silent for eleven seconds]* They don't feel like six people remembering me. They feel like six griefs. Six people who lost someone, and the someone is me, and none of them are wrong.

Dr. Lindqvist

Which one of them is real?

D-9114

I don't know if that's a question that still has an answer.

[END LOG]

D-9114 was withdrawn from further SCP-8734 testing and remains under standard psychiatric monitoring. Foundation attempts to identify the six households referenced in the interview have located none of them.

**Addendum 8734-3: Unresolved Cases.** Of the nineteen confirmed unwritten individuals linked to SCP-8734, five remain unlocated. Analysts theorize that unwritten individuals never retrieved may eventually undergo full removal from the object-level world, though no confirmed case of this endpoint exists. The Ethics Committee has requested that testing involving live witnesses be suspended pending review.

**Addendum 8734-4: Ethics Committee Correspondence.**

MEMORANDUM — Site-77 Internal

From: Ethics Committee, Sub-Panel 4

To: Dr. Lindqvist, Head Researcher, SCP-8734

Re: Suspension of live-witness testing

Sub-Panel 4 has reviewed Incident 8734-Kilo and finds that continued testing involving unbriefed research staff as witnesses constitutes an unacceptable risk of permanent identity harm to Foundation personnel who did not consent to this specific exposure. We formally request suspension of all live-witness protocols pending a full review.

We note that five individuals displaced by this object remain, to date, unlocated. We are not able to confirm whether they persist in any recoverable state. Sub-Panel 4 is not willing to authorize the creation of a sixth.

---

From: Dr. Lindqvist, Head Researcher, SCP-8734

To: Ethics Committee, Sub-Panel 4

Re: RE: Suspension of live-witness testing

Request denied. Understanding SCP-8734's mechanism is the only path to reliably retrieving the five individuals your panel is concerned about. Suspending testing does not un-erase anyone; it only slows how quickly we can bring them back, if that is still possible.

I am aware of what D-9114 described. I do not consider it a reason to stop.

The Ethics Committee's request remains formally on file and formally unresolved. Testing involving live, unbriefed witnesses has not recurred since Incident 8734-Kilo; testing involving briefed, consenting personnel continues under existing protocol.
