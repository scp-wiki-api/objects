#### ****Special Containment Procedures:****

Webcrawler SP-CR-10642 has been created to monitor forums dedicated to cinema for mentions of SCP-8743-2. Any flagged material is deleted, and its creator is to be located and amnesticized as soon as is possible.

All objects and media altered by SCP-8743-1 are under the possession Site-43’s Archives And Revision section. They are available to any researcher provided they have the permission of Site Director Harold Blank and have been memetically inoculated beforehand.

#### ****Description:****

SCP-8743 are two related anomalies affecting the Sony Pictures Animation sports comedy film *Goat* (2026).

SCP-8743-1 refers to anomalous alterations that affected movie theaters showing the film *Goat*. Once a theater had undergone an SCP-8743-1 event, all physical and digital copies of *Goat* within the building would have be replaced with copies of SCP-8743-2, and all merchandise items (e.g. toys, popcorn buckets, bottles) related to *Goat* within the building would have been replaced with equivalent items bearing the logo of [ Vikander-Kneed Technical Media (GOI-5889)](*https:*scp-wiki.wikidot.com/vikander-kneed-technical-media-hub ). All persons within the building after an SCP-8743-1 event were usually unable to discern these alterations had occurred, suggesting memetic/ontokinetic qualities. Before *Goat// ended its theatrical run, an estimated forty-two SCP-8743-1 events had taken place.

SCP-8743-2 is the sports drama film *Snow Yak Of Mine!* produced by GOI-5889. The film follows the fictional James “Jimmy” Sarlyk’s rise to prominence as a professional skier, while facing discrimination for being a talking yak. SCP-8743-2 is memetic in nature, generating heightened feelings of reverence and awe for the film within its viewers, and associating the concepts of an “underdog” and the ability to “pull oneself up by their bootstraps”, relating to the film’s protagonist, with GOI-5889 in their minds.

**James Sarlyk:** Come on, man. Can’t we talk about this? I’m sure we could come to an… arrangement.

**CIA Agent:** No, no, I don’t want you anymore.

**James Sarlyk:** Come on!

**CIA Agent:** No, you ruined the mood. You’re going away for a long time, at least until the third act redemption.

**James Sarlyk:** No, the humanity! Lock me up, throw away the key, but no more meta humor! 

**James Sarlyk:** *[Under breath]* Oh, god. Willy was right.

*[The talent scout sits on a metal chair in a dark, wet, concrete room. A sign above him reads in sharp lettering “INMATE VISITATION CENTER”. On the other side of the glass wall the talent scout is facing, a visibly more muscular James approaches. He sits down on a metal chair, immediately crushing it.]*

**James Sarlyk:** Warden said you had something to offer me.

**Talent Scout:** What would you say to one last game?

**James Sarlyk:** Something along the lines of “go blow your own brains out”.

**Talent Scout:** Look, kid, after what you did to Elvis-

**James Sarlyk:** I didn’t do anything to Elvis.

**Talent Scout:** Well, we here at the agency would be much more willing to believe you if you were out there getting us money. My point was, with what you did, you’d be extremely lucky to ever work in the industry again.

**James Sarlyk:** I don’t *want* to work in the industry again. Skiing ruined my life! It turned me into an obnoxious, psychopathic, quip-machine. My wife left me over Vegas, I don’t have anything anymore.

**Talent Scout:** Look, blame us all you want. “Oh, you exploited my identity.” “Oh, you got me addicted to gambling.” It won’t get you anywhere. What we are offering is one last chance. It’s just a small themed match, nothing fancy. Mull it over, why don’t you?

*[James remains silent, thinking. The talent scout gets up and leaves.]*

*[A mailmanPlayed by Joseph Cotten enters the front of Chuck Sarlyk’s rug store.]*

**Chuck Sarlyk:** Hello, there, friend, what type of rug are you in the market for? Could I interest you in our newest stock? It’s made from the fur of my good friend and neighbor Rita. She passed recently, of course, but what a woman!

**Mailman:** Can’t say I’m interested, no, but I’ve got a letter for you.

*[Chuck’s cheery persona drops, now glum. He takes the letter from the mailman.]*

*[There is a jump cut to Chuck at his home, reading the letter. James voices over its contents.]*

**James Sarlyk:** *[Head transposed on screen]* Hey, dad, it’s been a while. I know what you’re going to say. “How dare you speak to me again after all this time?” “You’ve ruined our family name.” “Was Elvis as handsome in person as he is in pictures?” I just want to skip over all that. I know I’ve been a horrible son, and there’s nothing I can do to fix that, but I have been trying to get better. I’m two months as of writing this letter quipless. I’m more sincere now. So, I hope that you can look past everything wrong I’ve done, and remember the son you love. Within this letter, I’ve enclosed tickets to my next, and most likely final, ski competition. Hope to see you there. Love, James.

*[Chuck places the letter down on his desk.]*

*[James walks out into the snow. The entire audience jeers at him, except for his father. They exchange knowing glances, before James boards the lift towards the top of the mountain.]*

*[Inside the lift, there sits a large man in all-black, including a mask over his face.]*

**James Sarlyk:** May the best man win, eh? 

*[The man remains silent.]*

**James Sarlyk:** Or best yak, I suppose?

*[James laughs. The man remains silent and stationary.]*

*[James and the black-clothed man begin to ski down the mountain. Two sports commentators speak over the scene.]*

**Commentator #2:**Voice matches Orson Welles. And they’re off! Marking the first race in the Kooky Characters Tacky Ski League since Marcus Smith, known more popularly by his competitive persona of Neoliberal Yeti, won the finals last winter. Hey, can you tell us a bit about our competitors?

**Commentator #3:**Voice matches Bing Crosby. Sure! They’re both newcomers to the league. One is known as the Night Skier, and beyond that, we don’t know all that much. In fact, we were originally planning to reject their application to the league, before we received thousands of dollars from a mysterious source to keep him in. The other is the infamous James Sarlyk, offered a spot as work release. Just a disgusting, wretched “person” all around.

**Commentator #2:** Hear, hear, friend. Can we hear some boos from the crowd?

*[The camera cuts to the audience, yelling and throwing tomatoes at James. All the tomatoes fall limply on the snow, as James is hundreds of yards uphill.]*

*[The screen cuts back to James, approaching a dense group of trees.]*

**James Sarlyk:** *[Through gritted teeth]* Alright, it’s time. The yak style maneuver.

*[James pushes off the snow, into the air, and locks his horns into the closest tree. He pushes off, slingshotting himself into the next tree, which he manages to lock into the following tree. He continues to repeat this “yak style” maneuver, gaining great distance. With each iteration of this maneuver, the audience’s jeers grow quieter and quieter. The camera cuts to Chuck Sarlyk, upright in the stands.]*

**Chuck Sarlyk:** That’s my son! That’s my son!

*[The audience erupts into cheers. The camera cuts back to James, having made it out of the trees. He speeds ahead triumphantly, before a loud thud is heard behind him. He glances to see that The Night Skier has attempted the same, but as he is a hornless human and not a yak, has failed and now lies sprawled out on the snow.]*

*[James looks ahead and then back again, and then sighing, digs his poles into the snow. He slows to a halt. He bites through the tape attaching his hooves to the poles, and then takes off his board underneath. He trots upwards to the Night Skier.]*

**Commentator #3:** Could it be? He’s sacrificing the victory!

**Commentator #2:** He may be a yak, but by god, he’s got the heart of a man!

**Commentator #3:** Maybe bovines aren’t as bad as we think they are.

**Commentator #2:** Except for highland cows!

**Commentator #3:** Of course not, fuck highland cows!

**James Sarlyk:** I’ve had enough winning for one movie’s runtime. What say we finish this race together?

**The Night Skier:** Are you serious? You’d do that for me?

**James Sarlyk:** *[Smiling]* What good is winning without someone beside you to share in the spoils?

**The Night Skier:** Oh, you’re so kind! And so gullible.

*[The Night Skier removes their mask, revealing their identity to be Carvalho, now with cybernetic implants in their face.]*

**James Sarlyk:** It’s you? I thought I killed you!

**Carvalho:** You were wrong! The agency took me away that day you ripped open my lungs, and they rebuilt me: better, stronger, faster. The ultimate skier; I am “Punished Carvalho”! [sic]

**James Sarlyk:** You can’t be serious? This can’t be happening!

**Carvalho:** *[Chewing the scenery]* You thought this race was your comeback? Nay, it is your doom! 

*[Carvalho removes their right arm, revealing a rifle attached to their body underneath.]*

**James Sarlyk:** No, this isn’t how it was supposed to go! I was being sincere, I was a good person.

*[Carvalho shoots James through the skull. The camera cuts to the audience, entirely silent.]*

**Commentator #2:** Well, that’s going to leave a mark!

*[Chuck begins to weep, before the noise is drowned out by the rest of the audience erupting into raucous laughter.]*

*[The screen fades into a dark void, with the only persons present being Arthur Wilson on the piano, and an undead Elvis PresleyPlayed by Elvis Presley. sitting atop.]*

**Presley:** Wow, wasn’t that something! VKTM’s newest certainly is-

*[Presley takes a notecard out of his coat pocket.]*

**Presley:** A banger! Now, we’d like to give some recognition out, first off, thanks to Stephen Curry for letting us hijack his newest endeavor, next, thank you to whatever poor D-Class sitting in [an extra-dimensional prison](*https:*scp-wiki.wikidot.com/scp-9000) had to transcribe all of this, and finally, thank *you//, the viewer. We’ve prepared you a little ditty as a treat. Play it again, Sam!Red text repeatedly flashes on screen after this line, reading “REFERENCE TO THING YOU LIKE!!!!”

**Wilson:** What if I don’t want to, huh? You know they don’t even say that in the movie?

**Presley:** *[Deadpan]* Anything under capitalism, be it sincere or ironic, saccharine or vile, good or bad, even if it attempts to fight back, inadvertently ends up serving it. The most powerful devil is money, and it is entirely of our creation.

**Wilson:** Oh, I could never stay mad at you!

*[Wilson plays Casablanca’s As Time Goes By while the credits roll.]*Despite the film’s cast mainly comprising of celebrities from the 1940s, all roles are credited to various cast and crew members of the sports dramedy film *Juwanna Mann*.
