scp 8763

Bernard holds the future

Object class :Euclid                                 

Special containment procedures:

SCP-8763 is held in a 68 liter cylindrical glass aquarium at Provisional Site-19 in Virginia. Continuous camera surveillance is maintained. Dr. ~~-- ~~---- holds sole authorization for direct verbal interaction with the subject. All other personnel are restricted to remote observation. Any deviation in SCP-8763's behavior requires immediate activation of alert protocol “Circle Break.”

Description:

SCP-8763 is a common yellow, somewhat scaly fish approximately 11 inches in length, originally named Bernard by the civilian owner. Since recovery in June 1978, the subject has maintained continuous counter clockwise circular swimming without interruption.

SCP-8763 exhibits limited capability, reliably detecting harmful events approximately five minutes prior to occurrence. Upon detection, the subject slightly changes its swimming pattern and produces a series of “blub blub” sounds. These vocalizations register as ordinary fish noises to all personnel except Dr. ~~-~~ ~~-~~-, who perceives them as fluent English speech. The subject’s communications are consistently rude and irritable in tone.

Recovery Details:

Acquired on June 14, 1978, following multiple incidents at a pet store in valdosta georgia Reports included customer dizziness, falling shelves, and repeated medical emergencies involving the store owner. Mobile Task Force personnel secured the subject without major complications.

Documented Predictions:

- July 2, 1978: Rapid clockwise swimming accompanied by vocalizations. Dr. ~~-~~- reported the message “Gas leak in the generator room.” Maintenance confirmed and corrected the issue four minutes later.  

- May 11, 1978: Repeated impacts against the tank wall. Translation indicated an imminent accidental discharge of a sidearm during cleaning. The situation was neutralized.  

- october 25, 1978: No warning given during scheduled testing. When questioned, the subject reportedly stated that some personnel “deserve what’s coming.” The test resulted in injuries to two staff members.
