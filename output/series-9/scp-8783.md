**Special Containment Procedures:** During the period of manifestation, SCP-8783 is to be publicly advertised as the TSB Summer Festival of Lights within Pukekura Park, New Plymouth. Embedded Foundation personnel are to monitor for spatial desynchronisation events and redirect civilian flow away from identified anomalous zones while maintaining the perception of a normal cultural event. Light exhibits are to be rotated on an annual basis to fulfil the requirements for baseline dimensional stability, with at least one exhibit remaining consistently active throughout the duration of SCP-8783's manifestation.

Mobile Task Force Lambda-19 “Nightwatch” is assigned to identify and detain SCP-8783-2 instances embedded within civilian foot traffic under the cover of intoxicated or distressed persons requiring security removal. Following the cessation of public attendance, all detained SCP-8783-2 instances are to be released before 2:00 AM to maintain dimensional stability during subsequent SCP-8783 cycles.

**Description:** SCP-8783 is an annually recurring anomalous phenomenon that manifests over a five-week period between December and January within Pukekura Park, New Plymouth, Taranaki. During this period, SCP-8783 seamlessly overlaps our baseline reality with a second reality designated SCP-8783-1 inside SCP-8783's affected area. The interaction between both realities occupying the same physical space results creates a localised dimensional instability, forming distortion zones throughout the affected area.

These distortion zones commonly affect pedestrian pathways, water features, and high-density public areas. Testing has shown that artificial lighting significantly reduces the severity of these distortions, with areas containing a higher level of illumination remaining comparatively stable. As a result, the light-based exhibits installed for the publicly operated TSB Festival of Lights have been placed intentionally to function as stabilising anchors for SCP-8783's manifested phenomena. It is highly believed that these distortion zones allow SCP-8783-2 to manifest into baseline reality.

SCP-8783-1 refers to the secondary reality that overlaps with baseline reality during SCP-8783's manifestation. SCP-8783-1 appears to be accessible through small tears in reality where spatial distortion is the strongest. Research into SCP-8783-1 shows the reality appears to be in a state of dimensional collapse, relying on manifestation into the baseline reality to keep it consistently stable. Individuals originating from SCP-8783-1 are designated as SCP-8783-2 instances. 

SCP-8783-2 instances are docile humanoid entities appearing to lack major distinguishing features, exhibiting physical and behavioural traits broadly consistent with baseline humans, with the exception of absent eyes. SCP-8783-2 instances demonstrate no signs of hostility and typically remain passive unless directly displaced from areas of SCP-8783 overlap, at which point they attempt to return to zones affected by SCP-8783. All SCP-8783-2 instances within a distortion zone appear to demanifest once it reaches 2:00 AM. Should an SCP-8783-2 instance remain manifested, the frequency in which distortion zones appear increases in substantial levels throughout the duration of SCP-8783's manifestation.

**Addendum 8783-1 | Initial Discovery Log - 1953/12/23:**

Instances of SCP-8783's manifestations were first reported in December of 1953 following the illumination of a fountain and Poet's Bridge in Pukekura Park to celebrate a royal visit by Queen Elizabeth II. Local residents reported the presence of a large congregation of unidentified individuals gathered around Poet’s Bridge during late evening hours. These individuals were later designated SCP-8783-2 instances.

Foundation investigations led by Dr. K███ Parker resulted in the initial discovery of SCP-8783 and Pukekura Park being cordoned off from public access under the cover of emergency structural maintenance and electrical safety concerns. Pukekura Park would reopen to the public the following year under Foundation supervision.

The Foundation would later oversee the establishment of the publicly operated TSB Festival of Lights in 1993, with the event functioning under revised containment protocols designed to stabilise SCP-8783 activity through controlled illumination and sustained civilian presence.

| **Addendum 8783-2 | SCP-8783-1 Recovery Mission Video Log Transcript - 2001/01/14:** |
| --- |
**Foreword:** Investigation into a missing persons case resulted in the discovery of a confirmed entry point into SCP-8783-1. Mobile Task Force Lambda-19 “Nightwatch" was formed and dispatched to the point of entry under the supervision of Researcher Princi Barristen. Operatives were equipped with shoulder-mounted recording devices providing a continuous live feed and instructed to locate missing person Tama Kereama.

Transcribe log originates from Agent Vale's recording. No recorded fatalities.

**[BEGIN LOG]**

*Recording begins with heavy static interference before stabilising. Footage is displaying a live feed from Agent Vale's shoulder camera. Camera is currently displaying a live feed of a section of Pukekura Park sealed behind temporary fencing. A visible distortion can be seen within the illuminated trees in front of Vale.*

**Barristen:** "Radio check, can you hear me, Lambda-19?"

**Vale:** "Vale receiving."

**Karlson:** "Karlson, checking in."

**Christoff:** "Christoff here."

**Barristen:** "Good, entry point currently remains stable. You know the rules: retrieval only. We locate Tama Kereama, extract him and withdraw. Stay cautious, SCP-8783-2 instances could be more hostile in their own territory."

**Christoff:** "Cool. Love hearing that before we walk into the nightmare dimension."

**Karlson:** "You're the one carrying the shotgun."

**Barristen:** "Just keep the comms clear."

*Agent Vale approaches the distortion. The surrounding festival's noise begins to drown out as she gets closer. Civilian chatter and music fade into a distant echo.*

**Vale:** "Are we clear to enter?"

**Barristen:** "Proceed."

*Agent Vale enters through the tear, with Karlson and Christoff following suit. For a moment, the camera loses focus. Vale readjusts her camera and looks around. The environment remains recognisably Pukekura Park, though heavily altered. Light sources appear far dimmer than usual, and colour appears much more desaturated. The sky appears empty, with a notable lack of stars. Park walkways appear to stretch on further than they should.*

**Christoff:** "Holy hell... It's still the park!"

**Karlson:** "Imagine playing hide and seek in here."

**Vale:** "Definitely not the worst place we've been. How's the air here, Princi?"

**Barristen:** "Radiation and atmospheric readings remain consistent with our world's survival range. You should be able to proceed.

*The three operatives continue forward. Barristen can be heard muttering about the readings. Several humanoid figures become visible in the distance, gathering around one of the light attractions in the playground. As the group approach, it is clear the humanoids are SCP-8783-2 instances.*

**Christoff:** "Look up ahead, it's those weird guys."

*The SCP-8783-2 instances turn their heads to face the group.*

**Vale:** "They're looking at us."

**Karlson:** "Should we shoot 'em?"

**Barristen:** "Not yet. Only shoot if you feel like you're in danger."

*The SCP-8783-2 instances slowly begin moving toward the group. Lambda-19 back up; however, the instances walk past them, heading toward the illuminated fountain. One instance briefly brushes past Christoff.*

**Christoff:** "Holy hell, they're freezing!"

**Barristen:** "Just ignore it, please keep moving."

*Further into SCP-8783-1, festival decorations from multiple decades become visible simultaneously. Modern electric installations stand beside older incandescent displays and paper lanterns in varying states of decay.*

**Karlson:** "Barristen, are you seeing this?"

**Barristen:** "Indeed."

**Christoff:** "Dude, some of these displays shouldn't exist anymore. I haven't seen that one since I was a kid..."

**Barristen:** "We can come back and document everything later. Right now, focus on the missing person."

*Faint music becomes audible. A distorted orchestral arrangement consistent with festival performances recorded during the 1970s.*

**Christoff:** "You guys hearing this?"

**Vale:** "Yeah."

**Karlson:** "It sounds like it's coming from under the water."

*Lambda-19 continue forward, approaching the Poet's Bridge. Up ahead, multiple SCP-8783-2 instances can be seen gathered on the illuminated bridge.*

**Christoff:** "Say what you want... These guys have good taste."

**Karlson:** "Really? The bridge is always so crammed during the festival..."

**Vale:** "Focus, you two, look."

*Camera zoom partially adjusts. An adolescent male, later confirmed as Tama Kereama, is visible leaning on the railing of Poet's bridge beside several SCP-8783-2 instances.*

**Vale:** "We have visual on Tama."

**Barristen:** "Can you confirm his condition?"

**Vale:** "He's alive."

**Karlson:** "Yeah, but the kid is surrounded by those things."

**Barristen:** "Get him out of there."

**Christoff:** "Copy that!"

*Christoff raises his shotgun toward the SCP-8783-2 instances, waiting for Vale's orders to engage. The SCP-8783-2 instances turn to face the group. Tama runs forward and stands in between Lambda-19 and the SCP-8783-2 instances.*

**Tama:** "Wait, wait! Stop!"

**Christoff:** "Step away from them, kid!"

**Tama:** "Stop pointing those things at them!"

**Karlson:** "Kid, you need to come with us, now!"

*An SCP-8783-2 instance standing beside Tama lightly grips his sleeve. Tama does not react with fear.*

**Vale:** "Barristen, any suggestions?"

**Barristen:** "Hold fire. You could hit Tama."

**Christoff:** "But they're surrounding the kid!"

**Tama:** "Stop! They're just scared!"

*A moment of silence passes before Vale holds up her hand, motioning for the others to lower their guns. The SCP-8783-2 instances remain stationary.*

**Vale:** "Tama, please come with us."

**Tama:** "But... But... I don't want to leave them."

**Christoff:** "What..?"

**Tama:** "If I leave them, they'll get lonely, they'll think I've forgotten about them..."

**Christoff:** "You've talked to them?"

**Tama:** "They told me that when the festival ends, so do they. They only come here to see the lights."

*The team stands still for a moment. Vale takes off her helmet and approaches Tama, kneeling down in front of him.*

**Vale:** "Tama, you need to come home. Your mum is worried sick."

**Tama:** "But what about them?"

**Christoff:** "We'll figure out something, right?"

**Vale:** "You don't need to worry about them, we'll take care of them, okay?"

*Tama hesitates, taking a moment to look back to the group of SCP-8783-2. Tama puts his hand on the hand of the SCP-8783-2 instance, holding onto his sleeve.*

**Tama:** "Do you promise we won't forget them?"

**Vale:** "I promise."

*Vale holds her hand out to Tama for him to take. Tama hugs the SCP-8783-2 instance and then takes her hand. The SCP-8783-2 begin to clear the path for Lambda-19.*

**Christoff:** "They're letting us go."

**Karlson:** "Yeah, it's a relief for sure."

*Lambda-19 make their way to the breach point, Vale exiting first alongside Tama. Once through, Barristen can be seen waiting on the other side. The music from the stage can be heard once again.*

**Barristen:** "Alright, Tama, are you ready to head back home?"

*Tama lets go of Vale's hand and looks up toward Barristen.*

**Tama:** "Are we gonna keep the lights on?"

*Lambda-19 and Barristen share a look before Barristen replies.*

**Barristen:** "Yes, of course."

*Tama visibly relaxes. Karlson and Christoff take him to the medical tent on-site.*

**Vale:** "So, what's now?"

**Barristen:** "I think we should look at revising the containment procedures."

**[END LOG]**

**Afterword:** Following Addendum 8783-2, Foundation containment policy regarding SCP-8783 was formally revised. Ongoing maintenance and expansion of the TSB Festival of Lights was reclassified as essential to long-term stabilisation efforts concerning SCP-8783-1.

Tama Kereama was returned to his family following administration of Class-A amnestics. Subsequent interviews conducted prior to treatment indicate Tama retained consistent sympathy toward SCP-8783-2 instances and repeatedly requested confirmation that they would not be forgotten.

| **Addendum 8783-3 | Follow-Up Review Transcript - Dr. L. Krowefitt & Researcher P. Barristen** |
| --- |
**Foreword:** The following transcript was recorded approximately six hours after Incident 8783-1. Discussion occurred between Research Head L. Krowefitt and Researcher Princi Barristen regarding proposed revisions to SCP-8783 containment policy.

**[BEGIN LOG]**

*Beginning of the log expunged for brevity.*

**Krowefitt:** "I reviewed the footage Vale submitted."

**Barristen:** "And?"

**Krowefitt:** "I'm very impressed, you made a good call not shooting the dash twos."

*Barristen lets out a sigh of relief and chuckles for a moment.*

**Krowefitt:** "Just be glad it isn't Parker overseeing this anomaly."

**Barristen:** "What a relief. Lambda-19 was expecting something hostile in there."

**Krowefitt:** "Most people would."

**Barristen:** "What do you think?"

**Krowefitt:** "I think you made the right call. We're not in the business of terminating things just because they look weird, you said the boy wasn't hurt, right?"

**Barristen:** "The medics said he was just dehydrated, they gave him something to drink and eat before amnesticizing him."

**Krowefitt:** "Good."

*Several seconds pass with neither side saying a word.*

**Krowefitt:** "Celebrating a good rescue isn't the reason you asked me to chat, is it?"

**Barristen:** "When is it ever? The boy, he asked me if we were going to keep the lights on."

**Krowefitt:** "I heard."

**Barristen:** "I told him yes before I could even think about it fully."

**Krowefitt:** "Good."

*Barristen chuckles briefly under her breath.*

**Barristen:** "Lincoln, it's not exactly standard procedure to keep civilians exposed to this anomaly."

**Krowefitt:** "I know, but it's not every day we come across another dimension existing alongside ours."

**Barristen:** "And the dash twos? What's your take? Do you think they're people?"

**Krowefitt:** "Could be, regardless of if they are, we still treat them as anomalies. Even so, it's our duty to protect them too, right?"

**Barristen:** "Ever so wise."

**Krowefitt:** "Comes with age, or something like that."

*Krowefitt can be heard rustling the report papers.*

**Krowefitt:** "Your report mentions overlap stabilisation increasing near areas with larger civilian gatherings."

**Barristen:** "Yes, sir. The readings were consistent the entire operation. Crowded areas remained substantially more stable than isolated sections."

**Krowefitt:** "So the festival isn't just containing the anomaly, but instead sustaining SCP-8783-1?"

**Barristen:** "That's the working theory."

*Krowefitt exhales slowly.*

**Barristen:** "Sir?"

**Krowefitt:** "We've been managing this anomaly for nearly fifty years, and we never once considered that idea."

*Brief silence.*

**Barristen:** "What happens if we stop?"

*Krowefitt does not answer immediately.*

**Krowefitt:** "Realistically? If they're relying on us to sustain them... I think SCP-8783-1 could collapse completely within a few years. Maybe less."

**Barristen:** "And what about the instances?"

**Krowefitt:** "They'll be gone with it."

*Barristen leans back audibly in her chair.*

**Barristen:** "How are we going to justify that?"

**Krowefitt:** "We don't have to."

**Barristen:** "What do you mean?"

**Krowefitt:** "We'll revise the containment procedures. Maybe we can increase lighting coverage near the major civilian hotspots. Coordinate with local organisers and see if attendance expansion is feasible."

**Barristen:** "You're approving preservation efforts?"

**Krowefitt:** "Princi, above all else, we secure, contain, and most importantly, protect. If the Foundation can keep an entire world alive simply by keeping the lights on once a year, I think we can afford the power bill."

*Barristen laughs quietly.*

**Barristen:** "Hey, don't you think there might be a greater risk of exposure, you know.. breaking the veil?"

**Krowefitt:** "That's why you'll be in charge of figuring out how to move the anomaly to a secure location."

**Barristen:** "That's going to leave a mountain of paperwork."

**Krowefitt:** "Most worthwhile things do."

**Barristen:** "You really think this is worth it?"

**Krowefitt:** "Of course I do."

**Barristen:** "May I ask why?"

*Several seconds of silence follow.*

**Krowefitt:** "It's because... I used to attend the festival when I was younger."

**Barristen:** "Really?"

**Krowefitt:** "Yeah, as a boy, my parents brought me every December, right before the New Year."

**Barristen:** "Hm, I see. Must hit close to home then."

**Krowefitt:** "Sure does. Is it a strange feeling, you know? Knowing they were all here with us the entire time."

**Barristen:** "Do you think the SCP-8783-2 instances understand what we're doing?"

**Krowefitt:** "I like to think so. Anyways, just make sure the lights stay on, Barristen."

**Barristen:** "Yes, sir."

**[END LOG]**
