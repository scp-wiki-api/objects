**Item #:** SCP-8829

**Object Class:** Zeno

**Special Containment Procedures:**  The Rocky Mountain Seafood Company has been annexed under terms of the NAPPRANorth American Paranatural Phenomena Regulation Alliance. treaty. Present and former employees are to abide by confidentiality agreements under threat of detention.  Mining and commercial operations to be continued under direct management of the Special Collections Program.  Use of penal labor is authorized.

The main containment site and Resolute Station are currently under quarantine.  Personnel and employees are not to cross the designed perimeter without direct permission from HMCL supervisor.  Individuals who display hard, irregular growths on the skin are to be isolated as a matter of urgency.

Meat excavated from SCP-8829 is cleared for sale to [major grocery chains](*scp-8796 ) as a food product.

**Description:**  The Rocky Mountain Seafood Company was established 1970 by Rufus Muller and operated at low capacity, excavating no more than 10 tons of product monthly. Larry Weiss, son-in-law of the Founder, took over operations in 1989 -- at this point the scope of mining began to gradually expand.

Company was annexed in 1997 following a disaster in which the existing mining site was completely destroyed, with over 300 estimated casualties. Destroyed infrastructure has since been reconstructed and expanded. Resolute Station was constructed nearby to manage transportation of persons and materials in and out of the containment zone.

SCP-8829 refers to an extensive tunnel system in the Canadian Rockies, located north of the River Saskatchewan. Environment within the tunnels can be loosely divided into 3 layers: littoral, pelagic and benthic.

The littoral layer resembles an abnormally large natural cave system. Tunnels occasionally converge into large caverns; these often contain shallow pools of standing briny water.

The most common organism in this layer is a type of sessile crustacean superficially resembling the goose barnacle.  These are found attached to the cave walls or the shells of other organisms, and produce a soft but unceasing crackling noise which can be heard throughout the tunnels. 

Very rarely, one of these organisms will disgorge an animate entity with the approximate shape of a terrestrial animal, which will then begin to aimlessly wander through SCP-8829.  These entities are composed of frothy seawater encased in a very thin organic membrane.  They are harmless and may be disposed of or ignored at discretion of personnel.

Other organisms resembling crabs and lobsters typically found on the pacific coast of North America are also occasionally present.  Behavior is lethargic, sustenance apparently unneeded.  Flesh rapidly decays when removed from the tunnel system. In lieu of normal reproductive behavior, upon molting, the molted shell will regenerate into a full organism over the course of several hours.

A number of subterranean facilities for research, manufacturing and meat-processing have been constructed in the littoral layer. An extensive network of rail tracks has been installed for transport of excavated tissue. Further developments are currently on hold pending reevaluation of project.

The following sub-items are present within the littoral layer:

**Item #:** SCP-8829.11 ("MEAT")

**Object Class:** Juvenalis

**Special Containment Procedures:** MEAT is to be sterilized, fully drained of blood and cooked to an internal temperature of 180 F prior to consumption.  Drainage channels are required to minimize accumulation of blood at work sites.  Blood to be transported in sealed barrels and disposed of in designated dumping sites within SCP-8829.

Essential personnel and security staff should be equipped with filtration equipment during mining operation to limit exposure to blood vapors.  Laborers should be rotated off excavation teams at first sign of symptoms.

**Description:** MEAT refers to large deposits of organic material surrounding the SCP-8829 tunnel system.   Tissue resembles that of a crab or lobster and is typically encased in a chitinous shell. Genetic material is absent, as are specialized organ systems.  

Blood is present.  MEAT blood is bright blue in color, produces a strong coppery scent and is rich in unknown organic compounds.  Chronic inhalation of these compounds causes a poorly understood condition characterized by difficulty breathing, electrolyte imbalance, strange dreams and ultimately psychotic episodes in which the victim seeks  to drown in any available pool of fluid.  Most victims will recover from acute symptoms after 2-3 weeks of non-exposure.  Long term effects presently unknown

Portions of a MEAT deposit which face into an open tunnel develop appendages, orifices and sensory organs resembling those of a crab or lobster, in varied sizes and no particular pattern.  Visual organs will track movement, and ~~ if arrangement permits ~~ MEAT deposits will attempt to use claw-like appendages to transport organic material within reach toward mouth-like orifices. Despite this behavior, orifices do not lead to any interior cavity.

Deposits in the pelagic and benthic layers occasionally lack the typical chitinous shell. These naked deposits may display appendages, orifices and sensory organs of non-crustacean animals.  Personnel are advised not to investigate cries for help in the lower layers unless a missing person has been reported, as these deposits are occasionally capable of echolalia.

**Item #:** SCP-8829.12 ("DROOLERS")

**Object Class:** Hippocrates

**Special Containment Procedures:** Preservation of remaining DROOLER entities is to be prioritized.  Each is to be kept in a dedicated holding vat within an established outpost.  Extraction of mucus to be curtailed to 8 l per DROOLER per day, and paused for a 3 hour period following each disturbance event.

Export of mucus for food production and medical use is currently suspended, as the full amount is required to maintain mining operations.

**Description:** DROOLERS are boneless organisms with a roughly humanoid body shape, originally found throughout the littoral layer.  Skin is entirely unbroken mucus membrane, lacking any form of orifice.

DROOLERS continuously secrete salty mucus from their entire bodies unless they are entirely immersed in it. While not immersed, they move very slowly downhill.  Typically, they will find a depression in the tunnel system and convert it to a standing pool of mucus.  While fully immersed, DROOLERS become completely inert except during disturbance events.

Disturbance events occur periodically once a DROOLER has established a significant pool of mucus.  Stages of event are as follows.

* A point near the edge of the pool begins to bubble and ripple without visible cause.

* DROOLER quickly migrates to the location of disturbance and contorts its body to engulf the area.

* Loud snapping and popping noises are audible for a period of 1-5 minutes.

* Mucus pool becomes tainted with copper and ammonia compounds, producing a blue-green tint and unpleasant odor. Fragments of chitin or organic tissue may appear.

* Event ends, DROOLER returns to center of pool.

* Lingering contaminants in the pool vanish within 1-2 hours following event.

Internal structure of DROOLER is composed entirely of lipids and connective tissue.  Bodily integrity is easily compromised -- internal contents can leak significantly through small breaks in membrane.  Additionally, sustained production of mucus will eventually lead to a major rupture.  The 7 DROOLERS currently in containment are believed to be the only surviving instances. Research into producing additional specimens is ongoing.

The beginning of the pelagic layer is marked by a drastic increase in the scope and complexity of the tunnel system.   Width of tunnels becomes more uniform and caverns less common. Some tunnel walls are lined with thick chitinous plating, others have veins of unusual rock not found elsewhere in the region. Pools and streams of briny water or crab blood can occasionally be found.  Barnacle-like organisms are present, but much rarer than in littoral layer.  Other organisms are largely absent.  

A rail system has been constructed to facilitate rapid transport of personnel and MEAT between the littoral and pelagic layers.  No residential facilities have been constructed due to the difficulty of storing rations.  One outpost was constructed but has since been lost, see Item 8829.33.

The following sub-items are encountered within the pelagic layer:

**Item #:** SCP-8829.21 ("RIPTIDE")

**Object Class:** Hypatia

**Special Containment Procedures:** Equipment and personnel passing through regions of known activity are to be externally marked with DROOLER mucus. Markings must be reapplied on an hourly basis.  Excavation teams are to travel with an open canister of mucus and a sealed backup canister in case the first is lost or damaged.

Reports of poltergeist activity in nearby towns and camp sites are to be carefully monitored, especially if associated with a fishy or briny scent.

**Description:** RIPTIDE is a disembodied kinetic force present within the pelagic layer.  Sometimes experienced as a gale-force wind downward toward the depths of the tunnels, sometimes as a vortex which dashes objects and persons against the walls.

Arrival is preceded by a distinct odor resembling that of an exposed tidal pool, which can be detected even through the copper scent produced during MEAT excavation. Occasionally leaves behind a residue of viscus, salty ectoplasm.

Effectively repelled by DROOLER mucus.

**Appendix 8829.21.1:** Following collapse of DROOLER population in littoral layer, RIPTIDE activity has been noted throughout SCP-8829 and, rarely, in above-ground locations near entrances to the cave system.  Situation to be monitored for further developments.

**Item #:** SCP-8829.22 ("CRAWLERS")

**Object Class:** Hypatia

**Special Containment Procedures:**  All personnel stationed in the pelagic layer are to be equipped with pesticide sprayers in order to manage swarm events. Laborers are to be provided with N95 respirators to minimize exposure.

Excavated MEAT is to be transported out of the pelagic layer with haste in sealed, refrigerated containers.  Containers to be left sealed for at least an hour after exiting the layer, to allow any CRAWLERS to expire.

If a swarm event occurs during excavation, security personnel are to prioritize safe extraction of laborers. Loss of 2-3 individuals will typically spiral into loss of the entire team.

**Description:**  CRAWLERS are organisms with a body plan similar to a centipede and components similar to a shrimp.  Each body segment is about 3 cm long and contains at least two legs, one claw and one mouth-like orifice on the underside. Number of segments varies wildly.  No sensory organs are present. Organic material which enters the mouths appears to vanish completely.

An impending swarm event can be anticipated by a drop in temperature, a drastic increase in humidity, and the pervasive smell and taste of salt.  During a swarm event, large numbers of CRAWLERS will be spontaneously generated from non-living organic materialFor example, excavated MEAT or deceased personnel. in the area and rapidly devour it.  Once the material which spawned them is gone, these CRAWLERS will attempt to convert any nearby living organic matter to non-living organic matter.  If successful, this will produce a new swarm of CRAWLERS.  

Within 20-40 minutes, the event will end. All CRAWLERS produced by it will then cease activity and begin to rapidly decompose.  Prior to this, it is very difficult to destroy a CRAWLER.  Specialized pesticide spray is instead used to paralyze them for the duration of the event.

Benthic layer is currently inaccessible; available information comes from unmanned mapping efforts prior to 2010.  Most tunnels are encased in chitin of unknown thickness and extremely large naked MEAT deposits can be found in the lower areas.  Layout of tunnels does not remain entirely consistent over time.  Some regions are entirely flooded with crab blood.  A variety of unknown organisms and entities are believed to be present.

The following sub-items are encountered in or related to the benthic layer:

**Item #:** SCP-8829.31 ("SLEEPER")

**Object Class:** Cogito

**Special Containment Procedures:**  Further exposure of personnel to SLEEPER's effect is to be avoided.

In the event of recontainment, SLEEPER is to be kept in an unmanned facility, above-ground and at least 2 km away from the nearest human habitation.

**Description:** First described in 1999, successfully extracted from benthic layer in 2004 via unmanned drone based on guidance from affected individuals.  Briefly contained in isolation vault in Resolute Station following extraction.  Currently occupies benthic layer.

SLEEPER is a nauplius larva of unknown species. Lifespan and resilience to harm are abnormally high.  No feeding behavior or passing of waste has been observed since discovery. Possesses spacial awareness of its immediate environment which does not appear linked to any physical sense.  An unknown parasite has infected the lower left dorsal region of the larva, causing discomfort.

Individuals who enter the benthic layer while SLEEPER is present immediately gain detailed awareness of SLEEPER and lose awareness of other concepts, including:

* Personal identity

* Linear time

* Social behavior

* Above-ground space

This has proved extremely detrimental to mining operations.  Effect also applies to those in the presence of SLEEPER outside of benthic layer.  Range of effect is presently unknown.  Some affected individuals have made limited progress toward reacquiring awareness of lost concepts.  No progress has been made toward excluding awareness of SLEEPER.

This effect can be temporarily blocked by a lead barrier with thickness of at least 0.5 m.

**Item #:**  SCP-8829.32 ("SINGER")

**Object Class:** Plato

**Special Containment Procedures:**  All personnel are to evacuate SCP-8829 and shelter in designated areas during periods of SINGER activity.  Visual and audio recording systems are to be deactivated during these periods.  Designated witnesses who have already observed SINGER and experienced its effect are to monitor its activities during this time.

**Description:** First encountered 2004, following initial containment of SLEEPER.  SINGER emerged from the benthic layer and proceeded rapidly upward through SCP-8829, unaffected by physical obstructions and other attempts at deterrence.  SCP-8829 was fully evacuated and surveillance equipment was deactivated once properties of entity were determined.  Contact with Resolute Station was lost before evacuation could be completed.

SLEEPER-affected personnel reported that SLEEPER was transported back to the benthic layer shortly following the emergence of SINGER from SCP-8829.  All personnel at Resolute Station were found dead following incident; lungs were filled with crab blood.

SINGER is an apparition described as resembling a very large, levitating, disembodied hepatopancreas.  It continuously leaks an unknown green fluid from a small tear on its left side.  Fluid boils on contact with air. The sound of claws clicking and rhythmically scrapping against rock can be heard in its vicinity.  

Despite the above, all witnesses insist that SINGER is a type of crab, and that the sound it produces is a type of song.

Individuals who visually perceive SINGER lose the ability to visually perceive other objects and phenomena.  Individuals who perceive the sounds it produces lose the ability to perceive other sounds.  These effects also apply to recording systems. Individuals who have been physically touched or passed through by SINGER continuously secrete a substance resembling seawater from tear glands, leading to discomfort and eventual blindness.

Following initial encounter, SINGER has remained active in the benthic layer.  Approximately once a month, generally on a full or new moon, will migrate to the surface.  Upon reaching surface, will travel through the air to the peak of a nearby mountain, arriving at the moment of high tide.  An entity resembling a shelled mollusc will then fall from the sky. SINGER will catch this entity and fully engulf it before retracing its path back to the benthic layer.

These events strongly correlate with spikes in water-related deaths and injuries throughout North America.  Causal link is unclear.

**Item #:** SCP-8829.33 ("OUTPOST 7")

**Object Class:** Unknown

**Special Containment Procedures:** OUTPOST 7 is believed to be non-operative, although this cannot be confirmed.  All sealed tunnels are to be continuously monitored for signs of activity.

**Description:** OUTPOST 7 was previously established at the bottom of the pelagic layer to facilitate unmanned expeditions into the benthic layer.  However, during the first emergence of SINGER, the benthic layer expanded considerably such that OUTPOST 7 is now entirely within it.

OUTPOST 7 continued to operate despite all personnel presumably being subject to the effect of SLEEPER.  Limited communication was reestablished over the course of several months, leading to the temporary establishment of mining operations in the benthic layer.  SCP-8829's output of food product approximately doubled during this time.

Personnel stationed at OUTPOST 7 presented with physical abnormalities.  Hemoglobin in blood was replaced with hemocyanin, resulting in a blue-green colour.  Skin was marked by barnacles which secreted salty fluid -- these proved to be tumorous growths, rather than parasites.  Most were not capable of speech.  Frequent delivery of supplementary personnel proved necessary to maintain mining operations.

In October of 2008, following a national election and corresponding shift in leadership at the Program, the turnover of personnel at OUTPOST 7 was deemed excessive and delivery of new laborers was halted.  This led to a breakdown in communications as physical condition of OUTPOST 7 personnel deteriorated.

During May of 2009, humanoid entities believed to be former OUTPOST 7 personnel began to attack mining parties in the pelagic layer, incapacitating security personnel through unknown means and dragging laborers into the benthic layer.  

In response to these raids, a low yield nuclear weapon was detonated in hopes of eliminating OUTPOST 7 with minimal structural damage to the installation or cave system.  Detonator failed to initiate a thermonuclear reaction; warhead was lost.  Conventional explosives were used to entomb OUTPOST 7 and seal off the benthic layer as a fallback plan.

The issues with OUTPOST 7 and associated drop in revenue led to an internal investigation of the SCP-8829 project.  General Murray assumed direct control of operations and directed resources toward expansion of mining.

Recruitment of civilians from surrounding towns was authorized to supplement labor pool.  Rail systems and underground storage were expanded. Increasing the labor force three-fold proved sufficient to restore the mining capacity lost with OUTPOST 7.

An escalation of paranatural activity in the region has been noted following this intensification of mining efforts, including:

* Trace amounts of hemocyanin found in rainfall throughout western Alberta and eastern British Columbia.

* A previously undescribed form of skin cancer, resembling the barnacle-like growths found on OUTPOST 7 personnel, occasionally appears in both personnel stationed at SCP-8829 and more rarely in residents of nearby towns.

* Between 2 and 5 am on April 6th, 2010, taps and faucets in Nordegg, Alberta produced strong-smelling briny water.

* Sightings of OUTPOST 7 personnel in the lower pelagic layer are occasionally reported by laborers who become temporarily separated from their group.  All tunnels to benthic layer confirmed to remain sealed.

* On October 28th, 2011, a swarm event occurred in a staff cafeteria in Resolute Station.  CRAWLERS were generated from cafeteria rations, leading to 21 fatalities among station staff and security personnel.  No other swarm events outside SCP-8829 have been recorded.

* General Murray was found to have drowned in his California residence on October 20, 2012.  Corpse was lying on the floor with face immersed in a large bowl of seawater.

The above containment procedures are currently under review.  A new head officer has yet to be selected.
