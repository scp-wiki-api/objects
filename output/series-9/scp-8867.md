### Special Containment Procedures

SCP-8867 is currently uncontained. All of SCP-8867's activities are monitored by fujo.aic; any posts containing potentially sensitive information are to be flagged and Foundation staff are to be alerted.

In the event of a data breach that could affect the integrity of the Veil, targeted misinformation is to be disseminated to enforce the idea that all content produced by SCP-8867 is its original creation and bears no relation to real Foundation activities.

### Description

SCP-8867 is the collective designation for a pair of social media accounts on the sites Twitter and Archive of our Own, both of which use the handle "ShipCutePairs". Attempts to identify the individual operating these accounts have been unsuccessful, as have attempts made to delete or disable said accounts.

Content posted by SCP-8867 consists of RPF, or “real person fiction”, A genre of fan-fiction written about public figures, celebrities, or other real-life people as opposed to fictional characters. depicting a romantic and sexual relationship between Foundation employees Vincent Smith and Chris Wesson. However, SCP-8867 describes the content it produces as “oc”, (or "original content"), and claims that both the affected employees and the Foundation are their own fictional creations. Followers of SCP-8867 accounts believe its activities to be entirely non-anomalous.   

The specific narrative details of material posted by SCP-8867 vary, but all depict some level of erotic content, with plots typically consisting of "slice of life"A term for material that depicts everyday activities as opposed to large overarching storylines. or "PWP"Which stands for "Porn Without Plot", a fanfiction term for stories primarily written for the purpose of sexual gratification. style stories. SCP-8867 displays accurate knowledge of events and personal details of the persons included within these stories, and often incorporates these details in its posts.

#### Addendum Two: Containment Attempts

Foundation Cybersecurity personnel began attempts to trace the user of SCP-8867's accounts following the initial discovery. Attempts at IP tracing were unsuccessful, with the associated IPs changing frequently. Attempts at determining the account's ISP (Internet Service Provider) were also unsuccessful, with no records of the user being found on any major databases.

Failing to locate a user, Foundation personnel embedded within Twitter moderation staff issued a ban to SCP-8867’s account, causing the account to go offline for approximately one hour. It subsequently reappeared on the site under the same handle, with no alteration or deletion of its contents.
