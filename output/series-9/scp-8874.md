**Special Containment Procedures:** The habitat of SCP-8874 has been designated the Palliser Provincial Nature Preserve with a hard exclusion perimeter maintained in cooperation with Alberta Parks and Environment and Climate Change Canada. Civilian access is restricted. Population surveys of SCP-8874 instances are to be conducted bi-annually.

Direct containment of SCP-8874 instances is not currently pursued. Any confirmed GOI-012Marshall, Carter, and Dark interest in SCP-8874 is to be escalated to the assigned Mobile Task Force Mu-3 ("Highest Bidders") command immediately.

---

**Description:** SCP-8874 is a population of anomalous humanoid organisms native to the Rocky Mountain foothills of Alberta, Canada. Current population is estimated between thirty and forty individuals.Down from an estimated peak of ~8500 spanning much of Alberta and Saskatchewan SCP-8874 instances stand from 2.4 to 3.1 meters tall. The gross morphology of SCP-8874 is bipedal and humanoid. Their tissue is white and irregularly dense. The arrangement of underlying musculature is known to vary between instances. 

The resting facial expression of SCP-8874 instances is an open display of the teeth. This expression does not vary with behavioral state. It has been observed across activities ranging from feeding and aggression to rest and flight response.

SCP-8874 instances maintain a pack social structure and are capable of coordinate behavior and low-frequency vocalization. SCP-8874 is not currently considered to be sapient. 

Under conditions of acute resource scarcity, SCP-8874 instances have been documented engaging in intraspecific predation. Available evidence indicates this is a stress response rather than a normative behavior.

**Addendum 8874-1: Embedded Asset Report**

The following log was recovered from the personal effects of Agent Ahmad Harroway (MTF Mu-3 ("Highest Bidders"), Personal Designation µ-12), upon his extraction from the GOI-012 operation on ██/██/2024. 

**TO:** Agent Ahmad Harroway, µ-12

**FROM** ███ ███████

**RE:** GOI-012 Operation, Kananaskis, Alberta

Your insertion into the MC&D Apex Experience operation has been approved. Primary objective is intelligence gathering on MC&D's acquisition framework for anomalous wildlife. Secondary objective is population assessment of SCP-8874.  You are not authorized to intervene in MC&D activities under any circumstances. Maintain cover at all costs. 

Standard extraction protocol applies.

---

****Entry One****

Flew into Calgary this morning on the MC&D charter out of Toronto. Private terminal, no signage anywhere. There were three black vehicles on the tarmac when we landed. Nobody around here introduces themselves.

The clients came in across a two hour window after that. MC&D had them processed separately, one group at a time. I counted eleven total not including security. I will note them as they become relevant. 

The first one I noticed was the man MC&D had designated KODIAK. He was the first to arrive. Former head of government, North American. He had a woman with him that nobody introduced. She's famous. I recognized her immediately and I think everyone else did too and nobody said anything about it. She looked out the window the whole transfer. KODIAK talked the whole transfer. I don't think he noticed the difference. 

The MC&D lead is called STILLS. He's not a client. He gave a briefing during the helicopter ride in. Threw in all the buzzwords about a "curated experience" and "apex thrills". The clients listened the way you listen to a safety demonstration on a commercial flight. STILLS didn't seem to mind.

We set down at forward camp around 1340. The equipment was already staged when we arrived. I have worked armory intake at Site-█ and this is more built up than that. 

The valley is visible from camp. SCP-8874 habitat starts at the tree line below. At this distance it looks like any other part of the Kananaskis.  

KODIAK stood at the ridge for a long time after we landed. Just looking down at it. One of the other clients came and stood next to him and said something I couldn't hear. KODIAK laughed. 

---

****Entry Two****

MC&D took the group to an observation ridge above the valley at 0800. STILLS called it the first phase of the experience. Everyone was given binoculars. The good kind. I don't know what they cost. 

I saw ~~3~~,~~4~~,~~7~~,9 instances in the valley below. They were moving through the tree line in a loose group. At this distance the proportions read wrong before anything else does. Each instance has this shambling gait that makes it seem like it hurts them to walk.

One instance turned toward the ridge and I saw its face properly for the first time. The mouth was open with large flat teeth that stretch back towards their occipital bone. It was carrying something in its mouth. I couldn't make out what. 

TITAN was standing to my left. He is a bald stocky man built up on a cocktail of HGHHuman Growth Hormone and an army of personal trainers. He had his binoculars up for about thirty seconds before he lowered them and muttered to nobody in particular.

Nobody responded.

CAMP was two people to my right. She came up here with KODIAK but she doesn't move like she's with him. Famous in the way my mom would know. She didn't use her binoculars. Just stood there with them hanging around her neck looking down at the valley. I couldn't read her face from where I was standing. 

I kept my head down through most of it. Did my own quiet survey of the valley during the observation period. No elk. No moose. No visible game of any kind. 

---

****Entry Three****

The drive started at 0600. Four helicopters total including ours. STILLS coordinated over comms the whole time. It was a very clean operation, I'll give them that much.

The SCP-8874 instances heard us before they saw us.  They started moving fast, faster than I expected from something that size, pushing deeper into the valley. The helicopter I was in came in low on the eastern flank. Our job was to make enough noise to drive them out of the thickest parts of the woods. 

They're not stupid. That's the thing I kept coming back to. They knew the shape of what was happening even if they didn't know what it was. They were trying to find the edges of it. Every time they pushed in one direction there was another helicopter waiting to make noise and drop flares. After the first forty minutes they stopped trying the edges and just kept moving. 

The pack started coming apart around the ninety minute mark. They seemed to be getting worn out. An instance snapping at another that came too close. Two larger ones cutting a smaller one off from the group for a few minutes before it caught back up. They kept baring their teeth the whole time in those awful fixed expressions. 

We set down back at camp around 0900 for a refuel. I was standing by the equipment line when I heard one of the other security guys, ex-military type, talking to the MC&D handler about the valley.

He asked why there was nothing down there. No game, nothing.

The handler said that was intentional. Said the advance team had spent three weeks making sure of it. Said it was part of the package. Something about it distracting them from the experience. 

---

****Entry Four****

They brought us to the blinds at 0700. Elevated, maybe 15 meters above the valley floor. Good sightlines. MC&D built them recently. The wood still smelled like sawdust. 

There were two SCP-8874 instances below us. We had been tracking them since the drive yesterday. The larger one had a bad shoulder with this awful sepia-toned hissing fluid leaking from it. It was moving differently than it had been, more carefully, keeping the left side angled away from things. It had figured out how to compensate.

The smaller instance stayed close to it. They moved together along the valley floor for a long time without doing anything. Just existing. Eventually the smaller SCP-8874 instance stumbled and made a sound I hadn't heard before, low and long. The larger one stopped walking and turned towards it. 

The larger instance  was thin. I hadn't noticed how thin until now, this close. The skin was shifting and clinging to the ribcage. You could see the structure of it too clearly. It had been a long time since either of them had eaten anything. 

The small one steadied itself and went down. Just sat down in the dirt and didn't get back up. The remaining instance scrambled over and covered the deceased instance's face with one hand while the other hand struck its own head and chest repeatedly. It was making this horrible dissonant wailing noise. It stayed this way for several minutes. 

It then began to consume the deceased instance's body. 

TITAN said, "Oh my god, it's like a buffet." 

The people in the blind around us laughed. STILLS reminded them that the first day of the hunt began at 0600 tomorrow. KODIAK asked if there would be drinks tonight. There would be. 

---

****Entry Five****

Staged at 0500. Fourteen instances in the kill zone. STILLS ran the briefing. Ten minutes out.

I have attached the relevant radio log and optical record below.

****Entry Six****

Cleanup took two hours. Trophy photographs after that.

STILLS ran a headcount. Thirteen confirmed. One unaccounted for. He noted it on comms and moved on. 

KODIAK had his photograph taken standing over the large instance at the top of the stairs. Someone held his rifle up for him so he could hold it properly for the shot. He hadn't fired it once.

The helicopters loaded at 1400. I did the client sweep. Checked the platform, checked the blinds, checked the equipment line. 

I was on the eastern side of the platform when I saw it. On the ridge above the tree line. Just standing there. It had come back. 

It was a long way up. High enough that I couldn't make out the face, but I knew what it was doing. 

The helicopters lifted up at 1430. I watched that ridge the whole way up. It watched us back. It stayed there until we were out of sight.
