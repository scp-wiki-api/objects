**Item #:** SCP-8923

**Object Class:** Euclid 

**Special Containment Procedures:** 

Annual exams are required to test for SCP-8923, regardless of how "humiliated" personnel may feel.

SCP-8923-01 instances are to be contained in standard humanoid containment cells with soundproof lining capable of muffling 20 db.¬†¬†Automatic sound sensors are required to be tuned to 5 db, triggering Class A Amnestic to be dispensed via ventilation.

Every week, instances should be provided 60-100 color dot sticker sheet, and standard rations daily. Felted uniforms should be dispensed yearly.

Perfectly tessellating patterns should be removed from any SCP-8923-01's containment area, including ventilation grates and woven clothing.

All Foundation Sites are now required to post Infographic-8923-03 at eye level, unobstructed, in a high traffic area accessible to all personnel. 

High traffic areas may include break rooms, common rooms, or cafeterias.

**Description:**

SCP-8923 is a contagious cognitohazard which affects primarily humans.¬†¬†Organisms who have strong pattern recognition, such as chimpanzees, dogs, cats, elephants and pigs are not affected. Parrots, crows, and one raven have been infected with SCP-8923-01. All animal instances of SCP-8923-01 after testing are to be euthanized.

SCP-8923 renders the perception of affected individuals to 'flip' noise and patterns. The importance of seemingly random, anisotropic stimuli increases, while interest in patterns lowers. SCP-8923 progresses in 5 stages.

SCP-8923 spreads when an instance of SCP-8923-01 sings, regardless of tone, pitch, or tune. Listeners who are within eyesight of SCP-8923 are infected 99.9%. The listener may be in another room, building, or dimension, at a rate of 87.4% SCP-8923 is transmitted if the audio is "live". The singer during this time shows no anomalous properties, and recordings of singing are non-anomalous. SCP-8923 may also spread through poetry, recited prose, or screaming, though listeners are only 45.5% likely to contract SCP-8923 through non-melodic audio.
